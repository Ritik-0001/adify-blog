## What Exists Under ₹500

At under ₹500 in India, electric toothbrushes are typically:

**Unbranded or generic sonic brushes:** Chinese-manufactured battery-powered brushes with no clinical backing, unknown bristle quality, and no replacement head ecosystem. Available on Amazon and Flipkart from unrecognised brands.

**Battery-powered oscillating (no brand name):** Basic rotating brushes with AA batteries — functional at a mechanical level, but with no clinical studies, uncertain durability, and no customer support.

**What you will not find under ₹500:** Any brush from Oral-B, Philips Sonicare, Colgate, Beurer, Oclean, or Mi. No rechargeable models. No pressure sensor. No 2-minute timer in most.

## Should You Buy an Electric Toothbrush Under ₹500?

### The Case For
- Still mechanically superior to manual brushing in most cases
- Vibration helps break up plaque regardless of brand prestige
- Zero risk trial for first-time users
- Appropriate for short-term use (travel, temporary)

### The Case Against
- Unknown bristle quality — hard bristles damage gums
- No replacement head system — entire brush must be replaced when bristles wear (every 3 months)
- No clinical evidence of effectiveness
- Total cost over 12 months may exceed buying a ₹999 branded model (replacement cost)
- Durability is typically poor — many fail within 3–6 months
