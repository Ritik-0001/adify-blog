## What Makes an Earphone Good for Calls?

Most earphone reviews focus on music quality. But call performance is a completely different engineering challenge. Here is what matters for clear calls:

**Microphone count and placement:** More microphones allow better voice pickup and background noise filtering. Single-mic earphones pick up ambient noise. Dual-mic ENC setups are significantly better. Professional earphones like the Jabra use 6+ microphones with beamforming.

**Environmental Noise Cancellation (ENC):** This is microphone-side noise suppression — it removes background noise from what callers hear when you speak. Different from ANC (which removes noise for your own ears). ENC is the critical feature for call quality.

**Active Noise Cancellation (ANC):** Helps you hear callers better in noisy environments by reducing background noise reaching your ears. Important in busy offices, cafes, and commutes.

**Microphone frequency response:** Good call microphones are tuned for voice clarity (300Hz–3kHz range) rather than music reproduction.

## What About TWS vs Neckband for Calls?

This is a common question. The short answer:

**TWS earbuds** are more convenient — no neckband, fully wireless, pocket-sized case. But call quality is often slightly worse because the microphones are on small earbuds far from your mouth.

**Neckband earphones** sit closer to your jawline when the earbuds dangle, allowing microphones to pick up voice more directly. They also offer longer battery life and are harder to lose. Many professional-grade call earphones (including the Jabra Evolve 65e) use the neckband form factor for exactly this reason.

For dedicated call use, especially if you are on calls for 2+ hours daily, a neckband with a good microphone is often the better choice.
