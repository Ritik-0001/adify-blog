## Buying Guide

### Panel Types Explained

**IPS (In-Plane Switching)** is the best all-purpose panel type at this price. Accurate colours, wide viewing angles (178°), and consistent brightness across the screen. Ideal for students, office workers, and anyone sharing their screen. Most monitors in this list use IPS for good reason.

**VA (Vertical Alignment)** panels have higher contrast ratios (3000:1 vs 1000:1) but narrower viewing angles. The better choice if you watch a lot of movies or use your monitor in a dim room. ViewSonic VA2215-H is the VA option in this list.

**TN (Twisted Nematic)** panels are the oldest technology — fast response times but poor colour and narrow viewing angles. Mostly found in the cheapest monitors; avoid for primary use if IPS is available at a similar price.

### Resolution Guide

At 22 inches, 1080p (1920×1080) looks sharp and clear. Every monitor in this list runs 1080p — the right choice at this screen size and price point. You won't feel the need for higher resolution on a 22" budget monitor. The 108 PPI pixel density at 22" is comfortable for most tasks.

### Other Key Features

**Connectivity:** HDMI is essential for modern laptops and systems. VGA is a legacy standard but still useful for older desktops common in Indian offices and cyber cafes. DisplayPort (Dell E2222H) gives the best signal quality but requires an adapter for HDMI-only devices.

**Eye Care:** Flicker-free backlighting and blue light filters are standard on most monitors listed here. For students doing long sessions, prioritise these — they genuinely reduce eye fatigue during the kind of extended screen time that entrance exam prep demands.

**Power Supply:** All monitors listed are compatible with India's 230V AC supply. The included adapters are universal — no additional converter or stabiliser needed under normal conditions.

**Indian Warranty:** LG and Dell have the broadest service networks. HP and Acer follow closely. ViewSonic has improved coverage but is still better in metros than smaller cities. Always check the included warranty card and register your product online.
