## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Crowns and Implants

**Why implants need special attention:** Dental implants are titanium screws that fuse with jawbone. The crown attached to the implant is held by an abutment and screw. Unlike natural teeth, implants have no periodontal ligament — meaning they cannot sense pressure, so you can over-tighten or over-brush without the normal pain feedback. The titanium surface is more susceptible to accumulating bacterial biofilm (peri-implant plaque) than natural tooth enamel.

**Sonic vs. oscillating for implants:** Most implantologists and the major implant manufacturers (Straumann, Nobel Biocare, Dentsply) do not restrict either technology for maintenance. The practical argument for sonic: no rotational torque on the abutment connection. The practical argument for oscillating: more clinical evidence on plaque removal specifics. Both work well when used with correct technique and appropriate brush heads.

**Brush heads for crowns and implants:**
- Philips Sonicare Premium Gum Care — softest, best for peri-implant tissue
- Oral-B Gentle Cleaning head (iO compatible) — soft, restoration-safe
- Avoid: whitening/polishing heads with abrasive elements near porcelain crowns

**Crown types and cleaning considerations:**
- **Porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM):** Most common in India — avoid abrasive toothpaste (>70 RDA) that scratches porcelain
- **Zirconia crowns:** Extremely hard, highly scratch-resistant — any brush head safe
- **All-ceramic (e.max):** Avoid high-force brushing at crown margins

**India-specific implant context:** India has a rapidly growing dental implant market — implants are increasingly accessible at government dental colleges (₹15,000–₹25,000) and private clinics (₹40,000–₹80,000). Many patients have implants without understanding long-term maintenance requirements. Peri-implantitis affects up to 20% of implants within 10 years — the majority of which are preventable with proper home care.
