## Display — Best Screen Under ₹15,000

The Redmi Pad SE's 11-inch 90Hz IPS display is the best display specification of any tablet under ₹15,000. At 1920×1200, pixel density is 206 PPI — sharp and comfortable for reading, video, and text work.

**90Hz:** At this price range, most tablets run at 60Hz. 90Hz makes scrolling and animations noticeably smoother — touch responsiveness feels premium. For students spending hours on PDF reading or social media scrolling, this matters.

**Brightness and colour:** The IPS panel is accurate, with good brightness for indoor use. Outdoor visibility is limited — like most budget tablets, direct sunlight viewing is difficult.

**11 inches:** The largest screen in the under-₹15,000 category. For video watching and online classes, this is a significant advantage over 8.7-inch (Samsung Tab A9) and 10.1-inch competitors.

## Performance — Smooth for Daily Use

The Snapdragon 680 handles daily tasks confidently:

**Works well:**
- YouTube, Netflix, Prime Video, Hotstar at 1080p — no buffering or stutter
- Google Meet and Zoom (including video calls) — smooth
- Google Classroom, MS Word, Excel, Google Docs — no lag
- WhatsApp, Instagram, social media — fast
- Light gaming (Subway Surfers, Among Us, Ludo) — excellent

**Limitations:**
- BGMI at high settings — 30fps with occasional drops; playable at medium
- Call of Duty Mobile — medium settings, frame drops in intense scenes
- Heavy multitasking (4+ apps simultaneously) — 4GB RAM variant struggles; get 6GB+

**Recommendation:** Buy the 6GB RAM variant (₹13,999) over 4GB (₹12,999) — the ₹1,000 difference is worthwhile for multitasking.

## Battery — All-Day and Then Some

8,000mAh is one of the strongest battery specs at any price under ₹20,000. In daily use:
- Online classes (screen at 60% brightness, video call) — 8–10 hours
- Video streaming (Netflix, YouTube) — 10–12 hours
- Mixed use (browsing, social, occasional video) — 11–13 hours

**18W charging:** Slow by 2026 standards, but the large battery compensates — a full charge from 0 to 100% takes approximately 2.5 hours. Charge overnight and it's full — not a daily concern.

## Audio — Best Quad Speakers Under ₹15,000

Four Dolby Atmos-certified speakers produce genuinely good audio for a budget tablet. The soundstage is wide, dialogue is clear, and bass is present (if not deep). For students watching lectures, this is the best audio experience at this price.

**Dolby Atmos:** The certified Dolby Atmos processing creates a spatial audio effect — particularly noticeable on headphones (when the tablet selects high-quality Bluetooth codec). On speaker, it's wider than dual-speaker alternatives.

---

## MIUI for Pad — Capable but Ad-Heavy

MIUI for Pad has strong tablet-specific features — split-screen multitasking, floating windows, and a desktop-like second space. However, the interface is more aggressive with ads (in Mi Browser, Mi Themes, and pre-installed apps) than Samsung One UI or OxygenOS.

**Mitigation:** Installing Google Chrome, disabling Mi Browser as default, and not using Mi pre-installed apps eliminates most ad exposure. It takes 20 minutes of setup but significantly improves the experience.
