## Is Spending ₹5,000–₹7,000 on a Toothbrush Worth It?

**What you gain over sub-₹5,000 models:** Smarter pressure sensors (color-coded LED rings vs basic red-light warnings), more brushing modes (tongue cleaning, whitening, gum care), superior build quality with IPX7 full waterproofing, and meaningfully better brush head technology.

**The dentist math:** A single dental cleaning in a Tier-1 Indian city costs ₹800–₹2,000. A deep cleaning or filling runs ₹3,000–₹8,000. Spending ₹6,000 on a toothbrush that genuinely prevents buildup pays for itself in 2–3 avoided dental visits — within the first year for most users.

**Who should skip this bracket:** If you brush correctly with a manual brush and visit the dentist every 6 months, a ₹2,500 model handles the upgrade. This bracket is for users who want the best daily tool, have gum sensitivity concerns, or who know they brush too hard and want real-time coaching.

## Buying Guide: What Changes from ₹3,000 to ₹7,000?

**Pressure sensor quality:** Sub-₹3,000 models have basic red-light warnings; ₹5,000+ models have color rings (Oral-B iO) or physical brush-pause (Sonicare 5100). This is the most meaningful upgrade.

**Brushing modes:** Budget models offer 2–3 modes; this bracket offers 4–5, adding dedicated gum care and tongue cleaning modes useful for Indian users who often neglect tongue hygiene.

**Brush head cost:** At this bracket, budget ₹400–₹900 per replacement head every 3 months (₹1,600–₹3,600/year). Oral-B iO heads are pricier than standard Oral-B heads — factor this into total cost of ownership.

**Voltage compatibility:** All models listed are 100–240V — Indian power (220–240V) works without adapters.
