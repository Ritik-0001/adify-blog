## Buying Guide

### Panel Types Explained

At this price range, IPS is the right choice for most users. Every monitor in this list uses IPS — you get accurate colours, wide viewing angles, and consistent brightness from any position at your desk. If you're frequently sharing your screen with someone sitting beside you, or if your desk faces a window, IPS's wide angles make a real difference.

### Resolution Guide

Every monitor here is 1080p (1920×1080). At 24 inches, 1080p looks sharp and clear from a typical desk distance of 60–70cm. You don't need 1440p or 4K at 24 inches for general use — the jump in price isn't justified unless you're doing pixel-precise design work.

### Other Key Features

**Refresh Rate:** 75Hz (most monitors here) vs 60Hz (Dell P2422H) — 75Hz feels noticeably smoother for scrolling and general use. Not a game-changer for non-gamers, but a welcome improvement.

**Ergonomics:** If you work 8+ hours daily, the Dell P2422H's height-adjustable stand is worth the extra ₹1,500. Proper monitor height (top of screen at eye level) reduces neck strain significantly.

**Connectivity:** HDMI 1.4 handles 1080p at 75Hz without issues. DisplayPort is preferable for future-proofing. USB-C connectivity starts appearing at higher price points.

**Indian Power Supply:** All monitors listed are compatible with 230V Indian power. Built-in surge protection is standard — but pairing your monitor with a UPS or surge protector is still recommended, especially in areas with power fluctuations.

**Brand Service in India:** Dell > LG > HP > Acer > ASUS for depth of service network across India. In metro areas, all brands are well-covered. In Tier-3 towns, LG and Dell have the most reliable reach.

**Q: Is ₹12,000–₹14,000 enough for a good work-from-home monitor?**
A: Yes — this is the sweet spot. At ₹12,000–₹14,000, you get a 24" IPS 1080p monitor with 75Hz. This is all you need for video calls, documents, spreadsheets, and media. Only consider spending more if you need 1440p resolution or a gaming-focused 144Hz panel.

**Q: Do 24-inch monitors fit typical Indian study and office desks?**
A: Yes. Most Indian desks (90–120cm wide) comfortably fit a 24" monitor with keyboard, mouse, and some space for books or a notepad. The footprint of these monitors is modest.

**Q: Should I buy a monitor with or without built-in speakers?**
A: Built-in monitor speakers at this price are generally poor quality. The Dell P2422H and most others here don't include speakers — which is fine. Use your laptop's speakers, or buy a basic Bluetooth speaker for ₹500–₹1,500.
