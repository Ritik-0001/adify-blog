## What Defines an Executive Chair?

Executive chairs differ from standard ergonomic chairs in key ways: they typically use leather or high-grade fabric (not mesh), have a wider and deeper seat, feature a full high-back design that supports the entire spine including the upper back, and project an aesthetic of authority and quality. In Indian corporate culture, the executive chair in a corner office or meeting room signals seniority — choosing the right one is both ergonomic and strategic.

## Leather vs Mesh for Indian Executive Chairs

Traditional executive chairs use leather or fabric coverings, not mesh — and this creates a genuine comfort challenge in India's climate.

**Genuine leather** is the most prestigious and develops character over time, but it absorbs heat and can become uncomfortable during long Indian summer days without air conditioning. Best for air-conditioned offices.

**Bonded leather and PU leather** are common in mid-range executive chairs. More temperature-neutral than genuine leather, but less durable — the covering can peel after 3–5 years in humid climates.

**Premium fabric** (used by Herman Miller, Steelcase) is the most breathable option and performs well in Indian heat, but lacks the traditional executive aesthetic that leather provides.

If your office is consistently air-conditioned, leather executive chairs are the right choice. For home offices or non-AC environments, consider fabric-covered executive chairs or premium mesh chairs with executive proportions.
