## Do You Actually Need 4K in a Dash Cam?

This is an honest question that deserves an honest answer — and the answer is: **probably not, but it depends on how you use the footage.**

Here's the real-world comparison:

**1080p (Full HD):** At normal driving speeds (40–80 km/h), number plates are readable if the plate is reflective and lighting is adequate. At highway speeds or in poor light, plates become borderline readable.

**2K (1440p):** Significant improvement. Plates are clearly readable in most daytime conditions. The extra pixels mean you can digitally zoom into a clip and still have readable text.

**4K (UHD):** Number plates are readable even when the car is some distance away and moving fast. You can zoom in significantly while maintaining detail. The difference between 2K and 4K is meaningful when you need to read a distant plate or identify a face.

**When 4K is worth it:**
- Highway driving where incidents happen at high speed
- You want to crop/zoom into footage and still have clear detail
- You're using the camera for commercial or fleet purposes
- Your car is worth protecting with the best possible evidence quality

**When 4K is overkill:**
- City driving at low speeds (2K is sufficient for most plate captures)
- You're on a budget (₹28,999 vs ₹4,499 is a big gap)
- You don't have storage space for 4K files (more on this below)

## 4K Storage Requirements — The Honest Maths

This is the part most buyers don't think about:

| Resolution | Bitrate (typical) | Storage per hour |
|---|---|---|
| 1080p Full HD | 10–15 Mbps | ~6–8 GB/hour |
| 2K 1440p | 20–25 Mbps | ~10–12 GB/hour |
| 4K UHD | 40–60 Mbps | ~20–28 GB/hour |

A daily 1-hour commute (each way) in 4K will fill approximately 40–56 GB per day. With loop recording, your 128 GB card will be overwritten approximately every 2–3 days. This means if you're involved in an incident and don't save the clip immediately, it could be overwritten.

**Recommendation:** Use a 256 GB card with a 4K dash cam and check your footage more frequently. A Class 10 U3 rated card is mandatory — 4K cameras write data fast enough to exceed lower-rated cards.
