## Digital vs Manual Air Fryers: Does It Actually Matter?

Yes — and here is why.

**Manual (mechanical dial) air fryers** require you to set temperature and time by feel. The dials typically click in 10–20°C increments, and the timer is an analogue spring mechanism. You cannot set exactly 185°C or precisely 14 minutes — you approximate.

**Digital air fryers** let you set exact temperatures (160°C, 175°C, 200°C) and precise times (14 minutes, 22 minutes). The countdown timer shows you exactly how much time remains. Presets mean you press one button for fries rather than dialling in time and temperature each time.

For someone who air-fries the same 3–4 dishes repeatedly, digital precision helps you nail consistent results. For occasional use with simple recipes (frozen snacks, basic fries), manual controls are adequate.
