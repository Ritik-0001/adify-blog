## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Periodontitis

**Electric toothbrush as adjunct, not replacement:** This is the most important point for periodontitis patients — no electric toothbrush, however advanced, replaces professional scaling, root planing, or periodontal surgery. Periodontal pockets deeper than 3mm cannot be physically cleaned by any brush head; only a periodontist's instruments and water flosser streams reach these areas. Your electric toothbrush maintains health between appointments; it does not treat active disease.

**When to see a periodontist:** See a specialist (not just a general dentist) if you have: pockets deeper than 4mm on probing, bone loss visible on X-ray, persistent bleeding despite 2 weeks of improved electric brushing, mobile (loose) teeth, exposed roots, or persistent bad breath despite thorough oral hygiene. In India's major cities, periodontists are available in most multi-specialty dental clinics; consultation costs ₹500–₹1,500.

**India-specific periodontitis risk factors:** Periodontitis prevalence in India is amplified by high diabetes rates (India has 77 million diabetics — diabetes and periodontitis have a bidirectional relationship where each worsens the other), tobacco use in all forms, and inadequate access to regular professional dental care in smaller cities and rural areas. Patients with diabetes should specifically seek dental care and mention their diabetic status to their dentist.

**Systemic links:** Research links periodontitis to cardiovascular disease (bacteria from periodontal pockets enter the bloodstream), preterm birth (periodontitis increases preterm birth risk by 2–7x), and glycaemic control difficulty in diabetics. Treating periodontitis through professional care and rigorous home hygiene has measurable systemic health benefits beyond oral health.

**Essential home care for periodontitis:**
1. Electric toothbrush twice daily (minimum 2 minutes each session)
2. Water flosser daily — reaches deeper into pockets than string floss
3. Interdental brushes for spaces between teeth
4. Antiseptic mouthwash (chlorhexidine short-term, as directed by dentist)
5. Professional cleaning every 3–6 months (not the standard annual visit)
