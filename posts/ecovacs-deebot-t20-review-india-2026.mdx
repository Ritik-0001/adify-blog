## Detailed Reviews

### Key Feature 1: Hot Water Mop Washing — India's Best Hygiene Feature

The T20 Omni's All-in-One station does four things automatically: empties the dustbin, washes the mop pads with hot water at up to 55°C, dries the mop pads with warm air, and refills the robot's water tank. Every part of the maintenance cycle is automated — but what sets it apart from Roborock is the temperature.

Hot water at 55°C kills bacteria and breaks down oil and grease far more effectively than cold water rinses. In an Indian kitchen context, where cooking leaves oily residue on tile floors, a cold-water mop wash leaves fats smeared across the pad. Hot water dissolves them. The result is a mop pad that is genuinely clean after washing, not just less dirty.

This matters specifically in Indian households because kitchen mopping is frequent and involves real soiling. Anyone who has hand-mopped a floor knows warm water works better. The T20 Omni brings this logic to its automated system — and it is the only robot vacuum in this price band to do so.

---

### Key Feature 2: OZMO Turbo 2.0 Rotating Mop

The OZMO Turbo 2.0 uses a high-speed rotating mop pad that presses against the floor surface with adjustable pressure. Unlike a static mop that simply trails behind the robot, the rotating action creates friction that physically scrubs the floor. On vitrified tile, marble, and polished stone — the typical Indian floor — this produces visibly better results than static mopping.

It handles dried stains, footprints, and light kitchen residue effectively. It is not as aggressive as a manual scrub for heavily soiled areas, but for daily maintenance mopping, it is excellent. Compared to Roborock's sonic mopping, the OZMO Turbo 2.0 holds its own — some users prefer the rotary action on tile, others prefer Roborock's sonic frequency. Both are significantly better than any basic drag-mop system.

---

### Key Feature 3: TrueMapping 2.0 LiDAR Navigation

TrueMapping 2.0 creates detailed floor maps using LiDAR scanning. Initial mapping is fast and accurate — most Indian apartments in the 1,000–2,000 sq ft range are mapped in a single session. Multi-floor mapping is supported, which is useful for duplexes or homes with mezzanine levels. Room labelling, no-go zones, and custom cleaning areas all work through the Ecovacs app.

The app is functional and works well on Indian networks, though it is not quite as polished as the Roborock app in terms of speed and UI smoothness. Core features work reliably — schedules, zone cleaning, and multi-floor management are all present.

---

### Key Feature 4: AIVI 3D Obstacle Avoidance

AIVI 3D uses a combination of structured light 3D sensing and AI object recognition to identify and avoid obstacles. It handles common Indian home hazards — shoes near the door, cables from phone chargers, furniture legs — reasonably well. It is not at the level of iRobot's PrecisionVision for identifying specific objects, but it is competitive with Roborock's ReactiveAI 2.0.

In testing on typical Indian apartment floors with the usual scatter of footwear, bags, and phone cables, the T20 Omni successfully avoided obstacles in most cases. Occasional edge-case misses occur in low light — as with all camera-dependent obstacle avoidance systems.

---

### Performance on Indian Floors

**Tile and Marble:** Excellent. 6,000 Pa suction handles fine Indian dust, and the OZMO Turbo 2.0 mop leaves tile noticeably cleaner than static mop systems. The rotating action on polished marble does not cause scratching — the mop pad material is designed for hard floor surfaces.

**Pet Hair and Long Hair:** Good, but not quite at Roborock S8's level. The T20 Omni uses a traditional brush roll, which handles hair well but requires periodic cleaning to prevent tangling. Roborock's DuoRoller has an edge here specifically for hair-heavy homes.

**Dustbin Capacity:** The T20 Omni's dustbin is 430 ml — adequate for most Indian homes on a daily cleaning schedule. The auto-empty station holds approximately 3 litres, which translates to roughly 30–45 days before the station bag needs replacement.
