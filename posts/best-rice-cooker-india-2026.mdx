## Rice Cooker for Indian Rice Varieties

**Basmati rice (North India):** Long-grain, requires exact water ratio (1:1.5 to 1:2). Basmati overcooking turns grains mushy — automatic shut-off at precise moisture absorption is critical.

**Sona Masoori (South India):** Medium-grain, requires more water than basmati (1:2 to 1:2.5), slightly more forgiving. Standard rice cookers handle this well.

**Idli rice (South India):** Parboiled short-grain — soaked overnight and used for idli-dosa batter, not direct cooking in rice cooker.

**Brown rice:** Requires 40–50% more water and 2× cooking time. Fuzzy logic rice cookers (with dedicated brown rice mode) handle this; standard cookers require manual adjustment.
