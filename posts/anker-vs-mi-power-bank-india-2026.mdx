## The Core Difference Between Anker and Mi

Anker and Mi (Xiaomi) take very different approaches to the power bank market:

**Mi's approach:** High-spec hardware at ultra-competitive prices. Xiaomi's massive scale in manufacturing allows them to offer 18W fast input at ₹800 where competitors would charge ₹1,500. The trade-off is thinner margins mean less premium in build finish and shorter warranty terms (6 months vs Anker's 18 months).

**Anker's approach:** Premium reliability with engineering focus. Anker invests in tighter quality control, better cell sourcing, and more robust protection circuits. They charge more (₹1,500 vs ₹800 for 10K) but the units are more compact, have better long-term cell retention, and carry a meaningful warranty.

Neither brand uses fake capacity — both are honest about what they sell. That alone puts them ahead of the no-brand market.

## Real-World Reliability: Which Brand Lasts Longer?

This is hard to measure precisely but there is enough independent data to draw reasonable conclusions.

**Anker:** Their cells are consistently rated for 500+ charge cycles at 80%+ capacity retention. Build quality on connectors and the outer shell is markedly better. In informal longevity reviews, Anker power banks from 2020–2022 are still commonly reported as functioning normally in 2025.

**Mi:** Xiaomi's cells are genuine (not recycled) but from a more cost-optimized supply chain. Capacity fade is slightly faster over 300–400 cycles compared to Anker. The Micro-USB port on many Mi power banks is a wear point that can loosen with daily plugging.

**The honest take:** Both brands will easily last 2–3 years with normal use. Anker has the edge in 3–5 year longevity. For Indian users who upgrade phones every 2–3 years anyway, Mi's value proposition is hard to argue against.
