## Clinical Evidence: Oscillating vs Sonic for Plaque

Independent Cochrane reviews (2014, updated 2019) comparing electric toothbrush technologies consistently show:
- **Oscillating-rotating (Oral-B):** 11% more plaque reduction than manual brushing at 3 months
- **Sonic (Philips):** 6% more plaque reduction than manual brushing at 3 months

**The caveat:** These differences, while statistically significant, are small in absolute terms. Both technologies provide substantial benefit over manual brushing. For most users, technique, consistency, and using a pressure sensor matter more than technology type.

**When plaque removal specifically matters:** Heavy calculus formers, patients with implants (plaque around implant-abutment junctions), smokers, diabetics with high cariogenic risk, and those with fixed orthodontic appliances all benefit most from maximum plaque disruption — making the oscillating advantage relevant.
