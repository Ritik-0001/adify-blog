## Bass Quality: What to Look For

**Sub-bass vs mid-bass.** Sub-bass (20–60Hz) is the physical, felt rumble of bass notes. Mid-bass (60–200Hz) is the punch of kick drums and bass guitar. Most budget headphones emphasise mid-bass (audible but not physically felt). Sony XB headphones extend into sub-bass for that chest-resonance quality.

**Bass without bleed.** Poor bass headphones let low frequencies bleed into the midrange, making vocals sound boomy and unclear. A good bass headphone keeps lows and mids separated — you can hear both the bass and the singer clearly. This separation is where Sony's XB line outperforms generic bass-heavy options.

**EQ as a tool.** If you have a headphone with a companion app (boAt, Sony), use the EQ to boost bass on your specific tracks rather than buying dedicated bass headphones. A good flat headphone with bass boost EQ can equal a bass-tuned headphone's output.
