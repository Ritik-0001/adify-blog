Gum recession is irreversible — once gum tissue retreats from a tooth, it does not regenerate without surgical intervention. The two main controllable causes are gum disease (bacteria) and over-brushing (mechanical trauma). An electric toothbrush with the right features addresses both. Here are the best options for receding gums in India 2026.

---

## What Causes Gum Recession in India

**Over-brushing (toothbrush abrasion):** Hard brushing with medium or hard bristle brushes is the most common cause of recession in Indian patients. Indian brushing habits tend toward aggressive scrubbing — cultural emphasis on "cleaning thoroughly" can lead to excessive force.

**Gum disease:** Untreated gingivitis progresses to periodontitis, which destroys the bone and tissue supporting teeth. Recession follows bone loss.

**Correct electric toothbrush features for recession:**
1. Physical pressure sensor (stops or slows brush when force exceeds safe threshold)
2. Soft brush head (medium and hard bristle heads accelerate recession)
3. Gentle cleaning mode (reduced intensity for exposed root surfaces)
4. Sonic fluid dynamics (clean without direct mechanical scrubbing on sensitive root surfaces)

## Recession-Specific Brushing Rules

1. **Always use soft bristle heads** — medium and hard heads are not appropriate for recession patients
2. **Gentle 45° angle** — position at the gumline, never scrub away from gums
3. **Let the brush do the work** — hold lightly, do not apply force
4. **Use sensitivity toothpaste** — Sensodyne, Oral-B Sensitive, or Colgate Sensitive provides relief for exposed dentine
5. **Professional treatment** — recession from gum disease requires scaling and root planing; a toothbrush maintains post-treatment health but cannot reverse recession
