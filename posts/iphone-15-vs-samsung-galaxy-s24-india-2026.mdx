## Display

**iPhone 15:** 6.1" Super Retina XDR OLED, 60Hz, 2000 nits peak brightness
**Samsung Galaxy S24:** 6.2" Dynamic AMOLED 2X, 120Hz, 2600 nits peak brightness

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24**

Samsung's 120Hz display is noticeably smoother for scrolling and gaming. The higher peak brightness (2600 vs 2000 nits) makes it more readable in direct Indian sunlight. The iPhone 15's 60Hz refresh rate is a significant omission at this price — every Android competitor at ₹30,000+ includes 90Hz or higher.

## Camera

**iPhone 15:** 48MP main (OIS) + 12MP ultrawide, 12MP TrueDepth front
**Samsung Galaxy S24:** 50MP main (OIS) + 12MP ultrawide + 10MP 3x telephoto, 12MP front

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24 (for versatility), iPhone 15 (for video)**

**Camera comparison:**
- **Daylight photos:** Both exceptional. iPhone 15 produces more natural colours; S24 produces punchier, more vibrant images preferred for social media
- **Telephoto:** S24 wins decisively with dedicated 3x optical zoom lens — iPhone 15 has no telephoto
- **Low light:** Very close — iPhone 15's Night mode produces warmer, cleaner results; S24 is brighter but sometimes overprocessed
- **Video:** iPhone 15 wins clearly — Cinematic mode, Log video recording, and Apple's video processing pipeline produce smoother, more cinematic footage
- **Front camera:** iPhone 15's TrueDepth delivers more consistent face exposure in challenging lighting

**For photography variety:** S24 (telephoto zoom)
**For video content creation:** iPhone 15

## Performance

**iPhone 15:** Apple A16 Bionic (4nm), 6GB RAM
**Samsung Galaxy S24:** Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 for Galaxy (4nm), 8GB RAM

**Winner: Tie (different strengths)**

Apple's A16 Bionic outperforms in single-core tasks — apps launch faster and feel snappier in direct use. Samsung's Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 wins in multi-core (sustained heavy tasks, gaming).

In real India use, both phones handle every app, game, and task perfectly — performance is not a differentiating factor at this level.

**Gaming:** S24 with Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 runs at higher sustained frame rates in demanding titles. iPhone 15 runs all games excellently but throttles sooner under sustained gaming load (common in iPhones).

## Battery

**iPhone 15:** 3349mAh, 20W charging, 15W MagSafe wireless
**Samsung Galaxy S24:** 4000mAh, 25W charging, 15W wireless

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24**

The S24's larger 4000mAh battery consistently delivers better battery life — typically 6–7 hours screen-on-time vs iPhone 15's 5–6 hours in Indian usage patterns.

Charging speed is moderate on both — neither is fast by 2026 standards. OnePlus and Realme phones at ₹30,000 charge significantly faster.

**MagSafe on iPhone 15:** Apple's magnetic wireless charging system enables MagSafe accessories (wallets, car mounts, power banks) — an ecosystem advantage Android doesn't replicate.

## Software and Updates

**iPhone 15:** iOS 18, guaranteed updates through 2029+ (Apple typically supports iPhones 5–6 years)
**Samsung Galaxy S24:** Android 14 with One UI 6.1, 7 years OS updates promised (through 2031)

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24 (by commitment)**

Samsung's 7-year update promise is the longest in the Android world and matches or exceeds Apple's historical iOS support timeline. The S24 will officially receive updates until 2031 — extraordinary for an Android device.

**Day-to-day software:**
- iOS: Simpler, consistent, smoother animations, better app quality on average (apps release on iPhone first), tighter privacy controls
- One UI: More customisable, better multitasking, split-screen, Samsung DeX (desktop mode), better file management, more free Google services
