## The 4100's Defining Feature: Physical Pressure Sensor

The Sonicare 4100's key differentiator from the cheaper Oclean F1 and Beurer TB30 is the physical pressure sensor:

**How it works:** A load sensor in the brush head detects force. When pressure exceeds a safe threshold, the brush motor slows down noticeably — the vibration frequency drops, providing clear tactile feedback that you're pressing too hard.

**Why this matters more than a warning light:** Many users don't look at warning lights during brushing (bathroom mirror, eyes elsewhere). A physical change in motor speed is felt through the hand — no visual attention required. This is more reliable at preventing gum damage than an LED warning.

**Comparison at this price range:**
- Oral-B Pro 2 (₹1,999): Pressure sensor is a red LED light (visual only)
- Sonicare 4100 (₹1,999): Pressure sensor physically slows the brush (tactile + visual)
- For users with gum sensitivity or those prone to over-brushing, the physical sensor is more effective

## 1 Cleaning Mode — Is That Enough?

The 4100 has only 1 mode — Clean. No Sensitive, Whitening, or Gum Care.

**For most users:** 1 mode is sufficient — daily cleaning is what 95% of brushing sessions require. The mode variety in the 5100 is genuinely useful for specific situations (inflamed gums → Sensitive, stain removal → Whitening) but not necessary for everyone.

**When 1 mode isn't enough:** If you have sensitive gums or gum disease, the Sonicare 5100's Gum Care mode at Low intensity is a meaningful clinical advantage. The ₹1,200 upgrade from 4100 to 5100 is worth it for gum-health-focused users.
