## Why 4L Is the Most Popular Air Fryer Size in India

The 4-litre air fryer is not just a middle-ground option — it is the most practical size for the way most Indian households actually cook. Here is why:

**It handles 2–3 people perfectly.** A standard Indian household of a couple with one child, or a young working couple, does not need a 6L air fryer for daily snacks. A 4L basket handles their needs in a single batch.

**It fits Indian kitchen counters.** Indian kitchen counters are typically narrower than in western homes, and counter space is at a premium. A 4L air fryer has a footprint of roughly 28cm × 28cm — manageable even in compact kitchens. A 6L+ model grows significantly larger.

**It is the price sweet spot.** The ₹3,500–₹5,000 range offers the best feature-to-price ratio in the Indian air fryer market — digital controls, preset programs, and 200°C reach, without paying for capacity you may not need.

For a family of 3–4 making snacks, a 4L handles a full family portion for most dishes. For main course chicken for a family of 4 (where you need 8–10 large pieces), a 4L requires two batches.

## 4L vs 6L: When Should You Size Up?

**Buy 4L if:**
- Your household is 1–3 people
- You primarily air-fry snacks and sides, not main courses
- Counter space is limited
- Budget is under ₹5,000

**Buy 6L (Cosori Pro II 5.8L) if:**
- Your household is 4+ people
- You want to cook a full chicken or a large fish in one batch
- You regularly cook main courses in the air fryer
- You can stretch to ₹5,999–₹6,999

The 4L-to-6L jump is not about snacks — it is about main course cooking. If you mainly air-fry samosas and fries and occasionally do chicken wings, 4L is perfectly adequate even for a family of 4.
