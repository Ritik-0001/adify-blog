## Low-Latency Mode Explained — Why It Matters for Gaming

Before jumping into recommendations, it is worth understanding what low-latency gaming mode actually does and why it matters.

Standard Bluetooth audio has a delay (latency) of approximately 100–200 milliseconds between the sound being produced and reaching your ears. For music listening, this is imperceptible. For gaming, it is highly noticeable: an explosion you see on screen reaches your ears a fraction of a second late, a gunshot sounds delayed, footstep audio does not match visual movement.

**Gaming or low-latency modes** reduce this delay to approximately 40–80 milliseconds by sacrificing some audio quality (using a simpler codec path) in exchange for speed. The result is audio that feels synchronised with on-screen action.

For mobile games like BGMI, Free Fire, and Call of Duty Mobile — where audio cues (footsteps, gunshots, vehicle sounds) are tactical information — reduced latency is a meaningful competitive advantage.

Most earphones marketed as "gaming" include a dedicated low-latency mode, usually toggled via a button on the earphone or in the companion app.
