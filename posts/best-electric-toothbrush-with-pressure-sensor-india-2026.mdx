Research consistently shows that 70% of people brush too hard — applying 3–5x the recommended 150g of force. Over years, this erodes enamel and causes gum recession that cannot be reversed. A pressure sensor is not a gimmick; it is the single feature most likely to prevent long-term dental damage.

---

## Why Pressure Sensors Matter More Than Most People Realize

**The gum recession problem:** Gum recession exposes tooth roots, which have no enamel protection. Once receded, gum tissue doesn't grow back naturally — surgical grafting costs ₹15,000–₹40,000 per tooth in India. Brushing too hard for a decade is the most common non-disease cause of recession.

**How hard is too hard:** Dentists recommend 150g of pressure — roughly the weight of a few grapes. Most people apply 400–600g. You cannot reliably feel the difference while brushing. A pressure sensor is objective feedback you cannot fake or ignore.

**Three sensor types in this segment:**
- **Red light warning:** Tells you to ease up. Easily ignored.
- **Color-coded LED ring:** Green (good), white (too light), red (too hard). Positive + negative feedback. Better behavioral change.
- **Physical brush pause:** Brush head slows or stops when over-pressure detected. Forces correction without conscious effort. Most effective behavior-modifier.

## Buying Guide: Pressure Sensors in India

**Why this matters more in India:** Indian dentists report higher rates of abrasive brushing habits than international averages — likely related to widespread use of hard-bristle manual toothbrushes (common in local pharmacies) and horizontal scrubbing technique taught by older generations.

**Pair with soft bristles:** A pressure sensor is most effective with a soft or ultra-soft brush head. Even with light pressure, medium or hard bristles abrade enamel more than soft bristles.

**Replacement brush head availability:** Oral-B brush heads are available in pharmacies and online across India. Philips Sonicare heads are predominantly available online (Amazon.in, Flipkart) — plan ahead.
