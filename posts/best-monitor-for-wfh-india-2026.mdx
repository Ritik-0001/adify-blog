## Buying Guide

### Do You Need USB-C?
If you use a laptop (especially MacBook, Dell XPS, HP Spectre, or ThinkPad), USB-C single-cable setup is worth the premium — it charges your laptop AND transmits video. For desktop users, standard HDMI is perfectly fine.

### 24" vs 27" for WFH
24" is ideal for most Indian desk sizes (smaller apartments, narrower desks). 27" is noticeably better for multitasking — keeping two documents side-by-side. At 27", budget for 1440p if possible (1080p looks soft at 27" close-up).

### Eye Care Features That Actually Matter
- **Flicker-free backlight** (not PWM — eliminates invisible flicker that causes headaches)
- **Low Blue Light mode** (reduces 400-440nm blue light emission)
- **Matte anti-glare** (critical in bright Indian rooms)
- **Brightness Intelligence** (BenQ's auto-adjustment is genuinely useful)

## FAQ

**Q: What size monitor is best for WFH in India?**
A: 24" is the most popular WFH size in India — fits most desks and doesn't require moving your head. 27" is better for multitasking but requires a wider desk (60cm+ recommended).

**Q: Is 4K worth it for WFH?**
A: For most WFH tasks (email, docs, video calls), 1080p or 1440p is sufficient. 4K is worth it if you edit photos/video or want future-proofing, but requires a powerful laptop/GPU to drive it smoothly.

**Q: Which brands have the best warranty service in India?**
A: Dell (3-year on-site service, widespread), LG (2-year, good metro presence), HP (1-year but largest physical store network in India). BenQ and ASUS are 1-3 year warranty but service centres are mainly in major cities.

**Q: Can I use a TV instead of a monitor for WFH?**
A: Technically yes, but TVs have higher input lag, lower pixel density, and are optimised for 3m+ viewing distance — not 60cm desk distance. A dedicated monitor is significantly better for all-day use.
