## Overview

India is a hard-floor country. Walk into any apartment in Mumbai, Chennai, Bengaluru, or Delhi and you will almost certainly find marble, granite, vitrified tile, ceramic tile, or Kota stone underfoot. These surfaces are beautiful, easy to maintain in theory, and absolutely unforgiving when it comes to showing dust, streaks, or smears.

This is actually good news for robot vacuum owners. Hard floors are where robot vacuums perform at their absolute best. Without carpet fibres trapping debris, the vacuum and mop combination can clean a large flat to a genuinely high standard in a single automated run.

But there are wrong ways to do this. The wrong brush type scratches polished marble. The wrong mop system leaves water streaks on granite. The wrong robot leaves grit behind because it cannot generate enough suction for India's fine outdoor dust. Choosing the right hardware matters.

## Buying Guide

**Rubber Roller vs Bristle Brush:** This is the most important hard floor decision. Rubber rollers or silicone rollers do not scratch polished marble or granite. They pick up fine dust, pet hair, and sand without leaving marks. Bristle brushes work on carpet but are unnecessarily aggressive on hard stone. Always choose rubber roller for Indian hard floors.

**Mop System Quality:** For bare floors, the mop matters more than for carpet homes. A drag cloth is adequate for daily light mopping. A vibrating mop (Q8 Max level) is suitable for most homes. A sonic mop (Dreame L10s Ultra, Roborock S8 Pro Ultra) is worth the upgrade if you have polished marble that shows every streak.

**Suction for Indian Dust:** Fine outdoor particulate pollution, sand carried in from footwear, and construction dust near active building sites all require meaningful suction. 3,000 Pa is the minimum; 5,000+ Pa is recommended for urban apartments.

**Grout Lines on Tile:** Standard ceramic tile with wide grout lines accumulates dust and grime in the grooves. A rotating mop (Ecovacs OZMO Pro) dislodges grout-embedded dirt better than linear mop systems.

**Streak-Free Marble:** Always use clean mop pads. Robots with dock-based mop washing (Dreame L10s Ultra, T20 Omni, S8 Pro Ultra) provide consistently cleaner pads than robots where you must wash pads manually.

## FAQ

**Q: Will a robot vacuum scratch my marble floor?**  
A: A robot with rubber rollers and soft edge brushes will not scratch marble. Avoid models with stiff plastic bristle main brushes. The robot's wheels are rubber and safe on polished stone.

**Q: Is mopping necessary if my floors are already clean after vacuuming?**  
A: Yes. Vacuuming removes dry dust and particles. Mopping removes the fine film of pollution and humidity residue that settles on Indian urban hard floors and makes them look dull. On marble, mopping makes a visible difference to the finish.

**Q: Can a robot vacuum clean between floor tiles and grout lines?**  
A: The suction picks up dust from grout lines effectively. Deep-embedded grout staining requires a spin scrubber or manual grout cleaning tool — that is beyond the scope of a robot vacuum.

**Q: How often should I run a robot vacuum on hard floors in Indian cities?**  
A: Daily scheduling is ideal in urban areas with high dust. A 5-minute daily run prevents buildup better than a weekly deep clean. Most robots can run daily on a schedule with minimal manual involvement.
