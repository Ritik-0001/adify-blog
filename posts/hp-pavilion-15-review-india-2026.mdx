The HP Pavilion 15 is one of the most purchased laptops in India — and for good reason. HP's reputation for reliable build quality, a nationwide service network, and consistent performance at accessible prices has made the Pavilion series a default recommendation for students and home users for over a decade.

Here's whether the 2024 model is still worth buying.

---

## Overview

The HP Pavilion 15 (2024) comes in Intel Core i5-1335U or AMD Ryzen 5 7530U configurations, with 8GB or 16GB RAM and 512GB SSD storage. It's a 15.6-inch FHD laptop designed for everyday productivity — college work, Office, web browsing, video streaming, and light creative tasks.

---

## Key Specifications

- **CPU:** Intel Core i5-1335U (10-core) or AMD Ryzen 5 7530U
- **RAM:** 8GB DDR4 (upgradeable to 16GB)
- **Storage:** 512GB PCIe NVMe SSD
- **Display:** 15.6" FHD IPS (1920×1080), anti-glare
- **Graphics:** Intel Iris Xe / AMD Radeon
- **Battery:** 41Wh, up to 6 hours
- **Ports:** 2× USB-A, 1× USB-C, HDMI, headphone, SD card
- **Weight:** 1.75 kg
- **OS:** Windows 11 Home

---

## Performance

The Core i5-1335U handles everyday computing without breaking a sweat — Microsoft Office, Google Chrome with 15+ tabs, Zoom calls, and light photo editing in Lightroom all run smoothly. Boot time from SSD is under 15 seconds.

Sustained tasks like video encoding or running a development server cause fan spin-up. The Pavilion 15 is not designed for heavy workloads — it's a productivity laptop, and in that role it performs reliably.

For casual gaming: Minecraft and League of Legends run acceptably. Don't expect more from integrated graphics.

---

## Display

The 15.6" FHD IPS panel is bright enough for indoor use (250–300 nits typical) with decent colour reproduction for everyday content. Anti-glare coating handles window reflections well — a practical choice for varied lighting environments.

It's not a premium panel — colours aren't calibrated for creative work, and 60Hz is the limit. For watching lectures, Netflix, and working in Office: perfectly fine.

---

## Build Quality

The HP Pavilion 15 uses a plastic body that feels solid relative to its price class. The keyboard deck has minimal flex under typing pressure. The keyboard itself is tactile, with good key travel for a budget laptop — comfortable for long writing sessions.

The dual-hinge design opens with one hand on most configurations. Build quality is HP's consistent strength in this segment.

---

## Battery Life

The 41Wh battery delivers 5–6 hours of real-world use — enough for a college day but expect to charge before an evening session. The 45W USB-C charger fills the battery in approximately 1.5 hours.

For all-day battery without a charger, look at the ASUS VivoBook 15's larger 50Wh battery. For most home and office users: the Pavilion's battery is adequate.

---

## Service & Warranty

HP's India service network is among the best of any laptop brand — service centres present in most Tier-1 and Tier-2 cities, and many Tier-3 cities. The standard 1-year carry-in warranty is expandable. For buyers concerned about post-purchase support, this is HP's most compelling advantage over competitors.

## Final Verdict

The HP Pavilion 15 in 2026 is a competent, reliable budget laptop that does exactly what it promises. It won't impress you, but it won't let you down either — which is exactly what most students and home users need.

The service network advantage is HP's strongest argument. If you've had good HP experiences before or you're in a city where ASUS or Lenovo service is limited, the Pavilion 15 is the safe, sensible choice.
