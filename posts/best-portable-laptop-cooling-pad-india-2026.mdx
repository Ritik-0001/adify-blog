## What Makes a Cooling Pad Truly Portable?

"Portable" is an overused word in laptop accessory marketing. Every cooling pad technically goes from one place to another — but truly portable means you forget it is in your bag until you need it.

The criteria that define a portable cooling pad:

**Fold-flat design.** A rigid flat pad is not portable — it takes up a fixed amount of bag space and does not compress. A fold-flat or collapsible design reduces thickness to 10–15mm when folded.

**Weight under 600g.** Most non-portable cooling pads weigh 700g–1.2 kg. The Portronics My Buddy Plus at 490g is light enough that you will not notice it added to a loaded bag.

**No separate power.** Any cooling pad requiring a power adapter is not portable. All truly portable pads draw power from the laptop's own USB port.

**Bag-safe edges.** Sharp plastic edges or protruding fan grilles snag on bag interiors and scratch other gear. Portable-focused pads round their edges or use fabric trim.

The Portronics My Buddy Plus meets all four criteria. The Targus Chill Mat meets three (it does not fold, but it is slim and edge-safe).

## Portronics My Buddy Plus — What You Get

What you get is enough cooling to prevent throttling during an extended work session in a warm co-working space or train journey.

## The Trade-Off: Portable vs Performance

This is the honest part of any portable cooling pad review: you are trading cooling performance for portability.

A performance pad like the Cooler Master NotePal X3 moves significantly more air than any portable pad because it uses a 200mm fan drawing full USB power from a desktop setup. The Portronics My Buddy Plus uses two 90mm fans with limited airflow.

For thin-and-light laptops (Dell XPS 15, HP Spectre, Lenovo ThinkPad E15), this trade-off is acceptable. These laptops do not generate extreme heat, so 4–6°C reduction is sufficient to keep them comfortable during travel.

For gaming laptops or high-performance workstations, a portable cooling pad is inadequate. You need a performance pad at your primary workspace, and just accept the heat during travel — or buy two pads.
