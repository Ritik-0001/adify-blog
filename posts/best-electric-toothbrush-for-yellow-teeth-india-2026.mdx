## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Yellow Teeth

**Surface stains vs. intrinsic yellowing — the essential distinction:**
- **Surface stains (extrinsic):** Caused by tea, coffee, tobacco, pan, red wine, turmeric. Appear as brownish or yellowish deposits on enamel. Removable with electric toothbrush whitening modes, polishing, and professional cleaning.
- **Intrinsic yellowing:** The natural colour of your dentin shows through enamel (dentin is naturally yellow). Cannot be removed by any toothbrush. Requires professional bleaching (in-office or home whitening trays with 10–35% carbamide peroxide). Electric toothbrushes cannot change intrinsic tooth colour.

**Clinical evidence for electric whitening:** A randomised controlled trial in *The Journal of Dentistry* found that participants using an oscillating-rotating brush with a whitening head achieved 70% more surface stain removal than manual brushers over 4 weeks. Sonic brushes showed 50% more surface stain removal. Both significantly outperform manual brushing for extrinsic stain management.

**Indian stain factors:**
- **Chai (milk tea):** Tannin content combined with milk proteins creates stubborn brownish staining
- **Pan and betel nut:** Causes deep reddish-brown staining that penetrates enamel — often requires professional polishing, not just electric brushing
- **Tobacco (bidi, cigarettes):** Tar creates yellow-brown staining requiring professional scaling to fully remove
- **Turmeric:** Surface yellow colouring — highly responsive to electric brush whitening modes

**Realistic timeline:**
- Surface tea/coffee stains: 2–4 weeks of electric whitening mode brushing
- Tobacco stains: 4–8 weeks partial improvement; professional polishing needed for full removal
- Pan/betel nut stains: Electric brushing alone insufficient; requires professional cleaning
- Intrinsic yellowing: Electric toothbrush has zero effect — professional bleaching required

**Whitening toothpaste caution:** High-abrasivity whitening toothpastes (RDA 100–200) remove surface stains by physical abrasion but damage enamel long-term. Use low-RDA whitening toothpaste (Sensodyne Gentle Whitening, RDA ~45) with your electric whitening mode rather than highly abrasive pastes.
