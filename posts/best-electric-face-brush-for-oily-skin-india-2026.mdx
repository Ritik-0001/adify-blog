## Oily Skin in India

**India's climate and oily skin:** India's heat (30–40°C average) increases sebaceous gland activity — sebum production increases in heat to regulate skin temperature. Indian oily skin produces 2–3× more sebum than dry-climate skin.

**Indian T-zone:** Indian women predominantly have oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) with drier cheeks — combination skin requiring different cleansing on different facial zones. Electric brushes with zone-targeting capability handle this.

**Blackheads:** India's pollution particles combine with excess sebum to create blackheads on nose and chin — India's most common skin concern among urban women under 30.

## Cleansing Routine for Indian Oily Skin

1. **Oil cleanser** (micellar water or balm) — dissolves sunscreen and makeup
2. **Electric brush with foam cleanser** — removes dissolved product + sebum from pores
3. **Toner** — pH balance post-cleansing (Indian tap water is alkaline — disrupts skin pH)
4. **Serum** — applied to cleansed pore for maximum absorption

Electric face brushing fits step 2 — replacing or enhancing manual face wash.
