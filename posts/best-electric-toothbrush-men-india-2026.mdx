## Men's Oral Health Context in India

**Tobacco staining:** India has one of the world's highest rates of tobacco use in various forms. Gutka, pan masala, cigarettes, and bidis cause significant surface (extrinsic) staining — primarily on the inner surfaces of front teeth and biting surfaces of back teeth. Whitening mode toothbrushes make a visible difference.

**Irregular brushing:** Men in India brush less consistently (single daily brushing, shorter duration) on average. Brushes with strong 2-minute timers and quadrant alerts provide the most impact for this pattern.

**Gum disease:** Indian men have higher rates of periodontitis than women, partly due to tobacco use and less consistent oral care. Brushes with gum care modes are relevant.
