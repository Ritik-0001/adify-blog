## Overview

iRobot is the brand that invented the consumer robot vacuum category, and the Roomba name still carries significant recognition in India. But the robot vacuum landscape in 2026 is very different from when Roomba first launched. Chinese brands — Roborock, Dreame, Ecovacs — have closed the technology gap and now offer more features at lower prices. So where does Roomba stand for Indian buyers?

The honest answer: iRobot makes the best obstacle-avoiding robot vacuum on the market, and that one strength is genuinely compelling for specific use cases. For homes with young children, pets, or simply a lot of obstacles on the floor — cables, toys, shoes, bags — no other brand matches iRobot's PrecisionVision AI for identifying and avoiding specific objects. For everyone else, the value proposition becomes harder to justify.

This review covers three Roomba models available in India: the Roomba i3 (entry-level), Roomba j7 (mid-premium), and Roomba Combo j7+ (premium with mopping). The goal is to help Indian buyers understand exactly what they are paying for — and whether that trade-off makes sense given what Roborock and Dreame offer at comparable prices.

## Detailed Reviews

### 1. Roomba i3 — Entry-Level Roomba for India

**Price:** ₹24,999

The i3 is iRobot's most accessible model in India and represents a solid entry point for the brand. AeroForce cleaning system uses rubber extraction rollers (similar in concept to Roborock's DuoRoller) that do well at picking up dust, debris, and hair from tile and hard floors — which is what most Indian homes have. The i3 is compatible with the Clean Base auto-empty station sold separately.

However, the i3 uses iAdapt 3.0 navigation — reactive sensors rather than LiDAR mapping. It cleans effectively but follows a pattern that is less efficient than map-based robots. In larger Indian apartments (1,500 sq ft+), this means longer cleaning times and occasional areas missed. There is no mopping capability, and obstacle avoidance is basic — it bumps and redirects rather than identifying and avoiding objects.

At ₹24,999, it competes directly with the Roborock Q5 Pro, which has LiDAR mapping, 5,500 Pa suction, and superior navigation. For most Indian buyers at this price, Roborock Q5 Pro is a stronger choice unless brand loyalty to iRobot is a factor.

**Pros:** AeroForce rollers handle hair well, reliable cleaning performance, Roomba brand reliability
**Cons:** No LiDAR mapping, no mopping, basic obstacle avoidance, weaker value vs Roborock at same price

### 2. Roomba j7 — Best Obstacle Avoidance in India

**Price:** ₹44,999

The Roomba j7 is where iRobot's current technological leadership is clearest. PrecisionVision uses a forward-facing camera and on-device AI to identify specific objects on the floor — socks, phone cables, shoes, pet waste — and navigate around them. This is categorically different from proximity sensors that merely detect objects are present. The j7 knows what the object is and can be trained to avoid specific types.

For Indian homes with children who leave toys scattered on the floor, or dog owners concerned about indoor accidents, this capability is genuinely unique. No Chinese brand — Roborock, Dreame, or Ecovacs — matches iRobot's object identification accuracy. ReactiveAI and AIVI 3D are good, but they are not the same technology.

AeroForce cleaning handles Indian tile well. The iRobot app is functional in India with room mapping, scheduling, and zone controls. The j7 does not include a mopping function, which is a notable gap for Indian buyers who rely on daily wet mopping.

**Pros:** Best obstacle avoidance available, excellent hair pickup with rubber rollers, reliable brand, official India service
**Cons:** No mopping, expensive vs Chinese brands at same price with LiDAR, US-centric app design, fewer India-specific features

### 3. Roomba Combo j7+ — Premium with Mopping and Auto-Empty

**Price:** ₹59,999

The Combo j7+ is iRobot's attempt to address mopping — the biggest gap in Roomba's India appeal. It adds a wet mopping pad to the j7 and bundles the CleanBase auto-empty dock, which stores debris for up to 60 days. The combination sounds compelling on paper.

In practice, the mopping implementation is iRobot's weakest area. The Combo j7+ uses a basic retractable wet pad — it lays flat and drags wet behind the robot. There is no vibration, no rotation, no sonic action. On Indian tile and marble that accumulates daily kitchen residue and tracked-in dust, this basic mopping leaves a lot to be desired compared to Ecovacs OZMO Turbo or Roborock sonic mop systems.

The CleanBase is excellent — it genuinely reduces maintenance to a monthly bag replacement. And PrecisionVision obstacle avoidance is still the best in class. But at ₹59,999, the Combo j7+ faces the Roborock S8+ (₹59,999–64,999) and the Ecovacs T20 Omni (₹44,999–54,999) — both of which mop significantly better and navigate with LiDAR.

**Pros:** Best obstacle avoidance, CleanBase auto-empty is excellent, pet waste detection, solid India warranty support
**Cons:** Mopping is basic and not suited to Indian daily cleaning needs, expensive for the feature set vs Chinese alternatives

## Buying Guide / Comparison Analysis

**iRobot's Strengths for India:**
PrecisionVision obstacle avoidance is iRobot's genuine differentiator. Pet waste detection (the j7 will avoid and alert you if it detects a possible pet accident) is a real feature for dog owners. AeroForce rubber rollers handle hair well. iRobot has official India service and a clear warranty process.

**iRobot's Weaknesses for India:**
Mopping is poor across all models — an enormous problem for the Indian market where daily wet mopping is standard practice. LiDAR-based navigation is absent from the i3 and the j7 uses Imprint Smart Mapping rather than live LiDAR — less efficient than Roborock's PreciSense. Chinese brands at the same price offer more cleaning features for less money. The app has a US-first design philosophy and lacks some granular controls Indian users want.

**Value Comparison:**
- Roomba i3 (₹24,999) vs Roborock Q5 Pro (₹24,999): Roborock wins on features and efficiency
- Roomba j7 (₹44,999) vs Roborock S8 (₹44,999–49,999): S8 wins on mopping and vacuuming; j7 wins on obstacle avoidance
- Roomba Combo j7+ (₹59,999) vs Ecovacs T20 Omni (₹44,999): T20 Omni wins on mopping quality and price; j7+ wins on obstacle avoidance

**Service in India:** iRobot has an official India presence with authorised service centres in metro cities. Warranty claims are handled locally. Response times and parts availability are generally good based on user reports.
