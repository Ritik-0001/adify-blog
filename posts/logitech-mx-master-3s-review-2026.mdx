## Overview

The MX Master 3S is an update to the beloved MX Master 3, adding quieter primary clicks (90% quieter, per Logitech) and an 8,000 DPI sensor upgrade. The core features — the iconic MagSpeed electromagnetic scroll wheel, horizontal thumb scroll wheel, and right-hand ergonomic sculpting — remain unchanged.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Sensor:** Darkfield, 200–8,000 DPI (1 DPI increments)
- **Primary buttons:** Quiet click (90% quieter vs standard)
- **Scroll wheel:** MagSpeed electromagnetic (ratchet + free-spin)
- **Thumb scroll:** Horizontal smooth-scroll wheel
- **Connection:** Bluetooth 5.1 + Logi Bolt USB (2.4GHz)
- **Multi-device:** 3 devices (Easy Switch button)
- **Battery:** Up to 70 days per charge (USB-C)
- **Weight:** 141g
- **Hand orientation:** Right-hand only

## Ergonomics & Comfort

The MX Master 3S fits a right hand like it was designed specifically for it — because it was. The sculpted hump supports the palm, the thumb rests on a rubberised shelf, and the pinky/ring fingers settle into a natural cradle.

After 8 hours of development work, this mouse causes significantly less wrist strain than a flat, symmetrical mouse. The design promotes a natural handshake grip rather than the pronated flat-wrist position that causes RSI over time.

At 141g, it has a satisfying weight — not so heavy it tires the hand, but substantial enough to feel controlled.

---

## MagSpeed Scroll Wheel

The MagSpeed scroll wheel is the single feature that will make you never want to use another mouse. It switches automatically between two modes:

**Ratcheted:** Precise, one-step-at-a-time scrolling. Perfect for navigating code line by line or selecting items in a menu.

**Free-spin:** Flick it and it spins for seconds, scrolling thousands of lines in one gesture. Perfect for scrolling through long documentation, CSV files, or web pages.

The transition between modes is automatic based on speed — scroll slowly for ratcheted, flick quickly for free-spin. After a week, using any other scroll wheel feels like going back to a Nokia 3310.

The horizontal thumb scroll wheel is the quieter complement — lets you scroll horizontally in wide spreadsheets or split-screen code editors without lifting your hand.

---

## Quiet Clicks

The 3S's quieter primary clicks are the main difference from the MX Master 3. In office environments or while on video calls, the reduced click sound is genuinely useful. The feedback is still present — it's not mushy — just significantly softer.

## Software: Logi Options+

Logi Options+ lets you remap every button, set per-app behaviours (different scroll speed in Photoshop vs Chrome), and configure the Smart Actions automation. On macOS, it integrates tightly with Mission Control. The software is lightweight and reliable — uncommon in peripheral software.
