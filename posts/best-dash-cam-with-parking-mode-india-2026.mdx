## Why Parking Mode Matters in India

Indian parking is chaotic, crowded, and often unmonitored. The scenarios where parking mode pays for itself:

**Hit-and-run in parking lots:** Someone clips your door, scrapes your bumper, or rolls into your parked car in a mall basement. With parking mode active, you have footage of the vehicle and ideally the number plate. Without it, you have a repair bill and no recourse.

**Scratches and dents in tight spaces:** India's narrow lanes and crowded parking areas mean contact damage from passing vehicles, handcarts, and motorcycles is common. Parking mode captures all of it.

**Vandalism:** Less common than accidental damage, but it happens — particularly for cars parked on roadsides overnight.

**Attempted break-ins:** Motion-triggered recording captures anyone approaching or touching your vehicle.

**Flooding and storm damage:** Time-lapse parking mode can capture water levels rising around your vehicle during heavy rain — useful for insurance claims on flood damage.

## How Parking Mode Works — The Three Types

Not all parking modes are equal. Understanding the types helps you choose the right camera:

### 1. Motion Detection Parking Mode (Most Common)

The camera continuously monitors the front camera frame. When it detects motion (a vehicle moving nearby, a person walking past, anything entering the frame), it starts recording and saves the clip.

**Pros:** Captures everything that moves near your car
**Cons:** High false positive rate in busy areas — a camera on a busy street will trigger hundreds of clips from passing traffic

**Best for:** Cars parked in relatively quiet areas, home garages, office basements

### 2. Impact Detection (G-Sensor) Parking Mode

The camera enters low-power standby when parked. The G-sensor (accelerometer) remains active. Any impact — a bump, a door ding, someone leaning on the car — triggers a recording.

**Pros:** Very low false positive rate, conserves power
**Cons:** Misses non-contact events (someone keying your car carefully, for example)

**Best for:** Cars parked in areas with lots of passing motion but few actual impacts

### 3. Combination Mode (Motion + Impact)

Most good cameras with parking mode support both simultaneously — motion detection triggers recording for nearby vehicle movements, while the G-sensor ensures any physical contact is captured even without visible motion in the frame.

The Viofo A229 Plus and Blackvue DR900X both support combination mode.

### 4. Time-Lapse Parking Mode

Instead of continuous recording, the camera captures one frame every few seconds. This creates a sped-up record of everything that happened near your car while parked, using very little storage and power.

**Best for:** Long-duration parking (airport, overnight) where storage and battery life are concerns

## Power for Parking Mode — Hardwire Kit vs Battery Pack

This is the critical decision for parking mode setup:

### Hardwire Kit

A hardwire kit connects your dash cam directly to your car's fuse box, drawing low current from the car's battery.

**How it works:** The hardwire kit has a voltage cutoff — when the car battery drops below a threshold (typically 11.8V), it automatically cuts power to the dash cam to prevent the battery from draining too far to start the car.

**Installation:** Requires opening the fuse box panel and connecting to an ignition-switched fuse (for normal recording) and a constant-power fuse (for parking mode). Any car accessories shop in India can do this in 30–45 minutes. Cost: ₹200–₹400 for installation, plus the hardwire kit price (₹800–₹1,500 depending on brand).

**Pros:** No separate battery to manage, continuous parking mode for as long as the car battery allows (typically 8–24 hours depending on battery age and camera draw)

**Cons:** Requires professional installation, draws from the car battery

### Dash Cam Battery Pack / Capacitor Pack

External battery packs (like the Viofo VB1 or Blackvue B-124X) provide dedicated power for parking mode without touching the car battery.

**Pros:** No battery drain concern, can power parking mode for 12–48 hours independently, no fuse box work needed

**Cons:** Expensive (₹3,000–₹8,000 for a quality battery pack), needs to be charged separately (via the car's power when driving)

**Recommendation for most Indian buyers:** Hardwire kit. It's cheaper, simpler, and works reliably with modern car batteries in good condition.

## Tips for Parking Mode in Indian Conditions

**Park in shade when possible:** Indian summers can overheat cameras even in parking mode. Most cameras have thermal protection that shuts them down above 70–80°C.

**Use a microSD card rated for constant write cycles:** Regular cards wear out faster with continuous parking mode recording. Look for cards marketed for dash cam or surveillance use (Samsung PRO Endurance, Transcend High Endurance).

**Check parking mode footage regularly:** In motion-detection mode on a busy street, you may accumulate hundreds of clips quickly. Review and clear them periodically so you don't lose important footage due to storage overflow.

**Be aware of fuse box locations:** Most Indian cars (Maruti, Hyundai, Tata, etc.) have the fuse box under the dashboard on the driver's side. Any good car accessories installer will know the fuse layout.
