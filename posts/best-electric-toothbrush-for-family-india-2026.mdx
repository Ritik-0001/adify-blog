## Strategy 1: Shared Handle with Individual Heads (Most Cost-Effective)

The most cost-efficient family setup: one Oral-B handle shared between adults, with each person having their own brush head. Oral-B's universal head system makes this practical — any Pro series head works on any Pro series handle.

**How to share hygienically:** Each family member has their own colour-coded brush head. Store heads separately (head holders or small cups). The handle itself never contacts teeth — only the removable head does.

**Cost comparison for 2 adults:**
- 2 separate handles + 2 heads: ₹6,000–₹7,000
- 1 shared handle + 2 heads: ₹3,300–₹4,000

**Best handle for sharing:** Oral-B Pro 3 3000 (₹2,999–₹3,499) — reliable, 3 modes, pressure sensor, the right feature set for multiple adults with different cleaning needs. Each person selects their preferred mode.

## Charging Strategy for Multiple Brushes

**The Indian family charging problem:** Multiple electric toothbrushes mean multiple chargers — bathroom counter space is limited and outlet access is constrained in most Indian bathrooms.

**Solutions:**
1. **Shared charging stand:** Oral-B pro series handles charge on the same cradle — buy one charger, charge handles one at a time (charge overnight, 12+ hours for a full charge)
2. **USB charging brushes:** Mi T700 and Oclean F1 charge via USB — one USB hub with multiple ports charges several brushes simultaneously from one outlet
3. **Charge scheduling:** Assign a weekly charging night for each brush — with 14–30 day batteries, weekly rotation works
