The Logitech G502 has been one of the most beloved gaming mice since 2014 — and the G502 X Plus is its wireless, LIGHTFORCE-equipped evolution. For gamers who want a high-performance wireless mouse with a comfortable, feature-rich design, this is the one to consider.

---

## Overview

The **Logitech G502 X Plus** replaces the mechanical primary switches of the original G502 with Logitech's LIGHTFORCE hybrid optical-mechanical switches, adds LIGHTSPEED wireless at 25K Hz polling, LIGHTSYNC RGB, and runs on the HERO 25K sensor. It's the most feature-complete gaming mouse in Logitech's lineup.

## Key Specifications

- **Sensor:** HERO 25K (100–25,600 DPI, 1 DPI steps)
- **Primary Switches:** LIGHTFORCE hybrid optical-mechanical
- **Connection:** LIGHTSPEED 2.4GHz wireless + Bluetooth
- **Polling Rate:** Up to 2,000 Hz (LIGHTSPEED)
- **Battery:** Up to 130 hours (no RGB) / 37 hours (full RGB)

## Design & Ergonomics

The G502 X Plus is a right-handed ergonomic mouse with an aggressive, sculpted shape that fits medium-to-large hands in a palm or claw grip. The textured rubber side grips prevent slipping during intense sessions. The angular design is distinctly gaming-oriented — not office-subtle like the MX Master 3S.

The weight customisation system (up to 4× 3.6g weights) lets you tune the feel from 114g (light) to 128g (heavier). Most competitive players remove all weights; those who prefer heft add them. This flexibility is rare at this price.

---

## LIGHTFORCE Switches

The standout hardware upgrade over the previous G502. LIGHTFORCE hybrid switches use light beams to detect actuation rather than relying purely on mechanical contact — this eliminates the brief bounce period that mechanical switches require to debounce, resulting in a 68% faster response than traditional mechanical switches.

In practice: clicks feel crisp, consistent, and virtually instantaneous. There's no mechanical pre-travel mushiness. First-time users of LIGHTFORCE switches immediately notice the difference from standard gaming mice.

---

## HERO 25K Sensor

The HERO 25K is one of the most accurate gaming sensors available. Zero smoothing, filtering, or acceleration at any DPI setting from 100 to 25,600. For FPS games (Valorant, CS2), the sensor tracks exactly what your hand does — no deviation.

At 800 DPI (common for FPS players), the sensor is flawless across hard and soft mouse pads. No skipping, no drift. The practical DPI ceiling for gaming is around 3,200 — the 25K maximum is for extreme use cases.

---

## Wireless Performance

LIGHTSPEED wireless at 2,000 Hz polling matches wired performance — there is no perceptible latency difference in competitive gaming. The LIGHTSPEED receiver (USB-A nano dongle) should be used over Bluetooth for gaming; Bluetooth (125 Hz polling) is for productivity use.

Wireless range exceeds 10 metres in open space — from across a room with no drops. This is Logitech's mature LIGHTSPEED technology and it shows.

---

## Battery Life

130 hours without RGB — with a 1-hour play session daily, that's 4+ months per charge. With full LIGHTSYNC RGB on maximum brightness, battery drops to 37 hours. Most gamers disable RGB for competitive play, making the 130-hour figure realistic.

Charging via USB-C while in use — no downtime while charging.

---

## Software: G HUB

Logitech G HUB allows full DPI configuration, button remapping across 13 programmable buttons, RGB customisation, and macro creation. The onboard memory stores 5 profiles — switching between Windows desktop and gaming profiles is instant.

The scrollable DPI shift button (adjusts DPI on the fly) and the dedicated sniper button (holds a lower DPI while pressed, for precision aiming) are workflow improvements that regular mice don't offer.

## G502 X Plus vs MX Master 3S

Both cost similar amounts but serve very different purposes. The G502 X Plus is built for gaming precision — HERO 25K sensor, LIGHTFORCE switches, 2,000 Hz polling. The MX Master 3S is built for productivity — MagSpeed scroll wheel, horizontal thumb scroll, ergonomic all-day comfort.

Don't buy the G502 X Plus for office work; don't buy the MX Master 3S for competitive gaming.
