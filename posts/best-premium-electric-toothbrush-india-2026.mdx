## Who Needs a Premium Electric Toothbrush?

**The honest answer:** Clinically, a ₹2,500 Oral-B Pro 3 or Philips Sonicare 3100 delivers 90% of the plaque removal benefit vs the priciest flagship. The premium segment adds coaching intelligence, convenience, and motivation — not fundamentally different cleaning physics.

**Premium makes sense if:** You have a diagnosed gum disease history and need maximum coaching accountability; you travel frequently and want a single device for the world; your dentist has flagged persistent plaque despite regular brushing; or you simply buy the best tools in every category and want no compromises.

**The dentist cost math:** A periodontist consultation in Mumbai or Delhi runs ₹2,000–₹4,000. A single scaling and root planing session costs ₹8,000–₹20,000. A ₹12,000 toothbrush that prevents one such session pays for itself within the first 18 months.

## Buying Guide: Is Premium Worth It for Indian Buyers?

**Annual cost of premium:** ₹12,000 purchase + ₹2,800/year brush heads (Oral-B iO heads at ₹700/head, 4/year) = ₹14,800 Year 1, ₹2,800 thereafter.

**Annual cost of budget:** ₹2,500 purchase + ₹1,200/year brush heads = ₹3,700 Year 1, ₹1,200 thereafter.

**The gap:** ₹11,100 extra in Year 1. For that, you get AI coaching, more modes, and better materials — but not dramatically better plaque removal.

**Verdict for most Indian buyers:** The ₹5,000–₹7,000 bracket (Oral-B iO Series 4, Philips Sonicare 5100) delivers 90% of premium cleaning results at 50% of the price. Go premium if you have a documented gum health issue, if coaching features will genuinely motivate you, or if you simply want the best.
