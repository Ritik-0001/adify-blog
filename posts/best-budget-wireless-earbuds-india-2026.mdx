Under ₹1,000, India's wireless earbud market offers functional but basic options — suitable for casual listening but with limitations in call quality, battery life consistency, and long-term durability. Here is an honest guide to the best available and when to spend a little more.

## What to Expect Under ₹1,000

**No ANC:** Active Noise Cancellation requires chip processing that doesn't fit this price bracket — passive isolation only.

**Adequate audio quality:** Sound is functional for calls, podcasts, and casual music. Bass is often artificially boosted to compensate for driver quality limitations.

**Variable build quality:** Components at this price vary — some earbuds last 12+ months, others fail within 3. Buying from known brands (boAt, Realme, Noise) reduces this risk.

**Call quality:** Basic 2-microphone setups — adequate for quieter environments, struggles with road noise and windy conditions common in India.

**Battery:** 4–6 hours per charge, 20–30 hours total with case.
