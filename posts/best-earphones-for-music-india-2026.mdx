## What Matters Most for Music Listening?

Call quality relies on microphones. Gaming relies on latency. But music listening depends on an entirely different set of parameters:

**Driver quality:** The transducer that converts electrical signals to sound. Larger drivers often produce better bass; high-quality small drivers can produce better overall accuracy.

**Audio codec:** The format used to transmit audio wirelessly. Standard SBC is lossy. AAC is better. aptX is better still. LDAC is the current gold standard for wireless, transmitting at up to 990kbps versus SBC's 328kbps.

**Frequency response:** Does the earphone reproduce lows, mids, and highs accurately or does it overemphasise certain ranges?

**Sound stage and imaging:** Can you tell where instruments are placed in a mix? Can you separate individual instruments?

**Distortion:** Does the earphone introduce distortion at high volumes?

For true music enjoyment, prioritise driver quality and audio codec over convenience features like ANC or gaming modes.

**Why wired can sound better:** Wired audio bypasses Bluetooth codecs entirely. The audio signal travels digitally (or analogue) directly from your phone's DAC to the earphone driver. ## Wired vs Wireless for Music: Which Is Better?

For pure audio quality, wired is still technically superior at the same price point because it eliminates codec compression. The boAt Bassheads 100 at ₹400 wired will sound better than most Bluetooth earphones at ₹1,000 in raw audio fidelity.

However, wireless is far more convenient, and at the upper end of the market (Sony WF-1000XM5 level), LDAC wireless is now close enough to wired that the difference is subtle for most listeners.

**Recommendation:** If you primarily listen at a desk or workstation and care deeply about sound quality, a wired earphone is excellent value. If mobility matters and you have a modern Android phone, invest in LDAC-capable wireless like the Realme Buds Wireless 3 or Sony XM5.
