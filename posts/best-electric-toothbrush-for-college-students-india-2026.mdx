## What College Students Need

**USB charging:** Hostel rooms often have limited power sockets shared between multiple students. A USB-C or micro-USB charging toothbrush plugs into a laptop, power bank, or phone charger — no dedicated plug socket required.

**Long battery life:** Students forget to charge. A brush with 30+ day battery means charging once a month, not tracking weekly.

**Compact and travel-ready:** Weekend trips home, inter-city travel for exams, internships — a brush with a cap or travel case fits in a backpack without the bulk of a charging cradle.

**Durability:** Shared bathrooms, bag drops, and general student entropy mean the brush needs to survive mild abuse.

**Price under ₹3,000:** Student budgets in India typically cap electric toothbrush spend at ₹1,500–₹3,000. Every pick here fits this range.

## Student Oral Health in India: Why It Matters Now

College years are when oral health habits form for life — and when dental neglect typically begins. Irregular meal timings, high-carbohydrate canteen food, late-night study snacking, and reduced dental visits create conditions for cavity development that shows up in the 25–35 age bracket.

An electric toothbrush in college is an investment in your dental bills in your 30s. A ₹1,500 electric brush replacing proper brushing is cheaper than a single filling (₹1,000–₹3,000 per tooth) or root canal (₹5,000–₹15,000).
