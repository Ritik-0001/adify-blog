## What Can You Realistically Expect Under ₹5,000?

Let's be honest upfront: a home theatre system under ₹5,000 is a **2.1 channel system** — two satellite speakers plus a subwoofer. You are not getting Dolby Atmos, DTS:X, HDMI ARC, or 5.1 surround sound at this price point. Those features require at minimum ₹8,000–₹10,000.

What you *do* get is a massive upgrade over your flat-screen TV's built-in speakers. Most 32–43 inch TVs ship with 10W–16W downward-firing or rear-firing speakers that sound thin and tinny. Even a 40W 2.1 system with a proper subwoofer will deliver fuller dialogue, more bass for movies, and a wider soundstage.

**Who should buy a sub-₹5,000 home theatre system?**

- Students or renters in a single bedroom or small studio apartment
- Anyone setting up a budget PC gaming or movie-watching desk setup
- Parents buying a TV upgrade for a child's room
- First-time buyers who want to try 2.1 audio before committing to a soundbar or 5.1 system

If you have a larger living room (above 150 sq ft) or want proper cinematic audio, consider saving up for the ₹10,000 bracket instead.

## How to Connect a 2.1 System to Your TV

Most budget 2.1 systems connect via **3.5mm AUX**. Here is how to do it cleanly:

1. Check if your TV has a 3.5mm headphone output — most LG and Samsung TVs do
2. Use a 3.5mm male-to-male cable (included with the speaker, or buy a 1.5m cable)
3. Set your TV audio output to "External Speaker" in the settings menu
4. Control volume from the speaker unit or your TV remote (if your TV supports it)

If your TV only has optical (TOSLINK) or HDMI ARC outputs, a sub-₹5,000 system will not connect directly — you will need the ₹8,000–₹10,000 bracket for those inputs.
