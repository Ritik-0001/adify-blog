## Performance

Both phones run Snapdragon 8 Elite — the same chip. Real-world performance is identical for daily use, gaming, and AI tasks. Samsung Galaxy S25 has the edge in sustained performance due to better thermal management (vapour chamber + graphite composite). OnePlus 13 runs slightly hotter under sustained gaming load.

**Winner: Tie** — both Snapdragon 8 Elite devices perform identically for all tasks

---

## Camera System

Samsung Galaxy S25's camera is versatile and consistent — the 3× telephoto covers most day-to-day zoom needs, and Galaxy AI features (Generative Edit, Object Eraser, Nightography) produce polished, ready-to-share photos. Skin tones are natural, processing is conservative.

OnePlus 13's Hasselblad-tuned cameras are the clear winner for photographers — 6× optical zoom (vs Samsung's 3×) is a significant real-world advantage for portraits and events. The 50MP ultrawide is sharper than Samsung's 12MP ultrawide. Hasselblad Natural Colour Calibration produces more accurate, film-like colour rendering.

**Winner: OnePlus 13** (6× zoom, sharper ultrawide, Hasselblad tuning)

---

## Battery & Charging

The most decisive category. OnePlus 13's 6,000mAh battery provides 9-10 hours screen-on time — all-day and into the next morning for most users. 100W SUPERVOOC charges 0-100% in 26 minutes.

Samsung Galaxy S25's 4,000mAh with 25W charging: 6-7 hours SOT, ~90 minutes to full charge. The smaller battery and slower charging are Samsung's consistent weaknesses vs OnePlus.

**Winner: OnePlus 13** (decisively — 50% more battery capacity, 4× faster charging)

---

## Software & Long-Term Support

Samsung Galaxy S25 gets **7 years** of OS updates and security patches — until Android 21 in 2032. Galaxy AI features will grow with each update — Circle to Search, Live Translate, Chat Assist.

OnePlus 13 gets 4 years OS + 5 years security — decent but 3 years shorter than Samsung's commitment.

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S25** (7-year updates is a significant differentiator for a ₹80,000 investment)

---

## Size and Display

Samsung Galaxy S25 at 6.2" is notably more compact — fits comfortably in one hand. OnePlus 13's 6.82" LTPO AMOLED is significantly larger and better for media consumption, gaming, and reading, but is a two-hand phone.

The OnePlus 13's LTPO variable 1-120Hz refresh rate is more efficient than Samsung's fixed 120Hz — contributes to better battery life.

**Winner: Depends on preference** — compact one-hand use (Samsung) vs large media screen (OnePlus)

## Ecosystem

Samsung Galaxy S25 integrates with Galaxy Watch, Galaxy Buds, Link to Windows (PC), SmartThings, and DeX mode. For Samsung ecosystem users, the integration across devices is seamless.

OnePlus 13 integrates with Nothing ecosystem (parent company) and other Android devices via standard protocols.
