## What Makes a Chair Good for Long Sitting?

A chair designed for long sitting needs specific features that shorter-use chairs can skip:

**Seat depth adjustment.** For extended sitting, your thighs must be properly supported. If the seat is too deep, you'll round your lower back to reach the backrest; too shallow and your thighs aren't supported. Adjustable seat depth is essential for 6+ hour sessions.

**Adjustable lumbar support.** Static postures fatigue muscles. Your lumbar support needs to be in exactly the right position to reduce that fatigue. Adjustable lumbar height and depth lets you find that position precisely.

**High-quality foam or mesh longevity.** Budget chair seats flatten and lose support within a year of daily 8-hour use. Long-sitting chairs need denser foam or high-tension mesh that maintains its properties over years.

**Tilt mechanism with adjustable resistance.** Changing your back angle throughout the day (even slightly) significantly reduces spinal compression fatigue. A tilt with adjustable resistance based on body weight allows comfortable, effortless position changes.

## Setup Tips for Long Sitting in India

No chair works optimally without correct setup. These adjustments are especially important for extended sessions:

**Monitor at eye level.** If your monitor is too low, you'll crane your neck forward, which strains the entire upper back and contributes to fatigue during long sessions. Use a monitor stand or stack books to bring the screen to eye level.

**Keyboard at elbow height.** Your arms should rest on the armrests or desk at approximately 90 degrees at the elbow. Reaching up or down to a keyboard causes cumulative shoulder and wrist fatigue during long sessions.

**Take a break every 45–50 minutes.** Even the best ergonomic chair cannot substitute for movement. Brief 2–3 minute movement breaks every 45 minutes reduce the spinal load accumulation that causes end-of-day back fatigue.

**Use the recline during reading and calls.** A 100–110 degree back angle reduces spinal disc pressure compared to a 90-degree upright position. Recline slightly during activities that don't require forward-focused work.
