## 62,000 Strokes per Minute — What It Means

Standard Philips Sonicare models (ProtectiveClean 4300, 5100) operate at 31,000 strokes/minute. The DiamondClean 9000 runs at exactly double — 62,000.

**Clinical impact of higher frequency:** At 62,000 strokes/minute, the fluid dynamics effect (pressurised water and toothpaste forced between teeth) is significantly stronger. Independent studies show cleaning at 62,000 strokes/minute reaches approximately 6mm interdentally — vs 4mm at 31,000 strokes. For users whose main concern is interdental cleaning without a water flosser, the DiamondClean's frequency provides measurably better between-teeth cleaning.

**Sensitivity:** Higher frequency is not harsher — the gentle sonic motion at 62,000 is actually comparable in feel to 31,000 at lower intensity settings. The 3 intensities (low, medium, high) at each mode allow full adjustment.

## Glass Charging — Genuinely Elegant

The DiamondClean 9000's charging system is unique: the brush charges wirelessly when placed in its glass charging dock. The glass looks like a drinking glass — not a plastic charger.

**Practical value:** The glass is dishwasher safe, sits naturally on a bathroom counter without looking like tech equipment, and requires no connector alignment. Drop the brush in, it charges. Remove it, use it.

**Travel charging:** A separate USB travel case is available for the DiamondClean — charges the brush while protecting it, using USB-C.
