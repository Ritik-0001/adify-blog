Xiaomi brings its aggressive value proposition to electric toothbrushes — the Mi T300 and T700 deliver features at prices that undercut Oral-B and Philips significantly. Here is whether the value is real.

## Mi T300 — Entry Xiaomi Electric Toothbrush

**Price:** ₹699–₹899

**Specifications:**
- 31,000 strokes/minute (same as Philips Sonicare standard)
- 2 modes (Clean + Sensitive)
- No pressure sensor
- 25-day battery
- USB charging (Micro-USB)
- IPX7 waterproof

**Performance:** The T300's sonic motor runs at 31,000 strokes/minute — identical frequency to Philips Sonicare ProtectiveClean models at ₹1,999+. Cleaning performance is adequate for daily use. The 25-day battery with USB charging means less charger management than Oral-B's proprietary induction charger.

**Limitation:** No pressure sensor. For users prone to hard brushing, this is a genuine gap. At ₹700, it is the most affordable sonic toothbrush from an established brand in India.

## Mi T700 — Best Xiaomi Electric Toothbrush

**Price:** ₹1,299–₹1,499

**Specifications:**
- 31,000 strokes/minute
- Pressure sensor indicator (LED)
- Mi app connectivity (brushing duration, mode tracking)
- 25-day battery
- USB charging
- IPX7 waterproof
- 3 modes

**App connectivity at ₹1,300:** The T700's Mi Home app integration provides brushing session reports — duration, pressure events, and mode used. No other toothbrush under ₹2,000 offers app tracking. For data-conscious buyers or those trying to improve brushing habits, this is a unique value proposition.

**Pressure sensor:** The T700's pressure indicator is an LED light (not a physical pause like Philips) — shows when pressure is too high. Better than nothing, but less reliable than a physical response.

## Where Xiaomi Toothbrushes Fall Short

**Head availability:** Mi replacement heads are available on Amazon India and Mi.com but not in pharmacies. For users who buy heads in person (pharmacies, medical shops), this is a practical limitation.

**After-sales service:** Mi service centres handle smartphones primarily. For toothbrush issues, customer service is online-first. Less convenient than Oral-B service available at most large pharmacies.

**Clinical evidence:** Oral-B and Philips have decades of independent clinical studies. Xiaomi's brushes are well-built but lack the same depth of published clinical validation.
