## iPhone Charging Port History — Which iPhone Has Which Port?

Before buying a power bank for your iPhone, you need to know which port your iPhone has. Apple switched from Lightning to USB-C with iPhone 15 in 2023.

| iPhone Model | Charging Port | Fast Charge Method |
|---|---|---|
| iPhone XS, XR, 11 series | Lightning | USB-A 5W standard OR USB-C to Lightning cable (18W PD) |
| iPhone 12, 12 Pro | Lightning | USB-C to Lightning cable (20W PD for 50% in 30 min) |
| iPhone 13, 13 Pro | Lightning | USB-C to Lightning cable (20W PD) |
| iPhone 14, 14 Pro | Lightning | USB-C to Lightning cable (20W PD) |
| iPhone 15, 15 Pro | USB-C | USB-C PD cable (27W max) |
| iPhone 16, 16 Pro | USB-C | USB-C PD cable (30W+ max) |

**Key insight:** iPhone 12 and later support USB Power Delivery (PD) fast charging at 20W. To access this on Lightning iPhones, you need a USB-C to Lightning cable AND a USB-C PD power bank. On iPhone 15+, any USB-C PD cable works.

## What Is MFi Certification and Do You Actually Need It?

**MFi (Made for iPhone/iPad/iPod)** is Apple's licensing program for accessories. An MFi-certified cable or power bank has been officially approved by Apple and contains Apple-validated chips.

**For charging purposes, MFi matters mainly for Lightning cables** — uncertified Lightning cables can trigger "This accessory is not supported" errors on newer iOS versions, and in rare cases have caused issues with charging.

**For USB-C to USB-C cables (iPhone 15+):** USB-C is an open standard. Apple cannot lock it down the same way. You do not need an MFi USB-C cable for fast charging an iPhone 15 or 16.

**For power banks themselves:** There is no MFi certification for power banks as a category. MFi matters for cables, not the power bank unit. So "MFi certified power bank" claims you see in listings are largely marketing — focus on the cable quality instead.

**Bottom line:**
- iPhone 15/16 (USB-C): Any quality USB-C to USB-C cable + any USB-C PD power bank. MFi is irrelevant.
- iPhone 12–14 (Lightning): Use an Apple-branded or MFi-certified USB-C to Lightning cable with a USB-C PD power bank for fast charging.
- iPhone XS/XR/11 (Lightning): Standard USB-A to Lightning cable works fine. No fast charge available without USB-C PD.

## How Fast Does a Power Bank Charge an iPhone?

Charging speed depends on the power bank's output and which iPhone you have.

**iPhone 15 (USB-C port):**
- Standard USB-A 5W from power bank → 100% in ~3 hours (too slow)
- USB-C PD 20W → 50% in ~30 minutes, 100% in ~1.5 hours
- The Anker PowerCore 10000 USB-A output (~12W) does not trigger PD — it charges at standard speed unless you use a USB-C power bank like the Anker III 20000 PD

**iPhone 14 (Lightning port) — fast charge setup:**
- You need: USB-C PD power bank + USB-C to Lightning MFi cable

## Why MagSafe Power Banks Are Overpriced in India

MagSafe-compatible power banks — those that magnetically attach to the back of iPhone 12+ and wirelessly charge via Magsafe — are sold in India at ₹3,000–₹6,000 from brands like Anker (MagGo series), Belkin, and Mophie.

**Why they are not recommended for most Indian buyers:**

1. **Slower charging:** MagSafe wireless charging from a power bank tops out at 7.5W in India (vs 15W with Apple's own MagSafe adapter). A wired USB-C PD connection is 2x–3x faster.

2. **Significant price premium:** A MagSafe power bank costs ₹3,000–₹6,000 for 5,000mAh — roughly 3x the cost per mAh of a wired alternative.

3. **Heat generation:** Wireless charging generates more heat than wired, which is hard on battery health over time.

4. **Limited capacity:** Most MagSafe power banks are 5,000–10,000mAh due to size constraints.

**When MagSafe makes sense:** If you already own the ecosystem, find cable-free charging genuinely valuable, and have the budget. For most Indian buyers — stick with wired, it is faster and cheaper.
