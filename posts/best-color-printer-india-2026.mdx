## Colour Inkjet vs Colour Laser — The Core Decision

Before buying any colour printer in India, you need to understand the fundamental technology split:

**Colour inkjet (ink tank)** — Best for: photos, home printing, mixed colour and mono
- Colour cost: ₹0.15–₹0.25 per page (ink tank) or ₹3–₹5 per page (cartridge)
- Photo quality: Excellent (especially dye-based inks)
- Speed: 5–8 ipm colour
- Volume: Low to medium

**Colour laser** — Best for: office colour documents, high volume, consistent output
- Colour cost: ₹4–₹8 per page
- Photo quality: Good for documents, poor for photos
- Speed: 4–12 ppm colour
- Volume: High, consistent

The verdict for most Indian buyers: **colour inkjet (ink tank) is dramatically cheaper per page for colour printing.** Colour laser makes sense only if you need very consistent, professional document quality at scale.

## Epson L805 — The Dedicated Photo Printer

If colour photo printing is your primary use case, the Epson L805 at ₹20,999 is a category apart from the other options in this guide.

**Key difference:** The L805 uses a **6-colour dye ink system** (CMYK + Light Cyan + Light Magenta) instead of the standard 4-colour CMYK. This additional ink range produces:
- More natural skin tones
- Smoother gradient transitions
- Deeper shadows and brighter highlights
- Lab-quality photo output on glossy or matte photo paper

**L805 specs:**
- Ink: 6-bottle dye system (C, M, Y, K, LC, LM)
- Photo size: Up to A4
- Optimum resolution: 5760 x 1440 dpi
- Print speed: 37 seconds per 4x6" photo at standard quality
- Connectivity: WiFi + USB
- Price: ~₹20,999

The L805 is NOT a good choice for document printing — it's a dedicated photo printer. It prints documents fine, but the 6-colour photo ink system is overkill (and higher running cost) for text.

**Who should buy the L805:**
- Photographers printing their own work
- Families that print lots of photos at home
- Anyone running a small photo print business
