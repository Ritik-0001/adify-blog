Modern laptops have too few ports. The Anker 7-in-1 USB-C Hub is the most popular solution in India — a small, affordable dongle that adds 4K HDMI, USB-A, USB-C, SD card, and power pass-through to any USB-C laptop.

## Overview

The Anker 7-in-1 USB-C Hub (model A8346) is one of Anker's most popular products globally — a compact 7-port hub connecting via a short USB-C cable. No power brick required; it draws power from the host laptop. Compatible with MacBook, Windows laptops, and any USB-C host.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Ports:** 4K HDMI, USB-C (100W PD), 2× USB-A 3.0, SD, microSD, USB-C Data
- **HDMI:** 4K/30Hz or 1080p/60Hz
- **USB-A Speed:** USB 3.0 (5Gbps)
- **Power Delivery:** Up to 100W pass-through
- **Bus Powered:** Yes (no external power needed)
- **Cable Length:** ~12cm attached cable
- **Dimensions:** 110 × 39 × 13mm
- **Weight:** 63g
- **Price (India):** ₹3,999–₹4,999

## Build & Design

The aluminium shell matches the MacBook's aesthetic perfectly — matte silver or space grey options. It's small enough to drop in any pocket and doesn't add meaningful bag weight.

The attached cable approach (vs detachable) means one less thing to lose. The cable is short by design — it sits flush against the laptop without hanging awkwardly. For a desk setup, the hub sits neatly beside the laptop.

---

## Port Performance

**HDMI:** 4K/30Hz works reliably with external monitors, projectors, and TVs. For productivity (text, code, browser tabs), 30Hz is acceptable. For video or animation work at 4K, the frame rate limitation is visible — 1080p/60Hz mode is smoother for motion.

**USB-A:** True USB 3.0 5Gbps — external SSDs read/write at full speed. File transfers from the Samsung T7 maintain 400–500 MB/s through the hub.

**SD/microSD:** Simultaneous use of both slots is supported. Transfer speeds are adequate for offloading camera cards (~80 MB/s), though a dedicated UHS-II card reader is faster for professional workflows.

**Power Delivery:** 100W pass-through charges MacBook Pro 16" at full speed while all other ports are in use. No compromise.

---

## Heat

The hub warms up during heavy simultaneous use (4K HDMI + SSD + charging). Not uncomfortable to touch, but warm. Anker's temperature management is adequate — no throttling observed in sustained use. Keep it on the desk rather than buried under papers.

---

## Compatibility

Tested with: MacBook Air M2/M3, MacBook Pro 14 M4, Dell XPS 13, Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon. Works plug-and-play on all. The 4K HDMI output worked on Samsung, LG, and Dell monitors without configuration.

On M1/M2/M3 MacBooks, only one external display is supported (Apple limitation on Air models). The M3 Air supports two displays, but the hub only carries one HDMI — you'd need a second USB-C to HDMI adapter for the second display.
