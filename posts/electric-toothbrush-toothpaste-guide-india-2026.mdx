Your electric toothbrush and toothpaste work together — the brush provides mechanical plaque disruption while the toothpaste delivers the active ingredients (fluoride, antimicrobials, whitening agents). Choosing the right toothpaste maximises your brush's effectiveness.

---

## The Most Important Factor: Fluoride

**Fluoride is non-negotiable.** It is the single most evidence-backed ingredient in any oral care product — remineralises early enamel lesions and prevents cavities. Any toothpaste without fluoride is not recommended regardless of other claims.

**Indian context for fluoride:** India has some of the highest rates of dental cavities in Asia — high carbohydrate diets (rice, roti, sweets) combined with inconsistent brushing create significant cavity risk. Fluoride is essential.

**Recommended fluoride levels:**
- Adults: 1,000–1,500 ppm fluoride (most adult Indian toothpastes)
- Children under 3: Smear of 1,000 ppm (use sparingly)
- Children 3–6: Pea-sized amount of 1,000 ppm
- Children 6+: 1,000–1,500 ppm

---

## Abrasiveness (RDA) — Why It Matters with Electric Brushes

RDA (Relative Dentine Abrasivity) measures how abrasive a toothpaste is. Electric toothbrushes, particularly oscillating (Oral-B), generate more mechanical action than manual brushing — using a highly abrasive toothpaste with an electric brush can accelerate enamel wear.

**RDA guidelines:**
- Under 70: Low abrasion — safe for daily use with any brush
- 70–100: Medium abrasion — fine for most users
- 100–150: High abrasion — caution with electric brushes, especially on sensitive teeth
- Above 150: Very high — not recommended for regular use with electric brushes

**High-RDA toothpastes to use cautiously with electric brushes:**
- Highly abrasive whitening toothpastes (some Colgate Visible White, HiOra variants)
- Activated charcoal toothpastes (typically very high RDA)
- Baking soda-heavy formulas

## What to Avoid

- **Charcoal toothpastes:** Very high abrasion (RDA typically 150+), no clinical evidence for whitening, can damage enamel with electric brush mechanical action
- **Toothpastes without fluoride:** No cavity protection regardless of other claims
- **Herbal/ayurvedic toothpastes without fluoride:** Popular in India (Patanjali Dant Kanti, etc.) — lack fluoride, providing no cavity prevention benefit
- **Very abrasive whitening toothpastes:** Use cautiously with electric brushes — check RDA if available
