## Why 5-Star AC Makes Financial Sense in India

India's Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) rates ACs on a star scale from 1 to 5, where 5-star is the most efficient. The ISEER (Indian Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) is the number behind the star label — a higher ISEER means less electricity consumed per unit of cooling.

The financial case for 5-star in India is strong:

**Sample calculation for 1.5 ton AC, 8 hours/day, 5 months of AC season, ₹8/unit:**

- 3-star AC (ISEER 3.5): Approximately 1,060 units per year → ₹8,480 per season
- 5-star AC (ISEER 4.7): Approximately 780 units per year → ₹6,240 per season
- **Annual savings: ₹2,240 per year**

For households using AC 10–12 hours daily (common in Delhi/NCR summer):

- 3-star: ₹10,500–₹12,000 per season
- 5-star: ₹7,800–₹9,000 per season
- **Annual savings: ₹2,500–₹3,000 per year**

The price premium for a 5-star over a 3-star AC is typically ₹7,000–₹10,000. Break-even is 3–4 years for 8 hours/day usage, 2–3 years for 10+ hours/day.

**For any household running the AC for 5+ months a year and 8+ hours daily, 5-star is the rational financial choice.**
