## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes After Dental Scaling

**What happens to teeth and gums after scaling:** Professional scaling removes calculus that has formed along and below the gumline. Once calculus is removed, the previously covered gum tissue is exposed and may be inflamed, red, and sensitive. Teeth may feel "loose" (they are not — the perceived movement was calculus-supported before), and exposed root surfaces may be temperature-sensitive. All of this is normal and resolves within 1–2 weeks as inflammation subsides.

**What is normal post-scaling:**
- Gum bleeding for 24–48 hours — normal, should decrease each day
- Tooth sensitivity to cold/hot — normal, peaks day 2–3, improves within 2 weeks
- Gum tenderness to touch — normal for 3–5 days
- Perceiving teeth as slightly separated — normal (calculus had filled these gaps)

**What requires a follow-up call:**
- Bleeding that continues beyond 72 hours or worsens
- Severe pain (not mild tenderness) beyond day 3
- Swelling that is increasing, not decreasing
- Fever (indicates possible infection)

**Preventing calculus re-accumulation:** Calculus is mineralised plaque — it forms when plaque (soft bacterial biofilm) is not removed consistently and saliva minerals harden it over days to weeks. Electric toothbrushes remove significantly more plaque than manual brushing in the critical gumline and interproximal zones where calculus preferentially forms. Regular electric brushing twice daily after scaling consistently extends the interval before scaling is again needed.

**Clinical evidence:** A systematic review in *The Cochrane Database* confirmed that consistent electric toothbrush use reduces calculus accumulation by 11.6% compared to manual brushing over 3–6 months — measurably extending the scaling interval and reducing the severity of calculus when the next scaling occurs.

**India-specific context:** The majority of Indian adults visit a dentist only when in pain — meaning scaling often happens infrequently and calculus accumulation is severe by the time professional care occurs. The post-scaling period is clinically the ideal moment to establish an electric brushing habit that prevents the return to heavy calculus within months.
