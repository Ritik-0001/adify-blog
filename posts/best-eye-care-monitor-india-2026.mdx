## Buying Guide

### Flicker-Free vs PWM — What You Need to Know
Most cheap monitors use PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) to dim the backlight — the screen flashes rapidly at 200–1000Hz. You can't see this consciously, but your eyes detect it, causing fatigue and headaches over hours. Flicker-free monitors use DC dimming instead — genuinely eliminating this flicker. Look for TÜV Rheinland Flicker-Free certification.

### Low Blue Light — Does It Help?
Blue light (400–450nm) suppresses melatonin and disrupts sleep when exposed in the evening. Low Blue Light modes filter this range. The effect is real but modest — you'll notice less eye redness at end of day and potentially better sleep if you work evenings. Windows Night Mode and macOS Night Shift do the same thing in software (free).

### The Most Underrated Eye Care Feature: Ergonomics
A monitor at wrong height forces you to tilt your neck — causing eye strain and headaches. Ideal position: monitor top edge at or slightly below eye level. If your monitor doesn't have height adjustment (most budget monitors don't), a ₹599 monitor stand or stack of books achieves the same.

### Night Routine for Indian WFH Users
1. Switch to Low Blue Light / Reader Mode after 7pm
2. Reduce brightness to 20–30% in dim rooms
3. Use Windows Night Light or macOS Night Shift
4. Take 20-20-20 breaks: every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds

## FAQ

**Q: Do eye care monitors actually help with headaches?**
A: Flicker-free genuinely helps for people sensitive to PWM flicker. Low blue light helps most with evening eye redness and sleep disruption. Ergonomics (monitor at correct height) often helps more than either.

**Q: What's the difference between BenQ Eye Care and ASUS Eye Care?**
A: Both cover Flicker-Free and Low Blue Light. BenQ's Brightness Intelligence+ adds an automatic ambient light sensor that adjusts brightness dynamically — no other budget monitor has this. ASUS Eye Care is standard certification without the smart sensor.

**Q: Can I just use Night Mode on Windows instead of buying an eye care monitor?**
A: Windows Night Light reduces blue light for free. But it doesn't fix PWM flicker — for that you need a hardware flicker-free monitor. If PWM headaches are your main issue, a flicker-free monitor is worth buying.

**Q: Is IPS or VA better for eye care?**
A: IPS is better for bright rooms (wider viewing angles, more consistent brightness). VA is better for dark rooms (higher contrast, less need for high brightness). Both can be certified flicker-free — the panel type matters less than the flicker-free certification.
