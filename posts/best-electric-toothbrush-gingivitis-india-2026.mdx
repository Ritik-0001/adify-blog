## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Gingivitis

**What causes gingivitis in India:** India's gingivitis prevalence is driven by a combination of factors — high-sugar and high-starch diets (refined carbohydrates accelerate plaque formation), low consistent flossing rates, tobacco use (pan, gutka, cigarettes), and diabetes rates among the world's highest (diabetes impairs gum immunity). Electric toothbrushes address the core issue: bacterial plaque removal at the gumline.

**Sonic vs. oscillating for inflamed gums:** Both technologies outperform manual brushing for gingivitis. Sonic brushes (Philips Sonicare) use fluid dynamics to flush bacteria from the sulcus without direct mechanical contact — gentler for acutely inflamed tissue. Oscillating-rotating brushes (Oral-B) wrap the round head around each tooth for precise plaque disruption — preferred by many periodontists for thoroughness. The right choice depends on your comfort and compliance.

**Key features for gingivitis:**
1. **Gum Care or Sensitive mode** — essential, not optional
2. **Pressure sensor** — pressing harder does NOT clean better; it causes recession
3. **2-minute timer with 30-second quadrant pacer** — most people miss gumline time
4. **Soft bristles only** — medium or hard bristles worsen gum irritation

**Clinical evidence:** A 2019 systematic review in the Journal of Clinical Periodontology found electric toothbrushes reduced gingival bleeding by 17% more than manual brushing. Philips' own clinical data shows Sonicare reduces gingivitis scores 7× better than a manual brush in 4 weeks — the most-cited statistic in electric toothbrush clinical literature.

**When gingivitis becomes periodontitis:** If gums bleed consistently for more than 2 weeks despite improved brushing, see a dentist. Gingivitis is reversible; periodontitis (with bone loss) is not. An electric toothbrush helps prevent the transition, but cannot reverse established periodontitis.
