## Do 15-Inch Laptops Need a Cooling Pad?

Not always — but the answer depends more on how you use the laptop than on screen size. The 15.6-inch form factor is the most popular in India, covering everything from the Dell Inspiron 15, HP Pavilion 15, and Lenovo IdeaPad 15 on the everyday end, to the Asus TUF Gaming A15 and HP Omen 15 on the performance end.

Everyday 15-inch laptops doing office work and browsing rarely break 70°C. These do not need a cooling pad — a clean vent and occasional compressed air is sufficient. The need for a cooling pad starts when the laptop is doing sustained work: video editing, Zoom calls for hours at a stretch, or any gaming. Once CPU temperatures regularly exceed 82°C during a task, performance starts to suffer.

Indian summers make this worse. At 35°C ambient, any laptop doing moderate-to-heavy work will run 8–12°C hotter than it would in a 25°C room.

## Why Fan Placement Matters More Than Fan Count

This is the most underrated aspect of cooling pad selection for 15-inch laptops. Every laptop has its bottom air intake vents in a specific location — and the cooling pad's fans must align with those vents to be effective.

**Where to find your laptop's intake vents:** Flip the laptop over. Look for the mesh or slot openings. Most 15.6-inch laptops have vents along the bottom centre or rear of the base.

The Havit HV-F2056's three-fan layout covers a wide area of the base, making it effective regardless of where your laptop's intake vents are positioned. The Deepcool N65's single 140mm centre fan works best with laptops that have centrally located vents (Dell Inspiron, HP Pavilion, Lenovo IdeaPad — most of them).

If your laptop has vents only at the very back (near the hinge), a cooling pad with rear-positioned fans is more effective. The Amazon Basics single-fan pad has a central fan position that misses rear-vent laptops.

## Which 15-Inch Laptops Run the Hottest?

Some 15-inch models are notorious for running hot under load:

- **Lenovo IdeaPad Gaming 3 15**: Frequently hits 88–92°C on CPU during gaming without a cooling pad
- **HP Omen 15**: GPU runs 80–85°C even with factory fan curves at maximum
- **Asus VivoBook 15 (Ryzen 5500U)**: Throttles above 80°C — a consistent complaint in user reviews
- **Dell Inspiron 15 5515**: Runs warm during sustained video editing; benefits from 5–7°C reduction with a cooling pad

For all of these, the Havit HV-F2056 or Deepcool N65 will deliver noticeable improvement.

## Setup Tips for Maximum Cooling

Once you have the right cooling pad, a few habits amplify its benefit:

**Clear space beneath the pad.** Cooling pads raise the laptop slightly off the desk. Make sure there is open space below the pad — not books or a fabric surface that traps heat.

**Run the laptop on a hard surface.** Putting a cooling pad on a soft surface (pillow, blanket, lap) defeats the purpose, as it blocks airflow below the pad itself.

**Clean the vents every three months.** Use a can of compressed air (available for around ₹199 on Amazon) to blow out dust from the laptop vents. A dusty laptop with a cooling pad is less effective than a clean laptop without one.

**Match your cooling need to the fan speed.** If you are doing light work and the fan noise bothers you, most cooling pads with speed controls let you run at lower RPM. For gaming or video editing, run the fans at full speed.
