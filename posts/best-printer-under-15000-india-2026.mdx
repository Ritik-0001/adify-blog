At under ₹15,000, you can finally get a printer that does everything — print, scan, copy, and connect wirelessly — while still keeping running costs extremely low through ink tank technology. This is the price point where you stop compromising.

The Epson L3252 represents exactly this combination. For ₹12,499, you get:

## Epson L3252 — The Best All-Rounder Under ₹15K

The Epson L3252 is the refined successor to the popular Epson L3151. It keeps everything that worked — the 003 ink tank system, flatbed scanner, compact body — and adds auto-duplex printing and a slightly faster print engine.

**Key specs:**
- Print speed: 10 ipm mono, 5 ipm colour
- Ink: Epson 003 bottles (included in box)
- Pages per ink set: 7,500 mono, 5,500 colour
- Connectivity: USB + 2.4GHz WiFi
- Scanner: Flatbed, 600 x 1200 dpi
- Auto-duplex: Yes
- Price: ~₹12,499

The auto-duplex feature alone saves significant paper costs if you print reports or study materials regularly — it automatically prints on both sides of the sheet without manual flipping.

Print quality is excellent for documents and good for colour prints. The Epson 003 ink system is well-proven — reliable, clog-resistant, and widely available as refill bottles across India (typically ₹200–₹300 per bottle).

Mobile printing works through Epson iPrint (iOS and Android) and supports AirPrint for iPhones. Setup takes about 15 minutes including WiFi configuration.

## HP LaserJet MFP M141a — The Laser Alternative

The HP LaserJet MFP M141a at ₹9,999 is the best budget laser printer in India, and it's worth serious consideration at this budget if your printing is predominantly text documents.

**Key specs:**
- Print speed: 20 ppm mono (laser speed)
- Technology: Laser (mono only, no colour)
- Connectivity: USB only (no WiFi on this model)
- Scanner: Flatbed
- Copier: Yes
- Toner: HP 105A cartridge (~₹1,800 for ~1,000 pages)
- Price: ~₹9,999

The laser advantage is real: 20 pages per minute versus 10 ppm on the Epson L3252. If you're printing 50-page reports or large documents regularly, laser speed matters. Laser toner also never dries up if you don't use the printer for weeks — unlike inkjet heads that can clog.

The M141a is USB-only, which limits it to wired connections. If you need WiFi on a laser printer, look at the HP LaserJet MFP M236sdw at around ₹14,999, which sits just within the ₹15,000 budget.
