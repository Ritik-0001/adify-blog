In the business ultrabook segment, the Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon and HP EliteBook 840 are the two most-recommended options for developers and enterprise professionals in India. Both are built for durability, security, and long-term use. Here's how they differ.

---

## Overview

The **ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 12** is Lenovo's flagship ultrabook — legendary keyboard, featherlight at 1.12 kg, MIL-SPEC durability, and excellent Linux compatibility. The **HP EliteBook 840 G11** is HP's premium business answer — excellent display options, Sure View privacy screen option, and a mature enterprise management ecosystem.

## Keyboard

The ThinkPad keyboard is the best on any Windows laptop — widely acknowledged by developers, analysts, and writers who have used both. The key travel, feedback, and layout (with the iconic red TrackPoint nub) make extended typing sessions comfortable in ways competitors don't match. If you type for a living, this matters.

The EliteBook 840's keyboard is good — better than most consumer laptops — but clearly behind the ThinkPad in feel and feedback. The EliteBook's trackpad is slightly larger and better than the X1's, though.

---

## Build & Durability

Both pass MIL-STD-810 testing. The X1 Carbon's carbon fibre lid makes it significantly lighter despite equal durability — at 1.12 kg, it's one of the lightest 14" business laptops on the market.

The EliteBook 840 at 1.34 kg uses aluminium construction — also extremely durable but 220g heavier. In a laptop bag carried daily, that difference is felt over time.

---

## Security Features

HP EliteBook 840 has an edge here: the optional HP Sure View privacy screen (tilt-based screen blackout for side viewers) is excellent for working in public or on flights. HP's Wolf Security platform and Sure Start BIOS protection are mature enterprise security tools.

ThinkPad X1 offers a physical camera shutter, fingerprint reader, IR face recognition, and TPM 2.0 — comprehensive but the privacy screen advantage goes to HP.

---

## Linux Support

The ThinkPad X1 Carbon is the gold standard for Linux laptops. Lenovo has partnered with Canonical and Red Hat; Ubuntu and Fedora run flawlessly with all hardware functioning correctly out of the box. Developers who work primarily in Linux should strongly favour the X1.

The EliteBook 840 works with Linux but requires more configuration — some features like the privacy screen and HP-specific biometrics don't work natively.

---

## Pros & Cons

### ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 12
**Pros:** Best keyboard on Windows, lightest at 1.12 kg, best Linux compatibility, longer battery, TrackPoint for keyboard-centric navigation
**Cons:** More expensive, privacy screen requires add-on, trackpad smaller than EliteBook

### HP EliteBook 840 G11
**Pros:** Built-in Sure View privacy screen, larger trackpad, HP Wolf Security ecosystem, lower entry price, excellent display options
**Cons:** Heavier, keyboard is good but not ThinkPad-level, Linux compatibility requires more work

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the ThinkPad X1 Carbon if:**
- You're a developer using Linux
- You type heavily and the keyboard matters
- Portability (weight) is a daily concern
- You want the best-in-class business ultrabook

**Buy the HP EliteBook 840 if:**
- You work in public frequently and need a privacy screen
- You're in an HP enterprise environment
- Budget is a consideration (₹20,000 savings)
- Trackpad size matters to you

---

## Final Verdict

For developers: **ThinkPad X1 Carbon** — the keyboard and Linux support make it the clear choice. For enterprise professionals in HP environments who need a privacy screen: **EliteBook 840** — the Sure View feature alone can justify the switch. For everyone else: the X1 Carbon's keyboard and weight advantage make it the better daily companion.
