Battery life matters more for Indian electric toothbrush users than for most global markets — frequent domestic and international travel, intermittent power supply in many cities, and India's humid climate (which can affect charging contacts over time) all make a long-lasting battery a practical necessity, not just a convenience.

---

## Why Battery Life Matters for Indian Users

**Travel frequency:** India's diverse geography means frequent long-distance travel — 36-hour train journeys, remote pilgrimage sites, hill stations without reliable power. A toothbrush that needs charging every week is a travel liability.

**Power cuts:** Load shedding remains common in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities, and less predictable in metro areas during summer months. A 14–30 day battery means you're never caught with a dead toothbrush from a single missed charging session.

**Charging contact corrosion:** India's humidity and hard water can accelerate corrosion on inductive charging contacts. Brushes that need charging weekly see more charging cycles and more contact wear. A 30-day battery halves your annual charging cycles vs a 14-day battery.

**Twice-daily brushing math:** Most battery claims are based on twice-daily 2-minute sessions = 4 minutes/day. A "14-day battery" means 56 minutes of total use between charges. A "30-day battery" means 120 minutes. Verify against this standard when comparing claims.

## Buying Guide: USB-C vs Proprietary Charging

**The growing USB-C advantage:** Oclean's USB-C charging is a practical differentiator for Indian travelers. One universal cable charges your phone, laptop, earbuds, and toothbrush. Oral-B and Philips proprietary chargers must be packed separately and are expensive to replace if lost.

**Inductive charging:** Oral-B and Philips use inductive (wireless contact) charging stands — the brush sits on a stand and charges without a physical connection. These are elegant but the proprietary base means finding a replacement charger in India is limited to authorized retailers and Amazon.

**Voltage compatibility:** All models listed are 100–240V universal — Indian power (220–240V) works without adapters or converters.
