## What Makes a Great Daily Commute Scooter?

Daily commuting puts different demands on a scooter than weekend riding. Here's what matters when you're riding the same 30km route every morning and evening:

- **Reliable range:** At least 60-70km real-world minimum (to handle one charge per 1.5 days minimum)
- **Storage:** Helmet under the seat plus daily essentials
- **Ride comfort:** Suspension tuned for Indian roads with potholes and speed bumps
- **Charging convenience:** Either fast public charging nearby or comfortable home overnight charging
- **Service proximity:** If something breaks, the service centre needs to be reachable
- **Running costs:** Electricity cost vs petrol savings over years

Let's see which scooters deliver best on these daily commute metrics.

## How Many Kilometres Do You Ride Per Day?

Before we recommend a scooter, here's a range reality check:

**If you ride under 30km/day:**
Every major electric scooter qualifies easily. Even a single overnight charge every 3-4 days is sufficient. Focus on comfort, storage, and service access.

**If you ride 30-50km/day:**
You want at least 80km real-world range to have a comfortable buffer. Ola S1 Pro, Ather 450X Gen 3, TVS iQube ST, and Hero Vida V1 Pro all qualify.

**If you ride 50-80km/day:**
This is where you need to think carefully. The Ola S1 Pro (130-150km real world) and Hero Vida V1 Pro (110-130km) are your strongest options. Home charging becomes important to ensure a full battery every morning.

## What Are the Monthly Running Costs of Commuting on an Electric Scooter?

Let's compare electricity costs to petrol costs for a 35km daily commute (about 1,050km/month).

**Petrol scooter (Honda Activa at 50km/litre, petrol at ₹105/litre):**
- Monthly fuel cost: 1,050 ÷ 50 × ₹105 = approximately ₹2,200/month

**Electric scooter (TVS iQube ST at 3.5km/kWh, electricity at ₹8/unit):**
- Monthly electricity cost: 1,050 ÷ 3.5 × ₹8 = approximately ₹2,400/month... 

Wait — that seems close. The key is that electricity tariffs for home charging are typically cheaper (₹4-6/unit residential vs ₹8 commercial). At home rates:
- Monthly electricity cost: approximately ₹1,200-1,500/month
- Savings over petrol: ₹700-1,000/month
- Annual savings: ₹8,400-12,000/year

Plus: Service costs for an electric scooter are significantly lower — no engine oil, no spark plugs, no air filters, fewer moving parts.

Over 5 years, the savings on an EV commuter scooter vs a petrol scooter can reach ₹60,000-₹80,000 or more.
