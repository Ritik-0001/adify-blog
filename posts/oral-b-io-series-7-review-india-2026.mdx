## AI Brushing Recognition — What It Actually Does

The iO Series 7's headline feature is AI brushing recognition — a position detection system that identifies which of the 6 zones of your mouth the brush is currently in (upper left, upper right, upper front, lower left, lower right, lower front).

**How it works:** Accelerometers and orientation sensors in the handle track brush position in real time. The Oral-B app displays a live mouth map — zone lights up green when brushed adequately, stays orange if you're spending too much time there, goes red if missed.

**Clinical relevance:** Most people consistently miss the same zones — typically the inner surfaces of lower front teeth and the back molars. The AI's zone tracking identifies these patterns and provides targeted coaching over time. After 2 weeks, the app generates a "brushing score" showing which zones improved.

**Honest assessment:** The AI brushing is genuinely useful — not marketing. Zone detection accuracy is approximately 85–90% in practice. It sometimes misidentifies inner vs outer surfaces, but the zone-level coaching (spend more time on lower left) is accurate and helps most users improve.

The iO Series 7 adds a colour display to the handle itself — no phone required for basic feedback:

**Value of the display:** For users who brush without their phone nearby (most people), the handle display provides brushing mode confirmation and timer without needing the app. The Series 4 has no display.

## 5 Cleaning Modes

**Daily Clean:** Standard comprehensive cleaning
**Whitening:** Higher frequency oscillation for stain removal
**Sensitive:** Reduced power for sensitive teeth and gums
**Intense:** Maximum power for deep plaque removal
**Gum Care:** Dedicated gum health mode with specific motion pattern

**The Gum Care addition:** This is the iO Series 7's critical mode difference from the Series 4 (which has only 4 modes). For users with gingivitis or gum recession, the Gum Care mode's specific motion — optimised for the gumline rather than tooth surfaces — is clinically more effective than using Sensitive mode as a substitute.
