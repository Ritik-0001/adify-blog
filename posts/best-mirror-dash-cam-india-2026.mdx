## What Is a Mirror Dash Cam?

A mirror dash cam is exactly what it sounds like — a device that combines a rear-view mirror and a dash cam in a single unit. It clips over your existing rear-view mirror (or replaces it entirely) and adds:

1. **A dash cam on the front** — records the road ahead
2. **A wide-angle rear camera** — mounts on your rear windshield and streams a live feed to the mirror display
3. **A built-in display** — shows the rear camera view as a wide-angle live image in the mirror

The key insight is that the built-in display replaces your traditional optical mirror view with a camera-based view — which is actually wider and not blocked by passengers, headrests, or cargo in the back seat.

## Why Mirror Dash Cams Are Popular in India

Mirror dash cams have carved out a solid niche among Indian car buyers for a few specific reasons:

**Blocked rear views are common in India:** Hatchbacks and sedans loaded with luggage, five adults in a Maruti Suzuki, goods partially blocking the rear windshield — traditional optical mirrors are frequently useless. The camera-based mirror shows a wide-angle feed that isn't blocked by anything inside the car.

**SUV rear visibility:** Compact SUVs like the Nexon, Creta, or Brezza have notoriously poor rear visibility due to thick D-pillars and steeply raked rear windows. A mirror dash cam with a rear camera mounted low on the rear windshield gives you a significantly better view.

**Hidden dash cam:** Many Indian buyers prefer that their dash cam is not obviously visible to outsiders — mirror dash cams are inconspicuous because they look like a slightly larger rear-view mirror.

**One unit, two functions:** Instead of buying a separate rear-view mirror gadget and a dash cam, you get both in one clean installation.

## How Is Installation Different for a Mirror Dash Cam?

Mirror dash cam installation has one extra step compared to a standard dash cam — the rear camera cable:

**Step 1: Clip the mirror unit**
The mirror dash cam has rubber clamps that grip your existing rear-view mirror. Some units replace the mirror entirely; most clip over it.

**Step 2: Connect power**
Route the power cable from the mirror unit down the A-pillar to the dashboard, then to the 12V socket. For parking mode, hardwire to the fuse box.

**Step 3: Mount and route the rear camera**
The rear camera is the most important installation step. Mount it centrally at the top of the rear windshield. Route the cable along the headliner (ceiling), down the B-pillar or C-pillar, and forward to the mirror unit.

**Cable length:** Most rear camera cables are 6–8 metres — sufficient for all standard Indian passenger cars. SUVs and MPVs may need to tuck the cable more carefully.

**Professional installation time:** 45–90 minutes. Expected cost at a car accessories shop: ₹500–₹800.
