## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Gum Disease

**Understanding gum disease stages:**
- **Gingivitis:** Reversible gum inflammation — bleeding when brushing, red/swollen gums. Electric toothbrush + correct technique resolves most cases.
- **Periodontitis:** Irreversible bone loss around teeth — requires professional treatment. Electric toothbrush maintains post-treatment health but cannot reverse damage.

**Essential features for gum disease:**
1. **Gum Care or Sensitive mode** — gentler cleaning for inflamed tissue
2. **Pressure sensor** — over-pressing worsens gum recession
3. **2-minute timer** — adequate duration for thorough gum cleaning
4. **Soft brush head** — hard bristles damage already compromised gum tissue

**Indian context for gum disease:** High prevalence of gingivitis in India due to combination of tobacco use, diabetes (one of the world's highest rates), inconsistent professional dental care access, and heavy carbohydrate diet. Electric toothbrushes are particularly impactful in this context.

**Professional treatment is essential:** Electric toothbrushes manage gum health but cannot replace professional scaling, root planing, or surgery for advanced periodontitis. Use your toothbrush between dental appointments, not instead of them.
