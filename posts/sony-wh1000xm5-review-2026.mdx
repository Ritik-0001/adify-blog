The Sony WH-1000XM5 launched in 2022 and still leads nearly every ANC headphone category in 2026. But is the now 4-year-old flagship worth buying at full price when newer alternatives exist? Here's the honest answer.

---

## Overview

The WH-1000XM5 is Sony's flagship noise-cancelling headphone — completely redesigned from the XM4 with a new earcup shape, 8-microphone array, new V1 ANC processor, and a refined driver for better sound across the frequency range. In 2026, it remains the gold standard for consumer ANC headphones.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Driver:** 30mm HD driver unit
- **Frequency Response:** 4 Hz – 40,000 Hz (Hi-Res Audio)
- **ANC:** 8 microphones, dual ANC processors (V1 + Integrated processor)
- **Codecs:** SBC, AAC, LDAC (Hi-Res Wireless)
- **Multipoint:** 2 devices simultaneously
- **Battery:** 30 hours (ANC on), 40 hours (ANC off)
- **Charging:** 3.5 hrs full; 3 hrs playback from 3-min charge
- **Weight:** 250g

---

## Noise Cancellation

Still the best. The XM5's ANC remains the reference against which other headphones are measured in 2026. In airplane cabins, it reduces engine noise to a whisper. In busy offices, it creates near-silence at your desk. The improvement over the XM4 is particularly noticeable at high frequencies (keyboards, voices).

The "Adaptive Sound Control" feature adjusts ANC based on detected activity — walking, sitting, commuting — automatically without manual switching.

---

## Sound Quality

The 30mm HD driver delivers a warm, engaging V-shaped sound signature — prominent bass, slightly elevated highs, moderately recessed mids. For music, podcasts, and general listening, it's excellent.

With LDAC codec on Android: the jump to Hi-Res audio detail is real. You hear spatial detail in well-mastered albums that budget headphones don't resolve. For Apple users, AAC is the ceiling — still very good but LDAC advantage is lost.

---

## Call Quality

The 4 beamforming mics dedicated to voice pickup are Sony's biggest XM5 improvement. On Zoom and Teams calls, your voice sounds clean, natural, and close even in rooms with background noise. The AI noise reduction filters out keyboard typing, AC hum, and street noise effectively.

In blind tests with colleagues, the XM5 is consistently rated "sounds like they're in a quiet room" even when you're not.

---

## Comfort

The redesigned earcups are rounder and softer than the XM4. For sessions up to 3–4 hours, comfort is excellent. For 8+ hour WFH sessions, some users report mild ear warmth from the synthetic leather cushions. The 250g weight is among the lightest in this class.

Not foldable — the XM5 doesn't fold flat for travel. A small but real inconvenience.

---

## Pros

- Best-in-class ANC — still unmatched in 2026
- LDAC Hi-Res Wireless on Android
- Excellent call quality (4 beamforming mics)
