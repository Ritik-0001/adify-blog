## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Bruxism

**The bruxism-brushing connection:** Bruxism creates a compounding dental damage cycle — grinding wears enamel, which increases sensitivity, which causes some patients to brush harder thinking they can brush away sensitivity, which causes further gum recession. An electric toothbrush with a pressure sensor breaks this cycle by enforcing correct force regardless of perceived sensitivity.

**Morning brushing with worn enamel — the crucial 30-minute rule:** After night grinding, salivary pH drops (mouth becomes more acidic from bacteria). The enamel surface is microscopically softened immediately upon waking. Wait at least 30 minutes before brushing — the same rule as after acidic food or drinks. Brushing on softened enamel, even with the softest electric brush, causes measurable enamel loss. Use water to rinse first, then brush after breakfast or 30 minutes after waking.

**Night guard is non-negotiable:** No electric toothbrush prevents bruxism damage — only a custom night guard from your dentist does. Cost in India: ₹2,000–₹8,000 for a custom-fitted occlusal splint. Over-the-counter boil-and-bite guards (₹300–₹800) provide partial protection. The electric toothbrush maintains oral hygiene; the night guard prevents the grinding damage. Both are required.

**Toothpaste for bruxism patients:**
- Use low-abrasivity fluoride toothpaste (RDA below 70) — many Indian whitening toothpastes are highly abrasive
- Sensodyne Repair & Protect is specifically formulated for worn enamel (contains hydroxyapatite/NovaMin)
- Avoid charcoal toothpastes — extremely abrasive, catastrophic for thin enamel

**Key features for bruxism:**
1. **Pressure sensor** — automatic protection against compounding abrasive damage
2. **Sensitive or Low mode** — for morning sessions when enamel is most vulnerable
3. **Soft bristles** — medium or hard bristles accelerate wear on compromised enamel
4. **Low vibration transmission** — especially if TMJ symptoms coexist

**Bruxism prevalence in India:** Stress-related bruxism is rising — epidemiological studies show increasing rates post-pandemic in Indian urban populations. Many patients are unaware they grind: common indicators are waking with a clenched jaw, morning headaches, flattened or chipped teeth, and hypersensitive teeth without obvious cavities.
