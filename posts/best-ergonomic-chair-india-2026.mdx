India's WFH revolution has created a back pain epidemic — Indian workers sitting on dining chairs and plastic stools for 8–10 hours daily. An ergonomic chair is one of the highest-return WFH health investments, with measurable back pain reduction within weeks of adoption.

---

## Why Ergonomic Chairs Matter Particularly in India

**Sedentary WFH culture:** India's IT and services sector employs hundreds of millions in desk-based work. Post-2020 WFH adoption means most Indian knowledge workers spend 8–10 hours daily seated — the highest occupational sitting duration globally.

**Back pain prevalence:** India has one of the world's highest rates of lower back pain — both from work posture and from previous habits of sitting cross-legged (on floor or chair) that creates posterior pelvic tilt. Ergonomic chairs address the lumbar region directly.

**Indian body dimensions:** The average Indian male is 5'6" and female 5'2" — shorter than the Western averages chairs are designed for. Seat depth adjustment and armrest height range matter — chairs designed for 5'10"+ users may not fit Indian body proportions correctly.
