## Design — Apple's Biggest iPad Visual Change

The 10th gen iPad redesigned from the classic rounded rectangle (iPad look since 2010) to a squared design matching the iPad Air and iPad Pro:

**Flat edges:** More ergonomic to hold, easier to grip on a desk, and provides the premium "iPad Air-like" look that many buyers prefer.
**Landscape front camera:** The 12MP front camera is on the long edge — aligned for landscape video calls on a stand (vs the 9th gen's portrait-edge camera). This makes the 10th gen genuinely better for FaceTime, Zoom, and online meetings, where users hold tablets in landscape orientation.
**Colour options:** Blue, Pink, Yellow, Silver — colourful options vs the 9th gen's Space Grey and Silver. A style consideration that matters to many buyers.

---

## Performance — A14 Bionic Is Still Fast

The A14 Bionic (2020 chip) remains capable in 2026:
- Handles all daily tasks without slowdown
- Runs all iPad apps smoothly — GoodNotes, Microsoft 365, Procreate, iMovie
- Gaming: Apple Arcade titles and most games at high settings
- Not as fast as M-series chips (iPad Air, iPad Pro) — perceptible in video export and complex Procreate files

**Comparison:** A14 Bionic vs Samsung Tab S9 FE's Exynos 1380 — A14 is significantly faster in single-core tasks (30–40%) and comparable in multi-core. For app performance, the iPad 10th gen feels faster than the Tab S9 FE.

---

## USB-C — The Most Important Change from 9th Gen

The 9th gen iPad uses Lightning (Apple's proprietary connector). The 10th gen switches to USB-C:
- **Same cable as MacBook, iPad Pro, iPhone 15:** One cable type for all Apple devices (and Android)
- **Faster data transfer:** USB 2.0 speeds (480 Mbps) — same as 9th gen but with wider accessory compatibility
- **Compatible with USB-C hubs:** Connect HDMI, USB-A, and SD card readers directly
- **Magic Keyboard Folio compatible**

**Why this matters in India:** USB-C is the universal standard. Students and professionals who carry multiple devices can consolidate to one cable type — a practical simplification.

---

## Apple Pencil Compatibility — A Compromise

The 10th gen iPad is compatible with the Apple Pencil 1st generation (₹7,900) — but requires an adapter (included) for charging, because the 10th gen has no Lightning port.

**The frustration:** Apple Pencil 1st gen charges via Lightning. The 10th gen has USB-C. The adapter solution is inelegant — a small dongle that connects Pencil to USB-C for charging. It works but feels like a design compromise.

**Alternative:** Buy the Apple Pencil USB-C (₹8,500) — charges directly via the iPad's USB-C port. A cleaner solution if you're buying a Pencil alongside the 10th gen.

## 10th Gen vs iPad Air — Is the Air Worth the Premium?

The iPad Air (₹59,900) over the iPad 10th gen (₹34,900) — ₹25,000 more — adds:
- M1 or M2 chip (significantly faster for professional apps)
- 90Hz ProMotion display (10th gen is 60Hz)
- Apple Pencil 2nd gen support (magnetic charging, double-tap)
- 5G option

**Is it worth ₹25,000 more?** For professional use (video editing, Procreate, music production) — yes. For students and everyday use — no. The 10th gen handles all student and productivity tasks adequately.
