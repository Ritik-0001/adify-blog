## OLED Display — The Main Reason to Buy

The Tab P11 Pro Gen 2's 11.2-inch AMOLED 120Hz display is its defining differentiator — no other tablet under ₹35,000 has an OLED at this size and refresh rate.

**OLED advantages:**
- **True blacks:** Each pixel turns off completely — blacks are genuinely black, not grey-black like IPS LCD
- **Contrast ratio:** 1,000,000:1 vs ~1,200:1 on IPS LCD — night-mode reading and dark-themed apps look dramatically better
- **Colour vibrancy:** DCI-P3 100% coverage — photos, videos, and creative work display with vivid, accurate colours
- **120Hz:** Smooth scrolling identical to premium Samsung and OnePlus tablets

**OLED for video:** Netflix, Prime Video, and Disney+ Hotstar content looks genuinely cinematic on OLED — the HDR10+ certification means properly mastered content (which most streaming content is) displays with the full range of highlights and shadows.

**OLED for night reading:** Many Indian users read in low light (bedroom, post-dinner). OLED at minimum brightness in dark mode produces near-zero glow — the most eye-comfortable reading experience of any tablet type.

## ZUI Interface — Lenovo's Tablet OS

Lenovo's ZUI (Zen UI variant) is less feature-rich than Samsung One UI or MIUI for Pad. It's clean and minimal, but lacks:
- A desktop-like mode (no DeX equivalent)
- Advanced floating window multitasking
- Ecosystem integration features

For pure media consumption, ZUI's simplicity is fine. For productivity, Samsung One UI offers more powerful multitasking tools.
