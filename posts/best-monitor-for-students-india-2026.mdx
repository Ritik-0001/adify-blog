## Buying Guide

### HDMI Connection: What Students Need to Know

Most Indian student laptops — whether budget Windows laptops or mid-range models — include an HDMI port. Connecting to an external monitor requires a standard HDMI cable (male to male), which typically costs ₹300–₹600 on Amazon India. This is not usually included with the monitor and needs to be purchased separately.

If your laptop only has a USB-C port (common on newer thin-and-light laptops and MacBooks), you will need a USB-C to HDMI adapter. These are widely available on Amazon India for ₹500–₹1,500. Confirm whether your USB-C port supports DisplayPort Alternate Mode before purchasing — most modern USB-C ports do, but older ones may not.

### Eye Care for Extended Study Sessions

Long study sessions — particularly those that extend into the night — put significant strain on eyes. Two features specifically help:

**Flicker-free backlights:** Standard monitors use PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) dimming that flickers rapidly at lower brightness settings. Flicker-free monitors use DC dimming, eliminating this invisible flicker that contributes to eye fatigue. Look for monitors with TÜV Rheinland flicker-free certification.

**Blue light filters:** Screens emit blue light that suppresses melatonin production, making it harder to sleep after evening study sessions. Most monitors now include a blue light filter mode that shifts the colour temperature warmer, reducing blue light emission. This is especially relevant before JEE, NEET, or semester exams when late-night studying is unavoidable.

### Laptop Extender Setups for Indian Student Desks

The most common and practical student setup in India: laptop placed to one side, external monitor as the primary display in the center. This gives you two screens — the laptop's built-in display and the external monitor — without any additional hardware beyond an HDMI cable.

Configure this in Windows under Display Settings (right-click desktop → Display Settings → Extend Displays) or on macOS under System Preferences → Displays. The laptop screen can hold a second window — notes, browser, or messaging apps — while the main monitor holds the lecture or document you are actively studying.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need an HDMI cable separately for these monitors?**
Yes. Monitor boxes typically include a power cable and sometimes a VGA cable, but rarely an HDMI cable. Purchase a standard HDMI cable on Amazon India for ₹300–₹500. A 1.5m cable is usually enough for most desk setups.

**Q: Which is better for studying — 22" or 24"?**
24" is the better choice if your desk space permits. The additional screen area makes a meaningful difference for keeping two windows open side by side — for example, a lecture video and your notes. 22" is adequate for single-window use on a very tight desk or budget.

**Q: Are these monitors good for occasional gaming too?**
The LG 24MK430H (75Hz, FreeSync) and ASUS VA24EHE (75Hz) are capable of casual gaming at 75Hz. For serious gaming, you would need a 144Hz monitor, but for occasional use with popular titles like Valorant or FIFA, these will work fine.

**Q: Does a bigger monitor hurt eye health for students?**
No — in fact, a larger monitor at a comfortable viewing distance (50–70cm) is less straining than squinting at a small laptop display. The key is maintaining proper viewing distance and using eye care modes during long sessions.
