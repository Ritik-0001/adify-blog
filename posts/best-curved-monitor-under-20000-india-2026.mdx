## Buying Guide

### 1800R vs 1500R Curvature

The curvature R-number is the radius of the curve in millimetres — a smaller number means a tighter curve. A 1500R monitor feels more dramatically immersive than 1800R. For single-monitor gaming setups where you sit within 50–80cm, 1500R delivers a noticeably wraparound feel. For general use, productivity work, or viewing from 80cm or more, 1800R is more comfortable and causes less visual distortion at the screen edges.

For Indian apartments with typical city desk depths of 55–70cm, 1500R actually works very well for gaming.

### VA vs IPS for Curved Monitors

The vast majority of budget curved monitors use VA panels — and for good reason. VA delivers contrast ratios of 3000:1 or higher, making blacks genuinely dark. This is particularly valuable in Indian homes where rooms are often dim in the evenings, and the contrast makes streaming content look dramatic and cinematic.

IPS panels in curved monitors offer better colour accuracy and wider viewing angles but sacrifice that deep black performance. For gaming and movies, VA wins. For colour-critical design or photo editing, IPS is the better choice.

### Is 1080p Enough on a 27" Curved?

At 27", 1080p delivers around 81 PPI — noticeably soft if you sit within 60cm. For video watching from 70–80cm it is acceptable, but for text-heavy work like coding or document editing the softness can become tiring over time. A 1440p curved monitor is a better long-term investment if you read and write for long hours.

## FAQ

**Q: Are curved monitors good for coding and office work?**
They are comfortable for wide spreadsheets and code editors, especially with the 1800R gentle curve. However, the slight colour shift at the edges on VA panels can be distracting for design work. For purely text-based productivity, the curve is neutral to beneficial.

**Q: Do curved monitors cause eye strain?**
Properly sized curved monitors at the right distance can actually reduce eye strain because your eyes do not need to refocus between the centre and edges. A 27" curved at 60–70cm is the sweet spot. Most modern curved monitors also include flicker-free backlights, which further reduces fatigue.

**Q: Can I use a curved monitor for PS5 or Xbox gaming?**
Yes — if the monitor has HDMI 2.0 and supports the resolution your console outputs. Most 27" curved monitors in this segment support 1080p 120Hz via HDMI, which is well within PS5 capabilities for supported titles.

**Q: Will budget curved monitors have good after-sales support in India?**
Brands like Samsung, LG, Acer, and MSI have established service centres across Indian cities. Dell has the strongest warranty support with next business day service. Avoid lesser-known brands without clear India warranty terms.
