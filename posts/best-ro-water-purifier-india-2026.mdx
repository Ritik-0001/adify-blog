## How Does RO Purification Work?

Reverse Osmosis works by forcing water through a semi-permeable membrane under pressure. The membrane has pores small enough to block dissolved salts, heavy metals, pesticides, fluoride, arsenic, and biological contaminants while allowing water molecules to pass through.

The process is highly effective — a good RO membrane reduces TDS by 90–95%. This means if your input water has 800 ppm TDS, your purified output could be as low as 40–80 ppm.

The limitation is water wastage. Traditional RO systems reject roughly 60–70% of input water as "reject water." Modern systems from AO Smith and HUL Pureit have significantly improved this ratio.

## What TDS Level Should Purified Water Have?

After RO purification, the ideal TDS range for drinking water is:
- 50–150 ppm: Ideal — clean, slightly mineral taste
- 150–300 ppm: Acceptable — some mineral content retained
- Below 50 ppm: Too pure — may taste flat, lacks minerals

TDS controllers in models like Kent Grand Plus let you adjust the output TDS to your preferred level. If your purified water tastes flat, the TDS controller can be adjusted to retain more minerals.
