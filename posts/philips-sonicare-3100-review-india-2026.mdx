## What the 3100 Gets Right

### Genuine Sonicare Sonic Technology
The 3100 runs at 31,000 brush strokes per minute — the same sonic frequency as the 5100 and 9000. The fundamental fluid dynamics cleaning effect (where sonic vibration disrupts plaque beyond direct bristle contact) is fully present at this entry price point.

**What this means:** You are getting real Philips sonic cleaning, not a cheaper imitation. Philips manufactures the 3100 to the same quality standards as higher models — the cleaning mechanism is the same.

### 3 Intensity Settings
Despite being the entry model, the 3100 includes low, medium, and high intensity settings — letting sensitive gum users run at lower intensity without buying a higher model.

### 2-Minute Timer with 30-Second Quadrant Alerts
The full Sonicare QuadPacer system is present — pauses briefly every 30 seconds to prompt movement to the next mouth quadrant. Missing in many budget competitors.

---

## What the 3100 Lacks vs 4100 and 5100

**No pressure sensor:** The 3100 has no reliable pressure sensor — neither physical pause (5100) nor LED warning (4100). For users who tend to over-press, this is a meaningful gap.

**Single mode:** Only Clean mode — no Gum Care, Sensitive, or Whitening. For most users, Clean mode is sufficient. But users with specific needs (pregnancy gingivitis, gum recession, whitening priority) benefit from additional modes.

**No BrushSync:** The brush head replacement reminder (based on actual bristle wear, not just time) is absent — you manually track 3-month replacement.
