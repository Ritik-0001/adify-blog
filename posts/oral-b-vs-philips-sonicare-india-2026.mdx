## The Core Technology Difference — Oscillating vs Sonic

**Oral-B oscillating-rotating-pulsating (ORP):**
The round head physically scrubs tooth surfaces by spinning clockwise, counter-clockwise, and pulsating in and out. This mechanical action removes plaque that has adhered to tooth surfaces — aggressive, direct, and highly effective at surface cleaning.

**Philips Sonicare sonic vibration:**
The brush head vibrates at 31,000–62,000 strokes per minute. This frequency creates fluid dynamics in the liquid layer between teeth — toothpaste and saliva are driven into interdental spaces and subgingival pockets that bristles never reach. The cleaning action is gentler on enamel and gum tissue.

**Clinical verdict:** Both outperform manual toothbrushes significantly. The Cochrane Review (2014, 56 studies, 5,068 participants) found ORP brushes reduce plaque by 11% more and gingivitis by 6% more than side-to-side sonic brushes after 3 months. However, Philips's more recent Sonicare trials show specific gum health improvements that the Cochrane comparison does not cover.

## Cleaning Effectiveness — What Indian Dentists Say

Indian dental professionals surveyed by dental associations consistently recommend electric toothbrushes without strong brand preference — both Oral-B and Sonicare are endorsed equally. The nuance:

**Oral-B recommended more often for:**
- Surface plaque and calculus removal (especially patients who haven't seen a dentist in 12+ months)
- Patients who brush irregularly (ORP mechanical action is more forgiving of poor technique)
- Children transitioning from manual brushing

**Sonicare recommended more often for:**
- Patients with gingivitis or early periodontitis (gum disease)
- Orthodontic patients (braces)
- Patients with crowns, veneers, or implants (sonic is gentler on restoration margins)
- Elderly patients with sensitive gums or exposed root surfaces

## Pressure Sensors — How Both Brands Handle Overbrushing

Excessive brushing pressure is one of the most common causes of gum recession and enamel erosion — and India has a particularly high rate of aggressive brushing habits, a legacy of manual toothbrush cultural norms.

**Oral-B Pro 3 3000:** Red LED pressure light — simple, visible, always on when brushing too hard
**Oral-B iO Series:** Three-color LED ring (green/white/red) — more nuanced, shows correct pressure positively
**Philips Sonicare 5100:** Pressure-pause — motor temporarily reduces power when excess pressure detected
**Philips DiamondClean 9300:** Pressure-pause + LED indicator — combination approach

**Which is more effective at changing behavior:** Oral-B's iO Series three-color ring is best for positive reinforcement. Philips's motor-pause creates a stronger immediate intervention. For habit formation, Oral-B's continuous visual feedback builds better long-term habits; Sonicare's motor-pause creates faster immediate correction.
