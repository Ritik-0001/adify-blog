Oral-B and Philips Sonicare are the two dominant electric toothbrush brands globally — and both are widely available in India. They use completely different technology, which is why the debate persists. Here is the definitive comparison for Indian buyers in 2026.

---

## The Core Technology Difference

**Oral-B:** Oscillating-rotating-pulsating (ORP) technology. The round brush head spins clockwise, counter-clockwise, and pulses in and out — physically scrubbing plaque off tooth surfaces. Up to 8,800 pulsations + 40,000 oscillations per minute.

**Philips Sonicare:** Sonic vibration technology. The brush head vibrates at 31,000–62,000 strokes per minute, creating fluid dynamics that drive toothpaste and saliva into spaces between teeth — cleaning beyond where the bristles physically touch.

**Clinical reality:** Both outperform manual brushing by a wide margin. Multiple independent studies show both reduce plaque and gingivitis significantly. The difference is in *how* they clean.

---

## Oral-B Strengths

**Better at surface plaque removal:** The physical oscillating action scrubs tooth surfaces more aggressively. If your main concern is visible plaque buildup on tooth surfaces, Oral-B performs slightly better.

**Pressure sensor reliability:** Oral-B's pressure sensors are the most trusted in the industry — the light turns red instantly when you press too hard, consistently preventing gum recession.

**AI brushing coverage (iO Series):** The iO Series app maps your entire mouth and shows which zones you've missed — the most advanced brushing guidance available.

**Round brush heads:** Designed to clean one tooth at a time, similar to professional dental cleaning tools.

**India availability:** Oral-B Pro 3, Pro 2, and iO Series are widely available on Amazon India at ₹2,999–₹8,999.

## Philips Sonicare Strengths

**Better interdental cleaning:** Sonic fluid dynamics reach between teeth and along the gumline without bristle contact — particularly effective for people prone to interdental cavities.

**Gentler on teeth and gums:** High-frequency vibration is less abrasive than oscillating action — preferred by dentists for patients with enamel sensitivity or existing gum recession.

**Better for gum health improvement:** Philips' own clinical studies show measurable gum improvement in 2 weeks — faster than most Oral-B models for gingivitis reduction.

**Longer battery life:** Most Sonicare models offer 14-day battery vs Oral-B's 12-day.

**Premium models (DiamondClean):** Philips' top tier (₹14,999–₹24,999) is superior to comparable Oral-B iO pricing for audio quality and travel case design.

## Which Should You Buy?

**Choose Oral-B if:**
- Surface plaque buildup is your main concern
- You want AI brushing zone coverage (iO Series)
- Budget is under ₹3,000 and you want the best value
- You grind your teeth (oscillating action is more thorough on heavy calculus)

**Choose Philips Sonicare if:**
- You have sensitive gums or existing gum recession
- Interdental cavities are a recurring problem
- You prefer a gentler cleaning sensation
- You have braces or dental work where aggressive scrubbing is not advisable

**Dentist consensus in India:** Both brands are recommended equally. Oral-B is slightly more common in dental office recommendations because of stronger clinical study marketing. Sonicare is preferred by periodontists (gum specialists) for patients with gum disease.
