## Deep Dive: The Color Handle Display

The iO Series 6's handle display is the feature that separates it from every other sub-flagship Oral-B brush. Understanding what it shows — and what it doesn't — helps set realistic expectations:

**What the display shows:**
- **Current cleaning mode** — mode name in the active LED color (e.g., "Daily Clean" in white, "Whitening" in yellow)
- **Pressure smiley face** — a real-time animated face: green smile for correct pressure, red grimace for too-hard brushing
- **2-minute countdown timer** — ticks down from 2:00, with a 30-second pause and pulse every 30 seconds for quadrant changes
- **Battery level indicator** — 3-level bar showing charge remaining

**What it doesn't show:**
- Zone-by-zone coverage mapping (still requires the app)
- Session history or trend data (app only)
- Specific pressure measurement (just correct vs. too hard)

For users who mainly want to know "am I brushing correctly right now?" — the handle display answers this completely without needing the app. For users who want detailed historical data and zone coverage — the app is still required.

## Upgraded Motor vs Series 4

Oral-B describes the iO Series 6's motor as "upgraded" from the Series 4 without publishing specific technical differences. In independent user testing and comparative reviews:

- **Pressure response:** The Series 6 transitions between pressure states faster — the display and LED ring react more immediately when pressure changes
- **Noise:** Marginally quieter than Series 4 (both quieter than all Pro-series models)
- **Vibration feel:** Slightly smoother against teeth and gums

The cleaning output difference is not clinically meaningful — plaque removal data between Series 4 and 6 is equivalent. The motor upgrade is a refinement, not a fundamental change.

## 5 Cleaning Modes — Full Breakdown

**Daily Clean:** 2-minute brushing with quadrant timer. The go-to everyday mode for 90% of brushing sessions.

**Sensitive:** Reduced intensity for post-dental sensitivity, orthodontic hardware, or implant margins. Start here if transitioning from a manual brush.

**Gum Care:** Extended 2-minute session with focused gumline strokes. Relevant for the majority of Indian adults with early-stage gum disease — ICMR surveys indicate 67–80% prevalence of some form of periodontal inflammation.

**Whitening:** High-frequency polishing for surface stain removal. Indian dietary habits (chai, coffee, turmeric, pan masala in some demographics) make regular whitening mode use particularly relevant.

**Intense:** Maximum iO motor output for heavy plaque. Use 1–2 times per week rather than daily.
