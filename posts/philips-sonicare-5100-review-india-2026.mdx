## Deep Dive: Sonic Fluid Dynamics — What It Actually Does

The Sonicare 5100's core technology is worth understanding because it's fundamentally different from how Oral-B works:

**Oral-B (oscillating):** Bristles physically scrub the tooth surface by rotating and pulsating. Plaque is mechanically dislodged by bristle contact.

**Sonicare (sonic):** The brush head vibrates at 31,000 strokes per minute — this creates microscopic fluid motion in the liquid layer (toothpaste + saliva) on the tooth surface. This fluid is driven into interdental spaces and subgingival areas (below the gumline) where bristles cannot physically reach.

**What this means for Indian buyers:**
- If you have interdental cavities (cavities between teeth) — Sonicare's fluid dynamics may reach those spaces more effectively
- If you have early gum disease (gum pockets) — the fluid action can reach deeper below the gumline
- If you have dental implants, crowns, or veneers — sonic vibration is gentler than oscillating on restoration margins
- If you wear braces — sonic fluid dynamics clean around brackets without the mechanical stress of oscillating heads

## 3 Modes × 3 Intensities — 9 Combinations

**Clean mode:** Standard everyday brushing — the recommended default for most users
**White mode:** Adds polishing action for surface stain removal — chai and coffee staining
**Gum Care mode:** Extended gentle strokes focused on the gumline — 3 minutes vs 2 minutes, with a dedicated gumline massage phase

**Intensity settings:** Each mode runs at Low, Medium, or High intensity. New Sonicare users should start at Low intensity — the sonic vibration sensation is unfamiliar and can feel intense at first. Most users settle on Medium after 2 weeks.

**BrushSync mode memory:** The 5100 remembers your last used mode and intensity — starts where you left off each session.

## BrushSync — Head Recognition and Replacement Reminder

The BrushSync chip embedded in compatible Sonicare brush heads communicates with the handle:

1. **Head recognition:** The brush identifies whether a Premium All-in-One, Sensitive, or Gum Care head is attached and adjusts recommended intensity
2. **Replacement reminder:** Tracks total brush head usage and activates a blue replacement indicator when the head reaches the end of its 3-month life — even if bristles look okay externally
3. **Intensity auto-adjust:** Gum Care heads automatically shift to a gentler intensity for gumline brushing

**India availability of BrushSync heads:** Compatible heads are available on Amazon India — Premium All-in-One (₹799–₹999 for 2-pack), Sensitive (₹799 for 2-pack), and Gum Care heads. Less widely available in offline stores than Oral-B CrossAction heads.

## Pressure Sensor — Protecting Gums

The ProtectiveClean 5100 includes a pressure sensor that pauses the brush motor when you press too hard. Unlike Oral-B's continuous LED approach, Sonicare pauses the motor and pulses a light — a more forceful intervention that users tend to notice more immediately.

Indian periodontists (gum specialists) often prefer Sonicare's pressure sensor response for patients with gum recession — the motor pause creates a more instinctive correction than a light that can be ignored.
