## Buying Guide

### Which Upgrade Path Is Right for You?

**Choose bigger (27" 1080p)** if: You multitask heavily, work with multiple windows, or want more screen real estate for everyday productivity. The LG 27MP400-B or Dell P2722H are the picks here.

**Choose sharper (1440p)** if: You sit close to your monitor, work with text or design, or plan to own this monitor for 4+ years. The Gigabyte G27Q at 1440p is future-proof.

**Choose faster (144Hz)** if: Gaming is a significant part of your daily use. The Acer XB253Q's 144Hz will transform competitive gaming. Pair it with a mid-range GPU for best results.

### Panel Types Explained

All five monitors in this list use **IPS panels** — the right choice for this price range. IPS delivers accurate colours, 178° viewing angles, and consistent brightness. VA panels with higher contrast appear at the ₹18,000+ mark but IPS remains the better all-rounder.

### Resolution Guide

**1080p at 24":** Still sharp (92 PPI). Great for gaming and general use.
**1080p at 27":** Acceptable but visibly softer (82 PPI). Fine for most tasks at normal distances.
**1440p at 27":** Noticeably sharper (108 PPI). Recommended if you sit 50–60cm from your monitor.

### Other Key Features

**HDMI versions:** HDMI 1.4 supports 1080p and 1440p at 60Hz. For 1440p at 144Hz, you need HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort 1.2. The Gigabyte G27Q correctly includes HDMI 2.0.

**FreeSync/G-Sync:** Synchronises your GPU's frame rate with your monitor's refresh rate, eliminating screen tearing. Increasingly important in the gaming monitors in this segment.

**Power Supply:** All monitors listed are compatible with India's 230V AC supply.

## FAQ

**Q: Which monitor brand has the best after-sales service in India?**
A: Dell leads at 150+ service centres with next-business-day on-site support. LG has the widest reach including Tier-2 and Tier-3 towns. Acer and BenQ have good metro coverage. Gigabyte's India service is expanding but still primarily metropolitan.

**Q: Is 1440p worth the upgrade over 1080p?**
A: At 27 inches, yes — noticeably. At 24 inches, the difference is there but less dramatic. If your budget allows and you have a capable GPU (or you're doing productivity work rather than gaming), 1440p at 27" is the better long-term buy.

**Q: Do I need a new GPU for a 144Hz monitor?**
A: For competitive gaming at 144Hz in titles like Valorant or CS2, a mid-range GPU (GTX 1650 or better) can achieve 144+ FPS at 1080p. For 1440p at 144Hz in demanding games, you need something like a GTX 1660 Ti or RX 5600 XT minimum. For productivity, any modern system is fine.

**Q: Is a 27" monitor too big for a typical Indian home desk?**
A: A standard Indian study or office desk (90–120cm wide) can comfortably accommodate a 27" monitor. The ideal viewing distance for 27" is 70–80cm. Measure your desk depth to ensure you're not too close.
