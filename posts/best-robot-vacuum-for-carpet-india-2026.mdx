## Overview

Carpet is less common in Indian homes than in Western ones, but it is far from absent. Bedrooms often have soft rugs or low-pile carpets. Living rooms use large area dhurries or woven rugs. Prayer rooms are almost universally carpeted. And children's rooms increasingly have foam play mats or plush rugs that need frequent cleaning.

The challenge for robot vacuums in India is not purely carpet — it is the hybrid floor scenario. A single flat will have marble or vitrified tile in the main areas and rugs or carpets in bedrooms. A robot vacuum that handles only one surface type is useless here.

What you actually need is a robot that detects carpet automatically, boosts its suction to pull dust and debris out of fibres, and lifts its mop pad clear so it doesn't drag a wet cloth across your bedroom rug. That last point is critical and often overlooked when buying.

## Buying Guide

**Carpet Boost Suction:** Low-pile bedroom rugs and dhurries need at least 3,000 Pa on boost. Thicker rugs and carpets benefit from 5,000–6,000 Pa. Check the manufacturer's boost specification, not just the maximum suction claim.

**Mop Auto-Lift:** If your home has both tile and rugs, this is non-negotiable. Without auto-lift, the mop pad will drag moisture onto carpet. Models with auto-lift: Roborock S8, Dreame L10s Ultra, Ecovacs T20 Omni.

**Brush Type:** Rubber rollers wrap around carpet fibres without tangling. Bristle brushes clean carpet aggressively but tangle on longer fibres and long hair. For Indian homes with medium-length rug fibres, rubber rollers are safer and easier to maintain.

**Carpet Detection Reliability:** LiDAR-based robots detect carpet using floor sensor data and bump response. Camera-based robots use visual detection. LiDAR models from Roborock and Dreame are consistently reliable in detecting Indian dhurries and low-pile carpets.

**Prayer Room and Kids Room Rugs:** These are often the dirtiest rugs in an Indian home. Program scheduled extra-pass cleaning for these rooms using room segmentation in the app.

## FAQ

**Q: Will a robot vacuum damage my Indian dhurrie or cotton rug?**  
A: No — robot vacuums are gentle on flat-weave dhurries and cotton rugs. Avoid using a robot with bristle brushes on handmade or antique rugs with loose threads. Rubber roller models are the safe choice.

**Q: My home has marble floors and one carpeted bedroom. Which robot works best?**  
A: The Roborock S8 (₹34,999) is the ideal pick — it mops marble effectively and auto-lifts the mop when it enters the carpeted bedroom, then boosts suction. One robot handles both floors seamlessly.

**Q: Do robot vacuums get stuck on rug edges?**  
A: Thick or heavily fringed rugs can stop a robot. Keep rug edges flat and trim any fringe. Most modern LiDAR robots navigate rug borders smoothly when the edge is under 2cm high.

**Q: Can I schedule different cleaning modes for different rooms?**  
A: Yes. Roborock, Dreame, and Ecovacs apps support per-room suction and mop settings. You can set maximum suction for the carpeted bedroom and standard suction for tile areas in a single scheduled run.
