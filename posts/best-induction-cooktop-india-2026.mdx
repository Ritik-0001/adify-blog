## Induction Cooktop for Indian Cooking — Key Considerations

**Wattage and heat range:** Indian cooking requires high BTU for tarka (tempering) and rapid boiling — minimum 1,600W, ideally 2,000W. Lower wattage struggles with dals that need sustained boiling.

**Indian cooking presets:** Look for dedicated presets for milk (boiling without overflow), chapati/dosa (dry high heat), and frying (high stable temperature). Generic presets (soup, stew) don't match Indian cooking patterns.

**Induction-compatible cookware:** Only flat-bottomed ferromagnetic cookware (stainless steel, cast iron) works with induction. Indian brass, copper, and aluminium vessels do not work — check before buying.

**Touch vs knob controls:** Touch controls are easier to clean (important for Indian cooking where splatter is common). Knob controls are more intuitive for precise heat adjustment.
