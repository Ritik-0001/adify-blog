## What the Vitality 100 Gets Right

**2-minute timer with quadrant alerts:** The most impactful single feature — a beep every 30 seconds prompts you to move to the next quadrant. Most Indians brush for under 60 seconds. Getting to 2 minutes consistently is the single biggest improvement most people can make. The Vitality 100 delivers this at ₹799.

**Oscillating-rotating cleaning:** 2D oscillating-rotating action removes 80% more plaque than manual brushing (Oral-B data). Even without the pulsating action of the Pro 3's 3D cleaning, the Vitality's mechanical action is significantly more effective than manual brushing.

**CrossAction head:** The included CrossAction head uses angled bristles to surround each tooth — more complete coverage than a standard flat manual brush head.

**Universal head compatibility:** All standard Oral-B brush heads (CrossAction, Sensitive, 3D White, Gum Care, Ortho) fit the Vitality 100 handle — the same heads used on premium Oral-B models costing ₹5,000+. Head variety is not a limitation.

---

## What the Vitality 100 Misses

**No pressure sensor:** The most important missing feature. Over-brushing (pressing too hard) causes gum recession and enamel erosion. The Vitality 100 provides no feedback when you're pressing too hard — entirely relying on your own judgement. For the ₹1,200 difference, the Oral-B Pro 2 adds a pressure sensor.

**1 cleaning mode only:** Daily Clean is the only mode. No Sensitive mode for inflamed gums, no Whitening mode for stain removal. For users who need mode variety, the Pro 3 at ₹2,999 is the step up.

**20-minute battery life:** The NiMH battery provides approximately 20 minutes of brushing per charge (equivalent to 10 days of 2x daily brushing). Battery must be kept on the charging cradle between uses — leaving it uncharged for extended periods degrades NiMH battery life. Unlike Li-ion batteries in premium models, NiMH requires regular top-up charging.
