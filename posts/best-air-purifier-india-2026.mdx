## What to Look For in an Air Purifier for India

**True HEPA H13:** Captures 99.97% of particles at 0.3 microns — the standard for PM2.5, which is the primary health hazard in Indian air. H13 is the minimum for serious air quality improvement.

**CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate):** Measures how quickly the purifier cleans a given volume of air. For Indian rooms — typical bedroom is 120–180 sq ft, living room 300–500 sq ft. Match CADR to room size: a purifier rated for 400 sq ft in a 600 sq ft room runs continuously without achieving clean air.

**Activated carbon filter:** Essential in India for cooking fumes, traffic pollution VOCs (volatile organic compounds), and odours. PM2.5-only HEPA without carbon leaves chemical pollutants unaddressed.

**Replacement filter cost:** Some purifiers have cheap initial prices but expensive filters (₹3,000–₹6,000 every 6 months). Calculate annual total cost of ownership, not just purchase price.
