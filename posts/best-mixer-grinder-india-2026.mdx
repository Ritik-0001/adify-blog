## What Indian Kitchens Need in a Mixer Grinder

**Motor power:** Minimum 600W for basic grinding. 750W+ for wet grinding (dosa batter, coconut paste). 1,000W for continuous commercial-level use. Underpowered motors overheat on hard grinding tasks.

**Jar types:** Standard set is 3 jars — 1.5L liquidiser jar (smoothies, lassi), 1L multi-purpose jar (dry masala, chutney), and a small 0.3L chutney jar. South Indian households benefit from a separate wet grinding jar.

**Stainless steel jars:** 304 food-grade stainless steel is standard. Avoid polycarbonate jars for hot liquids — India's post-boiling tomato puree and dal grinding requires heat-resistant jars.

**Overload protection:** Auto-cutoff when motor overheats — essential for Indian grinding sessions that can run 5–10 minutes continuously.
