## What Does ±20% Accuracy Actually Mean?

This confuses a lot of buyers. ±20% accuracy is the ISO standard for home glucometers — it does not mean the device is unreliable. Here is what it means in practice:

If your actual blood sugar is 100 mg/dL, the meter can read anywhere from 80 to 120 mg/dL and still pass the standard. For home monitoring — tracking trends, checking before and after meals, adjusting medication timing — this level of accuracy is acceptable and safe.

Where ±20% is NOT acceptable: diagnosing diabetes for the first time, or making critical insulin dosing decisions for Type 1 diabetes patients. For those cases, lab testing or a higher-accuracy CGM is needed.

For the typical Type 2 diabetic using a home meter to check fasting glucose and post-meal readings, the BG-03 at ₹399 does the job.
