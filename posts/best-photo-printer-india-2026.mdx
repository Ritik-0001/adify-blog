## What Actually Makes a Good Photo Printer?

Photo printing quality comes down to a few specific technical factors. Understanding them helps you avoid buying a printer that looks good in marketing but disappoints on paper.

**Number of ink colours:** Standard printers use 4 colours (CMYK: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black). Dedicated photo printers use 6 colours or more, adding Light Cyan and Light Magenta. These additional inks bridge colour transitions more smoothly — especially in skin tones and skies.

**Ink type — dye vs pigment:**
- **Dye-based ink:** More vibrant, saturated colours. Better for photos you'll look at in indoor light. Slightly less fade-resistant over time.
- **Pigment-based ink:** More water-resistant and fade-resistant. Better for photos meant to last 50+ years. Slightly less vibrant on glossy photo paper.

For home photo printing in India, **dye-based inks** are generally preferred — the vibrancy is more pleasing for everyday photo prints.

**Print resolution:** Photo printers should output at 1440 dpi or higher for smooth photo quality. The best models go to 5760 x 1440 dpi.

**Paper compatibility:** Good photo printers work with glossy, matte, and fine art photo paper. Some cheaper models only work well with specific branded paper.

## Dye vs Pigment Ink for Home Photos in India

This is a common confusion point. Here's the practical guidance for Indian buyers:

**Choose dye-based ink (Epson L805, Canon G3020) if:**
- You print photos for display — frames, albums, walls
- You want maximum colour vibrancy
- Photos will be kept indoors (away from direct sunlight)
- You prioritise visual impact over archival longevity

**Choose pigment-based ink (HP OfficeJet Pro 9020e, Canon MAXIFY MB2170) if:**
- You print documents that need to be water-resistant
- You're printing for archival purposes (50+ year longevity)
- Business documents that might get wet or handled frequently

For most Indian home photo printing: **dye-based ink is the right choice.** The vibrancy is better and photos stored in albums or displayed indoors maintain quality for decades.

## Photo Paper — What to Buy in India

Even the best photo printer produces disappointing results on plain A4 paper. Photo paper makes a transformative difference:

**Glossy photo paper:** Most popular for vibrant, high-contrast family photos. Available from Epson, Canon, HP, and generic brands on Amazon.in. Typical cost: ₹3–₹8 per A4 sheet.

**Matte photo paper:** Preferred for portraits, fine art, and any print that will be under glass. Less reflective, slightly softer colour. Typical cost: ₹3–₹7 per A4 sheet.

**Semi-gloss / lustre:** A middle ground — less reflective than glossy, more vibrant than matte. Popular for professional portraiture.

**4x6" photo paper packs:** Available on Amazon.in in 50–200 sheet packs. Cost: ₹300–₹600 per 100 sheets from branded suppliers.
