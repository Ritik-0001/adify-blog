## Deep Dive: 3D Oscillating-Rotating-Pulsating Technology

The Pro 3 3000's ORP technology works in three simultaneous motions:

1. **Oscillating:** The round head rotates clockwise and counter-clockwise — wrapping around each tooth to sweep plaque from the surface
2. **Rotating:** The bristle tips physically scrub the tooth surface — more aggressive than sonic vibration at surface plaque removal
3. **Pulsating:** The head pulses in and out, pushing bristles into the space between teeth and the gumline

This three-motion combination is why Oral-B's ORP brushes consistently outperform in plaque removal studies. The Cochrane Review comparing electric toothbrush types found ORP brushes remove 11% more plaque and reduce gingivitis 6% more than side-to-side sonic brushes. For Indian adults with heavy plaque buildup — common in populations with limited access to professional scaling — the Pro 3 3000's aggressive cleaning action is a genuine advantage.

## 3 Cleaning Modes

**Daily Clean:** The standard mode for everyday brushing — full ORP power with 2-minute timer and 30-second zone alerts. Use this mode 90% of the time.

**Sensitive:** Reduced motor speed for morning brushing, post-dental procedure sensitivity, or users with tender gums. Indian dentists recommend this mode for the first 2 weeks when patients transition from manual to electric brushing.

**Whitening:** Higher pulse rate for surface stain removal. Useful for removing chai and coffee tannin staining — relevant for most Indian adults who drink chai daily. Alternate with Daily Clean mode (not for exclusive use) for best results.

## 14-Day Battery Life

One full charge delivers 14 days of twice-daily 2-minute brushing. The charging stand is inductive — place the brush on the stand and it charges without any connector. No USB port on the Pro 3 3000 (unlike the iO Series 4's travel case) — the charging stand must be packed for extended trips.

Charge time: approximately 16 hours for a full charge (slower than iO Series models). The battery indicator blinks when low — no percentage display.
