Water quality in India varies enormously — municipal corporation supply in metro areas has different contamination profiles than borewell water in tier-2 cities or hard water in northern India. Choosing the right water purifier requires matching technology to your specific water source.

---

## Which Technology Do You Need?

**RO (Reverse Osmosis):** Removes dissolved salts (TDS), heavy metals (lead, arsenic), and most contaminants. Essential for hard water (TDS above 300 ppm) common in Delhi, Rajasthan, Gujarat, and many northern Indian cities. Wastes 3–5 litres for every litre purified — water efficiency consideration.

**UV (Ultraviolet):** Kills bacteria and viruses but does not remove dissolved solids. Sufficient for municipal supply with low TDS (below 300 ppm) in cities like Bengaluru, Pune, and parts of Mumbai.

**UF (Ultrafiltration):** Removes larger particles and some bacteria without electricity — useful as a pre-filter or for areas with electricity interruptions.

**TDS Controller:** Blends a small amount of pre-RO water back in after purification to maintain mineral content at 100–200 ppm — addresses the concern that fully RO-processed water lacks essential minerals.

**India hard water reality:** Most of India (60–70% of urban areas) has TDS above 300 ppm. RO is not optional — it is required for safe drinking water in most Indian homes.
