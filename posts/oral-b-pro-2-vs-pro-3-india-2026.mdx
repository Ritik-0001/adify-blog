## The Core Difference: What ₹1,000 Buys You

### 1. Battery Life: 7 Days vs 14 Days

This is the most practical difference between the two models.

**Pro 2:** 7 days between charges at twice-daily use. For a month of brushing, you charge it 4 times. This means remembering to charge it weekly — a habit many users find slightly inconvenient.

**Pro 3:** 14 days between charges. Charge it twice a month and forget it. For travel, the Pro 3 comfortably handles a 10-day domestic trip without a charger; the Pro 2 does not.

The 14-day battery is not just a convenience upgrade — it's a practical necessity for Indian users who travel regularly or live in areas with load shedding. If your brush dies unexpectedly, you fall back to your old manual toothbrush (or skip brushing), which defeats the purpose.

### 2. Pressure Sensor: Basic vs Upgraded

**Pro 2:** The pressure sensor on the Pro 2 is present but minimal — a basic indicator light that activates when over-pressure is detected. The light is small and easy to miss, particularly in a bright bathroom.

**Pro 3:** The "visible pressure control" on the Pro 3 is more prominently displayed on the handle. The indicator is easier to notice during brushing and provides clearer feedback. Not as sophisticated as the iO Series LED ring, but a meaningful improvement.

For users who are transitioning from a manual toothbrush and may habitually brush hard, the Pro 3's more visible feedback is a real advantage.

### 3. Brushing Modes: 2 vs 3

**Pro 2:** Daily Clean and Sensitive. This covers the baseline use cases — standard brushing and gentle brushing for sensitive teeth.

**Pro 3:** Adds Whitening+ mode. For Indian users who regularly consume chai, coffee, or pan masala (common staining agents), a dedicated whitening mode provides a lower-abrasion way to address surface staining than using whitening toothpaste on a standard mode. The Pro 3 runs at a slightly higher frequency in Whitening+ mode to lift stains more effectively.

### 4. Extra Brush Head

The Pro 3 comes with an additional brush head (usually a Cross Action or Sensitive head) in the box. At ₹350–₹600 for a replacement head, this represents 35–60% of the price difference between the two models. When factoring this in, the effective additional cost of the Pro 3 over the Pro 2 is closer to ₹400–₹650 after accounting for the extra head.

## Where the Pro 2 Wins

**Entry price:** At ₹1,999–₹2,499, the Pro 2 is the most affordable genuine Oral-B electric toothbrush available in India — a significant step up from no-name oscillating brushes sold at similar prices. If budget is the binding constraint, the Pro 2 is a solid choice.

**First-time users:** For someone testing electric toothbrushes for the first time, the Pro 2 minimizes financial commitment. If you find electric brushing doesn't suit you, you've spent ₹2,000, not ₹3,500.

**Gift for older family members:** Simple, two-button operation and a lower price make the Pro 2 an appropriate gift for parents or grandparents who want to try electric brushing.

## Where the Pro 3 Wins — And Why Most Buyers Should Choose It

**Better value once brush head cost is factored:** The Pro 3 includes an extra brush head worth ₹350–₹600. The net cost difference over the Pro 2 drops to ₹400–₹650 after this. For ₹400–₹650 extra, you get 14-day battery (vs 7-day), a better pressure sensor, and a Whitening+ mode. This is excellent value.

**14-day battery for Indian travel:** Domestic train travel, short temple visits, and weekend trips are part of daily Indian life. A 7-day battery means always packing the charger; a 14-day battery means most trips are covered without it.

**Whitening mode for chai drinkers:** India's chai culture is real — 3 cups of masala chai a day creates surface staining that the Pro 2's Daily Clean mode handles less effectively than the Pro 3's Whitening+ mode.

**For parents buying for teenagers:** The Pro 3's better pressure sensor is important for younger users who tend to brush with more force. The 14-day battery reduces the "my brush died" excuse for skipping brushing.

## Oral-B Pro 2 2000 — Detailed Review

**Price:** ₹1,999–₹2,499

The Pro 2 delivers Oral-B's core oscillating-rotating-pulsating technology at the lowest price in the line. The same 8,800 RPM oscillating motion used across the Oral-B range removes more plaque than a manual brush. Two modes, a 2-minute timer, and 30-second quadrant reminders are all present.

What it lacks is endurance (7-day battery requires weekly charging) and a prominent pressure sensor. For routine home use where charging discipline is easy to maintain, these limitations are manageable. For travel or users who brush hard, the Pro 3 is clearly better.

**Brush head compatibility:** All standard Oral-B brush heads (Cross Action, Sensitive, Precision Clean) — the same heads used on Pro 3, iO Series 3, and most other Oral-B handles. Widely available in Indian pharmacies and online.
