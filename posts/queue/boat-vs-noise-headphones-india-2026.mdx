## Sound Quality

**boAt:** boAt's products are tuned for elevated bass — the brand has built its identity around "bass heads" who want deep, punchy low frequencies for Bollywood, EDM, and hip-hop. The Rockerz series and Airdopes lineup consistently deliver this profile. Midrange and treble are secondary considerations.

**Noise:** Noise's recent releases (Evolve 2 Air, Buds X Pro) have moved toward a more balanced V-shape that still has good bass but doesn't overshadow vocals. If you listen to pop, classical, or vocal-heavy music, Noise's tuning is generally more accurate.

---

## ANC Performance

**Noise wins here.** At equivalent price points, Noise's ANC implementations consistently outperform boAt's. The Noise Evolve 2 Air (₹1,499) provides more effective ANC than the boAt equivalent. At ₹2000–3000, Noise Buds X ANC delivers genuine noise reduction; boAt's models at the same price offer weaker ANC.

---

## Battery Life

**boAt wins here.** boAt's battery claims are consistently higher than Noise at equivalent prices. The boAt Airdopes 141 at ₹1,099 offers 42 hours total; Noise earbuds in the same range typically offer 24–28 hours.

---

## Build Quality and Durability

Both brands use plastic construction under ₹3000. Noise products have a slightly more premium finish with metal accents on some models. Both brands have comparable long-term durability based on user reports. Service centre availability: boAt has more service centres across India's tier-2 and tier-3 cities.

---

## Which Should You Buy?

**Choose boAt if:** You listen primarily to bass-heavy music (Bollywood, hip-hop, EDM), want maximum battery life, or are buying as a gift for a younger audience who values colourful aesthetics.

**Choose Noise if:** You want better ANC for office or commute use, prefer a more balanced sound for varied music genres, or want a slightly more premium-looking product.
