## What Makes Water "Hard" and Why Does It Matter?

Water hardness is caused by dissolved calcium and magnesium salts — primarily calcium carbonate, calcium sulphate, and magnesium bicarbonate. The higher the TDS and the concentration of these minerals, the "harder" the water.

Hard water causes several problems:
- Scale buildup in pipes, geysers, and washing machines
- White deposits on vessels, taps, and bathroom tiles
- Bitter or mineral-heavy taste
- Dry skin and hair after bathing
- Kidney stone risk over long-term consumption (debated, but a concern)

**TDS and hardness classification:**
- Below 200 ppm TDS: Soft water — UV or UF is sufficient
- 200–500 ppm TDS: Moderately hard — RO recommended
- 500–1000 ppm TDS: Hard water — strong RO essential
- Above 1000 ppm TDS: Very hard water — premium RO with pre-treatment

## Which States in India Have the Hardest Water?

India's hardest water comes from:
- **Rajasthan:** TDS of 1000–3000 ppm common, high fluoride in some districts
- **Gujarat:** High TDS in Kutch, Saurashtra coastal areas
- **Haryana and Punjab:** Heavy agricultural chemicals plus high TDS
- **Andhra Pradesh and Telangana:** High fluoride in some regions
- **Maharashtra (interiors):** High TDS in Marathwada and Vidarbha
- **Delhi NCR (from borewell sources):** 500–1500 ppm TDS common

If you are in any of these regions, you need a purifier specifically rated for high TDS.
