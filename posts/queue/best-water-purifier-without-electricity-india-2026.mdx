## How Do Non-Electric Water Purifiers Work?

Gravity-based (non-electric) water purifiers use natural gravity to push water through filter cartridges. You pour water into the top chamber, it drips through the filter, and clean water collects in the bottom chamber.

The purification technology varies by brand:
- **HUL Pureit:** Germkill Kit with activated carbon and germicidal mesh
- **Tata Swach:** Silver nanotechnology for bacterial kill
- **Kent Gold Optima:** Ceramic candle + carbon block filtration
- **Generic ceramic filters:** Simple sediment and partial bacteria removal

These purifiers are effective against:
- Bacteria (including E. coli, Salmonella)
- Cysts (Giardia, Cryptosporidium)
- Sediment and turbidity
- Some organic compounds (with activated carbon)

They do NOT remove:
- Dissolved salts or TDS
- Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, fluoride)
- Viruses (only some advanced gravity models)

## Can Gravity Purifiers Handle Borewell Water?

No. Gravity-based purifiers are designed for municipal tap water with low TDS. They cannot:
- Reduce TDS or remove dissolved salts
- Remove heavy metals (iron, lead, arsenic)
- Handle water with very high turbidity

If you have borewell water, you need an RO purifier regardless of electricity availability. Consider an inverter/UPS to power your RO during outages.
