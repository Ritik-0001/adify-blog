## What Dolby Vision Actually Improves

**Dynamic metadata.** Unlike HDR10 (static metadata applied once per film), Dolby Vision adjusts tone mapping frame-by-frame. A film with a dark interior scene followed immediately by an exterior noon scene gets individual HDR calibration for each frame — resulting in both scenes looking correctly exposed rather than one being crushed to fit the other.

**Brighter highlights.** Dolby Vision supports up to 10,000 nits peak brightness (vs. HDR10's 1,000 nits). Even TVs that can't physically reach 10,000 nits benefit — the metadata instructs the TV's processor to use all available brightness headroom intelligently.
