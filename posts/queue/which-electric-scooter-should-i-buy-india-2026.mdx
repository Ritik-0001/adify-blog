## Who Is This Guide For?

This guide is for anyone in India standing in front of a showroom or a browser tab wondering which electric scooter to buy in 2026. The EV scooter market in India has matured significantly — you're no longer choosing between two bad options. The question now is which *great* option fits your life.

We'll walk through a decision tree by buyer type so you can skip straight to your answer.

## Which Electric Scooter Has the Best Range in 2026?

Range anxiety is the number one concern for new EV buyers, and it's valid. Here's how the top models compare on real-world range (not ARAI figures):

- **Simple One** — 248km ARAI, roughly 170-180km real world. Best in class.
- **Ola S1 Pro** — 195km ARAI, roughly 130-150km real world. Excellent for daily use.
- **Ather 450X Gen 3** — 126km ARAI, roughly 90-100km real world. More than enough for commuters.
- **TVS iQube ST** — 145km ARAI, roughly 100-115km real world. Solid mid-range option.
- **Bajaj Chetak Premium** — 108km ARAI, roughly 80-90km real world. Comfortable city riding range.
- **Hero Vida V1 Pro** — 165km ARAI, roughly 110-130km real world. Good value proposition.

For most Indian commuters riding 20-40km a day, every single scooter on this list is adequate. Range anxiety is overblown for city riders.

## What Kind of Buyer Are You? — Decision Tree

### Are You a Tech-First Buyer?

If you love software, OTA updates, connected features, and a scooter that keeps getting better after purchase — **choose Ather 450X Gen 3**.

Ather has the most mature software ecosystem in the Indian EV space. The Ather Grid charging network is dense in Tier 1 cities. The build quality is best-in-class. You pay a premium (around ₹1.3-1.4 lakh ex-showroom), but you get a scooter that feels like it belongs in 2026.

### Are You a Value-Seeker Who Wants Maximum Range?

**Choose Ola S1 Pro.**

Ola's Hypercharger network is the largest in India with over 4,000 fast-charging stations. The S1 Pro offers the best range in the sub-₹1.5 lakh category. Ola has had its share of software hiccups in the past, but the platform has matured considerably. If range and charging convenience matter most, Ola wins.

### Are You Buying for the Family — Especially a Spouse or Parent?

**Choose Bajaj Chetak Premium.**

The Chetak is the most "traditional scooter" of the EV bunch. It looks like a classic Italian scooter, rides smoothly, and doesn't overwhelm riders who are new to EVs. Bajaj's service network is among the widest in India. The Chetak is the scooter you buy when you want zero adjustment period.

### Are You a Practical Daily Commuter in a Tier 1 or Tier 2 City?

**Choose TVS iQube ST.**

TVS has built the iQube to be the most practical EV on the market. Large under-seat storage (42 litres), a proven motor, good real-world range, and a dealership network that reaches cities Ather and Ola have not yet touched. The iQube ST variant adds a 5-inch TFT display and navigation. For no-nonsense daily commuting, this is our pick.

### Are You in a Smaller City With Limited EV Service Access?

**Choose Hero Vida V1 Pro or Bajaj Chetak.**

Hero MotoCorp and Bajaj have the deepest service penetration in India. If you're in a city where Ather and Ola don't have service centres yet, going with a brand that has existing service infrastructure is a smart call. Hero Vida V1 Pro also gives you decent range and features at a competitive price.

## Which Electric Scooter Has the Best Charging Infrastructure in India?

This is often the deciding factor and it's underrated in most reviews.

1. **Ola Hypercharger Network** — 4,000+ stations, fastest growing
2. **Ather Grid** — Dense in Tier 1 cities, high reliability, fast chargers
3. **TVS iQube / Bajaj Chetak / Hero Vida** — Rely on home charging or third-party networks like BPCL, Tata Power EZ Charge, and ChargeZone

If you live in Mumbai, Bengaluru, Delhi, Pune, Chennai, or Hyderabad, any of the top four brands will serve you well on charging. If you travel intercity regularly, Ola's Hypercharger network gives you the most coverage.

## What About After-Sales Service?

This is where traditional brands have a clear edge — for now.

- **Best service network:** Bajaj, TVS, Hero (existing dealerships adapted)
- **Best EV-specific service:** Ather (dedicated experience centres, responsive)
- **Most improving:** Ola (has had service complaints, actively expanding)

Our honest take: Ather service is the best *EV-specific* experience. Bajaj and TVS win on sheer reach.

## How Much Should You Budget for an Electric Scooter in India 2026?

- **Under ₹1 lakh:** Ola S1 Air, Hero Vida V1 Lite — good starter EVs with minor compromises
- **₹1 lakh to ₹1.3 lakh:** TVS iQube ST, Bajaj Chetak Premium, Hero Vida V1 Pro — sweet spot
- **₹1.3 lakh to ₹1.5 lakh:** Ola S1 Pro, Ather 450X Gen 3 — best features and range
- **Above ₹1.5 lakh:** Simple One — for range maximalists

Remember: after FAME II subsidies and state EV subsidies (varies by state), prices can drop by ₹10,000 to ₹30,000 in many states. Always check the on-road price after subsidies.
