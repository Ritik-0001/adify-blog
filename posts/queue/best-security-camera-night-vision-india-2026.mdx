## IR vs Colour Night Vision — What to Know

Most budget cameras use **IR (infrared) night vision** — invisible LEDs flood the scene with IR light and the sensor captures black-and-white footage. This works up to 20–40 m depending on the camera and is effective for detecting movement, but you cannot see clothing colour or distinguish similar-looking individuals easily.

**Colour night vision** uses white or warm-light LEDs to illuminate the scene in visible light. You see the same full-colour footage at night as during the day. The trade-off is that the LEDs are visible and create a faint glow — but this doubles as a deterrence feature since intruders know they are visible and being recorded clearly.
