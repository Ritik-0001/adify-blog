## HDMI ARC vs Optical vs Bluetooth — Which TV Connection Is Best?

This is the most important practical question for soundbar buyers. The connection method directly affects audio quality and ease of use.

### HDMI eARC (Best)
**HDMI eARC** (enhanced Audio Return Channel) is the gold standard:
- Passes lossless audio — full Dolby Atmos, DTS:X in uncompressed quality
- Enables CEC — your TV remote controls soundbar volume
- One cable handles the connection

**How to identify eARC on your TV:** Look for the HDMI port labelled "ARC" or "eARC" — usually HDMI port 1 or 2. If your TV was made after 2020 and supports Dolby Atmos, it likely has eARC.

### HDMI ARC (Very Good)
Standard HDMI ARC is available on virtually all 2016+ TVs. It passes Dolby Atmos in compressed (lossy) format — still a great experience, though not lossless. If your TV or soundbar only has ARC (not eARC), you still get excellent results.

### Optical / TOSLINK (Good)
Optical passes 5.1 Dolby Digital in lossless quality, but cannot pass Dolby Atmos. If your TV does not have HDMI ARC, optical is a reliable fallback. You lose CEC (volume sync), but audio quality is clean.

### Bluetooth (Acceptable)
Bluetooth works for casual use but has two issues: potential audio delay (lip sync problems during video playback) and compressed audio quality (SBC or AAC codec, not lossless). Use Bluetooth only if wired options are not available.
