## Buying Guide: Hair Dryers for Indian Hair in 2026

**Wattage guide:**
- 1200–1400W: Basic drying, slow for thick hair
- 1600–1800W: Standard daily use — adequate for most Indian hair
- 2000–2200W: Fast drying for thick/long hair — recommended
- 2400W+: Professional speed — salon use

**Ionic technology explained:** All hair has positive charge — causes strands to repel each other (frizz). Ionic hair dryers release negative ions that neutralise positive charge — hair lies flat, looks smoother, and dries faster. Worth the premium for Indian hair that frizzes in humidity.

**Diffuser for curly/wavy hair:** A diffuser distributes airflow over a wide area without directly blowing on curls. This preserves curl shape while drying. Essential for 2B–4C hair types common in South India. Look for dryers that include a diffuser.

**Heat settings for Indian hair types:**
- Fine, straight hair: Low-medium heat (1400–1600W range)
- Medium, wavy hair: Medium heat with ionic
- Thick, coarse hair: High heat 2000W+ with ionic
- Curly hair: Medium heat with diffuser attachment
