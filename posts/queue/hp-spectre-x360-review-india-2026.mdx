The HP Spectre x360 is HP's flagship consumer laptop — a 2-in-1 convertible with a 360-degree hinge, OLED display, and a premium build that rivals MacBook Air in look and feel. In India, it starts at ₹1,24,990. Here's whether it's worth it.

---

## Key Specifications

**Processor:** Intel Core i7-1355U (13th gen, 2P+8E cores)
**RAM:** 16GB LPDDR5 (soldered)
**Storage:** 512GB PCIe NVMe SSD
**Display:** 13.5" OLED 3K2K (2880x1920) touchscreen, 120Hz
**GPU:** Intel Iris Xe integrated
**Battery:** 66Wh — rated 17hr
**Weight:** 1.34kg
**Ports:** 2x Thunderbolt 4, 1x USB-A, 3.5mm audio
**Pen:** HP MPP 2.0 stylus included
**Price India:** ₹1,24,990

---

## Display — Exceptional

The 3K2K OLED panel is the star. 2880x1920 at 13.5" delivers 255 PPI — text is razor sharp. OLED's infinite contrast ratio and 100% DCI-P3 colour coverage make this one of the best displays on any laptop at any price. The 3:2 aspect ratio (taller than 16:9) gives significantly more vertical space — better for documents, web pages, and spreadsheets.

HP Sure View Privacy mode (optional reflection mode) reduces screen visibility from angles — useful in Indian public transport.

---

## Performance — Solid for Office Work

Intel i7-1355U is a power-efficient 13th gen chip — excellent for everyday office tasks, web browsing, and light creative work. Not suitable for heavy video editing, 3D rendering, or gaming. Under sustained load, it performs competently without fan noise becoming disruptive.

**What it handles well:** MS Office, Google Workspace, Zoom, Lightroom (basic edits), web development, financial modelling.

**What it struggles with:** 4K video editing, heavy Photoshop work, gaming, running Docker containers simultaneously.

---

## 2-in-1 Convertible — Is It Actually Useful?

The 360° hinge lets you use the Spectre x360 as:
- **Laptop mode:** Standard use
- **Tent mode:** Presentations, video calling
- **Tablet mode:** Note-taking with HP stylus, reading, sketching

In Indian context: Tablet mode is useful for handwritten notes (CA articleship students, law professionals, architects) and for signing documents. Tent mode is useful for client presentations. The included HP MPP 2.0 stylus has 4,096 pressure levels — adequate for annotation, not ideal for serious illustration.

---

## Battery Life

HP rates Spectre x360 at 17 hours. Real-world Indian testing: **10–12 hours** with mixed use (web, Office, video calls). This is better than most Intel-based Windows laptops. The 100W USB-C charger charges from 0 to 50% in under 45 minutes.

---

## Build Quality

Gem-cut aluminium design with chamfered edges — visually distinct from generic laptop design. Premium feel in hand. 1.34kg is light for a 2-in-1. The hinge action is smooth and the display holds position without wobble in tent mode.
