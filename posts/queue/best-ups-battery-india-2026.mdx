## When to Replace Your UPS Battery

Most sealed lead-acid (SLA) UPS batteries last 2–3 years under regular Indian power cut conditions. If your area experiences 3–5 cuts per day, the battery cycles through charge-discharge faster than in stable grid areas, cutting lifespan to 18–24 months. Here are the key signs that your battery needs replacement:

**Warning signs:**
- UPS starts beeping more frequently than usual during a power cut
- Backup time drops to under 30 minutes when it used to last 1–2 hours
- The battery replacement indicator LED lights up on your UPS front panel
- UPS shuts off almost immediately after a power cut begins
- Battery feels warm or swollen to the touch (replace immediately)

Most Indian UPS models use a standard 12V 7Ah sealed lead-acid battery. Home inverter systems use much larger batteries — typically 12V 100Ah, 150Ah, or 200Ah tubular batteries. Getting the voltage (12V) and capacity (Ah) right is essential for safe replacement.

## AH Rating Explained Simply

The **Ampere-Hour (Ah)** rating tells you how much energy a battery stores. A 7Ah battery can deliver 7 amps for 1 hour, or 3.5 amps for 2 hours. A router drawing 1 amp can run for about 5–6 hours on a 7Ah battery (at 12V). A home drawing 8–10 amps needs a 100Ah battery for approximately 8–10 hours of backup.

## Sealed Lead-Acid vs Gel Batteries

Most budget Indian UPS batteries are **sealed lead-acid (SLA)** — reliable, affordable, widely available. **Gel batteries** use silica-based electrolyte that is more vibration-resistant and performs slightly better in deep discharge scenarios, but they cost 20–40% more. For a desktop UPS, SLA is perfectly adequate. For a home inverter in a rural area with 8+ hour cuts, gel batteries may extend the lifespan by 6–12 months.
