## Overview

If you have ever leaned slightly to one side while using a budget monitor and watched the colours wash out completely, you already understand why IPS panels matter. In India, where shared workspaces, small study rooms, and multi-person households mean monitors are frequently viewed from off-angles, an IPS (In-Plane Switching) display is not a luxury — it is a practical upgrade.

IPS panels deliver two specific advantages that make them especially well-suited to Indian conditions: 178-degree wide viewing angles in both horizontal and vertical directions, and noticeably more accurate colour reproduction compared to TN panels. In brightly lit Indian rooms — where direct sunlight or overhead tubelights are common — IPS panels also tend to look more vibrant and maintain contrast better than their cheaper TN counterparts.

The good news is that IPS technology has become affordable. In 2026, you can pick up a solid IPS monitor for between ₹10,999 and ₹14,999 — well within the reach of home users, students, and office buyers.

## Buying Guide

### Why IPS Over TN at This Price?

TN panels are cheaper but suffer from colour shift when viewed from even slight angles. In Indian homes and shared offices, this is a constant problem. IPS panels maintain colour accuracy across wide angles, making them far more practical for real-world use.

### What to Look for in an IPS Monitor Under ₹15,000

**Refresh Rate:** 75Hz is the sweet spot at this budget. It is not enough for competitive gaming but offers noticeably smoother scrolling and video compared to 60Hz.

**Colour Coverage:** Look for sRGB coverage above 95%. All five monitors above meet this standard.

**Connectivity:** Ensure the monitor has an HDMI port to connect to modern laptops and PCs. VGA is useful for older office machines. USB-C is not common at this price.

**Adjustability:** If you plan to use the monitor for long hours, pay for the Dell P2422H — the ergonomic adjustments protect your posture. Budget stands with tilt-only adjustment force you into fixed positions that cause neck strain over time.

**Eye Care:** If you study or work for 6+ hours daily, BenQ's GW2480 is worth the ₹2,500 premium over the Acer option. Flicker-free backlights and blue light filters make a genuine difference over months of use.

### Bright Room Considerations

Indian homes often have large windows or bright overhead lighting. IPS panels with higher brightness ratings (300 nits and above) handle these environments better than lower-brightness panels. All five monitors above fall in the 250–300 nit range, which is adequate for most indoor setups.

## FAQ

**Q: Is IPS better than VA for this budget?**
VA panels offer higher contrast ratios but suffer from "VA glow" in dark scenes and slightly slower response times. For office work, studying, and general use in well-lit Indian rooms, IPS is the better choice. VA makes more sense for movie watching in dark environments.

**Q: Can I use these monitors with a laptop?**
Yes. All five monitors include an HDMI port. Connect your laptop via HDMI using either a direct cable or an HDMI adapter if your laptop only has USB-C. The monitor will extend or mirror your laptop display.

**Q: Do any of these monitors have built-in speakers?**
None of the budget monitors in this list include built-in speakers. You will need external speakers or headphones for audio. This is standard practice at this price range.

**Q: Which is better for video calls — LG or Dell?**
Both offer good colour accuracy for video calls. The Dell P2422H's ergonomic adjustability makes it easier to position the screen at eye level, which is important for professional-looking video calls. The LG 24MK430H is the better value if you do not need adjustability.
