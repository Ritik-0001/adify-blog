## Overview

The ₹15,000–₹20,000 price band is where robot vacuums in India transform from novelty into genuinely useful home appliances. The single biggest reason: LiDAR navigation arrives at this price point. LiDAR — Light Detection and Ranging — allows the robot to build a precise map of your home and clean in systematic, methodical rows rather than bouncing randomly around the room. The difference in experience is dramatic. Instead of wondering whether the robot has covered every patch of your living room floor, you can open the app and see an actual map showing exactly where it has cleaned.

For Indian households, LiDAR matters even more than in other markets. Indian urban apartments tend to have open floor plans with multiple connected rooms — a common 2 or 3 BHK layout often has the living room flowing into the dining area and then the kitchen corridor. Random-navigation robots struggle with these longer spaces and frequently return to the dock before finishing. LiDAR robots map the entire space first, then clean it efficiently, making them far better suited to typical Indian apartment layouts.

This price range also brings the first credible vacuum-mop combinations to market. In India, mopping is not optional — tile and marble floors need it. The combination of vacuuming and wet mopping in a single pass is a significant quality-of-life upgrade, and the models at this price manage it well on polished hard floors, which is exactly what most Indian homes have. If you are choosing between a budget random-navigation robot and stretching to this segment, the LiDAR upgrade is worth every rupee.

## Buying Guide

### Key Features to Look For

**Suction Power:** Aim for 2,500 Pa or more. Indian homes accumulate fine particulate dust that lower-suction robots miss. At this price range, 3,000–4,000 Pa is achievable and worth paying for.

**Navigation Type:** LiDAR is the defining upgrade in this segment. Insist on it. The systematic cleaning patterns it enables are not just a convenience — they mean the robot actually finishes the job rather than missing patches.

**Mopping:** Vacuum-mop combos are particularly valuable in India because tile and marble floors need both dry vacuuming and wet mopping. Look for models with at least 200 ml water tanks to cover a full 2 BHK without refilling.

**Dustbin Size:** India's dust levels demand larger bins. The Dreame D9 Max (570 ml) and Roborock E5 (640 ml) stand out here. Anything under 400 ml will need frequent emptying.

**App and Wi-Fi:** All models in this segment have apps. Look for reliable scheduling, room-specific cleaning zones, and virtual boundary setting — these are the features you will use every day.

## FAQ

**Q: Is LiDAR navigation worth the extra cost over random navigation?**
A: Absolutely, especially in Indian apartments. LiDAR robots build accurate maps, clean systematically, and finish rooms completely. Random navigation robots may clean some spots multiple times and skip others. The difference is immediately visible in the app and in cleaning results.

**Q: Do these robots mop well on Indian marble floors?**
A: They mop adequately for daily maintenance. Marble floors stay cleaner when mopped regularly, and these robots do a good job of removing light dust and surface grime. For deeper mopping or grout cleaning, you will still need manual mopping periodically.

**Q: Can I use these robots in a 3 BHK apartment?**
A: Yes. Models with 150-minute batteries (Mi 2 Pro, Dreame D9 Max, Roborock E5) can cover a typical 3 BHK in one session. LiDAR models handle multi-room navigation well. Larger homes may require two cleaning runs.
