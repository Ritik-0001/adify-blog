## What Features Should You Prioritise Under ₹1,000?

Shopping for a budget cooling pad means making trade-offs. Here is what actually matters at this price point:

**Fan count and size:** Multiple fans cover more of the laptop base than a single fan. At the same total airflow, a few medium fans are better than many tiny fans. Under ₹1,000, look for 100mm+ fan diameter and at least two fans.

**USB pass-through:** A USB pass-through port means you do not sacrifice a USB slot on your laptop to power the cooling pad. Most budget pads under ₹1,000 include this, but verify before buying — the Amazon Basics pad does not.

**Laptop size compatibility:** Check the stated maximum laptop size against your laptop. If you have a 15.6-inch laptop, confirm the pad supports at least 15.6 inches. Most pads in this list support up to 17 inches.

**Height adjustment:** Even a single raised angle improves typing comfort. Two tilt positions are ideal. A flat pad with no tilt at all is a missed ergonomic opportunity.

**Avoid:** Cooling pads under ₹400. At that price, fan quality degrades to the point where they become noisy and fail within months.

## What You Should NOT Prioritise at This Price

**Brand prestige:** In cooling pads under ₹1,000, brand name means very little. The Havit HV-F2056 outperforms several more expensive branded products.

**LED lighting:** Aesthetic blue or RGB LEDs add cost without adding any cooling benefit. The Havit HV-F2063's LED version costs ₹200–₹300 more than the non-LED HV-F2056 for the same cooling performance.

**Metal build:** Some pads advertise a "metal mesh" surface. Below ₹1,000, this usually means a thin aluminium veneer over plastic — it looks premium but does not meaningfully improve cooling or durability.

**Exact temperature claims:** Manufacturers advertise "reduces temperature by up to 15°C" — this figure is from ideal lab conditions. Real-world reductions are 3–8°C for budget pads.

## Honest Limitations of Budget Cooling Pads

**Noise.** Three 110mm fans spinning at 1,000+ RPM is audible. In a quiet room, you will hear the Havit HV-F2056. In a busy office or while wearing headphones, it is not a problem. If silence matters, you need the Deepcool N65 at ₹1,499.

**Lifespan.** Budget fan bearings have a shorter service life than premium ones. At daily use, expect the fans to develop a slight rattle by month 10–14. For occasional use (a few hours per week), budget pads can last 3–4 years without issue.

**Limited build quality.** The HV-F2056 has noticeable flex in the centre. The height adjustment feels slightly loose compared to premium pads. None of this affects cooling performance, but it does affect the "premium feel" of the product.

**Fan failure pattern.** In multi-fan budget pads, one fan typically fails before the others. The HV-F2056's left or right fan is the most common failure point. When this happens, the pad still works with two fans — reduced but not eliminated cooling.
