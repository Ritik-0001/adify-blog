## Why Dual-Band Actually Matters

The biggest upgrade from the under-₹1,000 bracket to this one is **dual-band WiFi**. Here is what that means in practical terms:

**2.4GHz band:**
- Longer range — travels through walls better
- Lower speed (150–300Mbps on most devices)
- More crowded — neighbours' routers, microwaves, and Bluetooth all compete here
- Best for: smart TVs, smart home devices, devices far from the router

**5GHz band:**
- Shorter range — struggles through thick walls
- Higher speed (300–867Mbps depending on router and device)
- Less congested in most Indian apartments
- Best for: phones, laptops, tablets that are within 20–25 feet of the router

With a dual-band router, your devices automatically (or manually) connect to the best band. A 4K YouTube stream on your laptop stays on 5GHz while your smart bulbs and older devices sit on 2.4GHz without congesting each other. On a single-band router, everything fights over the same channel.
