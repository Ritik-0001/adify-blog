A ₹27,999 flagship smartwatch versus a ₹9,999 rising challenger — Samsung's Galaxy Watch 7 and Amazfit's Active 2 represent different philosophies. Is the Samsung worth nearly 3× the price? Here's an honest comparison for Indian buyers.

---

## Overview

The **Samsung Galaxy Watch 7** runs Wear OS 5 with Samsung's One UI Watch 6, the Exynos W1000 chip (5nm — the fastest smartwatch processor available), BioActive Sensor (heart rate, ECG, blood glucose trends, body composition), and Google's full app ecosystem. The **Amazfit Active 2** runs Zepp OS with a physical rotating crown, built-in GPS, AMOLED display, Zepp Coach AI training guidance, and 10-day battery life. At ₹9,999, it competes on features far above its price.

## Health Sensors

Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 is in a different class for health monitoring. The BioActive Sensor tracks:
- **ECG (electrocardiogram):** Detects AFib (atrial fibrillation) — a serious heart condition
- **Blood Glucose Trends:** Non-invasive monitoring indicates blood sugar direction (not absolute levels)
- **Body Composition:** Measures muscle mass, body fat percentage, and hydration
- **Advanced Sleep Tracking:** Sleep stages, blood oxygen, snoring detection

Amazfit Active 2 covers the basics well — heart rate, SpO2, stress, menstrual health, and sleep stages — but no ECG or body composition.

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy Watch 7** (significantly more advanced health monitoring)

---

## GPS Accuracy

Amazfit's multi-band dual-frequency GPS is one of the most accurate GPS systems in any smartwatch — it outperforms many watches at 2–3× the price. Running routes on the Amazfit Active 2 are precise and match professional GPS devices closely.

Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 also uses multi-band GPS with similar accuracy. Both watches provide reliable GPS for running, cycling, and hiking.

**Winner: Tie** — both deliver excellent GPS accuracy

---

## Battery Life

The biggest practical difference. Amazfit Active 2's 10-day battery means charging once a week. Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 lasts ~40 hours (1.5–2 days) — Wear OS and the powerful processor drain the battery significantly faster.

For users who wear their watch 24/7 for sleep tracking, the Amazfit Active 2's battery advantage is significant — you never need to remove it for charging during the day.

**Winner: Amazfit Active 2** (10 days vs ~2 days)

---

## Software & Apps

Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 on Wear OS 5 has access to Google Maps, Spotify, Google Pay (UPI via Samsung Pay in India), WhatsApp notifications with quick replies, and thousands of apps from the Play Store. It's a genuinely useful wrist computer.

Amazfit Active 2's Zepp OS is clean and fast but limited — no third-party apps, no UPI payments, no streaming. It does its specific health and fitness functions excellently but is not expandable.

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy Watch 7** (Wear OS ecosystem, Google apps, UPI payments)

---

## Ecosystem Integration

Galaxy Watch 7 integrates seamlessly with Samsung Galaxy phones — auto-switch phone calls to the watch, Galaxy Buds auto-connect, SmartThings home control, and Samsung Health deep sync.

On non-Samsung Android phones: Galaxy Watch 7 works but loses some Samsung-specific features. On iPhone: requires a workaround — not recommended.

Amazfit Active 2 works equally well on any Android phone (7.0+) and has basic iPhone support.

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 if:**
- You use a Samsung Galaxy phone and want full ecosystem integration
- ECG, blood glucose trends, and body composition matter to you
- Wear OS apps (Google Maps, Spotify, Google Pay) are important for daily use
- You're willing to charge every 1–2 days

**Buy Amazfit Active 2 if:**
- Budget is the primary consideration (saves ₹18,000)
- 10-day battery life is more important than apps
- You want fitness-first tracking without the complexity of Wear OS
- You use a non-Samsung Android phone

---

## Final Verdict

The Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 is the better smartwatch — Wear OS, advanced health sensors, and Google ecosystem make it genuinely more capable. But at 3× the price of the Amazfit Active 2, you're paying a premium for Wear OS apps and Samsung's advanced sensors that most users won't fully use.

**Value pick:** Amazfit Active 2 — exceptional fitness and health tracking for ₹9,999, with 10-day battery that the Samsung can't match.

**Premium pick:** Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 — ECG, blood glucose monitoring, and Wear OS make it worth the premium if you're a Samsung user who wants the most capable health wearable.
