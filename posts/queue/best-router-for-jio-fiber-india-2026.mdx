## Why Jio's Own Router Is Limited

Jio provides a free WiFi router (the JioFiber Home Gateway) with most plans. It works, but it has real limitations:

- **2.4GHz only on older models** — no 5GHz band means slower speeds and more interference
- **Limited control** — no custom DNS, QoS settings are locked, guest network configuration is minimal
- **Cannot replace or upgrade** — Jio owns the device; you cannot update the firmware or modify settings
- **Replacement takes days** — if it fails, you wait for Jio service to replace it
- **No advanced features** — no VPN passthrough, no dynamic DNS, no custom firewall rules

Using your own router with Jio Fiber gives you full control, better WiFi performance (especially with a dual-band or WiFi 6 router), and independence from Jio's hardware issues.

## How Jio Fiber Works — The Technical Part

Understanding how Jio Fiber delivers internet to your home makes the router setup make sense:

**Jio Fiber chain:**
```
Jio fiber optic cable → ONT (optical modem) → Your router → Your devices
```

The **ONT (Optical Network Terminal)** converts the fiber signal into a standard Ethernet signal. Jio's ONT has 4 LAN ports, and LAN1 is typically the active internet port.

**Key point:** Jio Fiber does **not** use PPPoE authentication for most residential plans. The ONT handles authentication with Jio's network. Your router just needs to receive the Ethernet handoff from the ONT and distribute it — making this simpler than BSNL or Airtel ADSL setups.

This means **any router with a WAN (Ethernet) port** works with Jio Fiber. There are no special credentials to enter in most cases.

## Step-by-Step: How to Use Your Own Router with Jio Fiber

**What you need:**
- Your Jio Fiber ONT (provided by Jio — do not return this)
- Your new router
- 1 ethernet cable (Cat 5e or Cat 6 recommended)

**Step 1: Connect the router to the ONT**
Plug one end of an ethernet cable into **LAN1 port of the Jio ONT**. Plug the other end into the **WAN (blue/yellow) port of your new router**.

**Step 2: Power on both devices**
Power on the ONT first, wait 30–60 seconds for it to fully initialize. Then power on your router.

**Step 3: Access the router's web interface**
Open a browser and go to **192.168.0.1** (TP-Link) or the address on the router's label. Login with default credentials (usually admin/admin or check the label).

**Step 4: Run the setup wizard**
When asked about the connection type, select **"Dynamic IP" or "Automatic"**. Jio Fiber typically assigns an IP automatically — no username or password required.

**Step 5: Configure your WiFi**
Set your 2.4GHz and 5GHz network names and passwords. Save and restart.

**Step 6: Verify connection**
After reboot, your router should show a WAN IP address in the status page. Open a browser and test internet access.

**If it doesn't connect:**
- Try power cycling the ONT (unplug for 60 seconds, then replug and wait 2 minutes)
- Some Jio ONTs have a MAC address binding — call Jio 198 and ask them to release or update the bound MAC address to your new router's MAC
- Try selecting "Static IP" if Dynamic IP doesn't work, and ask Jio support for the static IP details

## What If Jio Has Bound the ONT to a Specific MAC?

Some Jio installations bind the WAN port to the MAC address of the Jio-provided router. If you plug in a new router and it cannot get a WAN IP, this is likely the cause.

**Solutions:**
1. **Call Jio 198** — explain you are using a different router and ask them to update the registered MAC address to your new router's MAC (found on the router's label or web interface)
2. **MAC cloning** — in your new router's WAN settings, use the MAC clone feature to copy the Jio router's MAC address. This tricks the ONT into thinking the original Jio router is still connected.
