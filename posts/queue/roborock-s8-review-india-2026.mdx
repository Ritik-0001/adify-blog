## Detailed Reviews

### 1. Roborock S8 — The Entry Point

**Price:** ₹44,999–₹49,999

The base Roborock S8 introduces two defining features of the lineup: the DuoRoller rubber brush and the VibraRise sonic mopping system. The DuoRoller uses two counter-rotating rubber rollers instead of a bristle brush, which means long hair — whether human or pet — does not wrap around the brush the way it does on traditional robot vacuums. For Indian homes where long hair is nearly universal, this is a genuine quality-of-life improvement.

Suction at 6,000 Pa is at the top of the market. On polished marble and vitrified tile, the S8 picks up fine construction dust, pet dander, and dried food particles cleanly. The mop pad oscillates at 3,000 times per minute, which helps lift dried stains that a static flat mop cannot address. You do need to manually empty the dustbin on the base S8, which becomes tedious if you run it daily — the reason most buyers upgrade to the S8+.

**Pros:** DuoRoller prevents hair tangles, 6,000 Pa suction handles fine Indian dust, sonic mop scrubs dried stains, excellent LiDAR mapping
**Cons:** Manual bin emptying, no auto-wash for mop pad, requires you to handle dirty components daily

---

### 2. Roborock S8+ — The Sweet Spot

**Price:** ₹59,999–₹64,999

The S8+ adds an auto-empty station that suctions the dustbin into a 2.5-litre dust bag in the station. This changes the user experience completely. Instead of emptying the dustbin after every run, you now interact with the robot roughly once every 4–6 weeks to change the dust bag. For a household with pets or in a dusty city like Delhi or Mumbai, this is the difference between a product you love and one you tolerate.

The S8+ does not auto-wash the mop pad — you still need to rinse it manually every few days. But it retains all the vacuuming excellence of the base S8: DuoRoller, 6,000 Pa, sonic mop, and the best navigation system in its class. If you are choosing between the S8 and S8+, the price difference of roughly ₹15,000 is worth it for the hands-free bin emptying alone.

**Pros:** Auto-empty station eliminates daily bin management, all S8 vacuuming features retained, 2.5-litre dust bag lasts weeks
**Cons:** Mop still requires manual washing, station is bulky (30×18 cm footprint)

---

### 3. Roborock S8 Pro Ultra — The Flagship

**Price:** ₹74,999–₹84,999

The S8 Pro Ultra is what happens when you take the S8's excellent vacuuming and add a fully automated mop maintenance system. The RockDock Ultra station auto-empties the bin, washes the mop pads with clean water, dries the mop with warm air, and refills the robot's water tank. You set a schedule, walk away, and come back to genuinely clean floors.

The mop lifting mechanism is particularly clever and important for Indian homes where bedroom rugs are common. When the S8 Pro Ultra detects carpet, it automatically raises the mop pad by 5mm to avoid wetting the rug while continuing to vacuum. This means you can let it run a full-home schedule without worrying about wet carpet edges.

Obstacle avoidance uses a reactive AI system that identifies common household items — shoes, cables, small toys — and routes around them rather than ramming through them. In our testing, it correctly navigated around folded newspapers, charging cables, and pet food bowls placed in unpredictable locations.

**Pros:** Fully self-sufficient cleaning system, mop lifts on carpet automatically, ReactiveAI obstacle avoidance, best-in-class Roborock app with India support
**Cons:** Most expensive option on the market, station requires two water tanks (clean + dirty), premium price requires genuine commitment

## Indian Floor Performance

**Marble:** All three S8 variants clean polished marble excellently. The sonic mop leaves a streak-free finish that rivals a hand-mopped floor. The suction at 6,000 Pa picks up fine marble dust that collects in corners — something many robot vacuums miss.

**Vitrified Tile:** Tile grout traps dust and dried food. The DuoRoller's rubber contacts press firmly into grout lines, and the sonic mop's vibration loosens grime effectively. Post-clean tile looks visibly cleaner than after a pass with a manual flat mop.

**Kota Stone / Rough Stone:** The S8 handles rough stone flooring better than expected. The high suction compensates for the uneven surface, though the mop pad's contact can be inconsistent on very textured stone.

**Pet Hair Pickup:** The DuoRoller is transformative for homes with dogs or cats. In back-to-back tests with a labrador's shedding, the S8 emptied with far less hair tangle than competing bristle-brush robots.

## App and Connectivity

The Roborock app is the best robot vacuum app available in India. It supports Hindi for basic navigation and works completely without a VPN — unlike some Chinese brand apps that require workarounds. You can set no-go zones, create room-specific schedules, adjust suction and mop water levels per room, and monitor cleaning history. Multi-floor mapping stores up to four floor plans, which matters for duplex apartments and multi-storey homes.

Noise levels are moderate: around 68 dB on maximum suction. In quiet mode (around 58 dB), it is barely audible from an adjacent room. Most Indian users schedule it to run during the day while they are at work.
