The sub-₹35,000 segment in India is fiercely competitive, and Realme GT 6T and OnePlus Nord 4 are fighting for the same buyer. Both offer Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3 class performance, AMOLED displays, and rapid charging. Here's what separates them.

---

## Overview

The **Realme GT 6T** is Realme's performance flagship for the mid-range — with Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3, a 5,500 mAh battery, and 120W SuperVOOC fast charging at an aggressive price point. The **OnePlus Nord 4** brings a full metal unibody design, 100W SUPERVOOC charging, and OnePlus's cleaner OxygenOS experience.

## Performance

Both phones use the Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3 — benchmark performance is essentially identical. In gaming (BGMI, Genshin), both sustain high frame rates with minimal throttling. The Realme's slightly more aggressive Boost Mode pushes slightly higher peak benchmarks but also runs warmer.

For everyday use — apps, social media, browsing, camera — you genuinely cannot tell these phones apart in performance.

---

## Design & Build

This is where they diverge sharply. The OnePlus Nord 4's full metal unibody is a premium feel statement — it's one of the few phones at this price with a metal back and frame, giving it a flagship-like weight and solidity.

The Realme GT 6T uses a plastic frame with a textured back that's grippy and fingerprint-resistant. It's well-built for the price but clearly not in the same material class as the Nord 4.

---

## Cameras

Both use the same Sony IMX890 50MP sensor — photos in good light are nearly identical. Processing differs: Realme leans into saturation and sharpness (phones look great on Instagram); OnePlus processes more naturally (better for printing, editing).

Video shooting up to 4K/30fps on both. Stabilisation is marginally better on the OnePlus.

---

## Charging & Battery

Realme's 120W SuperVOOC charges the 5,500 mAh battery from 0 to 100% in approximately 28 minutes — genuinely game-changing fast. OnePlus at 100W charges the same capacity in about 35 minutes.

Both have the same battery size. Realme wins on charging speed; OnePlus wins on build. Life is trade-offs.

---

## Software

OxygenOS 14 on the Nord 4 is one of the cleanest Android experiences available on any non-Pixel phone — minimal bloatware, fast updates, and a near-stock feel.

Realme UI 5 is more feature-rich but heavier — some bloatware pre-installed, more aggressive notifications, though cleaner than it used to be.

---

## Pros & Cons

### Realme GT 6T
**Pros:** 120W charging (fastest in class), ₹5,000 cheaper, larger 5,500 mAh battery, aggressive performance mode
**Cons:** Plastic build, heavier Realme UI, less clean software experience

### OnePlus Nord 4
**Pros:** Full metal unibody design, cleaner OxygenOS, better video stabilisation, better resale value
**Cons:** ₹5,000 more expensive, 100W vs 120W charging (minor), no IP rating

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the Realme GT 6T if:**
- Fastest charging possible (120W) matters
- ₹5,000 savings is significant
- You use Realme accessories or ecosystem
- Premium build material isn't a priority

**Buy the OnePlus Nord 4 if:**
- Build quality and premium feel matter daily
- Clean software with minimal bloat is important
- You keep phones 2–3 years and value resale
- You're in the OnePlus ecosystem

---

## Final Verdict

For pure value: **Realme GT 6T** — ₹5,000 cheaper, faster charging, and effectively identical performance. Exceptional for budget-conscious buyers.

For premium experience: **OnePlus Nord 4** — the metal build, cleaner software, and OnePlus brand experience justify the premium for buyers who care about those things.

Both are exceptional phones for the price. If ₹5,000 matters: buy the Realme. If the metal build and OxygenOS appeal: buy the OnePlus.
