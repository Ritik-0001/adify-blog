The ₹45,000–₹60,000 budget laptop segment is where most Indian buyers shop, and the ASUS VivoBook 15 and HP Pavilion 15 are the two most recommended options every year. Both are updated for 2026 — here's the real difference and which one to buy.

## Overview

The **ASUS VivoBook 15** is ASUS's best-value 15.6-inch laptop — good display, solid build, AMD Ryzen or Intel options, and consistently lower pricing than competitors. The **HP Pavilion 15** is HP's mainstream 15-inch offering — familiar HP build quality, wide service network across India, and good overall balance of specs.

## Performance

Both laptops handle everyday tasks — college work, Office apps, web browsing, online classes, and light coding — without issues. The AMD Ryzen 5 7520U variant of the VivoBook offers better multi-core performance and significantly better integrated graphics (Radeon 610M vs Intel Iris Xe) for casual gaming like Minecraft, GTA V (low settings), and older titles.

For strictly Intel-based configurations, the Core i5-1335U in both is comparable — the VivoBook AMD variant edges ahead for graphics work.

---

## Display

Both offer 15.6-inch FHD IPS panels at 60Hz — adequate for study, streaming, and office work. The VivoBook 15 OLED variant (if available in budget) is dramatically better — OLED at this price is rare and genuinely transformative. Check Amazon for OLED VivoBook configurations, which sometimes appear at ₹54,990.

At 60Hz IPS, both displays look similar — good brightness, decent colours, and comfortable for daily use.

---

## Battery Life

ASUS's larger 50Wh battery gives the VivoBook a meaningful advantage — 6.5–7.5 hours of real use versus HP Pavilion's 5–6 hours. For students who move between lectures without always having power access, this difference matters daily.

---

## Build Quality & After-Sales

Both laptops use plastic bodies — typical for this budget. HP's build feels marginally more rigid. However, HP's service network in India (HP Service Centres in most cities) is excellent and widely praised. ASUS's service network has improved significantly but is still catching up in smaller cities.

If you're in a Tier-2 or Tier-3 city: HP's service availability may be the deciding factor.

---

## Pros & Cons

### ASUS VivoBook 15
**Pros:** Better battery, OLED option available, AMD integrated graphics advantage, ASUS ErgoLift hinge improves typing angle, often cheaper
**Cons:** Smaller service network outside metros, fan can be audible under load

### HP Pavilion 15
**Pros:** Stronger India service network, slightly more rigid build, HP brand confidence for many buyers, BIS certification widely available
**Cons:** Smaller battery, slightly more expensive, no OLED option in this range

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the ASUS VivoBook 15 if:**
- Battery life matters (daily campus/office use)
- You want AMD GPU for casual gaming
- OLED display is available in your budget
- You're in a metro city with ASUS service access

**Buy the HP Pavilion 15 if:**
- You're in a smaller city with better HP service access
- HP brand reliability and warranty support matter
- Build rigidity is a priority
- You've had good experiences with HP before

---

## Final Verdict

For most students and home users: **ASUS VivoBook 15** — better battery, AMD graphics advantage, and the OLED option make it the smarter spec-for-rupee buy.

For buyers in Tier-2/3 cities or those who prioritise service support: **HP Pavilion 15** — the service network advantage is real and matters over a 3–4 year ownership period.

Check Amazon deals on both before buying — prices fluctuate and the gap between them often narrows during sales.
