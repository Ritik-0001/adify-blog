## Why the Under ₹10,000 Budget Is the Sweet Spot for India

The under ₹10,000 price range is where Indian printer buyers finally get real value. This is where ink tank printers enter the picture — and they completely change the economics of home printing.

Instead of paying ₹250–₹400 per cartridge that dies after 200 pages, ink tank printers come with refillable tanks that hold enough ink to print thousands of pages. The Canon PIXMA G2020 ships with ink bottles worth 7,700 black pages and 6,000 colour pages. That's enough for a year or two of moderate home printing — included in the box.

Compare the cost per page:

| Printer Type | Cost Per Page (Mono) | Cost Per Page (Colour) |
|---|---|---|
| HP DeskJet 2331 (cartridge) | ~₹1.25 | ~₹4.00 |
| Canon PIXMA G2020 (ink tank) | ~₹0.09 | ~₹0.20 |

That's not a typo. Ink tank printing costs roughly **10–20x less per page** than cartridge printing. If you print 300 pages a month, you save ₹3,000–₹4,000 a year on ink alone — that pays for the printer itself within two years.

## Canon PIXMA G2020 — The Best Under ₹10K

The G2020 is Canon's entry-level ink tank printer, and it does everything right for the price. It's a print-only device (no scanner, no copier) but prints clean, sharp A4 documents and decent colour pages.

**Key specs:**
- Print speed: 8.8 ipm mono, 5.0 ipm colour
- Ink: GI-790 bottles (included in box)
- Connectivity: USB only
- Resolution: 4800 x 1200 dpi
- Price: ~₹7,999

The USB-only connectivity is the one limitation — you'll need to be near the printer or use a laptop/desktop to print. For home use, that's rarely a dealbreaker.

The G2020 builds on Canon's excellent colour reproduction. Even standard ink tank printing looks noticeably better than cartridge output from budget HP printers. Text is sharp, colours are vivid for flyers and presentations, and the ink doesn't smudge once dry.

## Ink Tank vs Cartridge at This Budget

If you're considering a cartridge printer under ₹10,000 instead of an ink tank — stop. There's almost no scenario where a cartridge printer makes sense for regular use in India at 2026 prices.

The math is clear:
- Buy a Canon G2020 at ₹7,999 → print 10,000 pages, spend roughly ₹1,500–₹2,000 on refill ink
- Buy a cartridge printer at ₹5,000 → print 10,000 pages, spend ₹8,000–₹12,000 on cartridges

The only case for a cartridge printer under ₹10,000 is if you print fewer than 20 pages a month — and even then, the ink dries out between uses and causes clogged heads.
