## Is a Water Purifier Under ₹5,000 Actually Effective?

Yes — with one important condition. Purifiers in this segment work best with pre-treated municipal water that has low TDS (below 200 ppm) and low turbidity. They are NOT designed for borewell water, high-TDS water, or heavily contaminated groundwater.

If your water supply comes from a municipality (Delhi Jal Board, BWSSB, Mumbai's BMC supply, etc.), a gravity purifier or UV purifier in this price range will handle bacteria, viruses, and cysts effectively. The catch is that these purifiers cannot remove dissolved chemicals, heavy metals, or reduce TDS — for that, you need an RO system which starts above ₹6,000.

## Do You Need Electricity for These Purifiers?

Gravity-based purifiers (Pureit Classic, Tata Swach) require **no electricity at all**. Water drips through the purification cartridge using gravity alone. This makes them ideal for areas with frequent or unpredictable power cuts.

UV purifiers (Kent Maxx, Aquasure Amaze) require a standard 230V power supply. They cannot purify water during power outages unless you have a UPS or inverter.

## When Should You NOT Buy a Purifier Under ₹5,000?

If any of these apply to your home, skip this budget and invest in an RO purifier:

- Your water comes from a borewell or tanker
- TDS is above 300 ppm (check with a TDS meter)
- Water has a distinct smell, taste, or colour
- You live in an area with high fluoride, arsenic, or nitrate contamination
- Your children are frequently sick from water-borne illnesses

In these cases, a purifier under ₹5,000 will not provide adequate protection regardless of brand.
