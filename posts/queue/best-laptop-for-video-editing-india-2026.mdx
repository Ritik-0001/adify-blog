India's creator economy is booming — YouTube, Instagram Reels, and short-form content creation have created massive demand for video editing capability. The right video editing laptop needs colour accuracy, fast rendering (GPU acceleration), sufficient RAM for multi-track timelines, and fast storage for 4K footage.

---

## What Video Editors Need

**Colour-accurate display:** sRGB 100% or DCI-P3 90%+ coverage for accurate colour grading. Standard laptop displays cover only 45–70% sRGB — colours graded on these appear differently on other screens. OLED panels typically exceed 100% DCI-P3.

**Dedicated GPU with NVENC/AMFNE:** NVIDIA's NVENC hardware encoder dramatically accelerates export — a 30-minute 4K export that takes 45 minutes on CPU takes 8–12 minutes with NVENC. Essential for high-volume creators.

**RAM — 16GB minimum, 32GB for 4K:** DaVinci Resolve's fusion compositor requires 32GB for complex multi-track 4K timelines. Premiere Pro runs adequately on 16GB for 1080p work.

**Fast SSD:** 4K footage files are large (100MB/s+ for RAW formats). Slow SSDs cause stuttering during playback. NVMe PCIe 4.0 minimum.
