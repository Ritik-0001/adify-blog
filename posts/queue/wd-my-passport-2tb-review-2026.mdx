## Design & Build

At 45g, the WD My Passport SSD is one of the lightest portable SSDs available. The flat credit-card-esque form factor fits in any pocket — genuinely pocketable in a way that bulkier drives are not.

The rubberised finish provides grip and some scratch resistance. No official IP rating, but the sealed enclosure handles occasional splashes. The recessed USB-C port protects the connector from cable leverage damage.

---

## Transfer Speeds

Real-world benchmarks via USB 3.2 Gen 2 port:
- Sequential read: 980–1,020 MB/s ✅
- Sequential write: 820–950 MB/s (varies, some throttling on sustained large writes)
- Large file (50GB video): averages ~800 MB/s throughput

The WD My Passport SSD shows more write speed variation than the Samsung T7 on sustained large transfers — acceptable for most use cases, but photographers offloading large batches may notice slight slowdowns.

For everyday use — backup copies, project file transfers, document storage — speeds are excellent.
