## What Do Students Actually Need From a Printer?

Let's think about real student printing in India in 2026:

- **Assignments** — 10–50 pages, text-heavy, black and white, submitted weekly
- **Lab reports and practical files** — formatted A4 sheets, occasional diagrams
- **Study notes** — printed slides, past papers, revision sheets
- **Project reports** — 50–100 page bound documents at semester end
- **Occasional colour** — charts, cover pages, photos for presentations

The volume spikes are the challenge. Students print almost nothing for weeks, then need 200 pages in two days before semester submission. A printer that runs out of ink at that moment is a disaster.

This is exactly why **ink tank printers are the obvious choice for students.** One ink set in a Canon PIXMA G2020 lasts 7,700 mono pages — that's roughly one year of heavy student printing. The chance of running dry before an exam is essentially zero.

## WiFi vs USB — What's Right for You?

**Choose USB (Canon G2020) if:**
- You use a laptop or desktop and it's always near your printer
- You're the only one printing
- Saving ₹500 matters (tight budget)
- Simplicity is important (WiFi setup can frustrate some users)

**Choose WiFi (Epson L3151) if:**
- You print from your phone regularly
- Multiple people share the printer
- You live in a hostel or PG where the printer sits in a common area
- You use tablets or Chromebooks that don't easily support USB printing
