## Oscillating vs Sonic — The Clinical Evidence

**Oscillating-rotating-pulsating (Oral-B standard):** The round brush head rotates while also pulsating (in-and-out movement). 48,000 pulsations + 8,800 oscillations per minute in standard models.

**iO Magnetic Drive (Oral-B premium):** Adds micro-vibrations to the oscillating motion, significantly reducing the mechanical feel while increasing efficiency at hard-to-reach areas — 4x more plaque removal from interdental areas vs manual brushing.

**The Cochrane review finding:** A 2014 Cochrane systematic review (the highest-quality evidence standard) found oscillating-rotating toothbrushes reduced gingivitis and plaque more than manual brushes with statistical significance. Sonic brushes had similar but not superior performance in the same review.

**India relevance:** Oral-B's oscillating models are available across all price points in India (₹699–₹18,999), making the full technology range accessible regardless of budget.

## Oscillating Technique Reminder

Unlike sonic brushes, oscillating brushes require **holding the brush head at each individual tooth for 2–3 seconds** — the round head is designed for one tooth at a time. Sliding across teeth reduces effectiveness. See the full technique guide for step-by-step instructions.
