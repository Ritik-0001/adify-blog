## Who Actually Needs WiFi 6?

WiFi 6 (802.11ax) is real technology with real benefits — but those benefits are conditional. Here is who will notice a genuine improvement:

**You will benefit from WiFi 6 if:**
- Your home has **10 or more simultaneously connected devices** (phones, laptops, smart TVs, IoT devices)
- You live in a **dense apartment building** where multiple neighbours' WiFi networks overlap — WiFi 6's OFDMA handles interference better
- You stream **4K content on multiple screens simultaneously**
- You game online and want lower, more consistent latency
- Most of your devices are 2020 or newer (WiFi 6-capable)

**WiFi 6 will not help you if:**
- You have 3–5 devices and live in a standalone home or bungalow
- Your internet plan is below 100Mbps
- Your devices are older than 2019 (they cannot use WiFi 6 features)
- You just want basic browsing and streaming on 1–2 devices

The bottom line: WiFi 6 improves **network efficiency** more than raw speed. In a congested environment with many devices, the difference is significant. In a simple household with a few devices, you may not feel it at all.

## AX vs AC — Explained Simply

| Feature | WiFi 5 (AC) | WiFi 6 (AX) |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 802.11ac | 802.11ax |
| OFDMA | No | Yes — serves multiple devices simultaneously |
| MU-MIMO | Downlink only | Uplink + Downlink |
| Target Wake Time | No | Yes — better battery life on connected devices |
| BSS Colouring | No | Yes — reduces interference from nearby networks |
| Typical Speed | AC1200–AC3200 | AX1800–AX6000 |
| Real Benefit | Good for few devices | Better with many devices |

OFDMA is the most important WiFi 6 feature for Indian homes. It divides each WiFi channel into smaller resource units, letting the router serve multiple devices in a single transmission instead of taking turns. In a 3BHK with 20+ devices, this is a genuine improvement.
