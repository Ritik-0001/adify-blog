## Buying Guide: Blood Pressure Monitors in India 2026

**Upper arm vs wrist monitors:**
- Upper arm: More accurate, standard for clinical use — recommended by doctors
- Wrist: Compact and convenient, slightly less accurate if positioning isn't perfect — good for travel

**Clinical validation is critical:** Only buy validated BP monitors (look for ESH, BHS, or AAMI validation). Unvalidated monitors can be 10–20mmHg off — which represents the difference between normal and high-risk readings.

**Cuff size matters:** Measure your arm circumference. Standard adult cuffs: 22–32cm. Large adult: 32–42cm. Wrong cuff size gives systematically inaccurate readings.

**When to measure:** Take readings at the same time daily — morning before eating/medication and evening before dinner. Sit quietly for 5 minutes before measuring. Take 2 readings 1 minute apart and average them.

**Normal ranges (for reference):**
- Normal: Below 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120–129/below 80
- High (Stage 1): 130–139/80–89
- High (Stage 2): 140+/90+

*Always consult a doctor for diagnosis and treatment — these monitors are for monitoring, not diagnosis.*
