## What Makes a Good Call Headphone

**Microphone array size.** Three or more microphones provide better background noise rejection than single-mic designs. Professional headsets (Jabra, Poly) use multi-mic arrays; consumer headphones typically use one or two mics.

**ENC (Environmental Noise Cancellation).** Not to be confused with ANC (which blocks sound from your ears), ENC filters noise from your transmitted microphone signal — so your caller hears less of your background noise. Essential for noisy Indian home environments.

**Boom mic vs in-line mic.** Boom mics (positioned near the mouth on a flexible arm) pick up voice more accurately and reject background noise better than in-line mics on the cable. For professional use, a boom mic is significantly better.
