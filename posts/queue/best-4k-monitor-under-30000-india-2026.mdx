## Buying Guide

### GPU Requirements for 4K — India's Popular GPU Segment

4K gaming requires significantly more GPU horsepower than 1080p. Here's what's realistic with GPUs common in India:

- **RTX 3060 / RX 6600 XT:** Can drive 4K 60Hz in less demanding titles and older games. Expect medium settings in AAA games.
- **RTX 3070 / RX 6700 XT:** Comfortable 4K 60Hz in most games at high settings.
- **RTX 4070 / RX 7700 XT and above:** Smooth 4K experience with DLSS/FSR support pushing effective performance higher.
- **Integrated graphics (Intel Iris Xe, Radeon integrated):** Fine for desktop productivity at 4K 60Hz but not for gaming.

For productivity, video editing, and media consumption, any GPU that supports HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort 1.2 will handle 4K 60Hz without issue.

### Do You Actually Need 4K?

4K makes the most sense if:
- You sit within 60–80 cm of your monitor (the typical Indian home desk setup)
- You work with high-resolution images, video timelines, or dense text
- You watch HDR content on streaming platforms (Netflix and Prime Video offer 4K content in India)

At 27 inches, the sharpness improvement over 1440p is visible but subtle. At 24 inches, 4K is arguably overkill unless you do professional image work. The bigger practical benefit is often the extra desktop real estate — 4K effectively gives you four 1080p panels' worth of screen space, which is transformative for productivity.

### Content Availability in India

Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Disney+ Hotstar all offer 4K HDR content in India on their premium plans. YouTube 4K works well on decent broadband (50+ Mbps). Most Indian OTT platforms cap at 1080p, but international content increasingly arrives in 4K.

## FAQ

**Q: Is 4K 60Hz good enough, or do I need 4K 144Hz?**
For productivity and media consumption, 4K 60Hz is perfectly smooth. 4K 144Hz monitors exist but cost significantly more (₹60,000+). For gaming, 4K 60Hz is the realistic budget option in 2026.

**Q: Can I connect a MacBook Air (M-series) to a 4K monitor?**
Yes. M1, M2, and M3 MacBook Air models natively support one external 4K 60Hz display via USB-C/Thunderbolt. Use a USB-C to USB-C cable or a USB-C to DisplayPort/HDMI adapter.

**Q: Is 28-inch too big for a desk?**
At 4K resolution, 27–28 inches is the sweet spot. The high pixel density prevents the "too large and soft" issue you get with 27-inch 1080p monitors. Most standard Indian study desks (120 cm wide) accommodate 27-inch monitors comfortably.

**Q: Which 4K monitor is best for a student in India?**
The ViewSonic VX2758-4K-Pro at ₹22,999 is the most accessible starting point. If budget allows, the Dell S2722QC at ₹27,999 adds USB-C convenience worth paying for.
