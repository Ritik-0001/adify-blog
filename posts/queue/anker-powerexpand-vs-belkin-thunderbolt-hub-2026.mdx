A good USB-C hub transforms a modern laptop into a full desk setup. Two names dominate the premium recommendations: Anker's PowerExpand series for value-focused buyers, and Belkin's Thunderbolt hub for maximum bandwidth. Here's which one to buy.

---

## Overview

**Anker PowerExpand 13-in-1** is a versatile USB-C hub with an impressive port array — 13 ports covering USB-A, USB-C, HDMI (dual), SD cards, Ethernet, and 85W pass-through charging — at a mid-range price. The **Belkin Thunderbolt 3 Dock Pro** uses Thunderbolt 3 protocol for dramatically higher bandwidth — daisy-chaining, dual 4K displays at full resolution, and charging up to 85W, but at a premium price.

---

## Bandwidth & Display Quality

Thunderbolt 3 in the Belkin runs at 40Gbps — enabling dual 4K at 60Hz simultaneously. The Anker PowerExpand's USB-C limits dual HDMI to 4K/30Hz or 1080p/60Hz per output — fine for productivity but not ideal for design work or gaming on external displays.

If you're running two 4K monitors from your laptop: Belkin Thunderbolt is the only option that delivers proper frame rates on both.

---

## Port Selection

The Anker PowerExpand 13-in-1 wins on sheer port count — 5 USB-A, 2 USB-C, dual HDMI, SD and microSD, Ethernet, and audio all in one affordable dock. For a home office connecting peripherals, it's extremely practical.

The Belkin is cleaner with fewer but higher-quality ports — the Thunderbolt 3 connection is the star, and the dock is designed for professional desk setups rather than port multiplication.

---

## MacBook & Laptop Compatibility

Thunderbolt 3 is fully backwards compatible with USB-C. The Belkin works with any USB-C laptop but only Thunderbolt-equipped systems get the full 40Gbps benefit. MacBook Pros (2018+), ThinkPad X1 Carbon, and Dell XPS all support Thunderbolt 3.

The Anker works with any USB-C laptop — including those without Thunderbolt — making it more universally compatible.

## Pros & Cons

### Anker PowerExpand 13-in-1
**Pros:** Excellent price (₹6,000 vs ₹25,000+), 13 ports covers every need, works with all USB-C laptops, SD + microSD slots, no drivers needed
**Cons:** USB-C protocol limits dual 4K to 30Hz, no Thunderbolt daisy chain, some ports share bandwidth

### Belkin Thunderbolt 3 Dock Pro
**Pros:** Dual 4K/60Hz display output, 40Gbps Thunderbolt bandwidth, Thunderbolt daisy chain, premium build, future-proof
**Cons:** Dramatically more expensive (₹25,000+), requires Thunderbolt port on laptop, fewer total ports than Anker

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the Anker PowerExpand if:**
- You need maximum port coverage at low cost
- You use 1 external 4K monitor or 2× 1080p monitors
- Your laptop lacks Thunderbolt 3
- Home office setup with multiple peripherals

**Buy the Belkin Thunderbolt 3 if:**
- You run dual 4K monitors and need 60Hz on both
- You have a Thunderbolt 3 MacBook Pro or ThinkPad
- You daisy-chain Thunderbolt storage
- Professional creative setup with high bandwidth requirements

---

## Final Verdict

For most Indian home office buyers: **Anker PowerExpand** — the ₹6,000–₹8,000 price gets you 13 ports that cover every realistic need. Tremendous value.

For professional creative workstations with dual 4K monitors: **Belkin Thunderbolt 3** — the only choice when you need real 4K/60Hz on two displays. The price is justified for that use case. For everything else, Anker wins.
