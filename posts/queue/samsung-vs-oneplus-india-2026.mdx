## Where Samsung Beats OnePlus

1. **Service network:** 12,000+ centres vs OnePlus's 600+ cities. Critical for buyers outside metro areas.
2. **Software support:** Samsung commits to 7 years total (4 OS + 5 security). OnePlus provides 3yr OS + 4yr security.
3. **Mid-range variety:** Samsung has phones from ₹7,000–₹1,60,000. OnePlus starts at ₹19,000.
4. **Samsung Pay (UPI):** Samsung's wallet and UPI integration is well-developed in India.

## Where OnePlus Beats Samsung

1. **Charging speed:** OnePlus SUPERVOOC (80W–100W) is fastest in its segments. Samsung lags (25–45W at same price).
2. **Performance chip:** OnePlus often uses Snapdragon where Samsung uses Exynos (which runs hotter and slower).
3. **Battery size:** OnePlus 13's 6000mAh vs Samsung's 4000mAh at similar price.
4. **Value:** OnePlus consistently offers better specs-per-rupee in the ₹25,000–₹70,000 range.

---

## Final Verdict

**Choose Samsung if:** You're in a tier-2/tier-3 city (wider service network), want 7-year software support, value Galaxy AI features, or are buying under ₹20,000 (Samsung's budget segment is strong).

**Choose OnePlus if:** You're in a metro city (OnePlus centres available), want best performance for the price, need fastest charging, or are buying in the ₹25,000–₹70,000 range (OnePlus typically offers better value here).
