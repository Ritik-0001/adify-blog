## Picture Quality

**Samsung:** Crystal 4K and QLED panels are bright and punchy — excellent in sunlit Indian living rooms where ambient light competes with the display. Samsung's Motion Xcelerator technology is among the best for sports content like cricket. HDR10+ (dynamic metadata) provides better HDR accuracy than static HDR10.

**LG:** The α5/α9 AI Processors deliver the best automatic picture mode selection in the industry. Dolby Vision support (preferred over HDR10+ by Netflix and Disney+) provides more content compatibility. webOS's FILMMAKER Mode disables all processing to show content as the director intended. LG OLED delivers genuinely superior picture quality at higher price points — no LED TV matches OLED's contrast.

---

## Smart TV OS

**LG webOS wins.** LG's webOS is consistently rated the fastest, most intuitive smart TV OS across professional reviews. The Magic Remote's gyroscope-based point-and-click navigation is faster than traditional TV navigation. Samsung's Tizen is excellent but lags webOS in ease of use.

---

## Service Network in India

**Samsung wins.** Samsung's service network extends to tier-3 Indian cities with authorized service centres. LG's service is excellent in tier-1 cities but thinner in smaller cities. For buyers in non-metro areas, Samsung's service availability is a practical advantage.

---

## Which Should You Buy?

**Choose Samsung if:** You watch primarily sports and cricket, your room has significant ambient light, you're in a smaller city where Samsung service is more accessible, or you prefer a brighter, punchier picture.

**Choose LG if:** You primarily watch movies and streaming content, you value the best OS interface, you want Dolby Vision compatibility, or you're in a metro city where LG service is readily available.
