The ASUS ROG Strix G16 is the gaming laptop for buyers who want raw performance without the compromises of a compact chassis. With a 16-inch 240Hz display, RTX 4070 at higher TGP than 14-inch competitors, and aggressive cooling, it's one of the most capable gaming laptops under ₹1,20,000.

## Overview

The ROG Strix G16 (2024) pairs AMD Ryzen 9 7945HX with NVIDIA RTX 4070 at up to 140W TGP — higher than most 14-inch gaming laptops running the same GPU. The 16-inch chassis allows better cooling, louder speakers, and a full-size keyboard with numpad. This review covers the RTX 4070 / 16GB / 512GB configuration.

---

## Key Specifications

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen 9 7945HX (16-core)
- **GPU:** NVIDIA RTX 4070 (up to 140W TGP)
- **RAM:** 16GB DDR5 (expandable to 64GB)
- **Storage:** 512GB PCIe 4.0 NVMe (secondary slot available)
- **Display:** 16" QHD+ IPS 240Hz, 100% DCI-P3
- **Battery:** 90Wh
- **Weight:** 2.5 kg
- **Ports:** USB-A × 4, USB-C (PD+DP), HDMI 2.1, RJ45, SD card, headphone
- **OS:** Windows 11 Home

## Gaming Performance

The RTX 4070 at 140W TGP delivers meaningfully better gaming performance than the same GPU at 100W in 14-inch laptops. At 1440p High settings: Cyberpunk 2077 runs at 75–90 FPS, Elden Ring at 100+ FPS, Valorant at 300+ FPS.

The Ryzen 9 7945HX (16-core) paired with the RTX 4070 handles CPU-heavy games and streaming simultaneously without bottlenecking. This is one of the fastest gaming laptop combinations available under ₹1,20,000.

MUX switch enables direct GPU output to the display, bypassing the iGPU — this alone adds 10–15% performance in games.

---

## Display

The 16" QHD+ (2560×1600) IPS panel at 240Hz is exceptional for gaming. 100% DCI-P3 coverage makes it equally useful for colour-accurate content creation — photographers and video editors can trust what they see. 240Hz at 1440p makes fast-paced games feel silky-smooth.

---

## Thermals & Noise

The ROG Strix G16's larger chassis allows a proper cooling solution — three heat pipes, three fans, and extensive vent cutouts. Under full gaming load, CPU peaks at 90–92°C and GPU at 80–83°C — hot but within spec. Fan noise at max is loud (~52dB) — gaming headphones recommended.

The keyboard surface stays manageable under load — hot at the top but comfortable at the WASD area.

---

## Build

At 2.5 kg, this is a desk laptop with occasional portability — not something you carry daily. The build is solid plastic over metal reinforcement, with an aggressive ROG aesthetic. The RGB per-key keyboard is one of the best on any gaming laptop — full travel, satisfying feedback.

---

## Battery

The 90Wh battery in gaming mode lasts 1.5–2 hours. In battery-saving mode (iGPU only), 5–6 hours for productivity tasks. This is a plugged-in gaming laptop — don't expect all-day battery.
