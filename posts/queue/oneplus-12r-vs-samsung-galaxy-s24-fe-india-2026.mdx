Two of the most compelling mid-range flagship phones in India go head-to-head — OnePlus 12R's raw performance and 100W charging versus Samsung Galaxy S24 FE's longer software support and Galaxy AI. Both sit in the ₹40,000–₹50,000 range. Here's which one wins.

---

## Overview

The **OnePlus 12R** runs Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 (one of the fastest chips in its price range), a 50MP Sony LYT-808 main camera, 5,500mAh battery with 100W SUPERVOOC charging (0–100% in 26 minutes), and OxygenOS 14 based on Android 14. The **Samsung Galaxy S24 FE** runs Exynos 2500, a 50MP main + 10MP 3× telephoto camera system, 4,700mAh battery with 45W charging, and Galaxy AI features with 7 years of OS updates (Samsung's longest ever software support).

## Performance

OnePlus 12R's Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 is faster than Samsung's Exynos 2500 in sustained workloads — benchmark scores and real-world gaming performance (Genshin Impact, BGMI at max settings) are consistently higher. Thermal throttling on OnePlus is better managed with the large vapour chamber.

Samsung Galaxy S24 FE's Exynos 2500 is no slouch — it handles all apps and moderate gaming smoothly. But for sustained workloads and gaming, OnePlus 12R edges ahead.

**Winner: OnePlus 12R** (performance and value)

---

## Camera

OnePlus 12R main camera (Sony LYT-808) delivers excellent daylight photography — natural colours, good detail, and wide dynamic range. The weakness: no telephoto lens. Cropped zoom degrades quickly beyond 2×.

Samsung Galaxy S24 FE's 10MP 3× telephoto makes a meaningful difference — portraits at 3× are clear and sharp, street and event photography at zoom is significantly better than OnePlus.

Galaxy AI photo features (Generative Edit, Circle to Search, Photo Assist) add genuine utility for casual users.

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24 FE** (camera versatility, telephoto, AI features)

---

## Battery & Charging

OnePlus 12R's 5,500mAh + 100W charging is a decisive advantage. A full charge from 0 in 26 minutes — faster than any competitor at this price. Daily use provides consistent 7–8 hours screen-on time.

Samsung's 45W charging takes ~65 minutes for a full charge — slower, but wireless charging (15W) is a convenience OnePlus doesn't offer. The 4,700mAh battery provides ~6 hours SOT.

**Winner: OnePlus 12R** (battery capacity + fastest charging in segment)

---

## Software & Long-Term Support

This is the critical differentiator. Samsung Galaxy S24 FE gets **7 years** of OS updates and security patches — that means software support until 2031. OxygenOS on OnePlus 12R gets 3 years OS + 4 years security — respectable but half of Samsung's commitment.

Galaxy AI (available on S24 FE) includes generative AI photo editing, Circle to Search (search anything on screen with Google), Live Translate, and Note Assist. These features will continue to grow as Samsung updates the AI capabilities.

**Winner: Samsung Galaxy S24 FE** (7-year update promise, Galaxy AI)

---

## Ecosystem & Connectivity

Samsung Galaxy S24 FE integrates with Samsung ecosystem — Galaxy Watch, Galaxy Buds, Samsung DeX, Windows 11 Link to Windows, and SmartThings. For Samsung Watch/Buds users, the integration is compelling.

OnePlus 12R integrates with other OnePlus devices and Nothing (parent company) products. Less comprehensive but OxygenOS is a cleaner Android skin.

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy OnePlus 12R if:**

**Buy Samsung Galaxy S24 FE if:**
