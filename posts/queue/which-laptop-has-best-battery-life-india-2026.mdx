## Why Battery Life Claims Are Often Lies

Laptop manufacturers test battery life under ideal conditions — low brightness, video playback only, no background apps. Real-world use (50% brightness, Chrome with 10 tabs, Zoom calls, occasional video) typically gives 40–60% of the claimed figure.

**Claimed vs real-world battery life:**
- MacBook Air M3 claimed: 18 hours → Real-world: 15–18 hours (Apple's claims are unusually accurate)
- ASUS Zenbook 14 OLED claimed: 14 hours → Real-world: 10–12 hours
- Dell XPS 13 claimed: 12 hours → Real-world: 6–8 hours
- HP Envy x360 15 claimed: 17 hours → Real-world: 8–10 hours
- Acer Aspire Lite Ryzen 5 claimed: 7.5 hours → Real-world: 5–6 hours

The MacBook Air M3 is the only laptop where claimed and real-world numbers are nearly identical — because the M3 chip's efficiency scales dramatically with actual workload.

## How to Maximize Any Laptop's Battery Life

1. **Reduce screen brightness:** Screen is the #1 battery drain. Drop to 50% brightness — your eyes adjust within minutes.
2. **Use efficiency mode:** Windows 11 Battery Saver or macOS Low Power Mode extends battery by 20–30%.
3. **Close background apps:** Chrome tabs each take CPU cycles. Close unused tabs.
4. **Disable Bluetooth and WiFi when not needed:** Small savings but they add up over hours.
5. **Dark mode matters:** On OLED displays, dark mode genuinely saves battery (each dark pixel is literally off).
