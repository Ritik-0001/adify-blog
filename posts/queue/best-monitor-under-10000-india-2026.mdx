## Buying Guide

### Panel Types Explained

**IPS (In-Plane Switching)** is the best all-purpose panel type at this price. Accurate colours, wide viewing angles (178°), and consistent brightness across the screen. Ideal for students, office workers, and anyone sharing their screen.

**VA (Vertical Alignment)** panels have better contrast ratios but narrower viewing angles. Good for solo media consumption in darker rooms.

**TN (Twisted Nematic)** panels are the oldest technology — fast response times but poor colour and narrow viewing angles. Mostly found in the cheapest monitors; avoid for primary use if IPS is available at a similar price.

### Resolution Guide

At 22 inches, 1080p (1920×1080) looks sharp and clear. Every monitor in this list runs 1080p — the right choice at this screen size and price point. You won't feel the need for higher resolution on a 22" budget monitor.

### Other Key Features

**Connectivity:** HDMI is essential for modern laptops and systems. VGA is a legacy standard but still useful for older desktops. DisplayPort (Dell E2222H) gives the best signal quality but requires an adapter for HDMI-only devices.

**Eye Care:** Flicker-free backlighting and blue light filters are standard on most monitors listed here. For students doing long sessions, prioritise these — they genuinely reduce eye fatigue.

**Power Supply:** All monitors listed are compatible with India's 230V AC supply. The included adapters are universal — no additional converter needed.

**Indian Warranty:** LG and Dell have the broadest service networks. HP and Acer follow closely. ViewSonic has improved coverage but is still better in metros than smaller cities.
