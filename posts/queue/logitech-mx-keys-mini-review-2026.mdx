The Logitech MX Keys Mini takes everything people love about the full-size MX Keys and shrinks it into a tenkeyless format. For minimalist desk setups, travel users, and anyone who doesn't need a numpad, it's one of the most refined compact wireless keyboards available.

---

## Overview

The MX Keys Mini is a 65% layout wireless keyboard — no numpad, no function row (Fn-layer activated), but with dedicated arrow keys and a compact footprint. It uses Logitech's spherically-dished scissor switches, Logi Bolt 2.4GHz wireless, and the same smart proximity backlight as the full MX Keys. Available in Pale Grey, Graphite, and Rose.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Layout:** 65% compact (no numpad, Fn-layer function row)
- **Switch Type:** Scissor with spherical dish keycaps
- **Connection:** Bluetooth 5.1 (3 devices) + Logi Bolt USB (2.4GHz)
- **Backlight:** Smart (proximity-activated, adjusts to ambient light)
- **Battery:** Up to 10 days (backlit) / 5 months (no backlight)
- **Charging:** USB-C
- **Compatibility:** Windows, macOS, iPadOS, Android
- **Weight:** 506g
- **Price (India):** ₹8,995

## Design & Build

The MX Keys Mini is a beautifully designed keyboard. The matte plastic body is slim and premium-feeling — not as cold as metal but more refined than most plastic keyboards. The Pale Grey variant pairs especially well with light-mode Mac setups; Graphite suits dark workstations.

At 506g, it's light enough to travel with but has enough weight to stay in place during typing. The four rubber feet prevent any slipping on desk surfaces.

---

## Typing Experience

The spherically-dished keycaps are Logitech's signature — each key has a slight concave dish that guides fingertips naturally. Combined with the 1.8mm scissor travel, typing feedback is comfortable and confident — quieter than mechanical, more tactile than a butterfly keyboard.

For someone coming from a MacBook's keyboard, the Mini feels like a premium external version of the same typing experience — familiar but better. For mechanical keyboard users, the low travel and scissor feel will take adjustment.

---

## Smart Backlight

The proximity sensor auto-activates the backlight when your hands approach and turns it off when you move away. After a few days it becomes invisible — you never manually manage backlight. The backlight also adjusts automatically to ambient room brightness.

In dark rooms: the backlight helps. In bright rooms: it dims to match. It's a small feature that Logitech's competitors haven't matched convincingly.

---

## Multi-Device & OS Switching

Three device slots — pair to Mac, Windows PC, and iPad simultaneously. Switching is instant with Fn+1/2/3. The MX Keys Mini ships with both Mac and Windows key legends printed on shared keys (e.g., "Command/Win"), and Logi Options+ automatically remaps keys based on the connected OS. No configuration needed.

---

## Layout & Missing Keys

The compact 65% layout removes the function row (F1–F12 require Fn hold) and the numpad. For most users, this is a worthwhile trade for the smaller footprint. The dedicated arrow cluster and Page Up/Down keys are present — critical for code navigation.

If you use F-keys constantly (game developer, specific apps), the Fn-layer access may be frustrating. The full MX Keys is the better choice in that case.
