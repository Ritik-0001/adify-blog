Food processors are among the highest-utility kitchen appliances for Indian cooking — where chopping onions, grinding masalas, kneading dough, and preparing chutneys are daily tasks that a good food processor reduces from 30+ minutes to under 5 minutes.

---

## What Indian Cooks Need in a Food Processor

**Dough kneading attachment:** Roti, puri, and paratha dough requires kneading that most mixers cannot handle well. A dedicated dough blade with sufficient motor torque (minimum 650W) is essential for Indian households.

**Masala grinding capability:** Indian cooking requires fine grinding of wet masalas (ginger-garlic paste, coconut chutney, wet spice pastes). This requires either a dedicated wet grinding jar or a powerful main bowl with wet grinding blade.

**Onion chopping consistency:** Indian recipes start with finely chopped onions in large quantities — a food processor that produces consistent, even onion pieces (not paste) without making them watery is highly valued.

**Capacity:** Indian family cooking for 4–6 people typically requires 3L+ main bowl capacity — smaller bowls require multiple batches.

**Motor power:** 800–1,000W minimum for Indian cooking demands. Underpowered motors overheat when processing hard vegetables, meat, or stiff dough.
