## What to Look For in a Coding Monitor

**Resolution:** 1080p is functional but 1440p or 4K shows significantly more code at once. At 27", 4K text is very sharp; at 24", 1440p is ideal.

**Size:** 27" is the sweet spot for a single primary monitor. Smaller for secondary/travel; larger (32–34") for ultrawide.

**USB-C:** One cable to laptop (video + charging) dramatically cleans up desk setups. Prioritise if you use a modern laptop.

**Eye Care:** Look for flicker-free (DC dimming) and low blue light. BenQ and LG Eye Care certifications are meaningful for long sessions.

**Panel Type:** IPS for colour accuracy and wide viewing angles. VA for deeper blacks. TN for fastest response (not useful for coding).
