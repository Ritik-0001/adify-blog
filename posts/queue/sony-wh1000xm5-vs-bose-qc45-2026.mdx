## Noise Cancellation

Sony wins this category clearly. The XM5's 8-microphone array with dual processors delivers the best ANC of any consumer headphone. On a busy flight, train, or in an open office, the silence it creates is remarkable.

Bose QC45's ANC is no slouch — it's second only to Sony in most tests. For most WFH or commute scenarios, you won't notice a meaningful difference. Only in sustained high-noise environments (flights, construction) does Sony's advantage become apparent.

---

## Comfort

Bose wins here, and it's not close. The QC45 uses Bose's signature soft protein leather cushions with an extremely light 238g frame. Wearing these for 8-hour work-from-home sessions feels effortless.

The XM5 at 250g is also light, but the earcup design creates more clamping pressure. Fine for 2–3 hour sessions; some users report ear fatigue on very long sessions.

---

## Sound Quality

The XM5 has a V-shaped tuning — punchy bass, slightly recessed mids, sparkly highs. Excellent for music, gaming, and podcasts. With LDAC enabled on Android, the audio detail at Hi-Res quality is genuinely impressive.

The QC45 sounds more balanced and natural — better for long listening sessions where fatiguing frequencies become a problem. Less exciting on first listen, but more enjoyable over 10 hours.

---

## Call Quality

Sony's XM5 made a significant leap with 4 beamforming mics dedicated to call performance. Voice clarity on Zoom and Meet calls is excellent — colleagues notice the difference compared to lesser headphones.

Bose QC45 is good for calls but not on the same level. In noisy environments, the XM5 picks up voice more cleanly.

---

## Pros & Cons

### Sony WH-1000XM5
**Pros:** Best ANC available, excellent call quality, LDAC Hi-Res audio, 30hr battery, Speak-to-Chat auto-pause
**Cons:** Not foldable (travel inconvenient), heavier clamping force, premium sound requires LDAC-compatible device

### Bose QuietComfort 45
**Pros:** Best-in-class comfort for long wear, foldable, balanced sound signature, lighter frame, easier to live with daily
**Cons:** Slightly weaker ANC in extreme noise, shorter 24hr battery, no Hi-Res codec support

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the Sony XM5 if:**
- You commute by train, flight, or work in loud offices
- Call quality on video calls matters
- You want the absolute best ANC
- You use an Android device with LDAC support

**Buy the Bose QC45 if:**
- You wear headphones 6–10 hours daily
- Comfort is your top priority
- You prefer balanced, natural sound
- You need a foldable headphone for travel
