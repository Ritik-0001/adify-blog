## Overview

Sensitive gums are an epidemic-level concern in India. Surveys consistently show that gingivitis — inflammation of the gum tissue — affects nearly 80% of Indian adults at some point. The most common complaint dental clinics hear is "bleeding when I brush." The instinctive response is to brush less or switch to a super-soft manual brush, but the correct answer is often the opposite: brush smarter, with a brush designed for gum care.

It is important to distinguish between sensitive gums and sensitive teeth — these are different conditions requiring different solutions. Sensitive gums (gingivitis, gum recession) need a brush with a dedicated Gum Care mode, a pressure sensor that stops the motor if you press too hard, and soft to extra-soft bristles. Sensitive teeth (dentinal hypersensitivity from enamel erosion or exposed roots) need low-abrasion bristles and enamel-safe whitening avoidance. Many people in India have both — but the priority should be gum health first.

Electric toothbrushes are actually superior to manual brushing for gum health, provided you use the right mode. The oscillating or sonic motion is more consistent than hand strokes, and the 2-minute timer ensures equal time on all quadrants — which manual brushers almost never achieve.

## Buying Guide

**Sensitive gums vs sensitive teeth — know the difference:**
Sensitive gums (red, swollen, bleeding on brushing) indicate gingivitis — a bacterial infection of gum tissue. The fix is better plaque removal with a Gum Care mode brush and a proper technique. Sensitive teeth (sharp pain to hot/cold) indicate enamel erosion or exposed dentin — use Sensitive mode and fluoride toothpaste, avoid whitening modes.

**Why pressure sensors matter for Indians:**
Many Indians were raised brushing with hard strokes — a habit from years of using firm manual toothbrushes. This over-pressure habit, when applied to an electric brush, is a leading cause of gum recession. A pressure sensor that visibly alerts you (or physically stops the brush) is the single most important feature for this demographic.

**Brushing technique for gum care:**
Hold at 45 degrees to the gumline. Let the brush head rest against each tooth for 2–3 seconds before moving. Do not scrub. For inflamed gums, start on Sensitive mode and step up to Gum Care once the acute inflammation reduces (usually within 2–4 weeks of consistent correct brushing).

## FAQ

**Q: Will an electric toothbrush make bleeding gums worse?**
A: Initially, you may notice slightly more bleeding in the first week as plaque is disrupted. This should reduce significantly within 2–4 weeks of consistent brushing. If bleeding persists beyond a month, see a dentist.

**Q: Which toothpaste to use for sensitive gums in India?**
A: Colgate Total (gum protection formula), Sensodyne Gum+ Sensitivity, or Oral-B Gum & Enamel Repair are widely available options in India.

**Q: Should I use Gum Care mode every day?**
A: Until your gingivitis is under control (gums no longer bleed, redness reduces), use Gum Care mode daily. Once gum health normalises, you can alternate between Daily Clean and Gum Care.

**Q: Can an electric toothbrush replace a dental cleaning?**
A: No. Electric brushing removes daily plaque. Calculus (tartar) that has hardened requires professional scaling. Visit your dentist every 6 months for cleaning, especially if you have a history of gum issues.
