The tongue surface harbours more bacteria per square millimetre than any other area in the mouth — and is the primary source of volatile sulphur compounds (VSCs) that cause bad breath (halitosis). Tongue cleaning is one of the most neglected areas of oral hygiene in India, yet has the most direct impact on breath freshness.

---

## Why Tongue Cleaning Matters

**Oral bacteria distribution:** Studies estimate 50–90% of bad breath originates on the tongue surface — specifically in the papillae (small projections) that trap food particles, dead cells, and bacteria.

**Indian diet consideration:** High-spice, dairy-rich, and protein-heavy meals (dal, paneer, meat dishes) leave more organic residue on tongue surfaces than lighter diets. Combined with chai milk coating, tongue bacteria loads are typically higher in Indian dietary patterns.

**Tongue coating and systemic connection:** A coated tongue (white or yellow film) is associated with digestive issues and dry mouth, both common in India. Regular tongue cleaning reduces this coating and the associated bacterial load.

### Using Standard Electric Toothbrush on Tongue

Any electric toothbrush can be used for tongue cleaning without a dedicated mode — with technique adjustments:

**Technique for tongue cleaning with standard electric brush:**
1. Switch to Sensitive or lowest intensity mode
2. Extend the tongue fully
3. Place brush head at the back of the tongue (as far as comfortable without triggering gag reflex)
4. Use very light pressure — tongue tissue is delicate
5. Brush forward in 3–4 light strokes, moving from back to front
6. Rinse thoroughly

**Best standard brush for tongue cleaning:** Any brush with a Sensitive mode and soft bristles — Oral-B Pro 3 (Sensitive mode), Philips Sonicare 3100 (low intensity).

## Complete Tongue Hygiene Routine

1. **Morning:** Tongue scraper (copper, 3–5 scrapes from back to front)
2. **Rinse** with water
3. **Brush teeth** with electric toothbrush (2 minutes)
4. **Optional:** 2–3 light forward strokes on tongue with toothbrush in Sensitive mode
5. **Spit, don't rinse** — allow fluoride residue to remain on teeth
6. **No food/drink** (except water) for 30 minutes
