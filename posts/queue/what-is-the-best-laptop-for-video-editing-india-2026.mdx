## What Video Editors Actually Need in a Laptop

Video editing is one of the most demanding tasks you can put a laptop through. Here is what matters:

1. **CPU:** Fast multi-core performance for timeline rendering and export
2. **GPU:** Hardware acceleration in Premiere Pro (CUDA/OpenCL), DaVinci Resolve (CUDA/Metal), Final Cut Pro (Metal)
3. **RAM:** 16GB minimum; 32GB for 4K or multi-camera editing
4. **Storage:** Fast NVMe SSD — 1TB minimum; your raw footage will fill 512GB fast
5. **Display:** Colour-accurate panel — wide colour gamut (DCI-P3) and high brightness for grading
6. **Thermal management:** The laptop must sustain performance under hours of render load

Most budget laptops fail on the display and thermal management — they throttle badly under sustained rendering, making exports take twice as long.

## HP Omen 16 with RTX 4060 — Best Windows Editing Laptop

For Windows users who edit in Adobe Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve, the **HP Omen 16 with RTX 4060** is the strongest option.

**Why the HP Omen 16 excels for editing:**
- NVIDIA RTX 4060 with CUDA cores handles GPU-accelerated rendering in Premiere Pro significantly faster than integrated graphics
- Intel Core i7 / Ryzen 7 CPU handles export workloads well
- 16.1-inch 2K 165Hz IPS display — bright and detailed enough for editing work
- 16GB/32GB DDR5 RAM configurations available
- 1TB NVMe SSD is adequate for a working edit

**Honest trade-offs:**
- At ~1.99kg, it is not a portable laptop for travel shooting
- Battery life is 3–5 hours real-world (gaming GPU + big display)
- The IPS display, while good, does not match the MacBook Pro's HDR capabilities

For YouTube creators, short film editors, and content creators who primarily edit on a desk and export to 1080p/4K, the HP Omen 16 delivers excellent value.

## Dell G15 — Budget Video Editing Laptop for Beginners

The **Dell G15** with an RTX 3050 or RTX 4050 is the most accessible entry point into GPU-accelerated video editing.

**What the Dell G15 offers editors on a budget:**
- NVIDIA GPU for CUDA acceleration in Premiere Pro
- Ryzen 5 or Ryzen 7 processor for decent export speeds
- 15.6-inch FHD display at 120Hz — acceptable for editing, not ideal for colour work

**Where it falls short for serious editing:**
- No colour-accurate display — do not trust final colour grading on this panel
- GPU headroom is limited — 4K timelines will stutter
- Heavy and loud under load

The Dell G15 is a stepping stone — enough to learn editing and produce solid 1080p content without the investment of a professional machine.
