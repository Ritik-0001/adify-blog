## What Can You Actually Expect From a ₹40,000 Laptop in 2026?

The sub-₹40,000 segment has improved dramatically in 2026. You are no longer stuck with outdated Intel Celeron or i3 processors from three generations ago. AMD Ryzen 5 7000-series chips have flooded this price bracket, offering genuine quad-core and hexa-core performance that can handle office work, college assignments, light coding, and casual media consumption without breaking a sweat.

Here is what ₹40,000 buys you in 2026:

- **Processor:** AMD Ryzen 5 7520U or Ryzen 5 7430U (most common)
- **RAM:** 8GB DDR5 (some models offer 16GB at this price)
- **Storage:** 512GB SSD (NVMe in most new models)
- **Display:** 15.6-inch FHD (1920×1080), IPS or TN panel, 60Hz
- **Battery:** 5–7 hours real-world
- **Build:** Plastic chassis, functional but not premium

You will not get a metal build, an OLED screen, or a dedicated GPU at ₹40,000. But for core productivity, this budget is more than capable.

## Is ASUS VivoBook 15 a Better Buy Under ₹40,000?

The **ASUS VivoBook 15** competes closely with the IdeaPad Slim 3 and often offers a slightly better display and build finish. Depending on the variant and current sale prices, it fits comfortably under ₹40,000.

**Why consider the VivoBook 15:**
- ASUS typically has a brighter and more colour-accurate display
- ErgoLift hinge design tilts the keyboard for better typing comfort
- NumberPad integration on the touchpad (useful for spreadsheet users)
- More colour options and a slightly more premium aesthetic

**Where it falls short:**
- Some variants still ship with 8GB soldered RAM (non-upgradeable)
- Battery life is similar to the IdeaPad Slim 3

If you care about display quality and keyboard comfort, the VivoBook 15 edges ahead. If you care about pure price-to-performance ratio, the IdeaPad Slim 3 usually wins.
