The Samsung T7 Shield takes Samsung's best portable SSD platform and wraps it in IP65 dust and water resistance with 3-metre drop protection. For photographers, filmmakers, and travellers who can't afford data loss in the field, it's the most compelling rugged SSD at its price.

---

## Overview

Built on the T7's NVMe internals with USB 3.2 Gen 2 connectivity, the T7 Shield adds a rubber bumper exterior, IP65 rating (dust-tight, jet-water resistant), and 3m drop resistance. It comes in three colours and two capacity options. This review is based on the 1TB model.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Interface:** USB 3.2 Gen 2 (10Gbps)
- **Sequential Read:** Up to 1,050 MB/s
- **Sequential Write:** Up to 1,000 MB/s
- **Durability:** IP65 dust and water resistant, 3m drop resistant
- **Encryption:** AES 256-bit hardware encryption
- **Capacities:** 1TB, 2TB
- **Dimensions:** 87.5 × 59.5 × 13.5 mm
- **Weight:** 98g
- **Connector:** USB-C (cable included)

---

## Design & Build

The T7 Shield's most visible feature is the rubber exterior — a ridged, tactile bumper that wraps the metal core. It adds grip (essential for outdoor use with wet hands), absorbs drops, and gives a completely different feel from the standard T7's sleek metal body.

The USB-C port is recessed for cable protection — a thoughtful detail. At 98g, it's heavier than the standard T7 (58g) but still pocketable. It ships with both USB-C to USB-C and USB-C to USB-A cables.

---

## Transfer Speeds

In real-world testing via USB 3.2 Gen 2:
- Sequential read: 980–1,020 MB/s (consistent)
- Sequential write: 940–990 MB/s (consistent)
- Large file transfer (100GB video): sustained at ~900 MB/s throughput

Performance is excellent and consistent — no significant throttling during sustained transfers. Offloading a 64GB SD card takes approximately 70 seconds. For video production in the field, this is transformative versus traditional HDDs.

---

## Durability Testing

IP65 means: completely dust-tight, and protected against water jets from any direction. In practical terms: rain, splashing rivers, dusty roads, and workshop environments are all handled without concern. It's not submersible (that would be IP67/68), but IP65 covers the vast majority of outdoor photography and travel scenarios.

The 3m drop resistance on concrete is real — Samsung's testing methodology is conservative, not marketing-exaggerated.

---

## Software & Encryption

Samsung Portable SSD software provides AES 256-bit hardware encryption with password protection. The software is lightweight and cross-platform (Mac and Windows). Setup takes 2 minutes. Once configured, the drive locks automatically when disconnected.

- Rubber exterior shows dust and debris more visibly
