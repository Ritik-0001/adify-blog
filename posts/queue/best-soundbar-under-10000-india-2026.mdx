## What the ₹10,000 Soundbar Budget Unlocks

The jump from a budget soundbar under ₹5,000 to the under-₹10,000 range is one of the most meaningful upgrades in home audio. Here is what the extra investment delivers:

**HDMI ARC — reliable implementation:** Budget soundbars often include HDMI ARC but with inconsistent CEC compatibility (your TV remote may or may not control the soundbar volume correctly). JBL and Yamaha implement HDMI ARC properly — volume syncs reliably with your TV remote on virtually all major TV brands.

**Virtual surround sound:** The Yamaha YAS-109's DTS Virtual:X processing creates a noticeably wider and taller soundstage than a standard stereo soundbar. It is not the same as real 5.1 speakers, but it makes movies sound significantly more enveloping.

**Better audio drivers:** At ₹7,000–₹10,000, manufacturers can afford larger driver arrays. JBL uses dual 2.5-inch full-range drivers plus passive bass radiators in the Bar 2.0 — the difference in clarity and bass extension versus a budget single-driver soundbar is immediately audible.

**Build quality:** Metal grilles, thicker enclosures, and sturdier mounting brackets are standard at this price point.

## What About Soundbars with a Wireless Subwoofer Under ₹10,000?

The **boAt Aavante Bar 1700** (₹8,999–₹10,999) includes a separate wireless subwoofer at this price point. If deep bass is your priority — for action movies, bass-heavy music, and gaming — this is worth considering. The separate wireless subwoofer delivers significantly more low-end impact than the JBL or Yamaha's built-in passive radiators.

The trade-off: you now have two units to place (bar + subwoofer), and boAt's audio tuning is not as refined as JBL's or Yamaha's. For movie-focused buyers who prioritise bass impact, the Aavante Bar 1700 is a compelling alternative.
