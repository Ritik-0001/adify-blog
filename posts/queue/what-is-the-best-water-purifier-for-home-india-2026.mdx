## What Type of Water Purifier Do You Actually Need?

Before spending money, you need to know your water source. This single factor determines whether you need RO, UV, UF, or a combination.

**RO (Reverse Osmosis):** Best for borewell water, high TDS water (above 300 ppm), or hard water with heavy minerals. RO membranes filter dissolved salts, heavy metals, fluoride, and arsenic. The downside is water wastage — most RO purifiers waste 3 litres for every 1 litre purified. Modern models like the HUL Pureit Eco Water Saver have improved this ratio significantly.

**UV (Ultraviolet):** Best for municipal tap water with low TDS. UV kills bacteria, viruses, and cysts but does NOT remove dissolved salts or chemicals. If your water is soft (below 200 TDS) and treated by your municipal authority, UV is often enough and far less wasteful than RO.

**UF (Ultrafiltration):** UF works without electricity and removes bacteria and larger particles, but cannot remove dissolved salts or viruses. It is useful as a pre-filter or as a standalone unit for very clean water sources.

**RO+UV+UF:** The combination found in most mid-range purifiers gives you protection against everything — dissolved salts, heavy metals, bacteria, viruses, and cysts. This is what most Indian households should buy.

Borewell water in India can have TDS ranging from 500 to over 2000 ppm. High hardness, iron content, and dissolved salts make RO non-negotiable.

**Kent Grand Plus** is the gold standard for borewell users. It uses a mineral ROTM technology that retains essential minerals while removing harmful dissolved solids. Its 20 litre per hour purification capacity and 9-litre storage tank make it ideal for families of 3–6. Priced around ₹13,000–₹15,000.

**AO Smith Z9** is another excellent choice for high-TDS water. Its 8-stage purification and Side Stream RO Technology reduce water wastage compared to older models. Rated for inlet TDS up to 2000 ppm. Available around ₹14,000–₹16,000.

## How to Check Your Home's Water TDS

You do not need to guess. A digital TDS meter costs under ₹200 on Amazon and gives you a reading in seconds.

- Below 200 ppm: UV or UF is usually sufficient
- 200–500 ppm: RO+UV is recommended
- Above 500 ppm: RO+UV+UF is essential
