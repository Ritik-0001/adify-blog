## What Does Herman Miller Actually Cost in India?

A brand-new Herman Miller Aeron in India costs between **₹1,20,000 and ₹1,60,000** depending on size and configuration. This is significantly more expensive than in the US (approximately ₹70,000–₹80,000 at current exchange rates) due to import duties, GST, and dealer margins.

The Herman Miller Embody costs ₹1,40,000–₹1,80,000. The Mirra 2 is slightly more accessible at ₹80,000–₹1,10,000.

**Certified refurbished Aerons** — commercially used chairs restored and re-warranted — are available in India at ₹40,000–₹70,000 and represent significantly better value than new.

## What You Get for the Price

The Herman Miller Aeron is not just expensive branding. Here is what the price genuinely buys you:

**PostureFit SL:** A dual-pivot sacral and lumbar support system that no other chair manufacturer has replicated. It keeps your pelvis in a forward-tilted position that reduces spinal disc compression. Physiotherapists consistently recommend it for people with lower back conditions.

**8Z Pellicle Mesh:** Eight zones of varying mesh tension, each calibrated to the pressure and movement patterns of a different back region. This is genuinely different from single-tension mesh and produces noticeably better pressure distribution.

**12-Year Warranty:** Herman Miller offers a 12-year warranty on parts and labour. No Indian chair brand offers more than 3 years. Amortised over 12 years, a ₹1,40,000 Aeron costs approximately ₹11,700 per year — comparable to a mid-range Indian chair replaced every 2–3 years.

**Resale Value:** A 10-year-old Aeron in good condition sells for ₹30,000–₹50,000 in India. No Indian chair retains meaningful resale value after 5 years.

## Who Should Buy Herman Miller in India

**Yes, buy it if:**
- You sit 8–12 hours daily, 5–6 days per week, long-term
- You have a diagnosed back condition (disc herniation, spondylosis, sacroiliac dysfunction)
- Your doctor or physiotherapist has specifically recommended a premium ergonomic chair
- You have already optimised all other ergonomic factors (monitor height, keyboard position, desk height) and are still experiencing pain
- You can afford it without financial strain
- You are considering a refurbished Aeron at ₹45,000–₹60,000 — at that price the value proposition is much stronger

**No, don't buy it if:**
- You sit fewer than 6 hours daily
- You are buying it primarily for status
- Budget is tight and the purchase would cause financial stress
- You haven't tried a good mid-range ergonomic chair first
- You don't have back problems and are just being preventative — a good ₹18,000 chair is sufficient for prevention
