## Brand Overview

**APC by Schneider Electric** is a global brand that has dominated the UPS market for decades. Their Back-UPS series is engineered to international standards with strict quality control, and they are the default choice for IT professionals and corporate buyers in India. Their products are priced at a premium, but the build quality, protection circuitry, and warranty support justify the cost for critical equipment.

**Microtek** is an Indian brand with over three decades of presence in the subcontinent. They understand the Indian power landscape — erratic voltages, frequent deep cuts, and the need for affordable, serviceable products in tier-2 and tier-3 towns. Microtek's strengths lie in value for money, widespread service availability in smaller cities, and products designed specifically for Indian power conditions.

## Which to Choose: APC or Microtek?

**Choose APC if:**
- You are protecting a desktop PC, workstation, or NAS device
- You live in a city with reliable APC service access
- You want AVR precision and shutdown software
- Your equipment cost justifies paying more for the UPS

**Choose Microtek if:**
- You want a UPS for a router, set-top box, or basic home use
- You live in a tier-2 or tier-3 city where Microtek service is easier to access
- Budget is the primary concern
- You want more backup time for the money at entry-level pricing

---

## Final Verdict

For **PC protection**, APC's superior build quality, AVR, and software make it the better long-term investment — the APC Back-UPS 1100VA is the clear winner for desktop users. For **routers and casual home use**, the Microtek EB 700 and UPSEB 900VA deliver better value per rupee, especially in smaller towns where Microtek's service network is genuinely stronger than APC's. Most Indian buyers will find the Microtek range perfectly sufficient for everyday needs.
