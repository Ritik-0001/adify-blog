## Brand Backgrounds

**MI (Xiaomi)** launched security cameras in India as part of its broader smart home push. MI cameras are designed to integrate with the Xiaomi/Mi Home ecosystem — MI routers, smart plugs, AI speakers, and air purifiers all connect within the Mi Home app. The camera range is relatively limited in India, focusing on indoor pan/tilt cameras rather than specialised outdoor or battery variants.

**TP-Link Tapo** is a dedicated security product brand within TP-Link, the world's leading consumer networking company. The Tapo ecosystem launched in India in 2020 and has grown rapidly, covering indoor cameras, outdoor cameras, battery cameras, video doorbells, and solar-powered variants. The Tapo app is purpose-built for security camera management and has been refined over multiple years of global use.

## Image Quality: MI vs TP-Link Tapo

**MI Basic Security Camera 2i** delivers 1080p full HD resolution (1920 x 1080). For everyday monitoring — watching over a living room, checking who is at the door, monitoring pets — 1080p is perfectly adequate. The colour reproduction is natural and the autofocus handles mixed Indian lighting conditions (tube lights, LEDs, and natural light) competently.

**TP-Link Tapo C210** records in 3MP 2K resolution (2304 x 1296). The 80% increase in pixels over 1080p translates to meaningfully sharper footage — you can zoom into any part of the frame and still extract useful detail. For identification purposes — reading text on packages, recognising faces, checking clothing — 2K is noticeably better.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — 2K resolution provides a genuine quality advantage for identification.

---

## App Comparison: Mi Home vs Tapo

The **Mi Home app** is a comprehensive smart home platform — it controls MI cameras, routers, air purifiers, smart bulbs, and dozens of other Xiaomi devices. As a camera management app specifically, it works well but feels secondary to the broader smart home purpose. The camera timeline view is functional, sharing access with family is straightforward, and geofencing works reliably. However, the detection customisation options are limited and the notification management is less granular than the Tapo app.

The **Tapo app** is built exclusively for Tapo devices (cameras, smart plugs, smart bulbs). The camera management experience is more focused — the timeline is intuitive, live view loads quickly, detection zones can be drawn precisely, and notifications can be filtered by time of day, type of event, and sensitivity level. The app also supports multi-camera viewing and family sharing with different permission levels (admin, viewer).

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — the Tapo app is more polished and feature-rich for camera management.

---

## Motion Detection and AI

The **MI camera's** motion detection includes basic person detection but false positives from shadows, fans, and passing light are relatively common. The Mi Home app's detection sensitivity slider is the primary adjustment tool, and fine-tuning it takes trial and error.

The **Tapo C210's** person detection uses a more refined AI model — it distinguishes between humans and other motion sources more accurately. The detection zone editor lets you draw specific polygonal areas to monitor, so the camera ignores motion outside those areas completely. The result is fewer unnecessary notifications in typical Indian home environments where ceiling fans, curtains, and light changes are constant sources of false triggers.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — detection zones and more refined AI reduce false alerts significantly.

## When to Choose MI Over TP-Link

Despite TP-Link Tapo winning overall, MI cameras have a clear use case: **Xiaomi ecosystem households**. If you own an MI router, MI air purifier, MI smart bulbs, and a Redmi or Poco smartphone, the Mi Home integration creates genuinely useful automations — the camera can trigger lights, the router can prioritise camera bandwidth, and geofencing can arm all your smart home devices when you leave. If the ecosystem value matters to you more than camera-specific features, MI remains a valid choice.
