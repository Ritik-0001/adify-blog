## What Can You Actually Expect from a Router Under ₹1,000?

Let's be honest: at this price, you are buying a **2.4GHz-only, single-band router**. There is no 5GHz band, no WiFi 6, no beamforming, no MU-MIMO. What you get is a simple, reliable box that puts your ISP's internet connection on WiFi.

Here is what the TL-WR820N actually delivers:

- **Speed:** 300Mbps theoretical on 2.4GHz (real-world: 80–150Mbps at close range)
- **Coverage:** 750 sq ft — one room to a small 1BHK
- **Ports:** 1 WAN + 2 LAN (100Mbps Fast Ethernet)
- **Antennas:** 2 external 5dBi antennas
- **Setup:** Simple web interface, no app required

The 2.4GHz limitation means two things: slightly lower speeds compared to 5GHz, but **better range and wall penetration**. For a single room, PG, or hostel use, this is actually fine.

## TP-Link TL-WR820N — Detailed Review

The TL-WR820N has been a bestseller in India for years, and it earns that position through consistency rather than specs.

**Setup is genuinely easy.** Plug in the WAN cable, open 192.168.0.1 in a browser, and the quick setup wizard walks you through the rest in under 5 minutes. PPPoE credentials for BSNL and Airtel go in the same screen.

**Coverage is honest.** TP-Link claims 750 sq ft and that holds up in a typical concrete Indian construction. One concrete wall reduces range by roughly 30–40%. Two walls and you are at the edge of usable signal.

**Reliability is the main selling point.** The TL-WR820N runs for months without a restart. There are no firmware update disasters, no random disconnections, no overheating under normal conditions.

**The 100Mbps LAN ports are the biggest limitation.** If your broadband plan gives you 200Mbps or more, this router cannot deliver that over a wired connection. Over WiFi it doesn't matter much at this range, but wired users will feel the cap.

## Tenda F3 — The Alternative

The **Tenda F3** (₹649–₹749) is the cheapest functional router you can buy in India. It offers 300Mbps on 2.4GHz with three fixed antennas — and at roughly ₹100–₹150 less than the TL-WR820N, it is a reasonable alternative.

The tradeoff: Tenda's software and long-term firmware support are less reliable than TP-Link's. The hardware is fine; the software occasionally needs a factory reset to recover from odd states. If you are comfortable with that, it saves you some money.
