The Poco X6 Pro launched with MediaTek's Dimensity 8300 Ultra — a chipset that benchmarks close to Snapdragon 8 Gen 2. At ₹26,999, it's one of the most aggressively spec'd phones in its price range. Here's the full India review.

---

## Performance — The Star of the Show

**MediaTek Dimensity 8300 Ultra, 8GB/12GB LPDDR5X RAM, 256GB UFS 3.1**

The Dimensity 8300 Ultra is the reason to buy the Poco X6 Pro. In Antutu benchmarks, it scores over 1.8 million — matching the Snapdragon 8 Gen 1 and coming close to the 8 Gen 2. This is near-flagship performance at mid-range pricing.

**BGMI/PUBG Mobile:** Ultra HD graphics + Extreme frame rate (90fps) — smooth with minimal thermal throttling. In a 45-minute gaming session, the phone reached 38°C — warm but manageable.

**Genshin Impact:** High settings with 60fps — consistent performance that only a handful of phones at this price can match. Frame drops are rare and brief.

**Daily performance:** Buttery smooth — app switching, multitasking, and 120Hz scroll are all instantaneous. No loading hiccups in everyday use.

**Sustained performance:** After 30 minutes of intensive gaming, Poco's LiquidCool 3.0 thermal system maintains 90% of peak performance. Some competitors throttle to 60% after similar duration.

---

## Display — 1.5K AMOLED Wins at This Price

**6.67" 1.5K AMOLED, 120Hz, 1800 nits peak, Dolby Vision**

The 1.5K resolution (2712 × 1220) is sharper than standard 1080p — noticeable in fine text, video, and gaming details. The AMOLED panel delivers deep blacks and vivid colours, and Dolby Vision support means Netflix HDR content looks genuinely premium.

**1800 nits peak brightness:** Excellent outdoor visibility — the display is readable even in direct sunlight at noon.

**Complaint:** The 120Hz refresh rate is standard, not LTPO — it doesn't adapt to 1Hz like premium phones. Battery impact is higher with 120Hz always active.

---

## Camera — Good, Not Great

**64MP OIS main + 8MP ultrawide + 2MP macro**

The camera setup is the Poco X6 Pro's compromise area. The 64MP main camera produces sharp, well-exposed shots in daylight. OIS helps with handheld stability.

**Daylight photos:** Punchy, saturated colours — POCO's processing style leans Instagram-ready rather than accurate. Most users will prefer this for social media.

**Low-light:** Adequate but not strong — Night Mode helps but produces some processing artefacts. At ₹27K, the Samsung Galaxy A54 and OnePlus Nord CE4 Lite outperform it in low light.

**Video:** 4K 30fps with OIS — acceptable quality. No 4K 60fps.

**Ultrawide:** 8MP ultrawide is disappointing — low detail and colour inconsistency vs main camera.

**Camera verdict:** Good enough for daily social media use. Not the reason to buy this phone — buy it for performance.

---

## Battery and Charging — Strong

**5000mAh, 67W Turbo Charging**

Screen-on-time averages 7–8 hours in mixed use with 120Hz active. Heavy gaming drops this to 5–6 hours.

**67W charging:** 0–100% in 46 minutes — fast enough that a 30-minute lunch break charge adds significant battery. Better than most Samsung competitors at this price.

No wireless charging — consistent with Poco's value-over-premium positioning.

---

## Software — Polarising MIUI

**MIUI 14, Android 14, 2-year OS update promise**

MIUI's India version is heavily customised — comes with pre-installed apps and more aggressive ads than stock Android. The performance is fast, but the MIUI experience divides users.

**Positives:** Game Turbo mode (dedicated gaming performance profile), excellent notification management, customisation depth.

**Negatives:** Ads in MIUI apps (can be disabled), 2-year OS update promise (short vs Samsung's 4 years), more notifications than clean Android.

If you're comfortable with MIUI or have used Redmi/Poco before, this isn't a dealbreaker. If clean Android matters, look at Motorola or Nokia alternatives.

---

## Build Quality

Metal frame with glass back — solid build for the price. 6.67" phone is slightly large for one-hand use. Polished aluminium frame looks premium but becomes a fingerprint magnet. The flat display edge (no curves) makes case fitting easier.

**No IP rating:** No official dust/water resistance — minor but noted. OnePlus Nord CE4 offers IP64 at a similar price.

---

## Poco X6 Pro Verdict

The Poco X6 Pro is the best-performing phone under ₹30,000 for gaming and demanding tasks. The Dimensity 8300 Ultra is genuinely exceptional value.

**Buy it if:** Gaming performance is your top priority, you want the fastest chipset under ₹30K, and you're comfortable with MIUI.

**Avoid if:** Camera quality matters more than performance, you want clean Android, or IP rating and long software support are priorities.

**Price:** ₹26,999 (8GB/256GB) — ₹28,999 (12GB/256GB)
