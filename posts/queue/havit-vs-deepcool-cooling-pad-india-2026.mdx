## Brand Overview: Havit and Deepcool in India

**Havit** is a Chinese peripheral brand that established itself in India through aggressive pricing. Their laptop cooling pads (HV-F2056, HV-F2063, HV-F2068) are the most popular budget cooling pads in the country, consistently appearing in Amazon's bestseller lists under ₹1,000. Havit's strength is value — you get functional hardware at prices that undercut almost every competitor.

**Deepcool** started as a PC component cooling brand — fans, CPU coolers, chassis — before entering the laptop cooling pad market. This background in thermal engineering shows in the N65 and other Deepcool pads: larger, quieter fans, better airflow science, and more durable construction. Deepcool products generally cost 50–100% more than comparable Havit products, with real justification for the premium.

## Head-to-Head: Havit HV-F2056 vs Deepcool N65

### Fan Performance

**Havit HV-F2056:** Three 110mm fans running at approximately 1,000–1,100 RPM. Total airflow is distributed across three points on the laptop base.

**Deepcool N65:** Single 140mm fan running at approximately 750–850 RPM. A larger fan diameter moves more air per rotation, and lower RPM reduces turbulence and noise while maintaining high volume airflow.

In pure cooling performance tests, the Deepcool N65 moves slightly more air than the HV-F2056 despite fewer fans. The single large fan creates more consistent, even airflow across the laptop base. The temperature reduction difference between the two is small (1–2°C), but the Deepcool does it much more quietly.

### Noise Level

This is the category where the gap between Havit and Deepcool is most significant.

**Havit HV-F2056 noise:** Approximately 30–35 dB at full speed. You will hear it clearly in a quiet room. During video calls, it can sometimes be picked up by laptop microphones. At night in a quiet bedroom, it is distracting.

**Deepcool N65 noise:** Approximately 20–24 dB at full speed. This is comparable to white noise — present but not distracting. During video calls, it is inaudible through most laptop microphones.

For a student using headphones in a dormitory, the Havit's noise is a non-issue. For a work-from-home professional on five Zoom calls per day, the Deepcool's silence is genuinely valuable.

### Build Quality

**Havit HV-F2056:** Lightweight plastic frame with some flex when pressed in the centre. The mesh surface is adequate. The USB cable is permanently attached and not braided — this is the most likely failure point over time. Expected lifespan with daily use: 1–2 years before the fans develop noise or the cable connection becomes unreliable.

**Deepcool N65:** Denser plastic frame with minimal flex. The mesh surface is firmer and feels more premium. The height adjustment mechanism locks more securely than the Havit. Fans are quieter because Deepcool uses better-quality bearings. Expected lifespan: 2–4 years with daily use.

### Long-Term Durability

Based on owner reports across Amazon India reviews and tech forums:

**Havit HV-F2056:** Fan rattle typically develops at the 8–14 month mark with daily use. Cable connection loosening at 12–18 months. A common complaint is that one of the three fans stops spinning before the others.

**Deepcool N65:** Fan noise remains stable for 18–30 months under daily use. The larger fan bearing has better longevity than the small fans in budget pads. The most common complaint is yellowing of the white plastic trim, which is cosmetic.

## Which Should You Buy?

**Choose Havit HV-F2056 if:**
- Your budget is under ₹1,000
- You are a student who replaces accessories every couple of years anyway
- You use the laptop in a noisy environment where fan sound does not matter
- You want maximum fan count at minimum price

**Choose Deepcool N65 if:**
- You work from home and noise matters during calls
- You want the cooling pad to last 3+ years
- You are willing to spend ₹700 more for meaningfully better quality
- You prefer a minimalist aesthetic without LED lighting

**Choose Havit HV-F2063 if:**
- You want LED lighting and are happy with the Havit build quality
- Your budget is ₹1,000–₹1,300

The short answer: if you are choosing between these two brands, **Deepcool is worth the extra money** for anyone spending more than four hours a day using their laptop. For occasional use or tight budgets, **Havit delivers more than enough for the price**.
