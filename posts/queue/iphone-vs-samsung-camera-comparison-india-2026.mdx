## Photography

**Samsung wins.** The S25 Ultra's 200MP main sensor and 50MP telephoto deliver more resolution and zoom versatility than iPhone 16 Pro. Zoom photos from 2x–5x are sharper on Samsung. Wide-angle shots show more detail at pixel level.

**iPhone advantage:** Apple's colour science is less saturated and more consistent with what your eye sees — Samsung's photos are vivid and punchy, which many users prefer, but iPhone's processing is more accurate. Portrait mode subject separation is better on iPhone.

---

## Video

**iPhone wins.** iPhone's Cinematic Mode, Log video recording (for professional colour grading), and Action Mode stabilisation are class-leading. 4K/120fps ProRes video on iPhone 16 Pro is the industry benchmark for smartphone videography.

Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra supports 8K video, but the storage requirements and processing demands make it impractical for daily use. At 4K/60fps, Samsung is excellent but behind iPhone in stabilisation quality.

---

## Indian-Specific Considerations

**Warm, high-contrast Indian daylight.** Samsung's vivid colour processing handles India's bright daylight well — punchy colours on outdoor shots look social-media ready without editing. iPhone's more neutral processing sometimes requires a slight saturation boost for the same visual impact.

**Wedding and event photography.** Samsung S25 Ultra's versatility — multiple focal lengths, 200MP main, high-resolution zoom — makes it more versatile for Indian weddings where you can't always move close to subjects.
