## When Is a UV Purifier Enough for Your Home?

UV purification kills bacteria, viruses, cysts, and other microorganisms by exposing water to ultraviolet light at 254 nm wavelength. It is extremely effective at biological decontamination but has one critical limitation: **it does not remove dissolved solids, chemicals, or heavy metals**.

UV is sufficient if:
- Your water comes from a municipal supply (Delhi Jal Board, BWSSB, etc.)
- TDS is below 250–300 ppm
- No detectable heavy metals or industrial contamination
- Municipal pre-treatment is reasonably reliable

UV is NOT sufficient if:
- Your water comes from a borewell or tanker
- TDS is above 300 ppm
- Water has a distinct taste, smell, or hardness
- You live in an area with known industrial or agricultural contamination

A quick TDS meter test (under ₹200 on Amazon) will settle this question for you immediately.

## UV vs RO vs UF — Quick Comparison

| Feature | UV | RO | UF |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kills bacteria | Yes | Yes (with UV stage) | Yes |
| Kills viruses | Yes | Yes (with UV stage) | No |
| Removes dissolved salts | No | Yes | No |
| Removes heavy metals | No | Yes | No |
| Removes TDS | No | Yes | No |
| Electricity needed | Yes | Yes | No |
| Water wastage | None | 60–75% waste | None |
| Best for | Municipal water | All water types | Pre-filter |

## How Long Does a UV Lamp Last?

A UV lamp in a water purifier typically lasts:
- **12 months** for continuous 24/7 operation
- **3 years** for typical home use (3–4 hours per day)
- Eureka Forbes UV lamps: rated to 4,000 hours

Most UV purifiers have an indicator that tells you when the lamp is nearing the end of its effective life. Replacement UV lamps cost ₹600–₹1,200 depending on brand.
