## Buying Guide: Gaming Mice in 2026

**What actually matters:**
1. **Sensor accuracy:** Zero acceleration/jitter — Logitech and Razer sensors set the standard
2. **Polling rate:** 1000Hz = position reported 1000 times/second — all gaming mice should have this
3. **Weight:** Lighter = less fatigue, faster aiming — but personal preference varies
4. **Grip style match:** Palm (large mouse), claw (medium), fingertip (small or light mouse)

**DPI — what it actually means:**
- 400–800 DPI: FPS competitive settings (low sensitivity for precise aim control)
- 1200–1600 DPI: General gaming and productivity
- 3200+ DPI: Fast movement in MOBAs or for large 4K displays
- "16,000 DPI" on cheap mice doesn't mean better — sensor quality matters more than DPI numbers

**Wired vs wireless for gaming:**
- Modern 2.4GHz wireless has identical latency to wired — no performance difference
- Wireless mice (Logitech G305 at ₹2,800+) are now tournament-legal
- Under ₹2,000, wired is more reliable value
