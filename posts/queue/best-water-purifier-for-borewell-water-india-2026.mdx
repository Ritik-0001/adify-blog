## Why Borewell Water Is Different From Municipal Water

Borewell water (groundwater) varies enormously across India. Here is what you might find:

- **TDS (Total Dissolved Solids):** 500–3000+ ppm vs. municipal water which is typically 100–400 ppm
- **Hardness (Calcium/Magnesium):** Very high in most of central and peninsular India
- **Fluoride:** High in Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and parts of UP
- **Iron:** High in West Bengal, Bihar, Jharkhand, and Assam
- **Arsenic:** Present in parts of West Bengal, Bihar, Assam, and Uttar Pradesh
- **Nitrates:** High in agricultural regions across India
- **Bacteria and Cysts:** Often high due to proximity to agricultural or sewage sources

This is why a simple UV purifier or gravity filter is completely inadequate for borewell water. Only RO can remove dissolved salts, heavy metals, and reduce TDS to safe levels.

## How to Know Your Borewell Water's TDS

Before buying a purifier, test your water. A digital TDS meter costs under ₹200 and gives you an instant reading.

- 500–800 ppm: Standard RO handles this well (Kent Pearl, Livpure GLO)
- 800–1500 ppm: Need higher-capacity RO (Kent Grand Plus, Aquaguard Marvel)
- Above 1500 ppm: Need specialised high-TDS RO (AO Smith X5 Plus, Kent Superstar)

## What About Fluoride Removal?

Standard RO membranes do remove fluoride — typically reducing it by 85–95%. However, if your borewell water has very high fluoride (above 3 ppm, which is common in Rajasthan and AP), you may want to add an activated alumina filter as an additional pre-treatment stage. Ask your service technician to check this when installing.

## Do You Need a Separate Pre-Filter for Borewell Water?

Highly recommended. Borewell water with high sediment or iron content can clog RO membranes prematurely, reducing their life from 2–3 years to under a year. A separate sediment pre-filter (5 micron) before the purifier costs just ₹200–₹500 and can triple membrane life.

Most RO purifiers include basic pre-filtration, but for particularly turbid or iron-heavy borewell water, an additional external pre-filter is worth installing.

## Water Wastage in Borewell RO Purifiers

Borewell water typically has higher TDS, which means the RO membrane has to work harder — resulting in slightly more water wastage than with low-TDS municipal water. Expect:

- Standard RO (Kent Pearl, Livpure GLO): 3–4 litres waste per 1 litre purified
- Advanced RO (AO Smith Side Stream): ~2 litres waste per 1 litre purified
- High-TDS input: May increase wastage further

Connect the reject water outlet to a bucket and use this water for mopping, gardening, or flushing — do not waste it.
