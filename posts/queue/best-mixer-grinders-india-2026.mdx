## Buying Guide: Mixer Grinders for Indian Kitchens 2026

**Wattage guide:**
- 500W: Light use — chutneys, smoothies, spice grinding (not for idli batter)
- 750W: Standard — handles all everyday Indian grinding (most households)
- 900–1000W: Heavy-duty — idli/dosa batter, large batches, continuous use

**Number of jars:**
- 3 jars: Liquidising (1.5L) + dry grinding (0.75L) + chutney (0.4L) — sufficient for most households
- 4 jars: Adds wet grinding jar or juicer jar — worth it if you juice regularly or make large batter batches

**Stainless steel vs polycarbonate jars:**
Always choose stainless steel interior jars — polycarbonate (plastic) jars leach chemicals when grinding hot ingredients and stain permanently from turmeric.

**Jar leak-proofing:** Check reviews specifically for jar leakage — the #1 complaint for Indian mixer grinders. Preethi and Sujata have the best leak-proof seals.

**Wet grinding:** If you regularly make idli/dosa batter, choose 750W+ models. Thin motors burn out grinding soaked rice and lentils — the most demanding task for a mixer grinder.
