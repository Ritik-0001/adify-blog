## Buying Guide

**AI brushing coaching — is it worth paying extra for?** The Oral-B iO Series 3's tooth-by-tooth brushing map is the most practically useful AI coaching feature in this price range. Clinical data from Oral-B's studies shows that users who use the app coaching miss fewer tooth surfaces over time — the back inner surfaces of lower molars and upper premolars are the most commonly missed areas by Indian brushers. If you've been told by a dentist that you have tartar build-up in consistent spots, app coaching is worth the premium.

**iO Series vs Pro Series — what changes?** The iO magnetic drive motor delivers a combination of oscillating-rotating and linear vibration that Oral-B calls "iO motion." It's noticeably quieter, produces less vibration in the jaw, and is generally better tolerated by first-time electric toothbrush users. The Pro series oscillates more aggressively, which some users — and some dentists — prefer for plaque removal intensity. Both technologies are clinically effective.

**Sonicare 5100 vs Oclean X Pro Elite — the 62,000 vs 42,000 question:** Frequency alone doesn't determine cleaning effectiveness — the brush head design, bristle configuration, and how the sonic waves are directed matter equally. In head-to-head tests, the Sonicare 5100's higher frequency doesn't translate to a dramatically different clean for most users. However, for patients with calculus prone to forming in the gum sulcus, the 5100's higher fluid disturbance is clinically relevant.
