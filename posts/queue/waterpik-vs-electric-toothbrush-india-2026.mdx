Waterpik water flossers and electric toothbrushes are not competitors — they clean different parts of your mouth. Understanding what each does (and cannot do) helps you build the most effective oral care routine for your budget.

---

## What Each Device Actually Cleans

**Electric toothbrush cleans:**
- Tooth surfaces (front, back, biting surface)
- Gumline (2mm below visible gumline)
- Between teeth (partially — fluid dynamics help but bristles can't physically enter tight gaps)

**Waterpik cleans:**
- Between teeth (interdental spaces — 99.9% better than brushing alone)
- Below the gumline (periodontal pockets up to 6mm deep)
- Around dental implants, crowns, bridges (areas where floss can't reach)
- Orthodontic appliances (under wires, around brackets)

**The critical insight:** No toothbrush — electric or manual — can physically enter the spaces between your teeth or into periodontal pockets deeper than 2mm. This is where gum disease bacteria live. A Waterpik's pressurised stream reaches 6mm into pockets.

## Who Needs a Waterpik

**Strongly recommended:**
- Gum disease patients (gingivitis or periodontitis) — the periodontal pocket tip is clinically proven for pocket depth reduction
- Implant patients — floss can snag on implant components; water flosser cannot
- Bridge patients — water flossing is the only effective way to clean under bridge pontics
- Brace wearers — water cleans under wires effortlessly; string floss requires threading through each gap
- Diabetics — Indian diabetics have significantly higher gum disease prevalence; intensive interdental cleaning is recommended

**Beneficial but not critical:**
- Regular users who want maximum oral health
- Anyone who consistently skips flossing (most Indians) — water flossing has much higher compliance than string floss

**Not necessary if:**
- You have perfect gum health, no interdental issues, and floss consistently
- Budget is a constraint — prioritise an electric toothbrush first

## Budget Priority Order

If you can only afford one device:

**1. Electric toothbrush first** (most people's starting point) — addresses the largest gap vs manual brushing for tooth surface cleaning.

**2. Waterpik second** (once you have a good electric toothbrush) — addresses interdental and subgingival cleaning that your electric brush cannot.

**Never:** Skip the toothbrush to buy a Waterpik instead. Water flossers do not clean tooth surfaces — they are interdental tools, not primary cleaning tools.
