## Understanding Scanner Types — Flatbed vs ADF

The Epson L3252, Canon PIXMA G3020, and most consumer MFPs under ₹15,000 use **flatbed scanners** — you open the lid, place the document face-down, close the lid, and scan.

**Flatbed scanner advantages:**
- Better scan quality (document held flat on glass)
- Can scan books, magazines, photos without damaging binding
- Works with any document format — receipts, thick cards, oddly shaped items

**Flatbed scanner limitations:**
- Manual page-by-page for multi-page documents
- Scanning a 20-page document means 20 lid opens and closes

**ADF (Automatic Document Feeder) scanner:**
- Load a stack of pages in the feeder, press scan — done
- Scans 20–50 pages automatically, one after another
- Much faster for multi-page documents
- Standard in office-grade MFPs

**For Indian home users:** Flatbed is perfectly adequate. You rarely need to scan 20+ pages at once at home. For office use or anyone who regularly digitises large stacks of documents, ADF is worth the premium.
