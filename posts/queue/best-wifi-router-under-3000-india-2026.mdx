## AC1200 vs WiFi 6 Entry-Level — The Real Debate at This Price

The most interesting question in the under-₹3,000 bracket right now is whether to buy AC1200 (the Archer C6) or stretch slightly for an entry-level WiFi 6 router like the Xiaomi AX3200.

**What is AC1200?**
AC stands for 802.11ac (WiFi 5). AC1200 means 300Mbps on 2.4GHz + 867Mbps on 5GHz = 1,200Mbps total theoretical bandwidth. In practice, real-world speeds on 5GHz peak at 400–500Mbps depending on device and distance.

**What is WiFi 6 (AX)?**
WiFi 6 (802.11ax) brings OFDMA (handles more simultaneous devices), better performance in congested environments (dense apartments), and theoretically higher speeds. The real benefit is not raw speed — it is **efficiency with many devices**.

**The honest verdict for under ₹3,000:**

**Our recommendation:** If most of your devices are smartphones, laptops, and smart TVs bought in 2021 or later, they likely support WiFi 6. In that case, the Xiaomi AX3200 at the top of this budget is a worthwhile investment. If your devices are a mix of older and newer, or if you want the most proven reliability, the Archer C6 is the safer pick.

## Xiaomi AX3200 — The WiFi 6 Option

The **Xiaomi AX3200** (₹2,999–₹3,499) brings WiFi 6 to this price segment. It offers 574Mbps on 2.4GHz and 2,402Mbps on 5GHz (theoretical), OFDMA for better multi-device performance, and four antennas.

In a household with 10+ WiFi 6-capable devices, the AX3200 handles simultaneous connections more gracefully than the Archer C6. The difference is subtle with 5–8 devices but becomes noticeable above that.

The downside: Xiaomi's long-term firmware support for routers in India has been inconsistent. TP-Link's track record is better.
