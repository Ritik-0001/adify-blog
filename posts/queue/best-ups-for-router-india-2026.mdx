## Why Your Router Needs a UPS

Most WiFi routers and fibre ONTs draw between 10W and 20W of power. A standard 600VA UPS has an effective output capacity of about 360W — meaning it can run your 12W router for 2–4 hours on a single battery charge, depending on battery condition. This is a game-changer for work-from-home professionals and students who depend on internet connectivity during India's frequent power cuts.

Unlike a full home inverter, a router UPS is compact, silent, and costs under ₹3,500. It can sit beside your router and switch to battery power within milliseconds — so fast that your WiFi connection never drops. Many users in cities like Patna, Lucknow, Bhubaneswar, and Nagpur report 3–5 power cuts per day during summer; a router UPS makes all of them invisible.
