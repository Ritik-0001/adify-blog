## The Core Difference: What Each Brand Prioritises

Understanding why boAt and Noise sound different starts with their product philosophy.

**boAt** was founded by audio enthusiasts who wanted to bring powerful, bass-heavy sound to mainstream Indian consumers at accessible prices. Their tuning philosophy has always been: more bass, high energy, exciting presentation. When you put on a boAt earphone, you feel the music. It suits India's most popular genres — Bollywood, EDM, hip-hop, and pop.

**Noise** (formerly known as GoNoise) entered the market with a sharper focus on smart features: noise cancellation for calls, environmental noise cancellation (ENC) in microphones, and companion app integration. Their sound tuning is more neutral and balanced, prioritising clarity over punch. This makes Noise earphones better suited for calls, podcasts, and spoken-word content.

Neither approach is wrong — they simply serve different users.

## Head-to-Head: boAt Airdopes 131 vs Noise Buds VS104

These are among the most popular models from each brand in the sub-₹1,000 segment.

**Sound Quality:**
The boAt Airdopes 131 wins convincingly on music listening. The bass extension is deeper, the low-mids are more present, and the overall sound feels more exciting. Playing a bass-heavy track like a Badshah song or an EDM playlist on the Airdopes 131 versus the Noise Buds VS104 is a clear comparison — the boAt sounds like it costs more.

The Noise Buds VS104, however, has cleaner mids and a more accurate vocal reproduction. For podcasts, audiobooks, and voice notes, it is the better option.

**Call Quality:**
Noise wins here, and it is not close. The Noise Buds VS104 features dual-microphone ENC (Environmental Noise Cancellation), which actively filters background noise from your outgoing voice. On a call in a café or on a noisy street, the Noise significantly outperforms the boAt. Callers report hearing you more clearly on the VS104.

The boAt Airdopes 131 has a single microphone that does the job in quiet environments but struggles with background noise.

**Battery Life:**
Both offer around 20 hours total with the charging case. The Noise VS104 edges slightly ahead on per-charge earbud life (5 hours vs 4.5 hours for boAt).

**Water Resistance:**
Noise VS104 has IPX5, boAt Airdopes 131 has IPX4. The Noise is marginally more suited for gym use and light rain.
