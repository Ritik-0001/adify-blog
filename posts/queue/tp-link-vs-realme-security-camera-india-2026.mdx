## Brand Overview

**TP-Link Tapo** is a dedicated security camera sub-brand from TP-Link, the world's largest consumer networking company. The Tapo ecosystem has been active since 2019 and includes a wide range of indoor, outdoor, wired, and battery-powered cameras. The Tapo app is available globally, regularly updated, and has a large user base that drives fast bug fixes and feature additions.

**Realme** entered smart home products as an extension of its smartphone brand. The Realme Smart Cam range is smaller, focused on indoor pan/tilt cameras, and tied to the Realme Link app ecosystem. Realme cameras are competitively priced and well-integrated with Realme smartphones and other Realme smart devices.

## Image Quality Comparison

**TP-Link Tapo C210** delivers 3MP 2K resolution (2304 x 1296 pixels) — sharper than the Realme Smart Cam 360°'s 1080p (1920 x 1080 pixels). In side-by-side testing, the Tapo C210 captures more legible text on packages, clearer facial details, and better colour reproduction in mixed indoor lighting.

**Realme Smart Cam 360°** produces solid 1080p footage that looks good on a smartphone screen. For basic monitoring — checking that your front door is secure or seeing who is in the living room — the resolution is adequate. It is only when zooming into footage or reviewing clips on a large screen that the 1080p limitation becomes noticeable.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — 2K resolution is a clear advantage for identification.

---

## App and Software Comparison

The **Tapo app** is polished, fast, and offers a timeline-based clip viewer that makes reviewing past footage intuitive. Live view loads in under three seconds on a typical 4G connection. The app supports multiple cameras on one screen, sharing access with family members, and custom motion zones for focused detection.

The **Realme Link app** integrates the Realme Smart Cam with other Realme devices but feels more limited as a standalone camera app. The timeline interface is less intuitive, notification customisation is minimal, and the app lacks the depth of features found in Tapo. For Realme smartphone users the integration is convenient, but the camera management features lag behind Tapo's offering.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — the Tapo app is more mature and feature-rich.

---

## Detection and Alerts

**TP-Link Tapo C210** uses AI-based person detection that works reliably in Indian home environments. The detection zone editor lets you draw specific areas to monitor — useful for focusing on a doorway or window rather than the entire room. False alerts from fans, curtains, and light changes are minimal.

**Realme Smart Cam 360°** also offers AI person detection but is prone to slightly more false alerts from moving shadows and light changes. The detection sensitivity cannot be fine-tuned as precisely as on the Tapo app, leading to more notifications that require manual dismissal throughout the day.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — more precise detection zones, fewer false alerts.

---

## Storage and Privacy

Both cameras support local microSD storage. The Tapo C210 supports up to 512 GB cards while the Realme Smart Cam supports up to 64 GB. Neither camera requires a paid cloud subscription for basic functionality, which is a major positive for Indian buyers who are wary of recurring monthly costs.

On privacy, both cameras store footage locally on-device. TP-Link Tapo is a global brand with transparent privacy policies. Realme is an Indian-registered company (though Oppo/BBK-linked), which may be a consideration for some buyers who prefer keeping footage within familiar brand ecosystems.

**Winner: TP-Link Tapo** — larger microSD support and a clearer global privacy track record.
