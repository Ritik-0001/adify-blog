## What Can You Actually Expect from a Power Bank Under ₹500?

Let's be honest upfront: at this price, you are getting the essentials — nothing more.

**What you get:**
- 10,000mAh capacity (enough for 2–2.5 phone charges for most Indian smartphones)
- Dual USB-A output ports (no USB-C output on most models)
- 5W–10W standard charging (no fast charging)
- Basic overcharge, short-circuit, and temperature protection
- BIS certification (on reputable brands — always check)

**What you do NOT get:**
- Fast charging (no Quick Charge, no USB Power Delivery)
- USB-C output
- Premium build quality (expect plastic, some flex)
- Long cable included

If you need fast charging, you will need to spend at least ₹800–₹900. For under ₹500, standard 5W charging is the norm.

## Why BIS Certification Matters More Than Anything Else

BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) certification under IS 16046 is mandatory for power banks sold in India. It means the unit has been tested for electrical safety — overcharge protection, short circuit protection, and cell quality.

No-brand power banks flooding marketplaces at ₹299–₹399 often skip BIS certification. These units can:
- Use recycled or low-quality cells (real capacity may be 3,000–4,000mAh despite claiming 10,000mAh)
- Overheat during charging
- Damage your phone's battery over time

Both Ambrane and Syska are Indian brands with BIS certification and service centre networks. That is why they are recommended over cheaper unknown alternatives.

**How to check:** Look for the BIS mark on the product page or packaging. Reputable sellers on Amazon will list it in specifications.

## The Fake Capacity Problem — How Bad Is It?

This is a serious issue in the under-₹500 segment. Unbranded power banks sold as "10000mAh" or even "20000mAh" on marketplace listings often contain cells with 2,000–5,000mAh of actual usable capacity.

How do they get away with it? Cheap cells lose capacity fast, the casing looks identical to genuine units, and most buyers never measure actual output.

Signs of a fake-capacity power bank:
- No brand name or a made-up brand you cannot find anywhere else
- Price below ₹350 for a claimed 10,000mAh
- No BIS mark listed
- Seller has no reviews or reviews are clearly faked

Stick to Ambrane, Syska, or boAt at this price range — all are Indian brands with accountability.

## How Many Charges Will You Get?

Assuming a typical Indian smartphone with a 4,500mAh battery:

Why not 2.2x (10000 ÷ 4500)? Because power banks lose 15–20% energy to heat and voltage conversion during transfer. This is normal and affects every power bank — cheap or expensive.

For phones with smaller batteries (4,000mAh), you get closer to 2 full charges. For phones with 5,000mAh batteries, expect 1.5 full charges.
