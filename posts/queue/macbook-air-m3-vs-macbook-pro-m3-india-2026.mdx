## The Key Differences

### 1. Thermal Performance (The Real Differentiator)
MacBook Air M3 is **fanless** — it throttles silently under sustained heavy loads (video export, compilation). MacBook Pro M3 has **active cooling (fan)** — it sustains peak performance indefinitely. If you regularly run 30+ minute exports, renders, or heavy compilations, the Pro maintains higher clock speeds throughout.

### 2. Display — Liquid Retina XDR vs Liquid Retina
Pro's Liquid Retina XDR hits 1000 nits sustained (vs 500 nit Air) — meaningful for outdoor work in Indian sunlight and HDR colour grading. ProMotion 120Hz makes scrolling and animations smoother. If display quality is critical (professional design/video work), the Pro's display is noticeably better.

### 3. Ports
MacBook Pro has HDMI, SD card reader, and an extra Thunderbolt port. MacBook Air users need a hub (₹2,000–₹5,000 extra). In India's office environments, HDMI matters for presentations.

### 4. M3 Pro Chip (₹2,29,900 model)
The M3 Pro variant of MacBook Pro (18GB unified memory, 14-core GPU) is in a different class. For professional video editors, ML engineers, and architects — M3 Pro's extra cores and 18GB RAM make a genuine difference.
