## Why Mesh Matters More in India

Indian home offices and smaller commercial offices frequently reach 28–35°C during summer months, especially in cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru. Standard padded office chairs trap heat between your back and the seat covering — which means increased discomfort, sweating, and fatigue during extended sitting. Mesh chairs solve this by allowing continuous airflow through the backrest. For most Indian buyers, a mesh back chair is the practical choice, not just the premium one.

## How to Evaluate Mesh Chair Quality

Not all mesh is created equal. When evaluating a mesh chair, consider:

**Tension mounting vs padded backing.** Good mesh is mounted under tension across a frame so it provides responsive support. Low-quality mesh is simply a breathable cover over foam padding — the mesh is doing nothing ergonomically.

**Weave density.** Tighter weaves provide more support but less airflow. Looser weaves provide more airflow but less support. The best chairs (like the Sihoo M90D) vary density across zones.

**Longevity.** Cheap mesh stretches out and sags within 12–18 months of daily use. Quality mesh maintains its tension for 3–5 years. Ask specifically about warranty and check user reviews for comments on mesh condition after extended use.

**Frame quality.** The mesh is only as good as the frame that holds it. Plastic frames flex and distort under load, reducing mesh tension over time. Look for reinforced nylon or metal frame construction.
