A portable monitor can double your screen real estate anywhere — hotel rooms, cafes, client offices. The ASUS ZenScreen and LG Gram +View are two of the best options for users who want a quality second display that fits in a laptop bag.

---

## Overview

The **ASUS ZenScreen MB16AHT** is a 15.6-inch Full HD IPS portable monitor with touch support and USB-C connectivity. The **LG Gram +View 16MQ70** is LG's 16-inch WQXGA portable monitor — higher resolution, wider colour gamut, and slightly heavier but dramatically sharper.

---

| Feature | ASUS ZenScreen MB16AHT | LG Gram +View 16MQ70 |
|---|---|---|
| Size | 15.6" | 16" |
| Resolution | 1080p (FHD) | 2560×1600 (WQXGA) |
| Panel | IPS | IPS |

---

## Display Quality

The LG Gram +View's 2560×1600 resolution at 16 inches is noticeably sharper than the ZenScreen's 1080p — particularly for text, code, and detailed design work. At typical viewing distances for a secondary laptop monitor, the pixel density difference is clear.

The LG also covers 99% sRGB and DCI-P3 — suitable for colour-accurate creative work. The ASUS ZenScreen covers 72% NTSC, which is adequate for productivity but not for colour-sensitive design.

---

## Touch Support

The ASUS ZenScreen's touch screen is its differentiating feature. For annotation, sketching rough ideas, signing documents, or navigating with a stylus or finger, the touch capability is genuinely useful. The LG offers no touch.

If your workflow involves touch interaction (designers, educators, presenters), the ZenScreen's touch screen is a meaningful advantage despite the lower resolution.

---

## Portability

Both fold into slim cases. The ZenScreen at 0.78 kg is lighter — meaningful when you're also carrying a laptop, charger, and accessories. The LG at 0.98 kg is still carry-in-bag portable, but the 200g difference over a long travel day is noticeable.

---

## Pros & Cons

### ASUS ZenScreen MB16AHT
**Pros:** Touch screen, lighter weight, lower price (₹6,000 less), good enough for productivity, two USB-C ports
**Cons:** 1080p resolution shows its limits for code and design, 72% NTSC limits colour work

### LG Gram +View 16MQ70
**Pros:** Stunning WQXGA resolution, 99% sRGB for colour work, larger screen, excellent panel quality
**Cons:** More expensive, no touch, slightly heavier, no built-in kickstand (requires LG compatible case/stand)

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the ASUS ZenScreen if:**
- Touch interaction matters (annotation, signing, sketching)
- Budget is tighter
- You travel light and weight matters
- Productivity and video are primary use cases

**Buy the LG Gram +View if:**
- You need sharp text for code or writing
- You do colour-accurate design work on the go
- Resolution quality justifies the premium
- You pair it with an LG Gram laptop

---

## Final Verdict

For developers and writers: **LG Gram +View** — the resolution jump from 1080p to WQXGA makes a visible difference for code readability and text sharpness. Worth the ₹6,000 premium.

For presenters, educators, and general productivity: **ASUS ZenScreen** — touch support and lower price make it the better all-purpose portable display. The 1080p resolution is entirely adequate for presentations, spreadsheets, and video.
