## What to Expect From an AC Under ₹35,000

Setting honest expectations is important before spending money on a budget AC.

**What you get:**
- Inverter compressor technology (energy-saving compared to fixed speed)
- 3-star or occasionally 3.5-star ISEER rating
- R-32 refrigerant (environment-friendly, good efficiency)
- Reliable cooling for standard Indian room sizes (up to 150 sq ft for 1.5T)
- Standard 1-year comprehensive warranty + 5-year compressor warranty from reputable brands

**What you give up compared to 5-star models:**
- Higher electricity bills — roughly ₹3,500–₹5,000 more per year at 8 hours daily use
- No Wi-Fi or app control (in most sub-₹35K models)
- Less advanced features (no auto-clean, no air purification in most models)
- Slightly louder operation in some models
- Less sophisticated compressor technology (though still inverter-based)

If your AC runs 6+ hours daily, the 5-star premium pays for itself in 2–3 years. If it is a light-use AC (guest room, home office used 3–4 hours/day), buying under ₹35,000 makes strong financial sense.

## The Electricity Cost Reality — What You Sacrifice at Under ₹35K

This is the honest conversation that budget AC buyers need to have.

Running a 3-star 1.5T inverter AC for 8 hours daily at ₹8/unit in Indian summer (5 months):

- 3-star AC (ISEER 3.4–3.5): Approximately ₹8,000–₹9,000 per season
- 5-star AC (ISEER 4.7+): Approximately ₹5,500–₹6,500 per season

**Annual savings from a 5-star over 3-star:** ₹2,500–₹3,000 per year.

If a 5-star AC costs ₹42,000 vs a 3-star at ₹30,000, the difference is ₹12,000. At ₹3,000 savings per year, break-even is 4 years. After 4 years, the 5-star saves money every year.

**Recommendation:** If you plan to use the AC intensively for 8+ hours daily, stretch your budget to ₹42,000–₹45,000 and buy a 5-star. If it is a guest room or light-use scenario, sub-₹35,000 is perfectly reasonable.
