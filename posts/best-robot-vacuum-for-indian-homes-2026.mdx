## Overview

Indian homes are not like European or American apartments, and a robot vacuum built for plush carpet and sealed double-glazed rooms will struggle here. If you live in India — whether a South Indian apartment with granite floors, a Delhi flat with constant outdoor dust, or a Mumbai building with open corridors — your robot vacuum faces a unique set of challenges every single day.

Dust ingress is the number one issue. Open windows, balcony doors, corridor dust, and high vehicular pollution levels mean floors in Indian cities get dirty two to three times faster than homes in low-pollution countries. A robot vacuum with a small dustbin or weak suction will simply fill up and stop cleaning halfway through a session.

Marble and granite are the dominant flooring materials in Indian homes, and they need a different approach than carpet. Rubber roller brushes scratch less than bristle brushes. A mop system that leaves no streaks is almost mandatory — because on shiny marble, every smear shows.

Many Indian homes also have open-plan ground floors, servant quarters to account for, multi-level access, or balcony thresholds that robots struggle to navigate. The right robot vacuum handles virtual no-go zones, room segmentation, and reliable mapping so you stay in control.

## Buying Guide

**Suction Power for Indian Dust:** Aim for at least 3,000 Pa for a small flat, 5,000+ Pa for larger homes or high dust areas near main roads.

**Marble and Granite Floors:** Rubber roller brushes are essential — bristle brushes scratch polished stone. Confirm the robot uses a rubber or silicone roller before buying.

**Mop System:** A sonic or rotating mop does far more than a drag cloth on shiny floors. If mopping matters to you, invest ₹30,000+ to get a true scrubbing mop.

**Dustbin Size:** Small bins (under 300ml) will fill up in a single session in dusty Indian homes. Prefer 400ml+ or choose a self-empty model.

**App and Smart Home:** Mi Home supports Hindi. Roborock, Dreame, and Ecovacs apps all work with Alexa and Google Home India. Check region availability before purchasing grey-market units.

**Open Doors and Dust Ingress:** Use virtual no-go zones in the app to block balcony thresholds or areas near open windows. Most LiDAR-based robots support this.

## FAQ

**Q: Do robot vacuums work on marble floors?**  
A: Yes, and they work very well — marble is smooth and robots glide easily across it. Use a rubber roller brush model to avoid micro-scratches. A sonic or rotating mop will leave marble looking polished rather than streaky.

**Q: Will a robot vacuum handle Indian outdoor dust effectively?**  
A: A good robot with 4,000+ Pa suction and a daily schedule will prevent dust from accumulating. It won't replace a deep clean but significantly reduces effort. Empty the dustbin frequently in high-dust areas.

**Q: Can I use a robot vacuum in a home with open balcony doors?**  
A: Yes. Set virtual no-go zones around balcony thresholds to prevent the robot from falling off. The robot will still clean the rest of the floor automatically.

**Q: Which robot vacuum works with Alexa in India?**  
A: Roborock, Dreame, Ecovacs, and Mi all support Alexa and Google Home India. Check that the specific model's skill is enabled for the IN region.
