## Study Chair Buying Guide for Indian Students

**Match the chair to study duration.** 1–3 hours daily: any decent chair under ₹3,000 will do. 4–6 hours daily: invest in a mesh chair with lumbar support (₹3,500–₹5,000). 7–10 hours daily (competitive exam prep): get a proper ergonomic chair with adjustable lumbar and 4D armrests (₹7,000–₹10,000).

**Seat height should match your desk.** Most Indian study tables are between 73–76 cm high. For students of average Indian height (160–175 cm), a seat height of 43–50 cm places the elbows at the right angle. Check the chair's height range before buying.

**Mesh is better for Indian study environments.** Students often study without air conditioning, especially in smaller towns and cities. A mesh chair reduces the sweat and discomfort that accumulates during long sessions in warm rooms.

**Avoid chairs with no lumbar support.** Many cheap "student chairs" in India are essentially padded dining chairs with wheels. These provide no lower back support and encourage the rounded-back posture that causes problems later. Always choose a chair with at least a basic lumbar curve.

**Castors for hard floors.** Indian homes typically have marble, granite, or ceramic tile floors. Standard castors can scratch these surfaces. Look for chairs with nylon or rubber hard-floor castors, or purchase a chair mat.
