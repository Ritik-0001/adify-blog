## Exfoliation for Indian Skin Concerns

**Sun tan:** Indian outdoor exposure tans skin quickly. Regular exfoliation (2–3 times weekly) accelerates tan fade by removing dead tanned cells, revealing fresher skin underneath.

**Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH):** Indian skin (Fitzpatrick III–V) develops dark spots after any skin irritation — acne, cuts, insect bites. Gentle regular exfoliation prevents buildup of dark cells over PIH marks.

**Hard water dullness:** Indian water mineral deposits leave a filmy residue on skin after washing. Regular exfoliation removes this film, restoring natural skin radiance.

**Caution:** Indian skin is more prone to over-exfoliation damage than lighter skin types. Use electric exfoliating brushes on gentle setting, maximum 3 times per week. Avoid sun exposure for 24 hours post-exfoliation.
