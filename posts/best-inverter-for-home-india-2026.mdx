## How to Choose the Right VA Rating for Your Home

Before picking an inverter, calculate your load. A typical 1BHK with 3–4 fans, 4–6 LED lights, one TV, and a router draws roughly 300–500W. A 900VA inverter (about 720W effective power at 0.8 power factor) comfortably handles this. A 2BHK with a refrigerator, more lights, and a ceiling fan in every room needs at least 1100VA–1500VA. Rural homes with long power cuts (6 or more hours) in states like UP, Bihar, Jharkhand, or Odisha should prioritise high-capacity batteries over just the inverter VA rating — the battery determines how long you last, the inverter determines how much load you can run.

Pure sine wave inverters are essential for sensitive electronics like DSLR chargers, medical equipment, and modern LED TVs with SMPS circuits. Modified sine wave inverters are cheaper but can cause humming in fans and damage some electronics over time.
