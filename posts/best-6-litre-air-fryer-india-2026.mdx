## Why the 6L Class Matters for Indian Families

The jump from 4L to 6L is the most impactful capacity upgrade in the air fryer range. Here is the practical difference:

**4L air fryer:** Handles snacks for a family of 4 in one batch. Cooking a main course chicken for 4 requires 2 batches (30–45 minutes total cooking time).

**6L air fryer:** Handles main course chicken for a family of 4 in a single batch. Full fish, a large paneer tikka tray, or a generous portion of chicken thighs — one batch, 25 minutes.

For Indian households where the air fryer is used for main course cooking rather than just snacks, the 6L class makes the appliance a genuinely daily-use tool rather than a snack machine.

## The Square Basket Advantage: Why 5.8L Beats 6L

This sounds counterintuitive. The Cosori Pro II has a 5.8L basket — smaller than the 6L basket on models like the Agaro Marvel. Yet in real-world food capacity, the Cosori holds more.

Here is why: basket volume in litres includes the full 3D volume — but the area available for food (the base area where food sits) is what matters for cooking. A round basket's area is π × r². A square basket's area is l × w.

For similar volumes:
- 6L round basket (diameter ≈ 24cm): base area ≈ 452cm²
- 5.8L square basket (22cm × 22cm): base area = 484cm²

The Cosori's square basket has 7% more flat cooking surface than a typical 6L round basket. Food in the corners of the square basket cooks just as well as food in the centre — the TurboStar-style airflow in Cosori's design prevents corner cold spots.

Practically: you can fit one extra chicken drumstick, or lay out pakoras in a single layer without stacking, or fit a slightly larger fish in the Cosori's square basket compared to most round 6L competitors.
