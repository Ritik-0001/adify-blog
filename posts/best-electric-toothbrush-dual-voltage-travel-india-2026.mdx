Indians travelling internationally face a unique combination of challenges: India uses Type D/M plugs at 230V, while destinations range from 110V USA to 220V Europe to 100V Japan. A non-dual-voltage electric toothbrush charger will fry at an overseas outlet. Here is what to look for — and the best picks for Indian travellers in 2026.

## Why Dual Voltage Matters Specifically for Indian Travellers

India's mains voltage is 230V/50Hz. The USA, Canada, Mexico, and much of Latin America run on 110–120V/60Hz. Japan runs on 100V. Europe and the UAE run on 220–230V (broadly compatible with India, but plug adapters are still needed).

Most modern electric toothbrush chargers from Oral-B and Philips are **universal (100–240V)** — meaning they auto-switch to the correct voltage and work anywhere with just a plug adapter. However, some older or budget Indian-market models ship with India-only chargers that will burn out on 110V circuits.

Always check the charger: look for "Input: 100–240V~50/60Hz" printed on the transformer. If it says "230V only" — do not plug it into a US outlet without a step-up converter.

## Indian Traveller Plug Adapter Guide

| Destination | Plug Type | Voltage | Need Adapter from India? |
|---|---|---|---|
| USA/Canada | Type A/B | 110V | Yes (adapter) — dual voltage charger handles voltage |
| UK | Type G | 230V | Yes (adapter) — voltage same as India |
| UAE/Saudi | Type G/D | 220–230V | Usually yes (adapter) |
| Europe (most) | Type C/E/F | 220–230V | Yes (adapter) |
| Singapore | Type G | 230V | Yes (adapter) |
| Australia | Type I | 230V | Yes (adapter) |
| Japan | Type A | 100V | Yes (adapter) — dual voltage charger handles this |

A universal travel adapter (₹799–₹1,499 on Amazon India) covers all destinations.

## Airline Carry-On Rules for Indian Travellers

- **Lithium-ion batteries** in electric toothbrushes are well under the 100Wh DGCA carry-on limit — all models listed here are permitted in cabin baggage
- The brush handle goes in carry-on, not checked luggage (checked luggage temperature variation can affect lithium batteries)
- The charging base is non-hazardous and can go in either carry-on or checked baggage
