## Fast Charging Standards Explained — Which One Matters for Your Phone?

Fast charging in power banks is more confusing than it needs to be because every major phone brand invented its own standard. Here is a clear breakdown:

### USB Power Delivery (USB-C PD)
- **Standard:** USB-IF standard, widely adopted
- **Who uses it:** Apple iPhone 15+, Samsung Galaxy S series, Google Pixel, MacBooks, iPads, many Android flagships
- **What you need:** Power bank with USB-C PD output (18W, 45W, 65W labeling)
- **Speed:** 20W for iPhone 15, up to 45W for Samsung Galaxy S24

### Qualcomm Quick Charge (QC 3.0 / QC 4.0)
- **Standard:** Qualcomm proprietary — works over USB-A or USB-C
- **Who uses it:** Phones with Qualcomm Snapdragon chipsets — Redmi, Poco, Realme, Motorola, Pixel (older), Samsung mid-range
- **What you need:** Power bank with QC 3.0 or QC 4.0 output on USB-A or USB-C port
- **Speed:** 18W–27W for QC 3.0; up to 100W for QC 5.0 (rare in power banks)

### VOOC / SuperVOOC (OnePlus / Realme / OPPO)
- **Standard:** Proprietary — requires both VOOC-certified cable AND charger to activate
- **Who uses it:** OnePlus, Realme, OPPO phones with Warp Charge, SUPERVOOC branding
- **Key fact:** Standard 18W QC chargers will NOT activate VOOC. You need a VOOC-certified power bank.
- **Reality check:** True VOOC power banks are rare and expensive. Most "OnePlus-compatible" power banks deliver 18W standard charge, not VOOC speed.

### Warp Charge (OnePlus specific)
- **Same as:** VOOC — OnePlus rebranded it as Warp Charge; requires proprietary circuitry
- **Power bank support:** Very limited; a Warp Charge–certified power bank is the only way to get Warp speed via power bank

## Two Types of "Fast Charging" — Input vs Output

When people say they want a fast charging power bank, they usually mean one of two things:

**Fast output (charges your phone faster):** You need a power bank with QC 3.0 / USB-C PD output. This fast-charges your phone from the power bank. The Anker PowerCore III 20000 PD (65W PD) is the best example.

**Fast input (recharges the power bank faster):** You need a power bank with 18W+ input. This means the power bank itself refills quickly after being depleted. The Mi Power Bank 3i 10000mAh (18W input) is the best budget example — it fills in about 3.5 hours.

**Both are important.** A power bank that is slow to recharge is annoying for daily users. A power bank that is slow to output means your phone charges at the same rate as a 5-year-old phone charger.

The Anker PowerCore III 20000 PD leads on both: 100W input (2.5 hours to full) and 65W PD output.

## Does Fast Input Charging Matter More Than Fast Output?

Depends on your usage pattern.

**Fast output matters more if:** You frequently plug in with low battery and need maximum charge in minimum time.

**Fast input matters more if:** You use the power bank daily and need it ready each morning. A unit with 5W input takes 12–15 hours to fully recharge. An 18W input unit takes 3.5–4 hours. A 100W input unit (Anker III) takes 2.5 hours.

For daily users who carry the power bank every day and plug it in overnight — fast input is critical. For occasional users who charge the power bank once a week — fast output is more important for in-the-field use.
