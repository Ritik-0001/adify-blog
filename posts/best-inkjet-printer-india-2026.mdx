## Ink Tank vs Cartridge Inkjet — The Decision That Defines Everything

Before picking an inkjet printer in India in 2026, you need to understand one thing: **ink tank inkjet printers and cartridge inkjet printers are entirely different propositions.**

**Cartridge inkjet printers** (like HP DeskJet 2331, Canon PIXMA iP2870):
- Low upfront cost (₹2,999–₹5,000)
- High running cost (₹1–₹4 per page)
- Cartridges die quickly, often dry out if unused
- Good for very occasional printing (under 50 pages/month)

**Ink tank inkjet printers** (like Canon PIXMA G3020, Epson L3252):
- Moderate upfront cost (₹7,999–₹15,000)
- Very low running cost (₹0.09–₹0.20 per page)
- Refillable tanks last thousands of pages
- Ideal for regular home or office use (100–1,000 pages/month)

In India, where cartridge prices have risen sharply and original cartridges are expensive relative to income, **ink tank printers are almost always the right choice for anyone who prints more than once a week.**

## When Does Inkjet Beat Laser?

Inkjet wins in these scenarios:

1. **You print in colour** — laser colour printers under ₹15,000 exist but cost significantly more per page for colour
2. **You print photos** — laser cannot match inkjet for photo output
3. **You print occasionally** — laser has a per-toner cost that makes low-volume printing expensive per page
4. **You need a compact all-in-one** — inkjet all-in-ones are smaller and lighter than laser equivalents

Laser wins when:
- You print exclusively text documents in high volume
- The printer will sit unused for weeks (laser toner doesn't dry up)
- You need speeds above 15 ppm
