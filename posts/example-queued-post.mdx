Most developers don't think twice about their keyboard — until they use a great one. After six weeks of daily typing across 12 boards, these are the ones we'd actually spend our own money on.

---

## 1. Keychron Q3 Max — Best Overall

**Switch options:** Gateron Jupiter Red / Brown / Banana
**Form factor:** TKL (tenkeyless)
**Connection:** Wired + Bluetooth 5.1

The Q3 Max is the sweet spot between build quality and price. The gasket-mounted design absorbs keystroke impact so typing feels cushioned, not clacky. It comes with a solid aluminium case that doesn't flex even with aggressive typing.

**What we love:**
- Pre-lubed switches straight out of the box
- Fully programmable via QMK/VIA
- Hot-swappable — change switches in minutes without soldering

**What we don't:**
- The included stabilisers need slight tweaking for best sound
- No volume knob on the base model

**Pricing:** ~$180–$200

**Verdict:** The keyboard we'd recommend to 80% of developers. Excellent value for what is genuinely a premium build.

---

## 2. NuPhy Air75 V2 — Best Wireless

**Switch options:** Wisteria / Cowberry / Aloe
**Form factor:** 75%
**Connection:** Wireless (2.4GHz + BT 5.0) + USB-C wired

For developers who move between desk, couch, and coffee shop, the Air75 V2 hits a rare trifecta: wireless reliability, low-profile feel, and a real aluminium frame under 700g. The 2.4GHz dongle mode is indistinguishable from wired in latency.

**What we love:**
- Low-profile switches reduce finger travel fatigue during long sessions
- Battery lasts ~3 weeks on BT, ~40 hours on 2.4GHz
- Function row + arrow keys in a compact layout

**What we don't:**
- Low-profile takes about a week to adjust to if you're coming from full-travel switches
- RGB is underwhelming at this price

**Pricing:** ~$120–$140

**Verdict:** The best keyboard for developers who don't want to be tethered. The low-profile switches are underrated for coding.

---

## 3. Glorious GMMK Pro — Best for Customisers

**Switch options:** Sold separately (any 5-pin MX switch)
**Form factor:** 75%
**Connection:** USB-C wired

The GMMK Pro is a platform, not a finished product. You buy the board, pick your own switches and keycaps, and build the keyboard you actually want. The gasket mount and brass weight make it punch well above its price once kitted out.

**What we love:**
- Full QMK/VIA support
- The most satisfying typing sound when built out properly
- Wide ecosystem of compatible components

**What we don't:**
- More expensive than the Keychron Q3 when you factor in switches + caps
- No wireless option

**Pricing:** ~$170 (board only) + switches + keycaps

**Verdict:** Best choice if you want to go deep on the hobby. Not ideal if you just want something good out of the box.

---

## 4. Logitech MX Keys S — Best for Productivity Switchers

**Switch options:** Scissor (proprietary)
**Form factor:** Full-size
**Connection:** Bluetooth / Logi Bolt USB receiver

Not everyone wants mechanical. The MX Keys S is the keyboard for developers who spend more time in Slack and Zoom than Vim. The low-profile scissor switches are quiet enough for open offices and satisfying enough that you won't miss the clack.

**What we love:**
- Smart backlighting adjusts to ambient light automatically
- One keyboard, three devices — switch instantly
- USB-C charging, no AA batteries

**What we don't:**
- Not hot-swappable, not programmable beyond Logi Options+
- Premium price for what are still scissor switches

**Pricing:** ~$120

**Verdict:** The best non-mechanical keyboard for professional developers. Makes no apologies for what it is.

---

## 5. Ducky One 3 Mini — Best Budget Mechanical

**Switch options:** Cherry MX Red / Blue / Brown / Silent Red
**Form factor:** 60%
**Connection:** USB-C wired

60% layouts feel extreme until you try one. Removing the numpad and function row forces your fingers to travel less and your posture to open up. The Ducky One 3 Mini is the most polished 60% in this price range, with PBT doubleshot keycaps that won't fade.

**What we love:**
- PBT keycaps included (most boards at this price use ABS)
- Extremely compact — fits in any bag
- Reliable Cherry MX switches

**What we don't:**
- No arrow keys — takes ~1 week to adjust to Fn-layer navigation
- Wired only

**Pricing:** ~$110

**Verdict:** The best entry point into mechanical keyboards for developers who want compact. If you can survive a week of adjustment, you won't go back.
