## What Does Under ₹1 Lakh Actually Get You in 2026?

The under-₹1 lakh segment in Indian EV scooters is the "entry-level but not bad" zone. These are not compromise machines — they're stripped-down versions of capable EVs. You give up some top-end features (faster charging, higher top speed, premium display), but the core riding experience remains solid.

Here's what you typically get in this price band:

- Real-world range of 70-110km per charge
- Top speed of 60-80 km/h
- Standard (slow) portable charger included
- Basic display (smaller or lower resolution)
- 3-5 hour charging time from 0 to 80%

Let's look at each model worth considering.

## What Compromises Should You Expect Under ₹1 Lakh?

This is important to set expectations before you buy. Here's what you *genuinely* give up compared to the ₹1.3-1.5 lakh premium models:

### Slower Charging
Under ₹1 lakh, you mostly get portable chargers that add range slowly — roughly 10-15km of range per hour. Premium models get faster onboard chargers. Plan for overnight charging rather than quick top-ups.

### Lower Top Speed
Most sub-₹1 lakh EVs cap at 80 km/h vs 90-115 km/h for premium models. For city commuting, this is irrelevant. For highway riding, it's a real limitation.

### Smaller Batteries, Less Range Buffer
The range numbers look fine on paper, but a smaller battery degrades faster. After 3-4 years, a 3kWh battery losing 20% capacity hits harder than a 4kWh battery losing the same percentage.

### Feature Set
Expect fewer ride modes, a simpler display, no built-in navigation in some cases, and fewer customisation options via the app.

## Who Should Buy a Sub-₹1 Lakh Electric Scooter?

**Buy one if:**
- Your daily commute is under 50km
- You have reliable home charging (parking with plug access)
- You're new to EVs and want a lower financial risk entry point
- You're in a city with the brand's service centre nearby

**Wait and buy the next tier (₹1-1.5 lakh) if:**
- You commute 50-80km daily
- You rely on public charging
- You plan to keep this scooter for 5+ years
- You frequently carry a pillion passenger
