## Overview

Getting kids to brush their teeth is a daily battle in most Indian households. Electric toothbrushes make that battle significantly easier — the built-in 2-minute timers, fun character designs, and gentle vibrations turn brushing from a chore into something kids actually look forward to. Most dentists in India now recommend switching to an electric toothbrush for children as early as age 3, provided parents guide the brushing session.

The Indian market has several solid options across price points, from budget Colgate kids electrics under ₹800 to the feature-packed Philips Sonicare For Kids with its interactive brushing app. The key is matching the brush to your child's age — smaller brush heads for toddlers, adjustable intensity for older kids, and always prioritising extra-soft bristles to protect developing gums and enamel.

Indian parents often ask whether electric toothbrushes are safe for young children. The answer is yes — with caveats. Children under 6 need parental supervision for every brush session. The toothpaste amount matters too: a grain-of-rice smear for under-3s, a pea-sized amount from ages 3 to 6. Once kids hit 8, they can generally brush independently with the right brush.

## Buying Guide

**When should children start using electric toothbrushes?**
Most Indian paediatric dentists suggest electric toothbrushes from age 3 onward, once the child can spit out toothpaste. Before that, a soft manual brush is fine. The key shift is at age 3 when the full primary (milk) teeth set is in place and more thorough cleaning becomes important.

**Age-appropriate brush heads:**
- Ages 3–5: Smallest available brush head, extra-soft bristles only
- Ages 6–8: Kids-size head, soft bristles
- Ages 9+: Can transition to a junior-size or even adult compact head

**How much toothpaste?**
- Under 3 years: Grain-of-rice amount of fluoride toothpaste
- Ages 3–6: Pea-sized amount
- Ages 7+: Standard pea-sized to slightly more

**Parental technique tips:**
Stand behind your child and cradle their head against your body. Angle the brush at 45 degrees to the gumline. Let the brush do the work — no scrubbing. Focus extra time on back molars where food traps.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a 2-year-old use an electric toothbrush?**
A: Most manufacturers rate their kids' brushes from age 3+. For 2-year-olds, a soft manual brush with parental help is recommended.

**Q: Do kids' electric toothbrush heads fit adult handles?**
A: Oral-B kids heads fit adult Oral-B handles (they share the same connection standard). Philips Sonicare kids heads do not fit adult Sonicare handles.

**Q: How often should I replace the brush head?**
A: Every 3 months, or when bristles fray — whichever comes first. Kids' bristles tend to fray faster as kids often bite or chew the brush.

**Q: Are electric toothbrushes safe for milk teeth?**
A: Yes, on the correct kids mode/brush with extra-soft bristles and parental supervision. They are gentler than aggressive manual scrubbing.
