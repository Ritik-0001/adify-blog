## MPPT vs PWM: Which Solar Charge Controller Do You Need?

This is the most important question when buying a solar inverter in India. **PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)** charge controllers are simpler and cheaper but waste 20–30% of your solar panel's energy in most conditions. They work well only when the panel voltage closely matches the battery voltage. **MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking)** controllers are smarter — they continuously adjust the operating point to extract maximum power from your panels, especially in low-light conditions like cloudy days or early morning. In Indian conditions where overcast monsoon days are common across states like Kerala, West Bengal, and Odisha, MPPT controllers recover 15–30% more energy than PWM. For any installation above 300W of panels, always choose MPPT.

**Grid-tied vs off-grid:** Grid-tied solar inverters feed surplus power back to the grid and earn you net-metering credits — the subsidy-eligible route under PM Surya Ghar. Off-grid solar inverters work independently with battery storage — ideal for areas with frequent power cuts where grid reliability cannot be assumed. Most Indian homes in semi-urban and rural areas benefit most from hybrid systems that do both.

## Indian Solar Subsidy Context

Under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana (2024–2025 expansion), households installing rooftop solar systems of 1–3kW capacity can receive central subsidies of ₹30,000–₹78,000 depending on capacity, plus additional state-level subsidies in states like Gujarat (up to ₹10,000 extra). A 2kW rooftop solar system with a good hybrid inverter and battery storage typically has a payback period of 4–6 years in high-sunlight states like Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Telangana, and 6–8 years in lower-irradiance states like Himachal Pradesh or the Northeast. After payback, you effectively get free electricity for the remaining 20-year panel lifespan.
