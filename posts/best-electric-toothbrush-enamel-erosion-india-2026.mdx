## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Enamel Erosion

**The 30-minute rule — most important habit for enamel erosion:** After consuming acidic food or drink (citrus juice, tamarind, amla, cola, sports drinks, vinegar-based pickles), the enamel surface is temporarily softened by acid demineralisation. Brushing during this window — even gently — removes the softened enamel layer. Wait a minimum of 30 minutes after any acidic consumption before brushing. Rinse with plain water immediately to dilute the acid, but do not brush. This single habit change prevents significant ongoing enamel loss.

**Indian diet and enamel erosion:** India's food culture is particularly erosive:
- **Tamarind (imli):** pH ~2.0–2.5 — extremely acidic, used in chutneys, sambar, and rasam daily
- **Amla (Indian gooseberry):** pH ~2.5–3.0 — highly acidic despite its health benefits
- **Nimbu pani (lime water):** pH ~2.0–3.0 — consumed regularly as a refreshment
- **Cola drinks:** pH ~2.5 — erosion risk compounded by sugar content
- **Citrus fruits (oranges, mosambi):** pH ~3.0–4.0 — moderately acidic

**Toothpaste selection is critical:**
- Use **low-RDA fluoride toothpaste** (Relative Dentin Abrasivity below 70)
- **Sensodyne Repair & Protect** (RDA ~70) — contains NovaMin which deposits calcium and phosphate into enamel
- **Colgate Sensitive Pro-Relief** (RDA ~67) — arginine-based, plugs dentinal tubules
- **Avoid:** whitening toothpastes (RDA 100–200), charcoal toothpastes (RDA often unmeasured but extremely high)

**Fluoride remineralisation strategy:** Use high-concentration fluoride toothpaste (1450 ppm — most Indian pharmacy brands contain this) and do not rinse after brushing — spit, but leave the residual toothpaste film. This maximises fluoride contact time for remineralisation of acid-weakened enamel.

**Soft bristles only:** Never use medium or hard bristle brush heads on eroded enamel. Soft bristle designation in brush heads means bristle stiffness RDA of approximately 70–85 — still within safe range for compromised enamel when combined with correct force (pressure sensor enforced).

**Clinical evidence:** A 2021 meta-analysis in *Caries Research* found that patients using electric toothbrushes with pressure sensors had 40% less enamel wear progression over 12 months compared to manual brushers — attributable primarily to force reduction rather than brush technology type.
