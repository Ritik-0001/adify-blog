## How Tea and Coffee Stain Teeth: The Tannin Science

Tea and coffee both contain **tannins** — polyphenolic compounds that bind to the protein film (pellicle) on teeth. This binding creates the characteristic brown-yellow staining. Chai's combination of black tea tannins with milk proteins actually creates a slightly more adherent stain than black tea or coffee alone — which is why chai drinkers often have darker staining than equivalent coffee drinkers despite drinking tea.

**Why manual brushes struggle:** Manual brushing removes fresh tannin deposits adequately, but the accumulated matrix of tannin-protein-mineral that builds up over months requires the mechanical intensity of an electric brush to disrupt effectively. Oscillating-rotating brushes at 8,800 RPM and sonic brushes at 31,000 strokes/minute generate forces that manual brushing cannot replicate.

**When to brush relative to chai and coffee:** Wait **30 minutes** after drinking tea or coffee before brushing. This is counter-intuitive but important: immediately after acidic/tannin beverages, enamel is temporarily softened and more susceptible to abrasion from brushing. Waiting 30 minutes allows saliva to reharden the enamel. For morning chai drinkers — brush before chai (removes overnight plaque), have chai, wait 30 minutes, then a quick rinse with water.
