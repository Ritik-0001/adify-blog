## Buying Guide

### GPU Requirements for 4K — India Context

4K gaming requires significantly more GPU horsepower than 1080p. Here is what is realistic with GPUs popular in India:

- **RTX 3060 / RX 6600 XT:** Can drive 4K 60Hz in older or less demanding games. Expect medium settings in AAA titles. Adequate for productivity use.
- **RTX 3070 / RX 6700 XT:** Comfortable 4K 60Hz gaming at high settings in most games. A solid pairing for this monitor category.
- **RTX 4070 / RX 7700 XT and above:** Smooth 4K experience with DLSS/FSR upscaling pushing performance further.
- **Integrated graphics (Intel Iris Xe, AMD Radeon iGPU):** Perfectly fine for 4K desktop productivity and media playback, but cannot drive games at 4K.

For productivity, writing, and content consumption — any GPU supporting HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort 1.2 handles 4K 60Hz without issue.

### Do You Actually Need 4K?

4K makes the most practical sense when:
- You sit within 60–80cm of the screen (standard Indian home desk distance)
- You work with high-resolution images, dense spreadsheets, or video editing timelines
- You watch Netflix, Prime Video, or YouTube 4K content regularly (all available in India on premium plans)

At 27 inches, the sharpness improvement over 1440p is real but not dramatic from normal viewing distance. The bigger practical benefit is extra desktop real estate — 4K gives you effectively four times the working area of 1080p, transforming multitasking on a single display.

### Content Availability in India

Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Disney+ Hotstar offer 4K HDR on their premium tier plans in India. YouTube 4K works well on 50Mbps+ broadband connections common in Indian metros. Most Indian regional OTT platforms cap at 1080p, but the international library increasingly arrives in 4K.

## FAQ

**Q: Is 4K 60Hz enough or do I need 4K 144Hz?**
For productivity, design, and media watching, 4K 60Hz is perfectly smooth. 4K 144Hz monitors exist but cost ₹60,000+. For gaming, 4K 60Hz is the realistic budget option in 2026 and pairs well with mid-to-high GPUs using upscaling technologies like DLSS or FSR.

**Q: Can a MacBook Air M-series drive a 4K monitor?**
Yes. M1, M2, and M3 MacBook Air models natively support one external 4K 60Hz display via USB-C or Thunderbolt. Use a USB-C to USB-C cable or a quality USB-C to HDMI 2.0 adapter.

**Q: Is 27 inches too large for a typical Indian study desk?**
At 4K resolution, 27 inches is the sweet spot. The high pixel density (163 PPI) prevents the "large and blurry" issue you get with 27-inch 1080p displays. Standard Indian desks at 90–120cm width accommodate a 27-inch monitor very comfortably.

**Q: Which 4K monitor is best value for a student in India?**
The ViewSonic VX2758-4K-Pro at ₹22,999 is the most accessible starting point. If budget allows, the Dell S2722QC at ₹27,999 adds USB-C single-cable convenience that is genuinely practical for students with laptops.
