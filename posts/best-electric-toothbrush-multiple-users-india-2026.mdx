## How Shared Electric Toothbrushes Work

The handle contains the motor, battery, and electronics. The brush head — the removable portion with bristles — is the personal hygiene component. Each user has their own brush head; they share only the handle.

**Is sharing a handle hygienic?** Yes, with proper practice. The handle never contacts the mouth. Rinse it after each use, store brush heads separately (heads-up in individual cups), and replace heads every 3 months per person. This is more hygienic than sharing a manual brush and costs significantly less than buying separate handles per person.

**Colour-coded heads:** Most major brands offer replacement heads in multiple colours. Assigning a colour per family member eliminates confusion — red is always Dad's head, blue is always Mum's.

**Per-user modes:** If family members need different cleaning modes — one user needs Sensitive mode, another uses Whitening — they can switch the handle mode when attaching their head. This works on all multi-mode handles.

## Multi-User Hygiene Best Practices

**Storage:** Store each person's head in a separate holder with bristles facing up and uncovered — bristles need to air dry between uses. Closed containers trap moisture and promote bacterial growth.

**After each use:** Rinse the handle body under running water, shake off excess water, and store upright. The brush head's user attaches and detaches their own head after use.

**Deep clean monthly:** Wipe the handle with a damp cloth, paying attention to the head attachment joint where moisture and toothpaste residue accumulate. The joint area can be rinsed under running water on most IPX7-rated models.

**Travel:** When travelling, each user takes only their brush head (minimal space) and shares the handle. This is one of the biggest practical advantages of the multi-user setup.
