## Overview

The U2723D is a 27-inch 4K (3840×2160) monitor with Dell's IPS Black technology — a new panel type that achieves 2000:1 contrast ratio (versus typical IPS's 1000:1) without the colour accuracy trade-offs of VA panels. It covers 98% DCI-P3 and includes a USB-C hub with 90W power delivery. Factory calibrated to Delta E &lt; 2.

## IPS Black: What It Means

Traditional IPS panels achieve ~1000:1 contrast ratio — blacks look greyish in low-light rooms, which is why photographers and designers often preferred VA panels for deeper blacks. Dell's IPS Black technology reaches 2000:1 contrast — blacks are noticeably darker and richer, while maintaining IPS's superior colour accuracy and wide viewing angles.

In a side-by-side comparison, the U2723D's blacks look substantially deeper than a standard IPS while remaining colour-accurate — the best of both IPS and VA characteristics.

---

## Colour Accuracy

Factory calibrated to Delta E &lt; 2 across 98% DCI-P3 and 100% sRGB. For photographers, video editors, and graphic designers, this means colours displayed on the U2723D accurately represent what clients and audiences will see in print and on other calibrated displays.

The out-of-box calibration is good enough for professional use without additional calibration hardware — though the monitor does support hardware calibration for those who need it.

## Connectivity & USB-C Hub

The U2723D is a capable USB-C hub in addition to being a monitor:

- Thunderbolt 3 (on some variants) for full 40Gbps bandwidth
- 4× USB-A 3.2 Gen 1 ports
- 2× USB-C ports
- RJ45 Gigabit Ethernet
- HDMI + DisplayPort inputs

For a desk setup where you want one cable to laptop = display + charging + all peripherals connected, the U2723D makes this possible.
