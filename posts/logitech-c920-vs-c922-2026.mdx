The Logitech C920 and C922 are among the most recommended webcams for WFH professionals and streamers. They look nearly identical, share similar specs, and are priced close together. So what's actually different? Let's find out.

---

## Overview

The **Logitech C920** has been the gold standard for 1080p webcams since 2012 — a testament to how well-designed it was. The **Logitech C922** was launched as a streaming-focused upgrade with better low-light performance and 720p/60fps support. Both remain actively sold in India in 2026.

---

## Video Quality

At 1080p/30fps — which is what 99% of users use — the C920 and C922 produce near-identical image quality. Same sensor, same lens class, same glass optics. Side-by-side in a well-lit room, you genuinely cannot tell the difference.

The C922 advantage kicks in at 720p: it supports 60fps versus the C920's 30fps. For streamers who game at 720p60, this matters. For WFH video calls and content recording at 1080p, it's irrelevant.

---

## Low-Light Performance

Both cameras struggle in genuine low light — as do most webcams at this price. Logitech claims the C922 has improved low-light handling, but independent testing shows the improvement is minimal without good room lighting. Neither will save a dark room.

If your room has decent natural light or a key light/ring light, both cameras look excellent. Without adequate lighting, neither shines.

---

## Who Is Each Designed For?

The C920 is designed for video calls — Zoom, Meet, Teams. It excels at this and has done so for over a decade.

The C922 adds the XSplit Broadcaster software licence and 60fps 720p for streaming. If you stream to Twitch or YouTube at 720p60, the C922 makes sense. If you do video calls and record 1080p content, the C920 is identical for your use case.

---

## Pros & Cons

### Logitech C920
**Pros:** Lower price (₹7,999), same 1080p quality, proven reliability across a decade, lighter weight, widely available
**Cons:** 720p capped at 30fps, no bundled streaming software

### Logitech C922
**Pros:** 60fps at 720p for streaming, XSplit licence included, marginally better low-light claims
**Cons:** ₹2,000 premium for minimal real-world difference in non-streaming use, still 30fps at 1080p

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the C920 if:**
- You primarily use it for Zoom, Teams, or Meet
- You record 1080p tutorials or content
- You want to save ₹2,000 on functionally identical output
- Reliability over 5+ years matters

**Buy the C922 if:**
- You stream at 720p60 on Twitch or YouTube
- You want XSplit for background replacement
- You're building a budget streaming setup

---

## Final Verdict

For WFH professionals: **Logitech C920**. It produces the same video quality in your use case and costs less. The C922 upgrade is not worth ₹2,000 unless you specifically need 60fps at 720p for streaming.

For streamers: **Logitech C922** — the 60fps support and XSplit licence make it the right tool.

For most people reading this in India for work-from-home use: save the ₹2,000, buy the C920.
