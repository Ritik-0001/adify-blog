## Why Indian Family Cooking Has Unique Air Fryer Requirements

Indian cooking differs from western cooking in several ways that affect air fryer choice:

**Higher oil in original recipes:** Pakoras, samosas, mathri, bhatura — Indian snacks are traditionally deep-fried in oil at 180–200°C. Air fryers replicate this at 1–2 tablespoons of oil, but only if they reach and sustain 190–200°C. Budget models that cap at 180°C produce acceptable but not excellent Indian snacks.

**Spiced marinades:** Chicken tikka, malai tikka, and paneer tikka rely on spiced yogurt marinades. These marinades can drip and burn at the bottom of the basket — the Agaro 12L's crumb tray catches drips cleanly; basket air fryers need the basket liner (silicone or parchment) for best results.

**Batch cooking culture:** Indian families often cook for the next day. The Agaro 12L's larger capacity means you can dehydrate mango or make large batches of mathri for the week in a single session.
