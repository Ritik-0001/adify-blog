## What Dentists Look for in an Electric Toothbrush

**Clinical priorities (in order):**

1. **Pressure sensor** — Over-brushing causes gum recession and enamel erosion. A pressure sensor prevents the most common brushing error.
2. **2-minute timer** — Most Indians brush for under 60 seconds. A timer is the single most impactful behavioural intervention.
3. **Cleaning technology** — Both oscillating (Oral-B) and sonic (Philips Sonicare) are clinically validated.
4. **Brush head replacement tracking** — Worn heads are less effective. BrushSync (Philips) or coloured indicators guide timely replacement.

## What Dentists Do NOT Recommend

- **Battery-powered non-rechargeable brushes:** Limited battery consistency, no pressure sensors
- **Generic/unbranded electric brushes:** No clinical evidence, inconsistent motor quality
- **Brushes without pressure sensors:** The risk of over-brushing is too high without feedback

---

## Dentist-Recommended Brushing Routine

**Technique validated by Indian dentistry associations:**
1. Use fluoride toothpaste (1,000–1,500ppm — Colgate Total, Oral-B Gum & Enamel, Sensodyne)
2. Angle brush 45° to the gumline
3. Let the electric toothbrush do the work — don't scrub
4. Cover all surfaces (buccal, lingual, occlusal) in 2-minute sessions
5. Follow with interdental cleaning (floss or water flosser)
6. Use fluoride mouthwash last (if recommended by dentist)
