## Brand Overview

### LG Monitors — Best for Everyday Value

LG's monitor lineup in India is anchored by its IPS panels at competitive prices. LG pioneered IPS technology and continues to manufacture some of the most consistent IPS panels available. Their India-market strategy prioritises volume — making quality IPS monitors accessible across a wide price range.

**LG's strengths in India:**
- **Best-selling budget monitors:** The 24MK430H is India's most popular external monitor on Amazon India, consistently topping bestseller charts
- **IPS panel quality:** LG produces its own IPS panels, which ensures consistent quality across its lineup
- **4K OLED for premium:** LG's premium segment includes OLED monitors with outstanding picture quality
- **Competitive pricing:** LG monitors are typically priced ₹1,000–₹3,000 below BenQ equivalents
- **Strong Amazon India presence:** Wide availability, fast delivery, and consistent stock

**LG's weaknesses:**
- Limited eye care technology compared to BenQ
- Less brand differentiation in professional and gaming segments

### BenQ Monitors — Best for Specialised Use Cases

BenQ is a Taiwanese brand with a focused product strategy — each monitor line is designed for a specific user type. The GW series targets eye care and general office use. The PD series targets professional designers and developers. The MOBIUZ line targets competitive gamers. This specialisation means BenQ monitors tend to offer more purpose-built features than equivalent LG models.

**BenQ's strengths in India:**
- **Eye care technology:** BenQ's Brightness Intelligence (B.I.) automatically adjusts screen brightness based on ambient light. Combined with flicker-free and blue light filter, the GW series offers the most comprehensive eye care in the budget-mid range
- **Professional PD series:** Factory-calibrated panels with Delta E < 2 colour accuracy, suitable for designers and developers needing precision
- **MOBIUZ gaming line:** Designed specifically for competitive gaming with HDRi and optimised response times
- **Indian service network:** BenQ has authorised service centres in major Indian metros with reasonable turnaround times

**BenQ's weaknesses:**
- Priced higher than LG equivalents for similar specs
- Fewer budget options under ₹10,000 in India

## Buying Guide

### Choose LG if:

- **You want the best value** for a reliable IPS monitor under ₹15,000
- **You are a casual to moderate user** who does not need specialised professional features
- **You want widely available stock** and fast delivery across India
- **You game occasionally** and want a higher refresh rate (75Hz at budget, 144Hz+ at mid-range)
- **You are buying your first external monitor** and want a trusted, straightforward choice

### Choose BenQ if:

- **You spend 8+ hours daily** at the screen and prioritise eye comfort over pure value
- **You are a professional designer or developer** who needs factory-calibrated colour accuracy and the PD series feature set
- **You want Brightness Intelligence** — the automatic ambient light adjustment is a genuinely useful feature in Indian homes with variable lighting
- **You are a serious competitive gamer** — MOBIUZ monitors are designed specifically for gaming performance
- **You need a KVM switch** for managing two computers from one desk

### Eye Care: Is BenQ's Premium Justified?

BenQ's eye care marketing is substantial, but the technology behind it is real. Brightness Intelligence genuinely works — the sensor adjusts screen brightness as you move between a sunny balcony-facing desk and a dim room, preventing the jarring brightness mismatch that causes squinting. If you routinely work in rooms with changing light conditions — common in Indian homes across different times of day — this feature provides daily utility.

However, for users who work in consistent room lighting (an air-conditioned office, a dedicated study room), the benefit is smaller, and LG's basic flicker-free and low blue light modes are adequate.

## FAQ

**Q: Which brand has better warranty support in India?**
Both offer 3-year warranties on most models. LG's service network is broader with centres in tier-2 and tier-3 cities. BenQ's support is strong in metros. For buyers in smaller cities, LG's wider network is an advantage.

**Q: Is BenQ's Brightness Intelligence a gimmick or genuinely useful?**
It is genuinely useful in Indian homes where multiple light sources — sunlight, overhead fluorescent, desk lamps — create variable ambient brightness throughout the day. If you work in a consistent-lighting environment, it matters less.

**Q: Which brand is better for gaming?**
LG's UltraGear line (27GP850-B and similar) competes directly with BenQ MOBIUZ. For pure gaming performance and value, LG's options are slightly better priced. For competitive gaming with feature-specific needs, MOBIUZ monitors are excellent.

**Q: Are LG monitors available at offline stores in India?**
Yes. LG monitors are widely available at Croma, Reliance Digital, and most local computer hardware stores across India. BenQ monitors have a smaller offline presence but are well-stocked on Amazon India.
