## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes for Sensitive Teeth

**Why sonic is usually better for sensitivity:**
Sonic brushes (Philips Sonicare) work via fluid dynamics rather than direct abrasive contact. They require less physical pressure to clean effectively — reducing enamel wear and gum trauma compared to oscillating brushes used aggressively.

**Essential features for sensitive teeth:**
1. **Pressure sensor** (physical pause preferred over light warning)
2. **Sensitive cleaning mode** (genuinely reduced power, not marketing)
3. **Soft or extra-soft brush head compatibility**
4. **Variable intensity** (Philips 5100's 3 intensities are the gold standard)

**Brush head matters:** Always use Sensitive or Extra-Soft heads. Hard or Medium bristles damage exposed dentine even with electric brushes. Replace every 3 months — worn bristles become stiffer.

**Technique for sensitivity:** Hold the brush at 45° to the gumline, use light contact, let the brush do the work. Do not press — this is the most common mistake causing sensitivity to worsen.

**When to see a dentist:** If sensitivity persists after switching to a gentle electric toothbrush, consult a dentist. Sensitivity may indicate cavities, cracked teeth, or aggressive gum recession requiring professional treatment.
