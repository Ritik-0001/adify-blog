## Buying Guide: Mirrorless Cameras in India 2026

**Sensor size comparison:**
| Sensor | Depth of Field | Low Light | Size/Weight | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MFT (Micro Four Thirds) | Moderate | Good | Smallest, lightest | Affordable |
| APS-C | Better | Better | Mid-size | Mid-range |
| Full-frame | Best (most blur) | Best | Largest, heaviest | Premium |

**For Indian beginners:** Canon EOS R50 or Nikon Z30 — both have the best autofocus for learners and extensive tutorial resources in Hindi/regional languages.

**Lens ecosystem matters:** Lenses cost more than camera bodies long-term. Sony E-mount has 400+ lenses; Canon RF-S has fewer but growing; Fujifilm XF has excellent quality lenses.

**Video vs photography:** If video is primary — Sony ZV-E10 II or Nikon Z30 (no overheating, microphone input). If photography is primary — Fujifilm X-S20 or Canon EOS R50.
