## The 185°C Technology — Why It Matters for Indian Hair

GHD's research division identified 185°C as the "Optimal Heat" for hair straightening — hot enough to set the hydrogen bonds in hair (responsible for curl and wave) without exceeding the threshold that damages the disulphide bonds (the protein structure that determines hair strength).

**For Indian thick hair:** Indian hair has higher disulphide bond density than fine hair types — it's inherently more damage-resistant. However, damage is cumulative. Daily styling at 220–230°C (typical flat iron setting for Indian thick hair) erodes protein structure over months. GHD Glide at 185°C delivers the same straightening with significantly lower cumulative damage.

**Fixed temperature advantage:** Other straightening brushes offer 150–230°C adjustment. GHD removes this choice — the brush operates at exactly 185°C always. Indian users who don't know their ideal temperature default to higher settings (wrongly assuming hotter = better). Fixed 185°C eliminates this error.
