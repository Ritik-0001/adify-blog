## Why Electric Toothbrushes Are Safer During Pregnancy

Counterintuitively, electric toothbrushes are often *more* appropriate during pregnancy than manual brushes, not less. Here is why:

**Pregnancy gingivitis is nearly universal.** Rising progesterone and oestrogen levels cause blood vessels in gum tissue to become more permeable and reactive to plaque bacteria. Gums swell, become tender, and bleed more easily — typically from the second trimester onward. Manual brushing with standard pressure on inflamed gums causes more trauma and more bleeding.

**Pressure sensors prevent inadvertent over-brushing.** When gums are tender, there is a natural instinct to press harder thinking it cleans better. A pressure sensor stops this reflex automatically — it reduces motor speed or triggers a warning before damage occurs. No manual toothbrush offers this protection.

**Sonic fluid dynamics clean gently at the gumline.** Sonic toothbrushes at 31,000–40,000 strokes per minute create fluid turbulence that dislodges plaque at the gumline without requiring physical bristle contact — gentler on inflamed gum tissue than scrubbing.

**Better plaque removal reduces the systemic risk.** Research published in the Journal of Periodontology links periodontal disease in pregnant women to increased risk of preterm birth and low birth weight. Better oral hygiene during pregnancy is a maternal and fetal health issue.

## Trimester-by-Trimester Guidance

**First Trimester (Weeks 1–12):** Morning sickness can make brushing nauseating. Use a smaller brush head, brush at a time of day when nausea is lowest (often midday), and use unflavoured or mild-flavoured toothpaste. The Oral-B Pro 3's compact round head is easiest to tolerate.

**Second Trimester (Weeks 13–26):** Pregnancy gingivitis typically peaks. This is when investing in a pressure-sensor toothbrush matters most. The Sonicare 5100 in Gum Health mode is ideal for this period.

**Third Trimester (Weeks 27–40):** Gum inflammation often stabilises but remains elevated. Continue consistent brushing, add interdental cleaning (water flosser is comfortable and effective), and ensure a dental check-up before the third trimester concludes — dentists recommend this timing specifically because local anaesthetic is safest in the second trimester if any treatment is needed.

---

## What to Tell Your Dentist

At your pregnancy dental appointment, mention:
- Any bleeding when brushing or flossing (common but worth noting)
- Changes in gum colour (deep red indicates higher inflammation)
- Any loose teeth (rare but possible with severe pregnancy gingivitis)
- Morning sickness frequency, as repeated acid exposure damages enamel — your dentist may recommend a fluoride rinse
