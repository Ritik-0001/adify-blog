## What Does "All-in-One" Actually Mean for Home Users?

An all-in-one (AIO) or MFP (multifunction printer) combines printing, scanning, and copying in a single device. For most Indian home users, this is the right type of printer to buy — here's why:

**Printing:** The core function. Everything from school assignments to office documents to family photos.

**Scanning:** Increasingly important in 2026. Scan Aadhaar cards, PAN cards, old photos, receipts, certificates. Digital copies of paper documents are essential for everything from bank accounts to insurance claims.

**Copying:** Make duplicates without a computer. Useful for forms, question papers, ID documents.

**The argument against buying a print-only printer:** If you need to scan eventually — and most people do — you'll either buy a separate scanner or end up buying a second printer anyway. An AIO covers all three functions for marginally more money than a print-only model.

## What to Look For in an AIO Printer for India

1. **Ink system:** Ink tank is almost always better for Indian buyers than cartridge for regular use
2. **WiFi:** Essential if multiple family members print, or if you print from a phone
3. **Scanner resolution:** 600 dpi is fine for documents; 1200 dpi for photos
4. **Auto-duplex:** Saves paper on long documents; worth having
5. **Ink availability:** Check that ink refills are easily available in your city/online
6. **Service network:** HP and Epson have strong India service networks; Canon is also good
