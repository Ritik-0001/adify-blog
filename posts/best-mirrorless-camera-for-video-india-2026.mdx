## 2. Sony A6400 — Best Autofocus for Video

**Price:** ₹72,990 (body only)

The Sony A6400 remains one of the most capable video cameras in its price range even years after its launch, largely because Sony's Real-time Eye AF is still among the best in the business. If you shoot interviews, talking-head videos, or content where the subject moves unpredictably, the A6400 will keep your subject in sharp focus more consistently than almost anything else at this price. It shoots 4K with full pixel readout and no binning, which results in video that genuinely rivals cameras costing significantly more.

The A6400's limitation for solo creators is its tilting screen — it can tilt upward but does not fully flip to the front, so you cannot see yourself while filming alone without a monitor or phone app. That said, for documentary-style work, travel content, or any video with a dedicated cameraperson behind the lens, the A6400 is exceptional. The E-mount ecosystem means you can pair it with affordable Indian market staples like the Tamron 17-70mm f/2.8 or the Sony 18-135mm kit lens for versatile coverage. Sony's service network in Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore covers the A6400 fully.

**Key features:**
- 4K 30fps with full pixel readout, no crop, outstanding detail
- Industry-leading Real-time Eye AF and subject tracking
- 1080p at 120fps for smooth slow-motion footage
- 3.5mm mic input and micro-HDMI output for external recorders

## 3. Canon EOS R50 — Best Budget Video Camera for Beginners

**Price:** ₹59,990 (body only)

The Canon EOS R50 is the most beginner-friendly mirrorless camera on this list, and Canon's Dual Pixel CMOS AF II autofocus makes it nearly impossible to miss focus during video recording. It shoots 4K in two modes — an oversampled 4K 30fps mode cropped from the sensor centre (ideal for telephoto reach), and an uncropped 4K 30fps mode using the full sensor width. The flip-out articulating screen is fully touchscreen enabled and rotates 180 degrees for vlogging, and the camera body itself is compact and light enough to use handheld for extended periods.

For Indian buyers, Canon's RF-S lens ecosystem is growing steadily, and kit lenses like the RF-S 18-45mm are affordable and competent for YouTube. Canon service centres are widespread across tier-1 and tier-2 Indian cities, which is a genuine advantage if you ever need repairs. The R50 also has a built-in pop-up flash, which the Sony and Nikon options lack, useful for mixed lighting situations indoors. At ₹59,990 for the body, it is the most affordable way to get genuine 4K video with reliable autofocus in India.

- 4K 30fps in both cropped and uncropped modes
