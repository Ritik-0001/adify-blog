## How Electric Toothbrushes Prevent Cavities

**Plaque is the cavity mechanism:** Streptococcus mutans and other oral bacteria in plaque ferment dietary sugars, producing lactic acid that dissolves enamel mineral. Removing plaque interrupts this process.

**Electric vs manual plaque removal:** A 2014 Cochrane review found electric toothbrushes (oscillating-rotating specifically) removed 21% more plaque and reduced gingivitis 11% more than manual brushing. Higher plaque removal = lower cavity incidence over time.

**Key areas for cavity prevention:**
- Gumline (where plaque concentrates) — electric brushes outperform manual here
- Between teeth — electric brushes still don't reach interdental spaces (floss/interdental brush required separately)
- Back molars — where most adult cavities form in India

## The Complete Cavity Prevention System

An electric toothbrush alone is not sufficient — cavity prevention requires the full system:

### Toothpaste (Critical)
**Use 1,450 ppm fluoride toothpaste — non-negotiable:**
- Colgate Total — widely available, 1,450 ppm fluoride
- Oral-B Gum & Enamel — 1,450 ppm + enamel strengthening
- Pepsodent Germicheck — budget 1,450 ppm option

**Avoid:** Fluoride-free herbal toothpastes (Patanjali Dant Kanti, many natural brands) — no cavity protection regardless of other claims

### Interdental Cleaning (Required)
Electric toothbrushes don't reach between teeth. 40% of tooth surfaces are interdental — floss, interdental brushes, or water flossers are required for complete cavity prevention.

### Brushing Frequency
Twice daily minimum — after breakfast and before sleep. Before-sleep brushing is the more critical session: overnight reduced salivary flow leaves teeth unprotected against accumulated acid.

### Diet Modification
For high-cavity-risk users in India: reduce between-meal snacking frequency (not necessarily quantity), rinse with water after sweet foods, avoid sticky confectionery (ladoo, barfi adhere to teeth longer than liquid sweets).
