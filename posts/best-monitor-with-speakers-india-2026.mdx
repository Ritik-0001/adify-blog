## Overview

Built-in monitor speakers are genuinely useful for Indian buyers who want a cleaner desk setup — no extra cables, no separate speaker taking up space, and a simpler connection for video calls. Most monitors with speakers include 2W–5W drivers, which is adequate for Zoom calls, YouTube, and background music in a quiet room.

The important caveat: monitor speakers are not audiophile-grade. For serious music listening or movie watching you'll want external speakers or headphones. But for WFH video calls and casual media consumption, a good built-in speaker saves ₹1,000–₹3,000 and reduces desk clutter.

## Buying Guide

### Are Monitor Speakers Worth It?
For video calls (Google Meet, Zoom, Teams) — **yes**. 3–5W speakers are more than sufficient for speech clarity. For music or movie watching — they're convenient but not great. Budget ₹1,500–₹3,000 for a separate speaker if audio quality matters to you.

### Wattage Guide
- **2W per channel**: Video calls and YouTube at low-medium volume — acceptable
- **3W per channel**: Clearer speech, comfortable video call volume — good
- **5W per channel**: Noticeably louder and clearer, best in class for monitors

### When to Buy a Separate Speaker Instead
If you watch movies or listen to music on your computer, even a ₹1,500 Bluetooth speaker (Mi Compact Bluetooth Speaker, JBL Go) will dramatically outperform any monitor speaker. Reserve monitor speakers for the convenience of fewer cables, not audio quality.
