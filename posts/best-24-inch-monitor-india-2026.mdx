## Buying Guide

### Why 24" Is the Perfect Size for Most Indian Users

At 24 inches, 1080p resolution delivers approximately 92 PPI pixel density — sharp enough for all tasks from text editing to photo viewing. The viewing distance is 50–70cm, which matches most Indian desk setups (both compact study tables and standard office desks).

A 24" monitor doesn't require you to move your head while looking across the screen. It fits within your natural focal zone, reducing neck fatigue during long sessions. And crucially, a 1080p GPU requirement means even integrated graphics can handle basic tasks, and mid-range discrete GPUs handle everything else.

### Panel Types Explained

All five monitors in this list use **IPS panels** — the right choice for 24" in 2026. IPS delivers accurate colours, 178° viewing angles, and consistent brightness edge-to-edge. For shared desks, open-plan offices, or monitors viewed from angles, IPS is significantly better than TN alternatives.

VA panels offer better contrast but narrower viewing angles — better suited to 27"+ monitors used for media in dim rooms.

### Resolution Guide

**1080p at 24":** The sweet spot. Sharp, GPU-friendly, and future-proof for casual use.
**1200p at 24" (ASUS ProArt):** Adds 11% vertical space — a meaningful upgrade for creative workflows with 16:10 software interfaces.
**1440p at 24":** Not in this list, but exists in the ₹20,000+ range. At 24", the sharpness improvement over 1080p is noticeable but less dramatic than at 27".

### Other Key Features

**Eye care:** Flicker-free backlighting and hardware-level blue light reduction matter more at 24" because you sit closer to this size. BenQ and ASUS have the strongest implementations.

**Ergonomic stands:** Only the Dell P2422H and ASUS ProArt PA248QV include full ergonomic stands with height adjustment. For daily professional use, height adjustment alone reduces neck strain meaningfully.

**Power Supply:** All monitors are compatible with India's 230V AC supply. No stabiliser required.

## FAQ

**Q: Which monitor brand has the best after-sales service in India?**
A: Dell is the clear leader with 150+ service centres and next-business-day on-site support in major cities. LG is second with the widest nationwide reach including Tier-2 and Tier-3 towns. Acer, HP, and ASUS are reliable in metros. BenQ has good but primarily metro-focused coverage.

**Q: Is 24" the right size for Indian desks?**
A: Yes, for most Indian home and office desks (90–120cm wide with 50–70cm depth), 24" is the optimal size. It fits comfortably, the viewing distance matches typical desk setups, and 1080p remains sharp at this size.

**Q: Do I need an expensive GPU for a 24" monitor?**
A: Not at all. For 1080p 60–75Hz (LG, Dell, BenQ picks), even integrated graphics like Intel Iris Xe handle everyday tasks. For 144Hz gaming (Acer Nitro), a GTX 1650 or better is recommended. For the ASUS ProArt, any GPU with DisplayPort works fine.

**Q: Are gaming and office monitors interchangeable?**
A: You can use a gaming monitor (like the Acer Nitro XV240Y) for office work — it's a perfectly capable display for documents and video calls. However, office monitors (like the Dell P2422H) are not ideal for gaming due to their 60Hz refresh rates.
