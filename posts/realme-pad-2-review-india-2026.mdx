## Display — The Best Under ₹16,000

The Realme Pad 2's 11.5-inch 2K (1200×2000) IPS display is the most-cited reason to choose it over the Redmi Pad SE:

**2K resolution:** 203 PPI on an 11.5-inch screen — text is sharp, website images are clean, and NCERT textbook PDFs are comfortable to read without zooming in. The Redmi Pad SE at 206 PPI is similar in density but has a smaller 11-inch screen, making the Realme Pad 2's 11.5-inch larger effective reading area a meaningful advantage.

**Colour accuracy:** Realme's display calibration is good for the price — sRGB coverage is adequate, and the panel has no obvious colour shifting when viewed at angles.

**Brightness:** The display peaks around 400 nits — adequate for indoor use. Direct sunlight viewing is limited, as with most tablets in this price range.

---

## Performance — Helio G99 Delivers

The MediaTek Helio G99 is one of the best value chips in Android tablets:

**Daily performance:**
- YouTube, Netflix, Prime Video — smooth and fast
- Google Classroom, Zoom, Google Meet — no issues
- BGMI at medium settings — 30fps stable, occasional dips at high settings
- Genshin Impact at medium — 30fps with occasional stutters
- WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok — instant response

**Multi-app use:** 6GB RAM (recommended variant) allows comfortable multi-app switching. 4GB RAM variants reload apps more frequently during heavy use — get the 6GB version for ₹1,000 more.

**Sustained performance:** The Helio G99 manages heat well — no significant throttling during 1-hour gaming sessions. The Realme Pad 2 stays warm but not hot under sustained load.

---

## 33W DART Charge — A Genuine Differentiator

This is the Realme Pad 2's most practical advantage over the Redmi Pad SE (18W charging):

| Charger | 0→50% | Full charge |
|---|---|---|
| Realme Pad 2 (33W) | ~35 minutes | ~80 minutes |
| Redmi Pad SE (18W) | ~60 minutes | ~130 minutes |

**Why this matters:** Students who wake up with a dead or low battery can get a meaningful charge in 30–35 minutes before morning classes. The Redmi Pad SE would take twice as long for the same charge level. This is genuinely useful.

---

## Audio — Quad Dolby Atmos Speakers

Like the Redmi Pad SE, the Realme Pad 2 has four Dolby Atmos speakers. Side-by-side, the audio quality is similar — both are excellent for the price range. The larger 11.5-inch body gives the Realme Pad 2 slightly larger speaker grilles, which translates to marginally louder maximum volume and slightly deeper bass.

---

## Realme UI for Pad — Cleaner than MIUI

Realme's UI is based on ColorOS — considered cleaner than MIUI for Pad with less aggressive ads and system notifications. The split-screen multitasking implementation is good, and the interface feels responsive with the Helio G99.

**Note:** Realme UI still has some pre-installed apps that can be uninstalled — a 10-minute cleanup after initial setup provides a cleaner experience.
