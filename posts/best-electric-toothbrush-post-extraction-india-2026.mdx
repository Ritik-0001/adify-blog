## Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes After Tooth Extraction

**The extraction healing timeline:**
- **Day 0–1 (day of extraction and first day):** Rest the area completely. Gentle rinse with warm saline only. No brushing near the socket. No toothbrush at all near the area.
- **Day 1–3:** Manual soft brush only, carefully avoiding the extraction site. Do not introduce electric brush yet — vibration transmitted through the jawbone can disturb the forming blood clot.
- **Day 3–4:** Electric brushing can resume for all teeth AWAY from the extraction site. Start on the gentlest mode/intensity. Do not place the brush head within one tooth-width of the socket.
- **Week 1–2:** Progress to normal electric brushing of all other teeth. Gently clean around (not directly in) the socket area as healing progresses. Follow dentist's specific instructions.
- **Day 7–10:** Most extractions have healed sufficiently for normal brushing to resume near the socket, if healing is progressing normally.

**Critical: What NOT to do post-extraction:**
- **No sucking or spitting forcefully** — negative oral pressure (from sucking on a straw, spitting, or even aggressive rinsing) can dislodge the blood clot
- **No smoking** — nicotine constricts blood vessels, preventing clot formation; smoking causes 3× more dry socket cases
- **No rinsing forcefully for 24 hours** — gentle rinsing only from day 2
- **No electric brush directly on the socket** — for at least 7 days

**Dry socket prevention:** Dry socket (alveolar osteitis) occurs when the blood clot in the extraction socket is dislodged or fails to form, exposing bone. It causes severe pain from day 2–4 post-extraction. Risk factors: smoking, contraceptive pill (hormonal), lower jaw extractions, forceful rinsing, and suction. If you experience worsening pain (not improving) on day 3–4, return to your dentist immediately.

**Wisdom tooth extractions:** Wisdom tooth (third molar) removal is more complex — swelling, trismus (limited mouth opening), and longer healing are expected. Delay electric brushing return to Day 5 for wisdom tooth extractions. The adjacent second molar needs careful cleaning — food trapping here is common and increases infection risk.

**India-specific context:** Many Indians have extractions performed without receiving detailed post-operative instructions — and without clear guidance on resuming oral hygiene. This buying guide fills that gap: maintain oral hygiene everywhere except the socket, resume electric brushing on Day 3–4, and follow the gradual return protocol above.
