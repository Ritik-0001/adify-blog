## DGCA Drone Registration in India 2026

India's DGCA regulates drones under the Drone Rules 2021:

**Nano (under 250g):** DJI Mini 4 Pro falls here. Registration via Digital Sky Platform required. Operator training not mandatory for recreational use.

**Micro (250g–2kg):** DJI Air 3, Mavic 3 Pro. Drone must be registered on DIGI SKY portal. Remote Pilot Certificate required for commercial use.

**No-fly zones:** DGCA specifies permanent no-fly zones (airports, government buildings, borders). DJI's drones have geo-fencing built-in that prevents flight in restricted areas.

**Green zone (no permission needed):** Areas outside restricted zones up to 120m altitude.

**Yellow zone:** Requires permission via Digital Sky Portal.

Registration is straightforward via the Digital Sky app — takes 30–60 minutes.

## DJI Drone Tips for Indian Conditions

**Summer heat:** Batteries discharge faster above 35°C. Carry extra batteries for summer shoots.

**Monsoon:** Do not fly in rain or high humidity. DJI drones are not waterproof.

**Hill stations:** Higher altitude = thinner air = reduced lift. DJI Mini 4 Pro and Air 3 fly fine up to 4000m (most Indian hill stations are under 2500m).

**Battery:** Indian power (240V) works with DJI chargers. Always use official DJI chargers.
