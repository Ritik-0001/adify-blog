## Glucometer vs CGM: What Is the Difference?

This is the most important thing to understand before buying any blood sugar monitor in 2026.

**Traditional Glucometer (SMBG — Self-Monitoring Blood Glucose):**
- Requires a finger prick to get a drop of blood
- Gives a single reading at that moment
- Test takes 5 seconds
- Cost: ₹349–₹1,499 for the meter + ₹12–₹15 per test strip
- Examples: Accu-Chek Active, Accu-Chek Instant, OneTouch Select Plus, Dr. Morepen BG-03

**Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM):**
- A small sensor worn on the arm (or abdomen)
- Measures glucose in the interstitial fluid under the skin every 1–15 minutes
- No finger prick needed for readings (some CGMs still require occasional calibration pricks)
- Gives continuous trend data — you can see glucose rising or falling in real time
- Cost: ₹4,000–₹7,000 per sensor, which lasts 10–14 days
- Examples: Freestyle Libre 3, Dexcom G7 (limited India availability)

The CGM sounds better — and for many patients, it is. But the cost difference is significant: a traditional glucometer costs roughly ₹360–₹450/month in strips at 2 tests/day. A CGM costs approximately ₹12,000–₹18,000/month in sensors. That is 30–40x more expensive for continuous monitoring.

## Who Needs a CGM vs a Standard Glucometer?

This is the key decision most Indian patients face.

**You likely need a standard glucometer if:**
- You have Type 2 diabetes managed with diet, exercise, or oral medication
- You test once or twice daily to track trends
- Budget is a consideration (strips cost ₹400–₹700/month vs ₹10,000–₹18,000/month for CGM)
- Your doctor has not specifically recommended continuous monitoring

**You should consider Freestyle Libre 3 if:**
- You have Type 1 diabetes or are on insulin therapy
- You experience hypoglycemia (dangerously low blood sugar) episodes — a CGM alerts you before a crisis
- You want to understand exactly how specific foods affect your glucose (CGM shows the full curve after a meal, not just a snapshot)
- Your employer, insurer, or doctor is covering the cost
- You are a professional athlete or someone who needs precise glucose control

**The honest cost reality:** For a Type 2 diabetic on oral medication who tests twice daily, a standard glucometer at ₹12–₹15/test is perfectly appropriate. The Freestyle Libre 3 at ₹10,000–₹13,000/month is a major ongoing expense that most Indian patients pay out-of-pocket since health insurance rarely covers CGM sensors fully.

## What Is "Time in Range" and Why CGMs Track It Better?

Traditional glucometers give point-in-time snapshots. You might test at 7am and at 9pm and see normal readings — but you have no idea what happened at 2pm after lunch or whether your glucose spiked to 250 mg/dL and came down.

CGMs measure "Time in Range" (TIR) — the percentage of time your glucose stays within the target range (typically 70–180 mg/dL for most diabetics). Research shows that higher TIR correlates with lower HbA1c and reduced diabetes complications.

The Freestyle Libre 3 app shows your TIR as a daily and weekly percentage. This is clinically meaningful data that a twice-daily finger-prick glucometer simply cannot provide.
