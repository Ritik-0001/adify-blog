## How Much Coverage Do You Actually Need?

Before buying, you need an honest calculation of your home's effective WiFi coverage requirement. The key variable in Indian homes is **construction material** — and it dramatically reduces router range.

**The 50% Rule for Indian Concrete Construction:**

Most Indian homes — especially flats and apartments built after 2000 — use reinforced concrete construction with 4–8 inch thick concrete walls and concrete ceiling slabs between floors. WiFi signals lose significant strength passing through these materials.

- **Open air:** 100% of rated range
- **One thin drywall/wood partition:** 85–90% of rated range
- **One standard brick wall:** 60–70% of rated range
- **One concrete wall:** 40–55% of rated range
- **Concrete ceiling slab (between floors):** 20–40% of rated range

**Practical calculation:**

A router rated for 2,500 sq ft in open air might only cover **1,000–1,300 sq ft** in a typical Indian concrete flat after wall losses. A 3BHK flat of 1,400–1,600 sq ft often needs two devices — either a mesh system or a router plus node — to cover every room reliably.

| Home Size | Configuration | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|---|
| 1BHK (600–800 sq ft) | Compact, 1–2 walls | Single good router (Archer C6) |
| 2BHK (900–1,200 sq ft) | 3–4 rooms, concrete walls | Single router (Archer AX23 or RT-AX55) placed centrally |
| 3BHK (1,400–1,800 sq ft) | 5–6 rooms, 2 floors possible | Mesh 2-pack or RT-AX88U centrally placed |
| 4BHK+ / Duplex (2,000+ sq ft) | Multiple floors, many walls | Mesh 2-pack or 3-pack |
| Bungalow / Villa (3,000+ sq ft) | Multiple floors, large area | Mesh 3-pack |

## Mesh vs Single High-Power Router for Large Homes

This is the central decision for large-home buyers.

**Single high-power router (like Asus RT-AX88U):**
- **Pros:** One device, simpler setup, no backhaul concerns, lower cost
- **Cons:** Signal still weakens through walls and floors — the laws of physics apply regardless of how powerful the router is. One router can only do so much in a multi-floor concrete structure.
- **Best for:** Large single-floor homes (bungalow ground floor, large open-plan spaces) where there are fewer solid walls

**Mesh system (like Deco XE75):**
- **Pros:** Each node covers its zone fully — you get strong signal everywhere, not just near the router
- **Cons:** More expensive, wireless backhaul reduces bandwidth if not using ethernet between nodes
- **Best for:** Multi-floor homes, homes with many concrete walls, 3BHK and larger flats in standard Indian apartment buildings

**The honest answer for most Indian homes:** A mesh system is almost always the right choice for homes above 1,500 sq ft with typical Indian concrete construction. A single powerful router will cover the area near it well and the far end poorly — the coverage map is uneven. A mesh system delivers consistent signal everywhere.

## Node Placement for Maximum Coverage

For a 3BHK flat:
- **Node 1:** Near the ISP entry point / main living room. Connect to your ISP's ONT/modem with an ethernet cable.
- **Node 2:** In the bedroom corridor or at the far end of the flat — roughly two-thirds of the distance from Node 1 to the farthest point.

For a duplex / 2-floor home:
- **Node 1:** Ground floor, central location
- **Node 2:** Upper floor, central location
- Run an ethernet cable between floors if possible — this eliminates wireless backhaul speed loss and is worth the installation cost
