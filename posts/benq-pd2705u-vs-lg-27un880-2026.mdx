The 4K USB-C monitor space for creative professionals has two dominant choices: the BenQ PD2705U and the LG 27UN880. Both are 27-inch IPS panels with 4K resolution and USB-C connectivity for laptop users. Here's which one to buy.

---

## Overview

The **BenQ PD2705U** is BenQ's designer-focused 4K monitor — factory calibrated, sRGB/DCI-P3 accurate, with excellent ergonomics and a KVM switch. The **LG 27UN880** (Ergo) is LG's USB-C 4K monitor with a distinctive spring-arm stand that clamps to your desk for unmatched positioning flexibility.

## Colour Accuracy

The PD2705U ships with a factory calibration report and Delta E &lt;2 performance out of the box — critical for designers, photographers, and video editors where colour accuracy affects deliverables. Hardware calibration support is included.

The LG 27UN880 covers 95% DCI-P3 and looks excellent but is not factory calibrated. For casual creative work it's fine; for print production or professional colour grading, you'd want to calibrate it independently.

---

## Stand & Ergonomics

The LG 27UN880's spring-arm Ergo stand is its defining feature. It clamps to your desk edge and allows the monitor to swing, raise, lower, tilt, and rotate with minimal effort — no desk real estate consumed by a base. For small desks, this is a significant space advantage.

The BenQ PD2705U's traditional stand is excellent with full height, tilt, swivel, and pivot adjustment. It's stable and smooth but occupies desk space. VESA mount compatible if you want an arm later.

---

## Connectivity

BenQ PD2705U is the winner here: 96W USB-C power delivery charges a 16" MacBook Pro, Thunderbolt 4 input, KVM switch (connect two computers and switch with one button), and 4× USB-A ports.

LG 27UN880 offers 60W USB-C PD (sufficient for 13–14" laptops, not ideal for 16"), no Thunderbolt, no KVM, and 2× USB-A.

For desk setups with multiple computers or a 16" MacBook: BenQ is clearly superior.

---

## Pros & Cons

### BenQ PD2705U
**Pros:** Factory calibrated (Delta E &lt;2), 96W USB-C powers larger laptops, KVM switch, 4× USB-A hub, hardware calibration support
**Cons:** Traditional stand takes desk space, slightly more expensive, less portable ergonomic positioning

### LG 27UN880
**Pros:** Ergo spring arm saves significant desk space, good colour coverage, LG IPS quality, lower price
**Cons:** Only 60W USB-C (may not charge 16" MacBook Pro fully), no KVM, no factory calibration, fewer USB ports

## Who Should Buy Which?

**Buy the BenQ PD2705U if:**
- Colour accuracy is critical for professional work
- You have two computers and want KVM switching
- You use a 15-16" laptop that needs full power delivery
- You want factory calibration out of the box

**Buy the LG 27UN880 if:**
- Desk space is limited and the Ergo arm solves a real problem
- Your laptop is 13-14" and 60W PD is sufficient
- You do creative work but not professional colour production
- You want to save ₹3,000

---

## Final Verdict

For professional designers and photographers: **BenQ PD2705U** — the factory calibration and 96W power delivery justify the modest premium.

For productivity-focused users and those with space constraints: **LG 27UN880** — the Ergo stand is genuinely transformative for small desks and the colour performance is more than adequate for most creative work.

Both are excellent 4K IPS monitors. Choose based on whether accuracy or ergonomic flexibility is your priority.
