Dry mouth (xerostomia) — caused by medications, medical conditions, mouth breathing, or dehydration — significantly increases cavity risk because saliva is the mouth's natural acid buffer. Electric toothbrushes are particularly important for dry mouth sufferers because superior plaque removal compensates partially for reduced salivary protection.

---

## Why Dry Mouth Increases Cavity Risk

Saliva neutralises the acids produced by bacteria after eating. Without adequate saliva:
- Acid remains on tooth surfaces longer
- Remineralisation slows (saliva delivers calcium and phosphate ions)
- Cavity formation accelerates — especially at gumlines and between teeth

**Indian dry mouth causes:** Antihypertensives, antidepressants, and antihistamines are among the most commonly prescribed medications in India — all are major dry mouth causes. Diabetic patients (India has one of the world's highest diabetic populations) have elevated dry mouth rates.

---

## What Dry Mouth Patients Need in an Electric Toothbrush

**Soft bristles:** Dry mouth causes gum recession over time. Soft bristles prevent additional gum trauma.

**Gentle/Sensitive mode:** Low-intensity cleaning mode for tender or receding gum areas.

**Pressure sensor:** Extra-light touch is required — dry mouth often comes with medication-induced gum sensitivity.

**Compatibility with high-fluoride toothpaste:** Dry mouth patients often use prescription fluoride toothpaste (5,000 ppm) — the brush should work effectively with low-foam high-fluoride formulas.

**2-minute timer:** Ensuring full 2-minute brushing is more important for dry mouth patients — thoroughness compensates for reduced salivary protection.

## Toothpaste Pairing for Dry Mouth

Dry mouth patients should use:
- **High fluoride (standard 1,450 ppm):** Sensodyne Pronamel, Colgate Total
- **Prescription fluoride (5,000 ppm):** If recommended by dentist — ask about Fluor Protector Gel available through dental clinics in India
- **Avoid:** High-RDA whitening toothpastes (accelerate enamel loss in dry mouth)
- **Avoid:** Alcohol-containing mouthwashes (worsen dry mouth)

---

## Additional Dry Mouth Management

- **Sip water frequently during the day** — India's heat accelerates dehydration-related dry mouth
- **Sugar-free xylitol gum:** Stimulates saliva flow — xylitol gum available in India (imported brands in pharmacies)
- **Brush after every meal** — not just twice daily — when cavity risk is elevated
- **Night guard consideration:** Night mouth breathing worsens dry mouth — discuss with dentist
