## Surface Stains vs Intrinsic Stains: What Electric Brushes Actually Fix

This distinction is critical for setting realistic expectations.

**Extrinsic (surface) stains** — from tea, coffee, tobacco, food colouring, pan masala — sit on or just inside the enamel surface layer. Electric toothbrushes with whitening modes and whitening brush heads can visibly remove these. Results are typically noticeable in 2–4 weeks of consistent use.

**Intrinsic (internal) stains** — from tetracycline antibiotics (historically over-prescribed in India), fluorosis (excess fluoride during tooth development — common in parts of Rajasthan, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh), or old dental work — cannot be addressed by any toothbrush. These require professional bleaching, veneers, or crowns.

**Pan and tobacco stains** — these fall in a challenging middle ground. Heavy, long-term tobacco staining penetrates into the enamel micropores. Electric toothbrushes will reduce and prevent new accumulation but will not fully reverse years of deeply set tobacco pigmentation. Professional polishing is needed first; then an electric toothbrush maintains the result.
