## Electric Brushes and Indian Acne — What to Avoid

**Rotating bristle brushes on active acne:** Standard rotating bristle brushes spread P. acnes bacteria from active acne spots to clear skin — contraindicated for Indian acne-prone skin with active breakouts.

**High-intensity on inflamed skin:** High-speed cleansing on inflamed Indian skin increases redness and may rupture active pustules — always use lowest setting on acne-inflamed areas.

**Non-hygienic brush heads:** Bristle brushes retain bacteria between uses — in Indian humidity, bacteria multiply rapidly. Using an unwashed bristle brush on acne-prone Indian skin reintroduces bacteria.

**Safe options:** Silicone heads (non-porous, no bacteria retention), sonic vibration (gentle pressure), and UV-sanitising brush options.

## Acne-Safe Electric Face Brush Protocol for India

1. **Cleanse first pass** — micellar water removes surface contamination and makeup
2. **Electric brush on lowest speed** — 60 seconds with gentle face wash, avoid active breakout spots
3. **Rinse thoroughly** — Indian tap water residue on face can cause breakouts; finish with cool water
4. **Sterilise brush head** — alcohol wipe for silicone heads after each use
5. **No sharing** — never share electric face brushes, especially acne-prone Indian skin
