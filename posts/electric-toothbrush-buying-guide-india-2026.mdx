## Oscillating vs Sonic — The First Decision

**Oscillating-rotating (Oral-B):** The brush head spins back and forth (oscillates) while also rotating. The mechanical action physically dislodges plaque from tooth surfaces and gumlines. The sensation is firm and mechanical.

**Sonic (Philips Sonicare, Oclean, Mi):** The brush head vibrates at 31,000–62,000 strokes/minute. At these frequencies, fluid dynamics (pressurised water/toothpaste) do much of the cleaning — reaching between teeth without direct bristle contact. The sensation is smooth and fluid.

**Which is better clinically:** Independent meta-analyses (Cochrane, 2014) show oscillating brushes remove slightly more plaque from tooth surfaces; sonic brushes are more effective for gumline and interdental cleaning. The difference is small — technique and consistency matter more than technology choice.

**The practical recommendation:**
- Choose oscillating (Oral-B) if: you have heavy surface staining, significant plaque buildup, or prefer a firm mechanical sensation
- Choose sonic (Philips/Oclean) if: you have sensitive gums, gum disease, or prefer a gentler cleaning sensation

## The 5 Features That Matter

### 1. Pressure Sensor (Most Important)
Over-brushing — pressing too hard — causes gum recession and enamel erosion, both irreversible. A pressure sensor alerts you (light, pause, or vibration) when brushing too hard.

**Minimum budget for pressure sensor:** ₹1,999 (Philips Sonicare 4300 or Oral-B Pro 2)

### 2. 2-Minute Timer
The recommended brushing duration. Most Indians brush for under 60 seconds. A built-in 2-minute timer with 30-second quadrant alerts (beep every 30 seconds to move to next quadrant) is clinically effective at improving brushing duration.

**Available from:** ₹799 (Oral-B Vitality 100)

### 3. Cleaning Modes
Multiple modes (Daily Clean, Sensitive, Whitening, Gum Care) allow adjusting brush intensity for different needs. For general daily use, Daily Clean mode is sufficient. Sensitive mode matters for gum-sensitive users.

**Minimum budget for 2+ modes:** ₹1,999

### 4. Battery Type and Life
**Rechargeable (recommended):** NiMH or Lithium batteries — charge every 2–4 weeks. More economical long-term than battery replacement.
**Battery-powered:** AA or AAA batteries — cheaper upfront, more expensive over time. No pressure sensor available.

**Recommended minimum:** Rechargeable at ₹1,299 (Mi T300) or ₹1,599 (Oclean F1)

### 5. Brush Head Availability
Replacement heads need replacement every 3 months. Check that your chosen brand's heads are available in your city or on Amazon India. Oral-B heads are available in virtually every Indian pharmacy. Philips heads are available in major cities and online. Generic brand heads may not be available locally.

## What NOT to Buy

- **Generic unbranded brushes:** No clinical validation, no reliable pressure sensors, heads often incompatible with standard sizes
- **Brushes without a timer:** The 2-minute timer is the most impactful feature — skip any brush that lacks it
- **Very cheap battery-powered brushes:** AAA-powered brushes have inconsistent voltage as battery drains — cleaning effectiveness declines mid-charge
