## Why 10,000mAh Is the Sweet Spot for Most People

The power bank market has two big capacity camps: 10K mAh and 20K mAh. Here is why 10K wins for most everyday users in India:

**Weight:** A 10K power bank weighs 180–230g. A 20K unit weighs 380–450g. That is the difference between forgetting it is in your pocket and feeling it every step.

**Size:** A quality 10K unit (like Anker PowerCore 10000) fits in a jeans pocket. A 20K unit fits in a bag pocket.

**Daily use coverage:** Most Indian smartphones have 4,500–5,000mAh batteries. A 10K unit gives you 1.6–2 full charges — enough to cover a full day of heavy use or a short trip.

**Price:** Good 10K units start at ₹800. Good 20K units start at ₹1,500. The extra ₹700–₹1,500 buys capacity you may never use.

**The only reason to go 20K:** If you regularly spend 2+ days away from power or need to charge multiple devices. Otherwise, 10K is the practical choice for 90% of users.

## Should You Buy a 10K Power Bank With or Without USB-C Output?

Most 10K power banks under ₹1,500 use USB-A output only. USB-C output at 10K mAh starts appearing from ₹1,200 onwards, but is more common in the ₹1,500–₹2,000 range.

**USB-A only is fine if:** You use standard USB-A cables (the majority of Android cables in India are still Micro-USB or USB-A to USB-C).

**USB-C output matters if:** You own an iPhone 15 or newer (USB-C port), a MacBook or iPad, or you want faster PD charging for compatible Android phones.

The Anker PowerCore 10000 uses USB-A output only at its standard price point. For USB-C PD output in the 10K size, look at the Anker PowerCore Essential 20000 PD (if you can stretch to ₹2,500+) or similar.
