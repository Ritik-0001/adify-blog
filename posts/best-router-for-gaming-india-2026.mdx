## What Actually Matters for Gaming Routers (It's Not Speed)

The most common misconception about gaming routers is that **speed is the most important factor**. It is not. Here is what actually affects your gaming experience:

**Latency (Ping) — This is what matters most**
Latency is the time it takes for a data packet to travel from your device to the game server and back. A 50Mbps connection with 20ms ping plays better than a 500Mbps connection with 80ms ping for online gaming. Your router affects latency in these ways:
- Processing efficiency (good routers add less delay)
- QoS (prioritising gaming packets over background downloads)
- Band selection (5GHz generally has lower latency than 2.4GHz on wireless)

**Jitter — Consistency of latency**
Jitter is variation in ping. A consistent 40ms is far better for gaming than ping that fluctuates between 20ms and 120ms. Jitter is caused by network congestion — when your family is downloading and streaming while you are gaming, background traffic creates jitter. QoS solves this.

**Speed — Mostly irrelevant above 10Mbps**
Online gaming consumes 3–10Mbps. BGMI, COD Mobile, and Valorant all work fine on a 20Mbps connection. The router's speed spec almost never affects gaming — unless someone else on the network is saturating the connection.

**What a gaming router actually does:**
A good gaming router uses **Quality of Service (QoS)** to identify gaming packets and send them first, even when other devices are downloading or streaming. This prevents your ping from spiking to 200ms when someone starts a YouTube video.

## Wired vs Wireless for Gaming

**Wired connection (ethernet) is always better for gaming.** Period.

On a wired connection:
- Latency is typically 1–5ms lower than WiFi
- Jitter is near zero
- No interference from neighbouring networks
- No signal fluctuation from physical movement

If your gaming setup allows it, run an ethernet cable from your router to your PC or console. The ₹200 cable investment pays off more than a ₹10,000 gaming router upgrade.

**When wireless gaming is acceptable:**
- Mobile gaming (BGMI, COD Mobile) — acceptable on 5GHz WiFi
- Casual gaming where a cable is not practical
- When the router has good QoS to manage wireless traffic

For wireless gaming, always use the 5GHz band. On 2.4GHz, interference from neighbouring networks creates unpredictable latency spikes — something no router can fully prevent.

## Gaming Router Buying Checklist

Before buying, confirm:
- [ ] Do you game wirelessly or wired? If wired, QoS matters less — any Gigabit router suffices
- [ ] Do others in your household share the connection while you game? If yes, prioritise QoS
- [ ] What games do you play? Most games need under 10Mbps and under 50ms ping — check your current ping first
- [ ] Is your internet ping already good (under 30ms)? If your ISP is the bottleneck, a gaming router cannot fix that
