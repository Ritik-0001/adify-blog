## What Does the ₹10,000 Budget Unlock?

Moving from the sub-₹5,000 to the sub-₹10,000 bracket is a significant upgrade in home theatre. Here is what the extra money buys you:

**Better bass and total power:** Systems in this range deliver 80W–120W RMS versus 40W in the budget tier. The difference is immediately audible — bass hits harder, dialogue sounds fuller, and the system can fill a medium-sized room (150–250 sq ft) without distortion.

**HDMI ARC connectivity:** This is the big one. HDMI ARC (Audio Return Channel) lets your TV send audio to the speaker system through the same HDMI cable used for video. You get digital audio quality and — critically — you can control the speaker volume with your TV remote. The boAt AAVANTE 2.1 supports HDMI ARC on its updated variants.

**Optical input (TOSLINK):** Several systems in this range include a digital optical input, which delivers cleaner audio than a 3.5mm AUX connection. The Philips SPA8000B specifically supports optical input.

**Better build quality:** Satellite speakers feel sturdier, subwoofer enclosures are larger and better-tuned, and overall fit and finish improves noticeably.

## 2.1 System vs Soundbar: Which Is Better Under ₹10,000?

This is a common question. Here is the honest breakdown:

**2.1 system advantages:**
- More total bass output — a dedicated subwoofer placed on the floor delivers low-end that a slim soundbar cannot match
- Usually more power for the money
- Subwoofer placement flexibility (you can put it in a corner for more bass reinforcement)

**Soundbar advantages:**
- Single unit, clean cable management
- Looks better under a wall-mounted TV
- No satellite speaker wires to manage

At the ₹10,000 budget, a 2.1 system like the boAt AAVANTE 2.1 will almost always sound bigger and more impressive than a similarly priced soundbar. Soundbars start making more sense at the ₹15,000+ bracket where HDMI eARC, Dolby Atmos, and multi-driver arrays become available.

**Our recommendation:** If you do not mind the extra cables and want the best sound for your money under ₹10,000, go with the 2.1 system. If clean aesthetics matter more than audio performance, look at the boAt Aavante Bar series soundbars in the ₹5,000–₹8,000 range.

## Connecting Your System to a Modern TV

Most buyers in this bracket have a 43–55 inch smart TV. Here is how to connect your 2.1 system optimally:

1. **HDMI ARC (best):** Connect an HDMI cable from your TV's ARC-labelled port to the system's HDMI ARC input. Enable "HDMI ARC" in your TV's sound settings. Your TV remote will now control volume.
2. **Optical (second best):** Connect a TOSLINK optical cable from your TV's optical output to the system's optical input. Set TV audio output to "External Speaker" or "Optical" in settings.
3. **AUX 3.5mm (fallback):** Use a 3.5mm cable from your TV's headphone jack. Works on almost any TV but audio quality is slightly lower than digital connections.
