The Logitech C920 HD Pro launched in 2012. It's 2026, and it's still one of the most recommended webcams in the world. Either Logitech got it right the first time, or the webcam market hasn't advanced much. The truth is both.

---

## Overview

The C920 captures Full HD 1080p video at 30fps using a glass lens and Logitech's image processor. It has dual stereo mics, a universal clip mount, and works plug-and-play on Windows and Mac. No drivers, no setup — plug in USB and it works.

---

## Key Specifications

- **Resolution:** 1080p/30fps (video), 15MP photo
- **Lens:** Glass (not plastic), 78° diagonal FOV
- **Autofocus:** Yes, with light correction
- **Microphone:** Dual stereo mics with noise reduction
- **Interface:** USB-A (2.0)
- **Mount:** Universal clip (laptop/monitor/tripod)
- **Compatibility:** Windows 10/11, macOS, Chrome OS
- **Weight:** 162g
- **Price (India):** ₹7,999 MRP

---

## Video Quality

In a well-lit room, the C920 produces clean, sharp 1080p footage. Colours are accurate, skin tones are natural, and the glass lens resolves fine detail better than plastic-lens webcams at similar price points.

The automatic light correction handles mixed lighting (window + lamp) reasonably well — it adjusts exposure continuously to avoid blown highlights or crushed shadows. In consistently good lighting, it looks nearly as good as webcams costing twice as much.

Low light is the C920's weakness. Below roughly 200 lux (a dim room), the image gets grainy and colours shift. A ₹1,500 ring light solves this instantly — essential investment for serious WFH use.

---

## Autofocus

The autofocus is fast and accurate for stationary use (sitting at a desk). If you move significantly or lean back, it takes 1–2 seconds to refocus. For standard video calls, this is imperceptible. For active teaching or presentation where you move around, a fixed-focus webcam might serve better.

---

## Microphone

The dual stereo mics are adequate for video calls in quiet rooms. They pick up keyboard typing, ambient noise, and room echo more than a dedicated USB microphone. For important presentations or podcasting, use a separate USB microphone. For daily standups and video calls: perfectly fine.

---

## Software: Logi Tune

Logi Tune (Logitech's management app) lets you adjust field of view, zoom, brightness, contrast, and white balance. It's lightweight and works well on both Mac and Windows. Not required — the webcam works fine without it.
