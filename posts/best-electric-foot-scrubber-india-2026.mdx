## Cracked Heel Problem in India

**Why Indian heels crack:** India's floor culture — walking barefoot at home, on marble and stone floors, and removing footwear at temples and guest homes — concentrates pressure on heel skin without footwear cushioning. Combined with dry climate in North India winters, heels harden and crack.

**Traditional Indian solution:** Coconut oil or ghee application before sleep is the traditional Indian heel care method — softens skin but doesn't remove the hardened dead skin. Electric scrubbers remove the hard layer that oil cannot dissolve.

**Open chappals:** India's predominant footwear style (open-back chappals, kolhapuris, hawai chappals) exposes heels to dust, drying, and impact without the coverage of Western enclosed shoes — accelerating heel hardening.
